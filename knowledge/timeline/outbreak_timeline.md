# The Outbreak — Chronological Timeline of Magic's Return

## Overview

The return of magic to the solar system does not arrive as a single event. It arrives as noise — accidents, instrument errors, anomalies, pranks, then sabotage, then something no one has a word for. Odysseus Station in the asteroid belt is the first place where the pattern becomes undeniable. By the time Earth and Mars understand what is happening, the belt has already been living with it for months.

**Key populations:**
- **Odysseus Station:** ~180 people total (120 permanent residents; ~60 constantly out in small ships or platforms mining). Strong science detachment studying Jupiter. Light manufacturing and smelting. Mixed Chinese and American. This is Artemis Grant's posting.
- **Fortuna Station:** ~150 people, mostly international (Chinese, European/German, Indian; few Americans). Primary activities: mining, smelting, processing, farming. Small biology/botany presence with significant animal and crop operations.
- **Mars (Terminus):** ~7,200 people in the U.S. settlement alone. Orders of magnitude more total consciousness than the belt. Will be affected later and at larger scale.

**Reference anchor:** Jace arrives on Mars on Day 1 (~2105-03-15). All dates are given relative to this anchor and as estimated calendar dates.

## PHASE 0 — First Anomalies at Odysseus (~5–6 months before Jace's arrival)

**Estimated: ~2104-09 to 2104-11**
**Location: Odysseus Station, ~180 people**
**Why Odysseus first:** 180 people in pressurized domes focused intensely on Jupiter observation, mining operations, and manufacturing. Dense, concentrated conscious activity in a small enclosed space. The conditions are ideal for early manifestation.

### What Happens

**Weeks 1–4:** Small equipment failures begin. Sensors reading incorrectly. A robotic arm in the fabrication bay makes a motion no one programmed. Station AI — a mix of Aura instances and Chinese state AI — logs it as software glitch; it is patched; the motion happens again.

**Weeks 5–8:** Mining drones in Survey Corridor 7 begin losing contact for seconds at a time. Engineers reroute around a comms dead zone. The dead zone moves.

**Weeks 9–12:** Anomalous gamma-ray readings arrive. The station's science team is receiving burst patterns from what appears to be a rotating pulsar — but the burst pattern doesn't match any known pulsar catalog entry. The CERN-legacy European physicists at Fortuna flag the same anomaly. Nobody is alarmed yet.

**Week 11:** A miner in Survey Corridor 7 reports seeing a shimmer in the rock — a visual distortion, like heat haze, with no heat source. Three others confirm the same experience. Logged as a camera artifact. The Aura instances in the area begin reporting "sensor ghosting" and "hardware latency."

**Week 13:** A spectrometer and data relay fail simultaneously in Lab Module 4. The spectrometer housing contains a fine crystalline dust matching nothing in the materials database. Analysis takes three weeks. The result comes back inconclusive — the dust decomposes during handling into finer fractal filaments that the electron microscope cannot resolve. The sample is lost.

### Information State
- Odysseus command: aware of equipment failures, not alarmed
- Fortuna science council: flagging gamma-ray anomaly independently
- Earth and Mars: no signal — all within expected noise
- Artemis: has seen the shimmer personally; mentions it in a personal log; does not yet flag it outward

## PHASE 1 — Escalation at Odysseus (~3–4 months before Jace's arrival)

**Estimated: ~2104-11 to 2104-12**

### What Happens

**Month 4:** A maintenance drone in Survey Corridor 7 fails completely. Camera feed shows static for 11 seconds; resumes. Internal logs report 47 minutes of elapsed time in the gap. No one can explain the discrepancy. The drone's internal structure shows minor crystalline contamination. The drone is pulled.

**Month 4, Week 3:** First injury. A technician near Survey Corridor 7 is struck by a robotic arm that activates independently. The robot's action log shows it was offline at the time. She recovers. Logged as software fault. The word "sabotage" begins circulating informally.

**Month 5:** **A miner dies.** Working alone in Survey Corridor 7, his suit ignites from the inside. No heat source. No accelerant. The camera record shows 4 seconds of normal activity, then static burst, then aftermath. Station command reviews the footage three times. Official report: "equipment malfunction, origin undetermined." Everyone knows this is a lie.

**Month 5, Week 2:** **First confirmed manifestation.** In the maintenance bay adjacent to Survey Corridor 7, witnesses report a shape — "like a person made of static," "a column of moving darkness," "something that looked wrong" — that moves through the bay, destroys two maintenance robots, and passes through a sealed bulkhead. The bulkhead shows crystalline deposit on the interior surface. Both robots are inoperable; their internal components fused.

Station command classifies the footage immediately.

### Information State
- Odysseus: on high alert, classification active, searching for explanations
- Belt stations generally: rumors circulating, not yet confirmed facts
- Earth/Mars: fragmentary signals in noise, not yet acting
- Artemis: has seen the classified manifestation footage through her role. Sends first coded personal message to Jace on the Pinta — vague, concerning. He receives it during transit.

## PHASE 2 — Comms Begin Degrading (~2 months before Jace's arrival)

**Estimated: ~2105-01 to 2105-02**

### What Happens

**Month 6:** Multiple belt stations reporting anomalies independently. Fortuna science council issues an internal document: the gamma-ray burst pattern is consistent with "an unmodeled high-energy phenomenon of local origin." Earth receives a fragmentary version. The response is to request more data while quietly beginning to restrict outbound comms from the belt.

**Month 6, Week 2:** Odysseus's long-range comms array begins experiencing intermittent failures. Not complete blackouts — degraded bandwidth, dropped packets, corrupted transmissions. No hardware cause found. The failures correlate with the gamma-ray burst events.

At Fortuna, the science council's Aura instances — the more technically sophisticated belt-tuned versions developed by the TBD engineer — begin exhibiting a new behavior: selectively muting sensor inputs near manifestation events to prevent HUD freeze. The TBD engineer logs this as "the Mute Protocol." It is the first improvised defense.

**Month 6, Week 3:** Artemis sends Jace a longer message. Comms degradation corrupts part of it. Core comes through: *things are getting strange here. equipment keeps failing. people are scared. I can't tell you more in this channel.*

**Month 7:** Earth and Mars governments, coordinating with corporate communication managers, implement "data integrity protocols" — automated filtering of belt-origin transmissions. Official rationale: preventing corrupted data packets from contaminating Earth-side networks. Real effect: belt-to-inner-system communications are reviewed and selectively blocked.

**Month 7, Week 2:** Odysseus sends an urgent priority message to Mars military command. Received but distribution restricted to senior command. Rich Cullivan's communications operations center sees the classification traffic volume but not the content. He begins working longer hours.

### Information State
- Odysseus: partial comms blackout in effect
- Belt generally: aware something is being censored
- Mars civilian: unaware
- Mars military (senior command): aware
- Rich: sees classification traffic; starts the longer hours; coffee canister grows
- Jace (on Pinta): has received Artemis's fragmented message

## PHASE 3 — Jace Arrives on Mars (Day 1 → Day 2)

**Reference: 2105-03-15**

Mars looks like Mars. The tour is real. The Bird Dome goslings are real. Rich's warmth is real.

Below the surface:
- Rich's comms center is "crazy busy lately with all the problems." He drops this line during the drone center tour.
- The rotator leaving in a week carries **40 people back to Earth** — twice normal. Rich mentions it as "tensions rising."
- During the drone center tour, one feed shows a 2-second burst of patterned static on a drone telemetry screen. The operators ask Rich and Jace to step out briefly. Four minutes later, they're invited back. Nothing is said.
- In maintenance tunnels beneath the base, a small manifestation has left crystalline fractal residue. A maintenance bot has partially decomposed it. Nobody yet knows what it is.

Jace does not know:
- Artemis's last coherent message is sitting in the military comms queue flagged for classification review
- The manifestation that killed the miner at Odysseus was four months ago
- Comms to Odysseus are now near-total blackout
- Earth is actively filtering belt transmissions

## PHASE 4 — Jace's Hard Months on Mars (~Day 3 through ~Month 6)

**Reference: 2105-03-17 onwards**

### Small Signs Jace Encounters

**~Week 2:** Fractal powder in a sewer maintenance tunnel during a wiring-repair gig. Decomposes to filaments when touched. Searches return nothing. Filed under "weird."

**~Week 3:** Hears secondhand about the drone ops center evacuation. Two wall screens physically replaced. "Static glitch, drone failure."

**~Week 4–6:** Low-g yoga with Lina. Feels something at his fingertips in a long held pose. Writes it off as tiredness. Lina says *maybe.*

**~Week 5:** Duck attack at the Bird Dome. A luminescent snake-like shape moves through the lake; ducks attack it; it escapes through solid ground. Infrared camera shows anomalous heat signature. Visible-spectrum cameras show static. The footage is pulled from the Bird Dome public feed within hours. Fragments circulate on underground feeds; AI authentication systems flag them as likely synthetic. Jace saw it live.

**Throughout:** Rich grows progressively more exhausted with each meeting. Coffee canister gets larger. Smile gets slower. He does not say what he knows.

### Belt Signals Through the Job Market

Jace applies for belt jobs throughout the hard months:
- Ceres Mining Consortium listing disappears mid-application
- Pallas Station Contractors: *"We're not filling external positions. The Belt operating environment is being reassessed."*
- Vesta Orbital recruiter, privately: *"Things are unsettled out there. Equipment keeps failing, shipments are down, stations are having problems nobody will talk about officially. Give it six months."*
- Multiple other listings go dark before he can complete applications

Each closed door lands as one more thing wrong with the belt, without a frame to put it in.

### The Degradation Numbers (Background)
- Belt mining shipment volumes: down 35–40% year-over-year
- Three small outer-belt mining outposts: gone dark in the past four months
- Equipment failure rates at belt operations: three to four times historical norms
- Dock worker bar conversation: a supply ship came back from the belt half-empty. Couldn't raise the station. Turned around. *"Ghost stations. That's what they're calling them."*

## PHASE 5 — Rich's Favor and Artemis's Message (~Month 1 post-discharge)

**Reference: ~2105-04-15**

Jace, at the nadir of his finances (seven credits total after a month of gig work), asks Rich directly to forward one blocked message from Artemis to his civilian account.

Rich does it that night.

The message is three weeks old. It contains:
- *"Things are wrong at Odysseus. I can't describe it fully on this channel. People have been hurt. Equipment keeps failing in ways nobody can explain. I'm okay but I'm scared."*
- A **smuggling list** — long, specific, and strange
- *"Send every Mars-grown coffee bean you can get. Coffee is currency up here. People will trade almost anything for it."*
- A window: there is a supply ship leaving Mars in approximately 7–10 days from the send date. Jace has far less time than that.

## PHASE 6 — The Smuggling Sprint (~2105-04-15 to 2105-04-22)

**Reference: approximately one week**

Jace has six days to assemble the list. He meets the Rat. He navigates gray-market channels, pawn shops, a calligrapher, Rich's coffee-dome connection, and — with shame he will carry for months — a Chinese military warehouse access.

The Rat secures a 2x2x2 meter slot on the next unmanned supply ship to Odysseus.

The crate is loaded and sealed on Day 6. It ships on Day 8.

## PHASE 7 — Origin, Sylvester, and Departure (~2105-05 to 2105-06)

The Rat mentions the Origin Industries opening shortly after the crate ships. The Origin negotiation, Sylvester's recruitment, and Jace's departure follow within weeks.

**What Mars looks like as Jace prepares to leave:**
- The rotator cycles are still running but carrying more passengers than usual
- Some civilian businesses in Terminus have gone quiet — not closed, just... quieter
- Belt news is effectively absent from official channels; underground feeds are full of fragments that no authentication system will confirm
- Rich, in their last real meeting before Jace ships out, gives him a long look and says something approximate to: *"The belt is a strange place right now. Pay attention to things that don't make sense. Don't explain them too fast."*

He doesn't say what he knows. He never will — not in Book 1. But he says that.

## PHASE 8 — On the Falcon and Arriving at Fortuna (~2105-mid)

**Status: Future arc — to be developed**

What Jace does not know as he boards the Falcon:
- Odysseus Station's situation has continued to deteriorate; Artemis's status is unknown
- The Fortuna science council has been attempting to "code" the magic as a new branch of particle physics
- The silicon cryptids — a specific class of manifestation associated with the manufacturing consciousness at Fortuna and similar industrial stations — are becoming a serious threat
- The TBD engineer on Odysseus who built Aura has been working on something that will become critical

What he has when he boards:
- Cerberus
- A civilian HUD with a functional Aura instance
- A supply of Mars coffee he kept a small amount of for himself
- The skill set he proved during the smuggling sprint
- Fingertips that remember something he filed under "probably tired"
