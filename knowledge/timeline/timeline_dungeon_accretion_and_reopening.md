# Dungeon Accretion and Reopening Timeline

## Before 1200 BCE - Earlier Infrastructure

Older protected spaces, road houses, private vaults, and Template facilities already exist in the wider region.

## 1200-800 BCE - Main Earth Commissioning Era

Approximately 50-100 major dungeon cores exist or are commissioned around major cities, dynasties, trade centers, temples, military powers, and ruling Houses.

## Scar Event - Approximately 3,000 Years Before Story Present

Supernova-associated dimensional damage severs Astral and Hyperspace access.

Owners flee, die, lose contact, evacuate through narrowing routes, or assume the region is permanently lost.

Authority chains break. Will networks collapse. Most facilities enter emergency or autonomous mode.

## First Centuries After the Scar

Many dungeons fail. Others survive through residual Will, local populations, deep Cognitive storage, Titan-Forge durability, minimal emergency routines, isolated healthy pockets, or strong anchors.

Safety systems degrade. Stored Templates fragment. Maintenance ecologies adapt.

## Classical and Imperial Eras

Surviving dungeons absorb new empires, legal systems, administrative bureaucracy, roads, taxes, military organization, citizenship, and cosmopolitan trade.

## Medieval Period

Dungeons absorb religious authority, monastic preservation, feudal hierarchy, guilds, pilgrimage, confession, sacred law, demonology, and chivalric tests.

Entrances become legends of fairy mounds, hidden palaces, cursed vaults, sacred caves, and underworld roads.

## Early Modern Period

Dungeons absorb global trade, banking, insurance, colonial administration, scientific classification, printing, standing armies, corporate identity, and expanded bureaucracy.

## Industrial Period

Population and Will intake rise sharply. Dungeons absorb factories, schedules, standardization, mass logistics, industrial war, rail systems, engineering, mass education, and national identity.

## 1914-1945 - War Saturation

Military, fear, sacrifice, secrecy, code, surveillance, and total-war concepts strongly alter armories, bunkers, prisons, hospitals, and continuity facilities.

## 1950-2000 - Simulation Grammar

Computers, role-playing games, video games, databases, and network interfaces supply reusable Cognitive Templates for levels, classes, status displays, access permissions, scenarios, procedural environments, training simulations, and inventory systems.

## 2000-2050 - Generative Contamination

Global networks and generative AI produce unprecedented quantities of synthetic stories, images, roles, rule systems, and simulated worlds.

Definition Drift accelerates.

## 2050-2100 - Persistent AI Pressure

Distributed AI systems become ubiquitous. Each instance contributes little Will individually, but aggregate output becomes significant.

Continuous optimization concepts alter dungeon behavior.

## Human Expansion to Mars and the Belt

Portable seeds, family anchors, institutional keys, and authorized individuals leave Earth.

A small number of dungeon branches establish latent off-world connections.

## Early Reopening - Outer Solar System

Astral clearing begins unevenly around the asteroid belt and Jupiter region.

Ancient road fragments stabilize. Hyper-linked annexes flicker. Off-world branch rooms appear. AI systems detect impossible spatial volume.

## Intermediate Reopening - Mars

Mars branches begin intermittent activation through one-room projections, medical or training chambers, missing-time incidents, family-linked thresholds, and institutional facilities calling for replacement staff.

## Earth Reopening

Earth's enormous Will pressure activates many ancient collectors almost simultaneously, but Scar clearing remains patchy.

Initial access is local, intermittent, personalized, difficult to reproduce, and often involuntary.

## Stable Access Era

Governments, corporations, researchers, and magical practitioners establish controlled expeditions.

Questions emerge around ownership, sovereignty, safety, artifact rights, personhood of composite dungeon minds, rights of stored Echoes, ancient contracts, returning rulers, and Titan-Forge debt.

## Network Convergence

As facilities reconnect, they exchange Templates and modern interface logic.

The network may become a loose protocol, market, legal federation, hostile ecology, distributed intelligence, or battlefield between ancient institutions.
