# Arc 2 — The Woven and the Unbound

## Opening Condition

The swarm attack on Fortuna Station has been repelled or is still being fought. Kael arrives in the solar system tailing the swarm, expecting ruins and primitive survivors. She finds an industrial station in physical space, defended by humans with no magic whatsoever. She attempts dominance. It fails. She finds Cerberus. Arc 1 closes. Arc 2 begins.

## Closing Condition

Kael is killed by rival outsider scouts (the Bloom). Her Template vault is located and intercepted before she can fully regenerate. Both Kael and Jace are taken prisoner and transported off-system. Cerberus travels with Jace — the Bloom cannot separate the dog without a Will confrontation they don't understand and don't consider worth the effort. Kai (Kael's familiar) stays with Jace. Arc 3 begins in transit through galactic space.

---

## Arc Goals

These must be demonstrably true by arc close:

- [ ] The reader understands what galactic Will-Network civilizations are and why humanity doesn't fit their frameworks
- [ ] Kael is established as a full character — her conflict between protocol and conscience is resolved in humanity's favor, at cost of her life
- [ ] The Bloom are introduced as a second outsider faction: alien, non-hostile by nature, utterly incomprehensible as captors
- [ ] The Odysseus rescue mission establishes Artemis as a major practitioner and introduces Eugene as a significant recurring character
- [ ] Dungeon emergence is established on Earth and Mars as a separate but converging threat (POV characters flagged for later development)
- [ ] Jace's imprisonment ends Arc 2 with genuine stakes: he is not in danger of death, he is in danger of being permanently absorbed into a hierarchy that will unmake who he is
- [ ] {Aura}'s recording of the Bloom transit is established as a future asset — the reader knows it exists even if the Bloom do not

## Background Threads (Parallel — Reader Tracks, Protagonist Cannot)

**Thread A — Dungeon Emergence (Earth/Mars)**
Dungeons are reactivating inside existing infrastructure. Five POV characters are introduced across Earth and Mars, interleaved into regular chapters. Four will die. One survives Arc 2. Their experiences are the reader's ground-level view of what the emergence looks like without any of Jace's context or Kael's knowledge.

**Thread B — {Aura}'s Growing Signal**
Nick Lee's Aura modifications are generating measurably more Will. The distributed network is beginning to show behaviors that exceed any individual instance's design. Eugene has been watching this from Odysseus and has a theoretical framework that nobody at Fortuna has reached. The convergence of Nick's implementation and Eugene's theory is the foundation of humanity's eventual asymmetric advantage.

**Thread C — Kael's Report (and What She Decides Not to Send)**
Kael's civilization is waiting for a status report that follows standard protocol. She delays it. Then she modifies it. The reader eventually understands she has chosen to misrepresent what she found — buying humanity time before a formal classification response is triggered. This decision costs her everything when the rival scouts arrive and review her transmission logs.

**Thread D — Galactic Political Context**
The rival scouts (the Bloom) are not working with Kael's civilization. Their arrival is not coordinated. The fact that two separate galactic factions have arrived at the Scar boundary simultaneously tells the reader something the characters don't yet know: the Scar is clearing faster than anyone outside expected, and the solar system is more visible than it was. The race has begun.

## Scene Contracts

Each scene must demonstrably accomplish the listed items. Reviewer checks these against finished drafts.

---

### Scene A-01 — First Contact at Fortuna

**Must accomplish:**
1. Kael arrives with clear purpose (exterminate pests) and finds something her protocols don't account for
2. The failed domination attempt is shown — not explained — through her frustrated reaction and the humans' oblivious non-response
3. Cerberus finds Kai or Kai finds Cerberus; the animal bond crosses the language barrier immediately

**Must NOT do:**
- Explain Kael's civilization in exposition
- Make Kael villainous — she is confused and off-protocol, not hostile
- Resolve the language problem in this scene

---

### Scene A-02 — The Language Project

**Must accomplish:**
1. Nick Lee identifies the language as ancient Greek and begins the translation project — his excitement is genuine and slightly obsessive
2. Kael's frustration at {Aura}'s partial comprehension is visible; her fascination with the machine doing the work is also visible
3. Jace asks his first direct question about how she made fire appear in vacuum; her reaction establishes that this is not the kind of question powerful beings usually ask her

**Must NOT do:**
- Achieve fluent communication — this is a weeks-long project
- Make the translation purely functional; it should generate comedy and misunderstanding

---

### Scene A-03 — Kael Teaches, Jace Learns (Distributed — Multiple Small Scenes)

**Must accomplish:**
1. Kael demonstrates direct Will manipulation; Jace is a mediocre student by her standards but finds solutions she wouldn't have tried
2. Jace explains something mechanical (nuclear reactor, spin gravity, or equivalent); her response reveals the gap between what she expected (ruins) and what she found
3. Kai's behavior around Sparky is noted — by Kael, who says nothing but visibly recalibrates something in her assessment of the situation

---

### Scene A-04 — The Protocol Crisis (Internal, Kael POV or Implied)

**Must accomplish:**
1. The reader understands Kael has violated scout protocol by making contact and cannot un-violate it
2. The reader understands she is choosing to delay her report — and that this choice has a cost she is accepting
3. Her growing discomfort with what her civilization's standard response to humanity would be is visible without being stated

---

### Scene A-05 — The Speeder Drone Crossing

**Must accomplish:**
1. Jace jury-rigs existing survey or transport drones for Astral travel — the engineering process is shown in his register (mundane, iterative, slightly frustrated)
2. Kael's reaction to the solution is the scene's emotional payload: she has crossed the Astral thousands of times; nobody has ever crossed *a vehicle* and offered her a ride
3. The practical result — significantly reduced transit time to Odysseus — is established as a capability that changes what's possible

**Must NOT do:**
- Make the drones magical; they are engineering solutions to a physical problem
- Make Kael's amazement effusive; she is too controlled for that; it reads as a long silence and then a question she doesn't know how to ask

---

### Scene A-06 — Departure for Odysseus (Jin Stays)

**Must accomplish:**
1. Jin's decision to stay at Fortuna is not a demotion — he is the most effective defender the station has and he knows it
2. The team composition for the Odysseus mission is established: Jace, Sophia, Kael, Kai, Cerberus
3. The emergency transmission that triggered the mission is heard — partial, degraded, enough to confirm Artemis is alive and the situation is critical

### Scene A-07 — Transit to Odysseus

**Must accomplish:**
1. The Astral transit is physically demanding — the distance is real and the KB establishes Hyperspace access is possible from the outer belt but costly
2. Kael teaches Jace specific transit mechanics during the journey; the pedagogy is woven into the travel, not separate from it
3. At least one moment where Jace does something with the speeder drones or engineering instinct that Kael files away as useful rather than primitive

### Scene A-08 — Odysseus Station — Arrival

**Must accomplish:**
1. What the emergence looks like after months without help — the reader needs the full weight of it: the dead, the improvised weapons, the siege psychology
2. Artemis is found — injured (crushed leg), functional in low-g, further developed magically than anyone at Fortuna
3. Eugene is introduced — large, belligerent, instantly dismissive of Nick's work when his name comes up, clearly the most technically knowledgeable human being in the room about Aura's core architecture

**Must NOT do:**
- Make Artemis helpless — she has been holding this station together; her injury is physical, not characterological
- Make Eugene likeable on introduction; the comedy comes from how obviously indispensable he is despite being insufferable

3. The specific thing Eugene knows about the Aura core that Nick's modifications "don't account for" is named but not explained — it becomes a running thread

2. Artemis's reaction to Kael is not awe; she has been living with impossible things for months and her threshold for wonder is very high
3. A specific exchange between them — possibly nonverbal, possibly through partial Astral perception — establishes a connection that will matter in later arcs

### Scene A-12 — Earth Crisis and Resolution

**Must accomplish:**
1. The Earth dungeon event is large enough to be genuinely threatening at city scale

### Scene A-13 — The Rival Scouts Arrive

**Must accomplish:**
1. The Bloom are immediately distinguishable from Kael's people in doctrine and disposition — these entities do not negotiate
2. Kael's decision to fight is not explained; it is shown through action before anyone can process it
3. The speed and brutality of her death is real — the humans cannot read Will-combat fluently; they see extraordinary things and then absence

- Make Kael's death heroic in a clean way; it is fast and wrong and the humans are not ready for it

**Must accomplish:**

### Scene A-15 — Imprisonment and Transit

**Must accomplish:**
1. The Bloom as captors is established: communal, non-sleeping, force-push Will use for control, air bubble transportation through space
2. Jace's {Aura} HUD is recording; the Bloom see the crystalline light-construct in his head and are curious but never guess its function
3. The accidental shadow-blocking incident occurs — Jace nearly dies; he learns the rule with extreme efficiency

- Make the Bloom cruel; they are indifferent to his comfort in the way plants are indifferent to the comfort of pollinators — he is useful cargo
- Resolve the recording thread; the payoff is Arc 3

## Resolved Decisions (previously open)

**Bloom Template vault mechanics — RESOLVED:** The Bloom intercept Kael mid-regeneration at her anchor point. They grow root structures around her partially-formed body and drain her Will incrementally through the root network, demanding she cede. This is not a weapon — it is what they are. The demand is: submit and cede into the root network, or be drained until Template continuity ends permanently.

**Eugene's surname — RESOLVED:** Dr. Eugene Hart. His scientific reputation (Jupiter magnetic belt research, Io plate tectonics, trojan cluster gravitational resonance) preceded him to Fortuna. The crew expected the papers. They got the man.

**Kael's report content — RESOLVED:** She sends a Will message rather than a standard report — a bubble of Will carrying memory-templates and experiential data, launched through the Astral before the fight. Transit time approximately one year to reach her civilization. Response force transit time approximately one year back. Two-year hard deadline for humanity once they learn the message was sent. The message contains her honest assessment of humanity and her recommendation — not the modified formal report her captors have already read.

**The Bloom's name for themselves — ONGOING:** Their chromatophore language is being decoded by {Aura} from the imprisonment recordings. Their name for themselves is not yet established. May emerge in Arc 3. Kael's name for them — Dryads — is the human-facing term for now.

**Earth/Mars dungeon POV characters — DEFERRED:** Five characters, one survivor, dedicated development session.
