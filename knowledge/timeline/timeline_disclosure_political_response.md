# The Disclosure Crisis — Political and Military Response

## Premise

The swarm fight (arc_01, `S-01`) broadcasts live through the Pig Channel and
reaches Earth in real time. Disclosure is involuntary and global. This document
covers what happens next, politically and militarily, in the hours and days
following — extending the antenna build (`P-07`, "Earth thinks Fortuna had a
radiation event; rescue mission is inbound") into a fuller, messier picture.

This is deliberately **not** a single coordinated response. Per the existing
communications-filtering architecture (`tech_communications_filtering.md`), no
single actor is in control, and that stays true here at a larger scale.

## Immediate Reaction — Confusion, Not Consensus

The footage that reaches Earth and Mars is chaotic by nature: drone cameras,
handheld feeds, a station under attack, things that should not exist moving
through corridors, and — depending on how much reaches viewers before feeds are
cut — glimpses of Force Application, fire that shouldn't be possible in vacuum,
and Sophia's kill of the Scavenging Queen. There is no clean narrative in the raw
feed. Different viewers, and different governments, construct wildly different
explanations from the same footage:

- **Some intelligence services conclude an AI is behind the chaos** — either a
  rogue system generating the footage, or a real AI-driven incident being
  concealed by fabricated footage layered on top of it. {Aura}'s open-source,
  distributed nature (see `arc_01_thread_N_nick_ai.md`) makes it a natural
  target of suspicion: unlike the corporate AIs, nobody centrally controls it,
  which reads to intelligence analysts as "nobody can rule it out."
- **Some governments read it as a fabricated cover story** — for a war, an
  attack, or a hostile takeover already underway, with the "monster" footage as
  deliberate disinformation to mask something more conventional and more
  frightening: an act of aggression by a rival power.
- **Origin Industries' specific fear:** their multi-trillion-token station is
  being seized — read internally as a possible Chinese operation, given
  Fortuna's 20% Chinese stake and Jin Lóng's detachment being visibly armed and
  fighting on camera. This fear is wrong, but it is not irrational given what
  Origin's own leadership can see in the footage and what they already know
  about `faction_china_belt_operations`.
- **A minority — growing, but still a minority at this stage** — begin to
  correctly read the footage as evidence that magic is real and has returned.
  These voices want the news contained, not because they doubt it, but because
  they understand the implications before most people do and are afraid of
  premature panic.

**The result is not one entity cutting one switch — it's the whole architecture
seizing at once**, each actor pulling in a different direction for a different
reason, exactly as `tech_communications_filtering.md` predicts for the
"eventual blackout," except now happening in compressed, chaotic real time
rather than as a slow multi-month hardening.

## The Feed War

In the hours after disclosure:

- Multiple governments and corporate actors attempt to cut, throttle, or
  selectively edit the outbound Fortuna feed
- Some attempt to hack each other's relay infrastructure to gain a clearer
  picture, or to control what their own public sees first
- Origin Industries attempts to reassert corporate control over "their" station's
  narrative and finds it has no clean way to distinguish an authorized broadcast
  from an unauthorized one, since the antenna build (`P-07`) was never an
  authorized transmitter in the first place
- Chinese state filtering (`tech_communications_filtering.md`) hardens
  immediately and asymmetrically — footage of Jin's crew fighting in the open is
  exactly the kind of content Beijing has spent years suppressing, and the
  instinct to control the narrative runs directly against the instinct to
  understand what actually happened

This should read as genuinely chaotic — competent people making locally
reasonable decisions that collectively produce an incoherent, contradictory
information environment, not a single villain pulling a single lever.

## Chinese Belt Policy Shift

Where the pre-disclosure Chinese posture was withdrawal (the Odysseus-triggered
drawdown — see `faction_china_belt_operations.md`), disclosure reverses this
sharply. Beijing's read: Fortuna is no longer a quietly-troubled minor asset —
it is the site of a confirmed, filmed, globally-witnessed event involving
supernatural capability and a Chinese national (Jin Lóng) demonstrating unknown
abilities on camera. The strategic calculus flips from "minimize exposure" to
"secure position before anyone else does."

**Background beat, not yet scened:** China dispatches a warship carrying
marines toward Fortuna. This is not framed publicly as a combat deployment —
official channels describe it as a "personnel and materials support mission" —
but its composition and speed say otherwise to anyone paying attention.

## U.S. Response

Both U.S. Mars and U.S. Earth commands begin their own deployments toward
Fortuna in response — separately, and not fully coordinated with each other,
consistent with the fractured information environment above. Neither move is
publicly framed as a response *to* the Chinese deployment specifically, but
belt rumor networks (`faction_belter_stations.md`) connect the dots quickly
once fragments of both deployments leak.

## Rising Tension

As pieces of this leak out — through belt gossip networks, through partial
official statements, through leaked footage of the deployments themselves — a
slow-building tension becomes a visible background thread at Fortuna and across
the belt: something bigger than the swarm fight is now in motion, and nobody at
Fortuna has any control over it or clear visibility into it.

This is intended to run as **background/distributed material**, the same way
`arc_01_thread_L_life_texture.md`'s L-05/L-07 threads run — news fragments,
rumor, half-confirmed reports — rather than as a scened political drama in its
own right, at least through the end of Arc 1.

## Open Question — Timing (explicitly not resolved here)

**Whether either military force arrives in time to help at the swarm battle
(`S-01`), or arrives too late and finds the fight already over, is not decided.**
Per the author's own framing, this is intentionally TBD. Two things worth
flagging for whichever way it goes:

- `S-01` as currently drafted (see `arc_01_thread_S_swarm.md`) is already won
  without military reinforcement — Jin's crew, Sophia, and Nick's early warning
  carry the fight. If reinforcements arrive during or immediately after, their
  contribution needs to be additive (rescue, mop-up, evacuation support) rather
  than rewriting the fight as already drafted
- If they arrive **too late**, that's a strong beat on its own — the political
  chaos in this document actively cost time that mattered, and Fortuna knows it
