# Modular Pod / Canister System

## Core Concept

"People do not live in spaceships. They live in buildings. The ship simply carries
the buildings."

Rather than building unique, one-off interiors into every ship and station, belt
infrastructure is built around a single standardized container: a roughly 13-meter
modular unit, descended in spirit from the 40-foot shipping container standard. The
expensive parts of a ship or station — propulsion, structure, life support backbone —
stay in service for decades. The modules plugged into them are swapped, relocated, and
reused for as long as they hold pressure.

A module can be a crew cabin, a family home, a medical clinic, a lab, a machine shop,
half of a livestock enclosure, a storage unit, or an emergency shelter. The shell is
identical; the fit-out is not.

## Where They're Used

The same module standard is used aboard the *Falcon* (36 berths, 18 per side — see
[ships/ship_falcon.md]) and is being scaled up for Fortuna Station's wheels (draft —
see [locations/location_fortuna_station.md]). A module built or fitted out in one
location can, in principle, be unberthed and relocated to the other. This is not just
an engineering convenience; it is a deliberate piece of belt culture.

## Canisters as "Tiny Houses"

For belters who move often — between ships, between stations, between postings — a
personally fitted-out canister becomes something closer to a tiny house than a
shipping container. People decorate them, modify them, and carry them from posting to
posting across years of a career. Where someone's canister currently sits tells you a
lot about where their life is at the moment.

**Lanchee** is the clearest example established so far: her home, her clinic, and her
medical supplies are all built into a single canister. When she moves between a ship
and a station, her practice moves with her, fully intact, rather than being rebuilt
from scratch at each posting. See [characters/char_lanchee.md].

This is meant to recur — other characters' canisters, and what's inside them, are a
quiet, low-exposition way to characterize people across the books without stopping to
explain it.

## Emergency Shelter Function

Detached or in transit, a module can run independently for a few hours on internal
life support — enough to function as an emergency shelter during a depressurization,
spin-loss event, or other local failure, without needing to evacuate to a different
part of the structure entirely. This is part of the broader "compartmentalize and
over-engineer for survival" design philosophy that also shows up as airlocks at nearly
every compartment boundary aboard the *Falcon*.
