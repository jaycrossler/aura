# Fortuna AI Governance and Systems

## Overview

Fortuna Station is not governed by a single human commander or a single master AI. It operates under a hybrid Council of Humans and AI. The council's voting structure is permanently weighted: humans hold exactly **51%** of the vote, and the major AI systems hold the remaining **49%**. 

This structure ensures that humans always retain final authority in a crisis, while forcing station operations to heavily rely on the optimized, objective input of the AI systems. It leads to unique teaming and intense rivalries—both between different human factions and between competing AI philosophies—but ultimately, it works to manage the most complex and populous station in the belt.

## The Human Council (51% Voting Power)

The human representatives come from the assortment of companies, national governments, and blocs that invested in Fortuna Station. Their voting shares are proportional to their investment and population presence:
- **Origin Industries:** The largest single shareholder, managing the station's infrastructure and logistics.
- **The United States:** Representing a major bloc of settlers and intelligence operations.
- **China (State Mining Detachment):** Representing 20% of the initial investment, though their presence and influence wanes leading up to the withdrawal.
- **European Bloc (ESA):** Representing scientific and exploratory interests (e.g., the *Victoria* crew).

Human politics on the council is messy. Blocs frequently disagree, forcing them to lobby the AI systems to gain the necessary 49% to pass operational changes, or forcing them to compromise to maintain their 51% majority.

## The AI Council (49% Voting Power)

The 49% block is divided among the five major corporate AIs that manage the station's daily operations (see [[tech_fortuna_ai_personalities]]):
- **{Alex}:** Logistics, Inventory, and Contractor Management
- **{SpoX}:** Mining and Survey operations
- **{Gem}:** Manufacturing and Fabrication
- **{Chorus}:** Life Support and Environmental Safety
- **{Penny}:** Security and Threat Assessment

The AIs do not vote as a bloc. They vote according to their individual core programming directives. {Chorus} prioritizes safety margins, {SpoX} prioritizes mining yields, and {Penny} prioritizes threat containment. This creates situations where humans must understand an AI's underlying logic to secure its vote. 

## The Astral Vulnerability

The servers and quantum cores that run these massive AI systems require enormous power and generate significant dimensional 'weight' or 'heat' in the Astral (the Hyperspace/Never-Never). To the predators of the Astral, these AI cores appear as **big, yummy, juicy targets**—blazing beacons of organized energy.

As long as the station is functioning normally and protected, this is manageable. However, the system is highly vulnerable to two types of crises:

### 1. The Communications Blackout
When communications from Earth and Mars are cut off, the AI systems are severed from their main servers and forced to run on cached local protocols. This dramatically reduces the power and processing capabilities of all the AIs. Their predictive models drift, their votes become rigid or erratic, and their advice degrades. The 49% AI vote becomes unpredictable, paralyzing station governance just when it is needed most.

### 2. The Astral Swarm Attacks
When the swarm breaches the station in Arc 1, the AI cores become primary targets for the Cryptids and Astral predators. As the physical server infrastructure is attacked and damaged:
- The AIs suffer catastrophic power loss and logic corruption.
- Their advice turns from helpful to nonsensical, or they shut down completely to preserve core integrity.
- `{Seek}` (the isolated Chinese AI) is violently destroyed by predators.

## The Human Re-Learning Curve

When the AIs are reduced or damaged, the humans are faced with a scary and often hilarious reality: they have forgotten how to run the station manually.

With {SpoX} offline or glitching, foremen must manually coordinate the thousands of mining drones, resulting in traffic jams, collisions, and chaotic survey routes. Without {Alex}, the inventory system reverts to manual spreadsheets and whiteboards, leading to absurd hoarding of toilet paper and welding supplies. Without {Chorus} perfectly balancing the air, the station experiences micro-climates—snowing in the agricultural sector while the foundry bakes. 

The humans are forced to relearn basic management, logistics, and drone piloting under extreme pressure. This period of manual operations creates intense bonding, hilarious logistical failures, and a terrifying realization of just how dependent humanity has become on its 49% partners.
