# Cybernetic Augmentation Systems

## Summary

By the story's present, cybernetic augmentation has progressed from medical prosthetics to a broader category of integrated neural and physiological enhancement. The technology is buggy, expensive, and unevenly regulated, but transformative for those who have access. Military, corporate, and elite users are typical early adopters. Civilian implants exist but are rarer and lag a generation behind.

The most common implant class is the **HUD implant** — a heads-up display projected directly onto the user's visual field via retinal or optic-nerve interfaces, paired with a local compute node and a personal Aura instance. Other categories include neuromuscular augments, sensory extensions (infrared, ultraviolet, low-light), and biometric monitors.

## Power Sources

Implants must be powered. Two dominant approaches:

**Electrical power.** Inductive charging via a wearable device, or direct battery replacement. Requires user to manage charging cycles. Adds bulk to wearables. Most common in commercial implants.

**Glucose metabolism interface.** Implants harvest energy from the user's blood glucose, similar to a biological fuel cell. Eliminates charging requirements but increases caloric demand on the user. The user must eat more — typically 20–30% more daily calories for a heavily-augmented person. Failure modes can include hypoglycemia, especially under stress. Most common in military implants where charging logistics are impractical.

Marcus's HUD uses the glucose interface — one of the reasons his caloric needs are higher than civilian baseline, and a vulnerability if food is short.

## The Military HUD Implant (Marcus's Class)

Marcus carries a current-generation U.S. Air Force-issued HUD implant. Specifications (working):

- **Visual overlay.** Retinal projection providing tactical data, navigation, target identification, sensor feeds from connected systems.
- **Audio.** Subvocal microphone (reads throat micromovements) and bone-conduction audio output.
- **Neural interface.** Limited — provides command-level control of paired drones and systems, not full neural integration.
- **Aura integration.** Hosts a military-licensed Aura instance with restricted federation and security-cleared knowledge stores.
- **Sensor suite.** Biometric monitoring, environmental sensing (oxygen, radiation, temperature).
- **Power.** Glucose interface; supplemental electrical charging available.
- **Persistence.** Runs continuously while the user is conscious; goes into low-power "dreaming" state during sleep.

When Marcus is discharged, the implant is permitted to remain — it is medical equipment, not government property — but its security clearances are revoked, the military Aura instance is wiped, and a commercial Aura license is provisioned in its place. The hardware is still a generation ahead of consumer; the software and access are now civilian.

## Side Effects and Risks

- **Phantom data.** Users often "see" overlay elements briefly when the implant is offline, the way amputees feel phantom limbs.
- **Dependence.** Heavy users find unaugmented life difficult and disorienting.
- **Glitching.** Buggy implants can produce visual artifacts, false alerts, or transient blackouts. The technology is still maturing.
- **Surgical complications.** Implant installation is significant surgery, with measurable rates of infection, rejection, and neurological complication.
- **Psychological effects.** Long-term integration of an Aura at the cybernetic level — voice in your head, data in your vision, biometrics shared — produces a relationship with one's own body and mind that is unprecedented and incompletely understood.

## Implants and Magic

This is the part that will matter.

A cybernetic HUD implant does not block magical sensitivity, but it does *mediate* it. A sensitive user with an implant may experience early flux phenomena as sensor anomalies, software glitches, or Aura uncertainty flags before they recognize them as magical. The implant is, in effect, a translator that filters the magical experience through a technological vocabulary.

This may delay recognition. It may also provide a measurement substrate that traditional practitioners never had. Implant-augmented practitioners, once they understand what they are perceiving, may have unique advantages — and unique blind spots.

There is preliminary speculation (story-internal, contested) that AI-paired implants in high-flux environments may amplify or stabilize a user's magical sensitivity. No one has tested this rigorously. By story's end, someone will.

## Cultural Distribution

- **Military and security forces:** High implant penetration. Standardized, supported, regulated.
- **Corporate elites:** High penetration, individualized, top-tier hardware.
- **Working professionals:** Moderate penetration, basic models common.
- **Belters:** Variable. Many belters have basic implants for harsh-environment work; high-end implants are rare. Maintenance and replacement parts are scarce.
- **General civilian population:** Low to moderate penetration. Many people remain entirely unaugmented by choice or cost.
