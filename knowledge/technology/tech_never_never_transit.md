# Never-Never Transit

## Summary

The Never-Never compresses distance dramatically relative to physical space. A traveler moving through the Never-Never can cross solar-system distances in days rather than months, and interstellar distances in weeks rather than centuries. This capability was the foundation of pre-Scar galactic civilization's trade, military, and political infrastructure. It was lost when the Scar severed dimensional connectivity three thousand years ago.

As the Scar fades and the Reopening proceeds, Never-Never transit will gradually become available again — first in the asteroid belt, later in the inner solar system. This is not merely a new transportation technology. It is a transformation of the strategic and economic geography of the solar system.

This document covers the mechanics, limitations, phases of rediscovery, and long-arc implications for the story's transportation context.

---

## Geometry Review

Never-Never transit efficiency (distance covered relative to physical distance traversed) follows:
- **Near large masses:** Roughly inverse-square relationship. Planetary-scale distances become traversable in days.
- **Away from large masses:** Approaches inverse-cube. More compressed geometry, less stable terrain.
- **On established ancient roads:** Optimal efficiency along mapped routes.
- **Off-road:** Reduced efficiency, higher danger, unmapped topology.

For the solar system at story start, the most immediately useful zone is the asteroid belt and outer system, where both flux exposure and Never-Never accessibility are greatest.

## Three Phases of Human Adoption

### Phase 1 — Accidental Discovery (Books 1–3)

In Phase 1, no human has intentionally entered the Never-Never. Access is accidental:

- **Wildlife incursions:** Never-Never organisms bleeding through weakened dimensional barriers. These create crises but also evidence that there is something to enter.
- **Sensitive intrusion events:** Practitioners experiencing strong magical workings who briefly perceive the Never-Never as an overlay on physical space. Not physical entry — more like involuntary Sensing of the other layer.
- **Echo encounters:** Degraded Template fragments that bleed through dimensional barriers near the oldest Scar-region sites.

No one is deliberately traveling through the Never-Never in Phase 1. The practical transportation infrastructure remains entirely physical: cyclers, nuclear ferries, conventional spacecraft.

**Story implications:** The Scar's history starts becoming apparent through these incursions. Ruins and Echoes are encountered. The scale of what was lost — and the scale of what is opening — becomes visible. But none of this is usable transportation yet.

### Phase 2 — Experimental Access (Books 3–6)

In Phase 2, a small number of practitioners develop the capability to enter and survive the Never-Never deliberately. This is not routine and is not comfortable:

- Entries are short — minutes to hours of traversal before stress becomes dangerous.
- The terrain is wilderness, unmapped, hostile in places.
- Recovery time after a traversal is significant.

However, even this limited access begins to have implications:

- **Scouting:** A practitioner can traverse short distances in the Never-Never to observe what physical-space equipment cannot reach.
- **Rapid local transit:** Within a belt station cluster, a practitioner can cover distances in hours that physical transit would take days or weeks.
- **Intelligence advantage:** Access to the Never-Never's accumulated historical residue provides archaeological and strategic information unavailable any other way.

At this phase, never-never transit is not a commercial or military transportation system. It is a special-operations capability. The analogy is not "we now have FTL ships"; it is "a handful of people can do something extraordinary that has never been possible before in three thousand years."

**Story implications:** The first deliberate entries are major narrative events. The first deaths from travel stress are also major events. The question of who has this capability — and who wants to control it — becomes politically significant.

### Phase 3 — Viable Infrastructure (Books 7–15)

In Phase 3, multiple developments converge to make Never-Never transit viable at scale:

- Enough practitioners trained to serve as guides and navigators.
- Partial mapping of the Scar-region Never-Never terrain.
- Understanding of Template-stress management developed to the point where longer traversals are survivable.
- Contact with outside galactic civilization bringing pre-Scar technical knowledge about Never-Never transit engineering.
- Possible recovery of pre-Scar infrastructure fragments — vault remnants, road reinforcement artifacts, waystations — from the Scar's archaeological deposits.

**The vehicle question:** Can non-sensitive travelers use the Never-Never? Pre-Scar civilizations had vehicles — enclosed environments that a practitioner navigator could guide through the Never-Never, providing the Will and Template necessary for traversal while passengers experienced only a strange journey. Whether such vehicles can be reconstructed, and how they interact with the human science and engineering tradition, is one of the long-arc technology questions.

**The infrastructure question:** The ancient roads exist. Some are structurally sound. Can they be restored? Can new roads be constructed? Pre-Scar road maintenance required Will investment from civilizational-scale networks. Human civilization does not currently have that capacity. But human civilization has engineering, AI, and a completely novel approach to material construction.

## Comparison: Physical vs Never-Never Transit

| Mode | Earth–Mars | Mars–Belt | Belt station to station | Earth–Proxima |
|------|------------|-----------|------------------------|--------------|
| Current physical (cycler/ferry) | 6–9 months | 4–5 months | Weeks | Centuries |
| Phase 2 Never-Never (practitioner) | Not yet accessible | Days | Hours | Not yet accessible |
| Phase 3 Never-Never (guided vehicle) | Days (when accessible) | Hours–Days | Minutes–Hours | Weeks |
| Full Reopening (mature infrastructure) | Hours | Hours | Minutes | Days |

These are working estimates. The long-arc series ends with a solar system where the cycler and ferry system is historical infrastructure, like ocean liners after aviation.

## What This Means for Existing Infrastructure

**The cyclers** (*Niña*, *Pinta*, *Santa María*) are economically significant for decades regardless of Never-Never developments. The Reopening reaches Earth at a 20–30 year lag behind the belt. For most of the series' early arc, cyclers are the only reliable Earth-Mars transit. Their eventual obsolescence is a long-arc economic and political story, not an immediate one.

**The nuclear ferries** face earlier pressure. The belt is where the Reopening is happening first. As Phase 2 Never-Never access develops in the belt region, the ferry economics begin to shift — not dramatically at first, but directionally. A practitioner who can guide cargo through a short Never-Never traverse in days is not yet competing with a ferry run, but they are demonstrating the proof of concept.

**The corporations that own the physical infrastructure** — the megacorps — will not be indifferent to this transition. Control of Never-Never transit is potentially a more valuable monopoly than control of cyclers or ferries. The political fight over who controls dimensional transportation is a series-long arc.

## Galactic Context

For the civilizations outside the Scar, Never-Never transit is the baseline. They have not had it disrupted in the Scar region, and they have been maintaining and using their own transit infrastructure for thousands of years. When contact occurs, the technological gap in this domain is enormous — the outside galaxy has functional dimensional transportation, mapped routes, trained navigators, and vehicles designed for it. Humanity has explorers stumbling into a wilderness they do not yet understand.

However: humanity has three things the outside galaxy does not.

1. **Scientific instrumentation.** Humanity can measure and model Never-Never physics in ways that galactic practitioners, who learned through tradition, cannot. The first systematic maps of the Scar's Never-Never terrain may be made by human AI-assisted instrument arrays, not by experienced galactic navigators.

2. **AI.** Whether AI systems can serve as Never-Never navigators — whether they have Templates or some functional equivalent — is the most consequential unanswered question in this domain.

3. **No Template-vault conservatism.** Humanity does not have the risk-aversion of Template-stabilized civilizations. Human engineers will try things that galactic practitioners would consider reckless. Some of those attempts will fail catastrophically. Some will work.
