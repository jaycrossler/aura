### What People Notice First
He is tall — noticeably so in belt corridors, where he has bumped his head on
more mounting brackets than he can count. Broad-shouldered, pale white skin, hazel
eyes, a haircut that says "I stopped trying." His hands are always marked somehow
— paint, polymer residue, callouses, a recent burn. He reads as competent before
he says anything. Not military-sharp (years out of uniform), but the underlying
structure doesn't quite leave. People place him as an engineer, or someone who works
with animals, or possibly both, and all of it is correct.

He is half-smiling almost constantly — not because he is pleased but because his
mind is running an internal commentary it rarely bothers to share. He is shyer than
his size suggests. As he gets more comfortable with people, the commentary starts
coming out; Kael eventually orders him to stop talking so much.

### What He Doesn't Look Like
Not styled. Not fashionable. Not wealthy. Not currently military (the posture is
residual, not active). Not someone who has thought about his appearance recently.

## The Bone Disease — Background

Jace was honorably discharged from the USAF on Mars for a medical condition: his
anti-radiation medication (taken prophylactically for Mars exposure) interacts with
his bone density over time, making his bones increasingly brittle. The prognosis for
returning to Earth's full gravity well is poor — the combination of brittle bones and
full-g loading could be permanently damaging. He exercises obsessively, not for
aesthetics, but because he wants to be strong enough to survive going home if he ever
has the chance. This makes him sad and anxious in a way he doesn't discuss. Observers
see the obsessiveness; they rarely see the grief under it.

The military no longer has Veterans Affairs or equivalent benefits in this era. He is
on his own.

## The Exercise Obsession

Jace works out to exhaustion, consistently. Not for vanity. For survival math.

**Aboard the Falcon:** A small gym module he had to unpack personally (Maureen charged
him for it). Resistance bands anchored to walls for pulling work and low-gravity
exercises. A bulky compression suit he wears when running the ring to add loading.
He pushes himself to the point of exhaustion, then drinks water and — when his budget
allows — coffee.

**Yoga:** He does stretches that approximate yoga because his bone disease benefits
from sustained mobility work. He has noted that unlike how yoga is supposed to work,
instead of clearing his mind, he reorders his task list. He considers this the most
productive part of his day. He is doing yoga wrong, probably. It still helps.

**At Fortuna:** The station gym is larger and has more options. He uses it religiously.
The weight vest goes on for any cardio because the 1/3g isn't loading his bones
the way he needs.

**The peanut butter:** He loves peanut butter. It is a luxury item. Its presence or
absence in his diet is a reliable indicator of his current token budget aboard the
Falcon and later his financial situation at Fortuna.

## HUD Details

Minimalist by deliberate design — his first reaction to Nick's {Aura} installation
pitch was "this isn't a video game, stuff constantly in my eyes is a tripping hazard."

**Externally:** Not visible in normal light. In dim rooms, a careful observer can see
a faint LED glow inside the cornea — fascinating from the Astral, where it appears
as a bright crystalline concentration at his left temple. People who know about HUDs
say "his eyes are flashing" when they catch the micro-movements of him reading
overlaid text.

**His view:** Apple-Glass-style overlay on his natural sight. Primarily: task lists,
schematics, drone telemetry feeds, email (he has aggressive muting for email — it
gets overwhelming fast). The HUD mirrors his list-making cognition and externalizes
the to-do management he does in his head constantly.

## Behavior and Tells

### The Half-Smile
Resting expression of someone who is always thinking of things to say and not saying
them. He's shy. As he becomes more comfortable with people across Arc 1 and Arc 2,
more of the commentary surfaces — and eventually Kael tells him to stop.

### Animal Handling
Grew up on a horse farm in Virginia. The habits are in his body:
- Always leaves a hand on a large animal's flank to avoid spooking it
- Shows his hands first so animals can sniff before he reaches
- No sudden movements near animals, unconsciously
- Very patient; does not escalate when an animal escalates

This reads as natural rather than trained. People who know animal people recognize it.

### Stress Response
Under physical stress, he goes **very still** first (military training) then jumps
into action, sometimes before he has fully thought it through. This has occasionally
been the wrong sequence. When stressed, he also tends to misjudge the low gravity
(he grew up in Earth-standard and the body memory persists) and may bump his head,
overreach, or have to catch himself.

### With Cerberus
Automatically leaves space for Cerberus wherever he sits. On a couch, Cerberus
climbs the back and puts his chin on Jace's shoulder — a position Jace has accepted
as normal and arranges himself around. His voice with Cerberus is distinctly different
from his voice with anyone else: lower, more direct, warmer.

### Verbal Tells Under Stress
- "Copy" → he heard you and is processing; stress is low-to-moderate
- "Negative" → stronger stress register; military override mode

### Physical Tells (non-verbal)
- Hands go still when thinking (he does not fidget)
- Under high stress: the still → action pattern; occasionally accompanied by
  hitting his head on something because he moved too fast
- Half-smile disappears when he is genuinely alarmed; its absence is a tell to
  people who know him
