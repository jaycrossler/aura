### What People Notice First
He is compact and dense, with the specific physicality of someone who has done
actual hard work in dangerous environments for twenty-plus years. The scar on his
right cheek crosses the corner of his mouth and makes him look permanently like he
is about to say something cutting, or permanently angry — neither of which is his
default state. He reads the room the moment he enters it: one hand rests on his
belt axe tool, which he claims is required gear for all Chinese miners. Station
residents have learned to ignore this. The scanning is harder to ignore — he notes
every person, peers into every shadow.

Under {Seek}: the scanning reads as paranoia. Post-liberation: the same habit at
a lower temperature, professional rather than hunted.

### What He Doesn't Look Like
Not a bureaucrat. Not comfortable in any administrative context. Not someone
who got his scars decoratively. Not aggressive in a performed way — the menace
is quiet and matter-of-fact, the quality of someone who has been through disasters
and has nothing to prove about it.

### The Liberation Moment
When {Seek} shuts down, systems in the Chinese section of the station fail — doors
won't open, lights won't turn off, equipment lights go dark. Jin lets out a long
breath. A tear forms in the corner of his eye. He doesn't wipe it.

For months, {Seek} had been "going crazy" — refusing to believe the evidence of
Astral monsters, blaming the anomalies on American conspiracies, thinking its own
video feeds were being deep-faked. When crew members complained or disobeyed, it
would punish them or threaten to cut their air. They are now, suddenly, free.

## The Fire Kata

Jin's [Fire Shaping] emerged from a martial arts movement vocabulary he already
had. When he produces fire, the gestures mirror Kung Fu kata — specific hand
positions and flowing sequences that his crew also know and practice together.

**The sequence:**
- Fire starts as a ball floating just above his right hand (not springing from
  his palm — it appears above it, suspended)
- He "pushes" it with his left hand, like flowing water, directing it
- The moves are large: wide-stance lunges with extended bending-knee poses,
  arms pulled wide then swept forward for strikes, explosive punches crossing
  into giant circles
- His crew strings the moves together in sequences — clearly practised together
- They bow afterward: left hand flat-palm, right hand as a fist

From a distance this looks like a martial arts demonstration that happens to be
on fire. This is accurate.

## Command Body Language

His crew positions behind him and faces forward when he addresses anyone. There is
a specific gesture for "this is an order, not a suggestion" — subtle, held in the
hands, his crew reads it instantly. Outsiders often miss it until they've been around
him for a while. He selflessly protects his crew; they know it; it is the foundation
of everything.

## Relationship with Mei

Jin is unfailingly polite to Mei but never invites her to join his team. He treats
her as a peripheral figure — tells her to bring food when his crew is hungry. She
complies and controls her expression. He is not cruel; he simply does not trust her
yet, and is waiting to see what she actually is. Post-{Seek}, this begins to shift.
