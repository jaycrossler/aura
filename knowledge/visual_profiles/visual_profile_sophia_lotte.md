### What People Notice First
She is short, pale, and slightly chaotic — the specific chaos of someone whose mind
is outpacing her surroundings. The quick bun never fully holds. Her clothes are
colourful pastels and florals; her scarf is slightly askew. She has the delicate
features and expressive face of someone from Alsace who has always lived too much
in her head. She doesn't register that Jace is much taller than her. She gestures
when she speaks, energetically enough that he has learned to step back.

She loses things constantly. She never loses her pen.

### What She Doesn't Look Like
Not an adventurer. Not athletic. Not someone who belongs outside a comfortable lab.
The fact that she has been doing things alone in another dimension for a month is
quietly remarkable precisely because she looks like this.

## Background

- **Born:** Colmar, Alsace (French territory at time of birth; the region alternates
  between French and German sovereignty every few decades; she notes this with dry amusement)
- **Raised:** Strasbourg; parents were diplomats; grew up multilingual and culturally hybrid
- **Heritage:** Alsatian — French expressiveness plus German precision; warm and spicy with
  friends, cold and aloof with strangers
- **Career:** PhD physicist; ESA science mission aboard the *Victoria*; was planning a
  six-month trip to Odysseus Station to study Jupiter's magnetic fields; when Odysseus went
  silent, the *Victoria* stayed at Fortuna and pivoted to crystal growth in vacuum
- **Gravity decision:** Has accepted she will not return to Earth. Plans to live and retire
  at Fortuna as a scientist. Not a wound — a pragmatic acceptance.
- **Publications:** Will publish approximately 20 papers by Arc 1 end; they become the
  foundation of humanity's scientific understanding of the Astral
- **Money:** Very rich. Very cheap. Will walk to the other side of the station to save
  1 token on something. Does not connect these two facts.

## Characteristic Behaviours

### Losing Things
She loses things constantly. The fountain pen is the primary casualty. Also: whichever
scarf she needs, small tools she was just holding. She never loses data. Every notebook
is backed up across multiple servers.

### Dictating
Paces constantly while dictating. Gestures energetically with both hands — Jace has
learned to stand a metre back. Switches between French, German, and Alsatian when excited.
Often trails off mid-sentence, grabs her pen, writes the idea down immediately, then
comes back to finish. Sometimes comes back ten minutes later.

### "Hopla"
Her Alsatian verbal tic — means approximately "Let's go," "Okay," "Sorry," or "Right then."
Appears at the end of statements. Increases in frequency when excited or flustered.

### Observation Mode
When watching something in the Astral that interests her: hunches forward, stops blinking,
murmurs description in French under her breath while drawing in her notebook. Ignores
interruptions in this state. Will continue a sentence ten minutes after she started it.

### Height Dynamic with Jace
Does not notice or care that Jace is 6'1". He has unconsciously adopted a slight crouch
when talking to her. She gestures vigorously enough that he has learned to lean back.

### Post-Crossing Cold Ritual (pre-survival suit)
After returning from the Astral at -20°C near Fortuna: wraps in a blanket with a cup
of *Glühwein* (German spiced mulled wine) and writes frantic notes in her journal.

### Christmas
Loves decorating the *Victoria* for Christmas. Decorations appear as early as she can
justify them. Very excited about the new station cheeses being developed from the
livestock — brings up the cheese possibilities at somewhat unrelated moments.

### Trauma Aftermath (Arc 1 end)
- Flinches at sudden loud noises
- Always carries an extra rebreather; doesn't discuss why

## The Victoria — Her Workspace
Three-physicist crew in a small ship; she has one of three small cabins.

**Her cabin:** Tiny and very organised. Walls colourful:
- Large woodcut-style map of Colmar (her birthplace)
- Large woodcut-style stork print (Alsace's symbol)
- A Christmas tree image (up most of the year)
- Scarves hanging everywhere on available hooks
- A shelf of paper journals — Arc 1 ends with notebooks 1–17+ filled
- A closet of colourful clothes, carefully stored

Not much accumulated "stuff" overall — but the room is warm and distinctly hers.
