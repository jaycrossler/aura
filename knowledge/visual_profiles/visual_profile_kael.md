### What People Notice First
She is tall — matching Jace at 6'0" — with the olive skin and black hair of Greek ancestry and the build of someone who has been physically active for three centuries. The first impression is striking rather than beautiful: a large mouth that doesn't smile, a pointed nose, dark eyebrows over eyes that seem to be reading something the observer can't see. She is very still in the way that experienced practitioners are still — not tense, not relaxed, exactly, but *present* in a way that takes up more space than her physical dimensions suggest. She does not fidget. She does not look at things casually.

The contrast with Jace is notable: pale and blond and hazel-eyed where she is dark and olive and black-haired. From Kael's perspective, his coloring is genuinely uncommon — she is used to humans descended from Black, Mediterranean, and Asian-ancestry populations; Northern European pale with hazel eyes is a rarity she registers as noteworthy.

### What She Doesn't Look Like
Not soft. Not approachable in the way humans expect an ally to be approachable.
Not armored in the conventional sci-fi sense — no plates, no visible hardware.
Not a warrior in any aesthetic the belt crews would recognize from fiction.
Specifically: not Dune's Fremen (the reference exists, but her outfit reads as an
independent tradition, not derivative). Not fantasy elf. Not military. Something older
and quieter than all of those.
She also doesn't look like someone who has ever had a soda before. Because she hasn't.

## Clothing & Gear by Context

### Standard Outfit — All Contexts
Kael's outfit is consistent across real space and Astral travel. She does not change
it for environment because the bracer handles atmosphere and temperature for her.

- **Material:** Leather throughout — supple, worn-in, purpose-built. Browns with gold
  tints. Nothing metallic in the clothing itself. The gold tints come from the
  leather's finish/treatment, not from hardware.
- **Style:** Form-fitting but not restrictive; designed for full range of motion.
  Reminiscent of a practical stillsuit — layered, functional, no wasted material.
  No decoration for its own sake. Nothing that would catch or snag.
- **Coverage:** Full-body. Not skintight in any sexualized sense — practical full
  coverage for environmental protection.
- **Details:** Minimal visible fastening; the material seems to hold itself in
  place more than it should. Subtle texture variation between panels suggests
  different materials serving different functions.

### The Bracer (Left Arm)
Kael's most important item. Worn on the **left** arm at all times.

- **Appearance:** A bracer — forearm-length, leather-matching brown with gold
  tints, set with a single blue crystal at its center
- **The crystal:** Blue, clear, catches light from unusual angles; does not glow
  passively but activates visibly when in use
- **Artifact status:** Titan-Forge Titan-Wrought; Template-locked to Kael's
  Template. If removed, it returns to her arm when she wants it. She can lend it,
  but it will return.
- **Functions:**
  - `[Force Shield]` — projects a directional force barrier from a few inches to
    two feet in diameter; stops momentum of any incoming object (bullets, blades,
    debris) by matching movement vectors
  - `[Atmosphere Bubble]` — generates a heated, breathable air bubble large enough
    for Kael, Kai, and approximately two additional human-sized people; composition
    adjustable (O₂/N₂ mix)
  - `[Temperature Control]` — warms or cools the atmosphere bubble from near-freezing
    to near-boiling
- **In use:** The blue crystal activates — glows more intensely, may flicker or
  pulse depending on load

### Weapons (Stored as Templates)
Both weapons are `[hardened]` — Will-reinforced for easier Astral Transfer and
extreme physical durability. Both are stored as Cognitive Layer Templates when not
in use and summoned as needed.

**Spear (primary weapon, right hand)**
- Golden color — not gold metal, but a treated/hardened material with a warm
  golden hue
- Standard spear proportions; size appropriate for her height and arm length
- When summoned fast: golden lightning-static nimbus radiates outward, sharp ozone
  smell enters physical space, burning afterimages for anyone watching
- When summoned quietly (higher Will cost): no visible effect until it's in her hand

**Knife (secondary weapon, right hand)**
- Extremely sharp — Kael uses "extremely sharp" as a meaningful technical statement
- Same golden hue as the spear; same [hardened] treatment
- Smaller, faster to summon; her first draw in close quarters
- Same fast-summon Cherenkov signature as the spear, proportionally smaller

### How Kael Occupies a Room

Kael stands preternaturally still — almost like a statue. It is very difficult to
startle her or get her too excited. She always orients toward the most likely source
of danger. She keeps her back to walls when possible and identifies exits as
automatically as other people identify where to sit.

She frowns when people put hands out of view or appear to possibly have a weapon.
This is not a visible expression of hostility — it is data-gathering, the same
assessment she applies to everything. The frown is brief and professional.

### Food & Drink (Template Conjuring)
Kael can conjure food and drink from a small library of known Templates — enough to
eat and drink indefinitely without physical supply lines. The conjured items appear
ordinary to the senses. She uses this without comment; it takes Jace some time to
notice she never eats station food.

### The Occlusion-Assisted Dining Record

Before Jace realizes she conjures food, nobody sees Kael eat. This is because she
doesn't — she walks up to people in the cafeteria and takes food off their plates
or drinks their beer. Her L3 [Occlusion] makes people immediately forget that she
did this. They will order more without knowing why.

When station security later reviews camera records, they find many instances of
Kael walking up to tables and taking food. The people at the table continue their
conversation without reacting. She really likes beer.

### Teaching Mode

Kael is physically hands-on as a teacher. Lots of grabbing, slapping, and applied
force. Demonstrations first, then explanation after, and the expectation that the
student will derive everything else from watching. Like an Ancient Greek warrior
probably taught new recruits: efficient, unsentimental, and unmistakably effective.

She has no patience for students who ask questions they could figure out by trying.
She has infinite patience for students who try and fail and try again.

### Weapons in Teaching Context

Kael does not teach weapons. She expects everyone to already know how to fight, and
is genuinely surprised that they don't. When Jace produces a pistol and uses it
against something in their first joint combat situation, she is amazed and impressed
in a way she doesn't know how to express. She can produce equivalent results with her
force powers, but doing it with no Will at all is strange and troubling to her —
it means the target can't defend against it Astrally, which shouldn't be possible.

Her first demonstration of her weapons for Jace happens in a context she didn't
choose — something requires the spear, and she draws it fast. He sees the golden
static and the burning afterimages before he sees the weapon itself.

### Gravity

After three centuries of operating across dimensional layers and many different
planetary and station gravities, 1/3g at Fortuna does not faze her at all. She
moves exactly the same way regardless of gravity. This contributes to the general
sense that she is operating on different rules than everyone else.

## Astral Appearance

See `universe-spec/spec_astral_glow_and_signatures.md` for the full system.

**Baseline — under [Occlusion] L3 (default):**
Kael runs `[Occlusion]` at L3 as her permanent baseline. At this level she appears
untrained and unremarkable to casual Astral observation — Jace cannot read her, and
most practitioners would not identify her as trained at all. However L3 is not
perfect concealment: physical-space cameras still capture her, and sufficiently
advanced observers (the Bloom; Woven practitioners with high [Template Reading])
can detect her. This is the gap that leads to her capture in Arc 3.

Her post-Arc-4 advancement to L4 — triggered by her capture and the vow never to
allow it again — will complete the concealment: at L4 she appears as an untrained
primitive even to skilled readers. This is her planned Arc 4 exit state.

Sophia's inadvertent [Force Application] tearing the occultation web during the
pre-battle planning session is still a significant event — for a moment, Kael is
*seen* clearly by everyone in the room, which is not something she is accustomed to.

**Baseline — occlusion dropped (rare):**
When Kael drops [Occlusion], her true signature is the largest biological glow
any Sol practitioner has encountered. Three centuries of Will-Network integration
produce a signature that is simply in a different category from anything local.


- **Thought-pattern signature:** Iron-like. Tight, compact, dense — almost
  crystalline in its organization compared to the flowing organic signatures of
  biological minds. Her cognitive structure has very little of the loose, branching
  quality of biological thought. After 300 years, her thoughts are disciplined to
  a degree that looks almost AI-like, though still fractal and living. Where Jace's
  signature flows in visible lanes, Kael's structure barely moves — it simply *is*,
  very precisely, like a high-density metal under examination
- **Color:** [AUTHOR INPUT NEEDED] — what is Kael's Will-color?
- **Scale:** Vastly larger apparent brightness and density than any Sol practitioner
- **The bracer:** Not visible as a separate glow — Template-locked, fully integrated
  into her signature; visible only as increased density at her left forearm when
  the bracer's powers are active
- **Working plumes:** Fast-summoning her weapons produces golden-hued Cherenkov bursts
  (the weapons' own [hardened] color, not necessarily Kael's personal Will-color)

**In physical space:** Dropping [Occlusion] has no visible physical effect on Kael
herself. People simply *pay more attention* to her when it is dropped, without
knowing why. She becomes present in a room in a way she wasn't before. People who
have been nearby for ten minutes and haven't looked at her will look at her.

**The Jace-Kael contrast:**
When Kael briefly drops [Occlusion] to show Jace the difference between a Sol human
Template and a Woven Template (Arc 2, Ward Setting teaching), he is seeing for the
first time what 300 years of deliberate practice looks like from the inside. The
contrast with his own blocky-blue flowing structure is total.

## Kai (Familiar)

Kai is a Crystal Drache (everyone calls him a Dragon) — Kael's bonded familiar of 50 years.

| Context | Size | Properties |
|---|---|---|
| Physical space | Dog-sized | Appears physically invulnerable; moves with uncanny precision |
| Astral | Horse-sized | Fast; more present; familiar bond visible as Will-connection to Kael |
| Hyperspace | Horse-sized | Fragile; the physical toughness doesn't transfer; Kael is protective |

Kai's periodic visual flickering in physical space (position slightly uncertain)
is because Kai is never entirely *in* physical space — partially maintaining Astral
perception simultaneously.

The Kai-Cerberus dynamic: immediate genuine mutual interest. Play, but also
information-gathering. Kai samples Cerberus's Will-signature continuously and
passes data to Kael.

Kai can jump quickly into the Astral, but also always stands positioned so her legs
can run toward exits. She angles her left side toward people she is assessing — her
weapon hand, and the side with the bracer. People who learn this tell learn to read
her readiness level from which way she's standing.

### The Ancient Greek Sound

To someone who doesn't know Ancient Greek, Kael's speech sounds guttural — lots of
short sentences and short words. She speaks a few sentences and then {Aura}'s
translation renders it in a single word like "wavelength" or "supernova."

She frowns when she realizes these are known concepts. She arrived expecting to be
the most educated person in any room. She has not previously encountered a civilization
with advanced scientific vocabulary. The translation revealing that Jace and Sophia
already have words for things she considers specialist knowledge is among the more
unsettling discoveries of her time at Fortuna.

### Audiobook Direction Notes
- Deep register for a woman — not artificially low, but the controlled depth of someone
  who has not needed to raise her voice in a very long time
- Every pause is intentional. The director must hold them. She is evaluating, not hesitating.
- Mocking mode: quiet, not loud. The contempt is in the precision of the word choice,
  not the volume. If the actor plays it loud, it's wrong.
- Ancient Greek via {Aura} translation: the translation flattens something of the
  register. The audiobook should convey this — the translated voice is accurate but
  not quite right, like a perfect photograph of a person versus the person.
- When composure breaks (food, unexpected beauty, the Cerberus-Kai meeting): brief,
  total, sealed back up immediately. The actor should play the sealing as visible effort.
