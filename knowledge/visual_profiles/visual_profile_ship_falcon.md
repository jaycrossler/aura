## What the Falcon Is

The *Falcon* is thirty years old and has not stopped moving long enough to be
properly restored. Saul has treated her as an ongoing engineering project for three
decades. The result is a ship that works — every system functions, every seal holds,
every animal arrives alive — but looks assembled by someone solving specific problems
one at a time rather than designing a coherent whole. Because that is exactly what happened.

She smells like animals. She also smells like whatever Jace most recently printed.
Sometimes Cerberus. The air cyclers do their best.

---

## Exterior Appearance

Three major components from any angle:

**The Candle (spine):** Long grey central shaft. The nuclear tugboat that everything
else is bolted to. Forward end: large umbrella-like conical debris shield flaring
outward. Aft end: nuclear-thermal engine bells with heat radiator fins extending
outward. Along the spine: fluid tanks attached at every available point — cylindrical,
spherical, different colors, different ages, not the same size. Cargo containers are
mounted wherever structurally possible, with heavier containers closer to the spin
axis for balance and items that might be needed mid-voyage accessible from corridor
airlocks. The packing is not random but it doesn't look designed.

**The Spokes:** Three structural spokes at even angular intervals. Each is thick
enough to carry elevator (one spoke: the industrial cargo cage), stairs (the other
two), fluid lines, power conduit, and structural load. Collision marks on the leading
edges. Industrial and unsexy.

**The Donut (ring):** 80m diameter rotating habitat ring. The outer surface is a
dense patchwork of module hulls, external fluid tank zones, maintenance hatches, and
cargo containers bolted to any surface that would take a mount point. Some containers
are recent; some look original to the ship.

**Surface condition:** Micrometeorite scarring across every forward-facing surface.
Collision marks from thirty years of docking. Discoloration at repair sites. No
consistent color scheme — the spine is grey, the ring sections are darker grey,
the add-on containers are whatever industrial color their manufacturer used.

One special container has small guidance rockets attached to it near the machine room
section — a cargo pod Jace will detach and fire toward Artemis's station in an early
scene. After launch, that mounting point's door is permanently sealed.

## The Ring Corridor — Sensory Bible

### Dimensions
- **Width:** ~1.5m / 5 feet — two people can pass if both turn sideways; a
  maintenance robot coming the other way requires actual navigation
- **Ceiling height:** ~2.4m / 8 feet — Jace (6'1") is comfortable; protruding
  equipment brackets have caught him before
- **Ring outer hull structural height:** ~4 meters (floor to outer hull top); the
  corridor occupies the inner portion with fluid ballast/shielding canisters in the
  remaining outer hull space

### Visual Character
- **Walls:** Fake wood laminate paneling — budget hospitality from thirty years ago;
  lifting at seam edges; scuff marks at knee-to-hip height from robot wheels and
  bumped cargo
- **Ceiling:** Half-dome LED fixtures every 2-3 meters; adequate but not generous;
  visible shadowing between fixtures; **these fixtures are a plot point** — after the
  plumbing crisis, specific fixtures fail or behave oddly, creating a lighting map
  of which sections were affected
- **Floor:** Industrial carpet with deep tread for robot wheel traction on the ring's
  gentle grade; worn but not destroyed; color indeterminate after thirty years
- **Module doors:** Every 3-5 meters on the outboard wall; airlock frames visible at
  module boundaries, permanently open during normal operations
- **Emergency gear:** Suit and rebreather wall recesses every few meters; standard
  belt-design
- **Windows:** A small number exist with auto-shutters. They auto-close when the ring
  is spinning and auto-open when it's not. Everyone opens one at least once, then
  never opens one again. The view of stars wheeling past is beautiful and immediately
  nauseating.
- **Maintenance:** Exposed panels at irregular intervals; something is always being
  repaired; a half-functioning robot of indeterminate model is usually involved
  (Saul buys one new model per year and uses them until they fail; the Falcon
  carries several generations at different stages of breakdown)

### Sound
- **Constant:** Ballast water moving through pipes in walls and ceiling — the Falcon's
  fluid system trims the ring's mass distribution continuously, responding to every
  movement aboard. You can tell when someone is walking on the other side of the ship;
  the ballast pumps above you in response. When the pumping changes rhythm or stops,
  everyone notices.
- **Constant:** Fan circulation; the air smells slightly recycled
- **Animal smell:** Present near the livestock bays; fans dilute it significantly
  away from that section; does not reach the cafeteria meaningfully (what wins in
  the cafeteria is coffee)
- **Periodic:** Thing One and Thing Two on maintenance rounds; Jace's various drones;
  the industrial printers in the machine shop — the large steel ones produce vibrations
  that can be felt faintly in the corridor but not heard in sleeping quarters

### Grade
Running the ring gains 3.1m of elevation over a full 251m circuit — like climbing
a long flight of stairs spread over a quarter-kilometer. Subtle but real. Jace knows
exactly where the low point is.

## Key Spaces

### The Cafeteria / Maureen's Token Economy

One standard module berth. Bolted tables, bench seating, a serving counter with a
hatch to the kitchen prep area behind it. The robot chef has a **posted board** showing
what it made or can make that day. Everyone transacts via HUD or physical button
— not everyone has a HUD so both methods work.

The **coffee machine** is the emotional heart of the cafeteria. Large, fancy, entirely
incongruous with the rest of the ship. Maureen bought it herself. She charges for it.

**Maureen's pricing (token economy):**
- Jace's starting daily food budget as an Origin employee: **40 tokens/day**
  — gets him a "steak and potatoes" experience (not real steak, but good)
- After first mistake: drops to **20 tokens/day**
- Progressive punishment steps: **10 → 5 → 3** (legal minimum under Origin's
  crew contract)
- At 3 tokens/day: Jace is eating free algae slop (yogurt-consistency, no flavor)
  and has lost coffee privileges
- **Peanut butter:** A luxury item Jace loves; one of the first things he stops
  buying when his budget drops. Its absence is a reliable Arc 1 deterioration marker.

### The Spoke Elevator

The cargo elevator on the first spoke: an open cage, industrial, 3m × 3m — can carry
a large box. Gravity fades gradually as the cage rises toward the spine, the same
sensation as climbing the ladders on the other two spokes but without the physical
effort. The transition from 0.3g to weightlessness takes a few minutes of travel.

**At the spine (zero-g):** Everything in the Candle that isn't magnetically secured
or strapped down floats. The Candle is almost entirely storage, plumbing, and the
drive section — not a living space. The weightlessness is functional and slightly
disorienting. People who spend extended time in the Candle know to keep their feet
tethered. People who don't know get motion-sick fast.

### The Shared Bunk (Jace and Mei)

One standard module, divided precisely in half with a **firm physical divider** and
two doors, one per side, each with a lock.

**Mei's half (left side):** Decorated. Chinese art on the walls, clothing carefully
arranged, personal items organized. This is her permanent home, or at least her
cover home. It is lived-in with intention.

**Jace's half (right side):** Spartan. A bed. A lamp. A few screens mounted to the
wall showing drone telemetry and task lists. A closet of practical clothes. A
breathing device on the bedside surface. A dog bed for Cerberus in the corner —
which Cerberus has never once used, because he always climbs onto Jace's bed.
At night, Sparky rolls in and parks at the foot of the bed. Jace sleeps between the
dog and the drone.

The partition between their halves is thin enough to hear television through. Mei
watches something most evenings. Jace does not know what it is. He has not asked.

### Maureen's Quarters

**The nicest thing on the ship.** A hand-made wooden door — actual wood, or an
extremely convincing facsimile — with a decorative doorway and entry area, nice
lighting, always the sound of a television or radio from beyond. If anyone has ever
glimpsed inside, the description is: bright, well-furnished, lots of rugs and couches.
A "rich grandmother's house" aesthetic, entirely incongruous with the fake-wood-
laminate corridor it opens onto.

It is at the end of the crew section. Its location means walking past it on any
errand to the livestock bays or machine shop. The contrast between Maureen's door
and the rest of the corridor is quietly telling.

### The Clinic (Lanchee's Module)

Lanchee's entire professional and personal life in one 13m canister. Medical equipment
on the walls, examination surface that converts to surgical prep, a small personal
section at the far end. The smell is antiseptic under the ship's baseline. Her presence
makes it feel cared-for in a way that the rest of the ship, which is maintained but
not tended, is not.

### Livestock Bays

Five double-berth configurations (two modules linked = ~26m each). Ten pens total.
Full-spectrum animal-husbandry lighting — brighter and more natural-toned than the
corridor. Drainage channels in the floors. Feed systems along the walls. Biosecurity
seals at every entrance. The smell here wins all arguments.

### The Machine Shop / Forge (Jace's Space)

Two module berths permanently linked: ~26m / ~80 feet of continuous industrial floor.
Jace negotiated access. Maureen charges him for every material he uses, which he
argues is fine because at least he's getting value — Saul never used the shop this much.

**Contents:**
- Industrial 3D printer (large format; steel and polymer; loud enough to feel as
  vibration in the corridor; runs frequently and on schedule, not just when Jace
  is present)
- Manual machine tools (lathe, mill, hand tools on magnetic wall strips)
- Long workbenches running both walls
- Parts storage in bins Saul organized and Jace has been reverse-engineering
- Overhead track for moving heavy components
- **Good lighting** — the one room on the ship where the lighting was chosen for
  precision work

**Jace's corner:** Sparky's components in various states of assembly. Print queue
running. Nick's spec documents printed on paper and annotated in two ink colors.
Two coffee cups with identical stain rings. Cerberus under the bench, asleep.

**Air quality:** The air cyclers work, but Jace isn't sure whether the persistent
smell is machine oil or whether Cerberus had an accident somewhere he hasn't found.
This is a recurring concern he mentions to no one.
