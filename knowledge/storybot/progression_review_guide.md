# Progression & Chapter Review Guide

Developmental guide for evaluating chapter drafts of *The Aura Chronicles* — a long-form
Science-Fantasy progression web serial (Royal Road-style). Evaluate pacing, progression,
character growth, mystery development, engagement, and serial viability. Identify
weaknesses and opportunities; do **not** rewrite the author's voice.

## Publication Goals
- ~3 chapters/week; ~3,000-5,000 words each (~10-15k/week).
- Series expected to exceed 500,000 words across multiple books/arcs.
- Readers should consistently feel: curiosity, discovery, progress, competence, wonder,
  anticipation. They should rarely feel: stagnation, purposeless confusion, repetition,
  meaningless travel, or excessive exposition.

## Core Identity
Science-Fantasy; hard-SF-influenced; progression; exploration-focused; mystery-driven;
civilization-building; character-centered. **Not** pure LitRPG, pure military SF,
traditional epic fantasy, or slice-of-life. Technology and engineering stay important;
magic should *augment* science, not replace it.

**Primary hook:** "What happens when humanity unknowingly becomes one of the most
technologically advanced civilizations in a magical universe?" Secondary: why is magic
returning? what is the Scar? what happened to the old civilizations? what are gods
really? why is Earth unique? what's beyond the solar system? what role will humanity play?

## The Jace Principle
Jace succeeds through engineering, problem-solving, observation, persistence, creativity,
and teamwork — rarely brute force. Solutions should emerge from technology, planning,
improvisation, and understanding systems. **Flag** chapters where Jace wins because the
plot requires it rather than because he earned it.

## Progression (advance ≥1 track nearly every chapter)
- **Personal:** Aura/Will/certainty/knowledge/skills/physical capability. ("What can he
  do now that he couldn't?")
- **Relationship:** friendships, rivalries, mentorships, team formation, trust.
- **Resource:** equipment, drones, ships, manufacturing, infrastructure.
- **Influence:** reputation, leadership, political importance, responsibility. ("Who
  listens to him now who didn't?")
- **Civilization:** station growth, human understanding of magic, new discoveries.

## Mystery Management
Maintain a short-term, a medium-term, and a long-term mystery at all times. Resolve one
regularly; introduce new ones *before* existing ones are exhausted. Track which mysteries
are active, which were answered, and whether curiosity is rising or falling.

## Serial Pacing
Every chapter should contain ≥1 of: discovery, conflict, advancement, revelation,
problem-solving, character development (ideally several). Flag chapters where little
changes.

## Arc Structure (10-30 chapters)
- **Begin:** new challenge / objective / mystery.
- **Middle:** escalation, obstacles, discoveries.
- **End:** resolution, consequences, new opportunity, larger question.
Every completed arc should make the world feel larger; the universe should feel bigger
every ~20-30 chapters (new environments, phenomena, cultures, creatures, technologies).

## Travel & Side Characters
- Travel is never filler — it must carry development, discovery, training, mystery, or
  conflict.
- By ~Ch.50 readers should have several favorite characters. Side characters need goals
  independent of Jace; they must not exist solely to support him.

## Engineering Advantage & Competence
Celebrate diagnostics, repair, manufacturing, drones, sensors, logistics, systems
thinking. Competence should be *interesting.*

## Stakes Escalation
Power growth must not reduce tension. Every advancement creates new opportunities, new
dangers, new responsibilities. ("What new problem/risk emerged because of this gain?")

## Wonder vs Explanation
Readers should understand enough to stay oriented but not receive complete explanations
too fast. Magic should feel discoverable, consistent, and vast. **Mystery should survive
explanation.**

## Chapter Evaluation Checklist
Engagement (why continue? strong final hook?) · Progression (which tracks advanced?) ·
Mystery (advanced/answered?) · Character (relationship changed? anyone grew?) · Setting
(world larger?) · Competence (meaningful problems solved?) · Tension (up/down/static?) ·
Serial strength (would a reader open the next chapter immediately?).

## Warning Signs (flag)
>2 consecutive chapters without meaningful progression · excessive exposition · travel
without purpose · repetitive training · interchangeable side characters · mysteries
answered faster than new ones appear · Jace solving via luck · long stretches without
discovery · power growth without consequences.

## Ideal Reader Reaction (each chapter)
"I learned something new, the world became larger, the characters moved forward, and I
need to know what happens next."
