# Scene — Day 2: Acclimation Day

## Summary

Jace has two days to acclimate before formally reporting for duty. Day 2 is his alone — no obligations, no schedule, no sponsor. He is a golden-boy lieutenant on Mars for the first time with a free day. He uses it the way Jace uses most things: actively.

This scene is deliberately brief in the text — it exists as a paragraph or at most a page, referenced obliquely when Rich asks about it on Day 3. Its job is not to be a scene but to create a scene's worth of texture in retrospect.

---

## What Jace Does

### The Geese (Morning)
He goes back to the Bird Dome. He paid 100 credits to see the goslings yesterday; today he reasons he already paid, and the dome is large enough that staff don't clock repeat visitors at the gate. He is wrong — they do notice, and they let him back in for half price because he is in uniform and because one of the handlers recognizes him from the Mars Goose Cam incident the previous day. He spends an hour just watching the geese.

This is not the purposeful Jace of the gym and the drone center. This is Jace standing at the fake lake edge, hands in his pockets, watching baby geese argue over a piece of synthetic grass. He is not doing anything. He is, possibly for the last time for a long while, just standing somewhere and letting it be pleasant.

He records a short video for his sister Athena and sends it. It goes through (Day 2 comms are still working fine for civilian channels). Athena responds within six hours: *"why do you live there and I don't."*

### The Mine Tunnels (Afternoon)
He goes down into the tunnel system below the civilian domes to see the mines. He gets a day-visitor pass through the Mars settlers' public access program — it is a two-hour guided group tour of the upper working levels, not the active extraction zones but close enough to hear them.

It is loud.

The fans are enormous. The machinery — autonomous mostly, robots and loaders and conveyor systems — produces a continuous wall of noise that is not painful exactly but is impossible to ignore. Every surface vibrates slightly. The air tastes of iron and dust despite the filtration. The scale is larger than Jace expected; the tunnels are not cramped but cavernous, lit in functional yellow-white, with machines moving through them with no particular care for the presence of humans.

Jace watches an autonomous drill unit the size of a shuttle segment work a rock face at the end of the corridor. He cannot imagine working here daily. He looks at the civilian workers running the machinery and sees earplugs on every one of them — small bright orange ones on strings around their necks, ready to pop in at any moment. He files that away.

He climbs back up to the surface domes as the tour ends. His ears are ringing slightly. He is glad for the air pressure of the civilian dome.

## Function in the Story

This scene exists primarily to pay off on Day 3, when Rich asks how Jace's off-day went:

> Rich: "How was your day off?"
> Jace: "Good. I went back to see the geese in the morning. Then went down in the tunnels to see the mines. God, it's loud in there."
> Rich: [looks at him, then taps the small orange earplugs on a string around his own neck] "Did you notice almost everyone wears earplugs?"
> Jace: "I did. I didn't have any."
> Rich: "Mars is all about fans and bumps and machines. The noise is everywhere — dome fans, conveyors, maintenance systems, ventilation. The tunnels are worse but the surface domes aren't quiet either. Once you start noticing it you can't stop." [pops his earplugs in briefly, then takes them out] "You'll want a pair. Military covers a standard set. Check your HUD."
> Jace: [checks] "...there's a whole noise-mitigation benefit schedule."
> Rich: "There's a whole everything-mitigation benefit schedule. Speaking of which — let me show you the drone cell."

The earplug beat is a small Mars-texture moment that rewards the reader's memory of the mine tunnels. It also sets up the transition into the drone center tour.

## Character State

**Entry:** Well-rested, free-floating, slightly wired on WiFi and game downloads from the previous night.

**Exit:** A little quieter. He has seen some of what Mars is when nobody is performing it for him — the noise, the scale, the civilian workers with their earplugs, the baby geese arguing over grass. He is ready for the actual job now.
