# Book 1 — Opening Notes

This file is a working sketch of the early scenes of Book 1, capturing structural decisions and prompts for drafting. Not draft prose. Use this as a planning surface; promote material into proper `/scenes/` files as scenes get written.

---

## Pre-Story Backstory (Off-Page)

Before the reader meets Jace, the following has happened:

- Jace has spent six months on Mars in command of part of the U.S. Air Force drone fleet at the United States of Mars settlement.
- He received his cybernetic HUD implant during pre-deployment training; it is now well-integrated.
- He is good at his job and believes in it.
- The medical screening that detects his disqualifying condition has occurred; he is awaiting formal discharge processing.

## Chapter 1 Sketch — The Discharge

**POV:** Jace.

**Setting:** U.S. Air Force Mars Detachment medical and administrative offices. A pressurized building on the surface of Mars. Cool blues and grays. Sterile. Familiar.

**Action:** Jace receives formal notice of his honorable discharge. His commanding officer is sympathetic but constrained. The medical officer explains the condition again, in clinical detail. The administrative officer walks him through the timeline: thirty days to vacate base housing, stipend details, medical care provisions, no return-trip cycler berth provided.

**Beat:** The cybernetic HUD remains. The military Aura license is wiped during the meeting; a commercial Aura license provisions in real time as Jace watches. The new Aura introduces itself. Jace — by training — is polite but distant.

**Beat:** Jace walks out. The base feels wrong. Familiar corridors, but he is no longer of them.

**Closing image:** Jace standing at a window looking out at the Martian surface. The supernova is a bright fixed star in the dark sky. He has never paid it any attention before.

## Chapter 2 Sketch — The Maths of Ruin

**POV:** Jace.

**Setting:** Jace's quarters on base, then a public commercial-services center in the settlement.

**Action:** Jace sits with his Aura and works through his finances. The numbers are bad. Student debt, accumulated military debt, no Earth return trip provided, commercial cycler passage cost. Even with the medical stipend and the military severance, he cannot clear his obligations and live. He cannot afford to go home.

**Beat:** The Aura is helpful in a way the military version never was. It surfaces alternatives without preaching. It does not flatter. It does not push. It just... lays out options. Jace notices.

**Beat:** Among the options: ten-year contracts in the asteroid belt. Animal handler / engineer roles on livestock-primary ferries. The pay is enough to clear his debts and have something left at the end. The contract is binding. The trip out is four months. The recruiter office in the settlement is open today.

**Closing image:** Jace walking through the settlement to the recruiter's office. He passes a window with a Mars-born child looking out. He does not register it.

## Chapter 3 Sketch — The Recruiter

**POV:** Jace.

**Setting:** A consortium recruiter's office. Less polished than the military offices. Practical, slightly run-down, busy.

**Action:** The recruiter (no name yet — establish character) is direct, informed, and unsentimental. She walks Jace through the contract. She does not soft-pedal the work. She emphasizes that ten years is ten years; the contract has limited exit clauses, all of them costly.

**Beat:** She also does not push. She tells Jace that if he is signing because he sees no other option, he should think harder. The belt is hard work and harder living. People who go for the wrong reasons do not last.

**Beat:** Jace signs.

**Beat:** The recruiter — perhaps the first character in the story to genuinely see Jace as Jace rather than as Lieutenant Hale — gives him a list of what to bring, what not to bring, and the date to report for ferry training.

## Chapter 4-6 Sketch — Ferry Training

Four-week intensive training program on Mars before the ferry departs. Jace learns:
- Basic livestock handling for the species he will be responsible for.
- Shipboard engineering basics.
- Emergency life-support procedures.
- Animal welfare and veterinary basics.
- Sensor operations for the rotating cylinder.

**Character introductions:** Jace meets some of his future ferry crewmates during training. Establish:
- The captain (TBD name) — older, experienced, has done this run multiple times. Hard but fair.
- The engineer (TBD name) — sardonic, technically brilliant, pre-existing relationship with the captain.
- The senior animal handler (TBD name) — the person Jace will report to. Possibly a Mars-born or earlier-belt-experienced person. Patient teacher; will be one of Jace's first real cross-cultural relationships.
- One or two settler passengers introduced briefly — perhaps a family with children, or an older couple emigrating. (Establish stakes for the journey beyond just the crew.)

## Chapter 7 — Departure

**POV:** Jace.

**Setting:** Mars orbit, at the ferry. Jace has just been ferried up from the surface in a shuttle.

**Action:** Boarding. Stowing personal effects. First experience of the rotating cylinder under transit conditions. First meeting with all the animals he will care for over the next four months.

**Beat:** Jace and his Aura have a conversation about the strangeness of leaving Mars. Aura asks something gently insightful. Jace deflects.

**Beat:** Departure burn. The reactor engages. The ferry accelerates. The view from the small port shows Mars receding.

**Closing image:** Jace in his cabin (small bunk, shared with one other crewman), unpacking his minimal possessions. The HUD overlay flickers briefly with a warning he has never seen before — sensor anomaly, confidence: low. He dismisses it.

## Beats and Threads to Establish in Early Chapters

- **Jace's debt and need.** Concrete numbers. Reader must feel the trap.
- **Jace's relationship with the new Aura.** It is becoming a partner, not yet a friend.
- **The HUD as both useful tool and source of anomalies.** Plant the idea early that it shows things the world might not yet account for.
- **Cross-cultural texture on Mars.** Even in the U.S. settlement, Jace's interactions are not exclusively American. Mars-born characters, settlers from other countries, Aura-mediated translation.
- **The supernova as a constant background presence.** A bright star always visible. Mentioned in passing repeatedly. Never explained until much later.
- **Animal-handler skills as foreshadowing of relational learning.** Jace, who has commanded drones, must learn to attend to creatures that have their own minds.

## Anomalies to Plant in Transit Chapters

A series of small, deniable, increasingly difficult-to-explain incidents during the four-month transit:

1. An animal in one canister behaves anomalously. Patterns the senior handler can't explain.
2. A sensor reading disagrees with what Jace's eyes report. Aura flags low-confidence anomaly.
3. A small object moves when no one is looking. Jace dismisses it.
4. Jace has a dream that turns out to predict a real event two days later.
5. A crewmember has a moment of behavior that seems to affect the room without contact.
6. Late in transit: a more serious incident — a pressure scare, a near-emergency, with cause that cannot be determined.

Each incident should escalate. Each should be deniable until the cumulative weight forces recognition.

## Climax of Book 1

(*Sketch only.*)

After arrival at Demeter Station: an unambiguous magical incident, witnessed by Jace. Possibly involving an animal he has bonded with. Possibly caused by Jace himself. The reader sees it; Jace sees it; the in-universe community is forced to acknowledge that something physically impossible just happened.

Coupled with: the communications blackout being formally imposed. Jace realizes he is now isolated from Earth and Mars in a way he never imagined.

Closing image: Jace standing on the station looking at the bright fixed star of the supernova, realizing — perhaps for the first time consciously — that something is changing.
