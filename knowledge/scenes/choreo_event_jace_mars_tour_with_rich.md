## Setting Layout (Spatial Map)

The scene moves through six distinct spaces in sequence. Each has its own sensory profile.

### Space 1: The *Pinta* Disembarkation & Mars Terminal
- **Type:** Industrial passenger terminal, cruise-ship-equivalent
- **Population:** ~150 disembarking passengers + terminal staff + base personnel
- **Pace:** Slow, processional, weary
- **Layout:** Long curving terminal building connected to Pinta's docking arm. Multiple processing checkpoints. Final exit into the Observation Dome.

### Space 2: The Massive Medical Scanning Machine
- **Type:** Room-sized full-body scanner, latest generation
- **Where:** A specific checkpoint within the terminal processing flow
- **Significance:** This machine catches Jace's bone disease. Foreshadowing happens *here* — but Jace doesn't know yet.
- **Visible details:**
  - Scanner is the size of a small room — Jace walks into it
  - Multiple imaging stages: standard scan (~30 sec), then deeper scan (~2 min)
  - Display screens visible inside and outside
  - Operating technician at a control console
  - **[STORYBOT]** Specific make/model/manufacturer of the scanner — extrapolate; could be a Mars-fabricated unit, possibly newest-generation made on Mars, possibly with brand recognition

### Space 3: The Observation Dome
- **Type:** Massive glass-paneled welcome bubble for arrivals
- **Function:** First impression of Mars; meant to be dazzling
- **Population:** Sparser than terminal; arrival families, sponsors, base reps waiting
- **Defining feature:** The Redwood (see sensory primer below)

### Space 4: The Connected Domes (Walking Tour Route)
- **Type:** Network of pressurized domes connected by airlock-equipped doorways
- **Function:** Civilian + commercial + agricultural Mars settlement infrastructure
- **Domes Rich shows Jace (in approximate order):**
  1. Observation Dome (start)
  2. Commercial/civic dome (food vendors, shops)
  3. Park dome (large trees, hydroponic greenery)
  4. Algae farm dome (food production, distinctive smell)
  5. Coffee Tree Dome: As detailed in Patch DD.2. Sensory: warmer air, different atmospheric pressure, faint green-coffee smell, rows of coffee trees, Rich's favorite spot. Time: ~15 minutes.
  6. Bird Dome: As detailed in Patch DD.3. Sensory: higher air pressure (Jace feels it in his ears), taller dome, fake lake, geese with goslings, real birdsong. 100-credit entry per person; Rich pays. Time: ~20 minutes.
  7. Animal-handling dome (foreshadowing — Jace will work here later)
  8. Rover garage dome (transition to military base)

### Space 5: The Rover (Surface Transit)
- **Type:** Pressurized surface vehicle, 4-6 person capacity
- **Function:** Inter-dome transit on Mars surface
- **Duration:** 10-minute drive
- **Sensory:** First time Jace experiences Mars *surface* outside a dome

### Space 6: The Military Base
- **Type:** Hardened utilitarian facility, separate from civilian infrastructure
- **Function:** U.S. Air Force Mars Detachment headquarters
- **Population:** Mostly uniformed, mix of officers and enlisted, drone operators, security
- **Defining feature:** Concrete construction, drone hover pads, military rigor

## Sensory Primer

### Visual

**Mars surface (visible through dome panels):**
- Color palette: deep red, rust, ochre, dusty pink, occasional flashes of black volcanic rock
- Light: stark, hard shadows due to thin atmosphere — almost no atmospheric scattering
- Sky: pale pinkish-tan during the day; deeper red/brown at horizon
- Two moons (Phobos, Deimos) visible during day in Mars's thin sky
- Sun appears about 60% the size it does from Earth
- Boulders, mountains, cliffs are all *bigger* than Earth equivalents — no erosion has worn them down
- Long shadows everywhere, even at midday

**The Redwood Tree (signature installation):**
- Massive Earth redwood, ~80 years on Mars
- Plaque: "Gift from Earth" (or similar — **[STORYBOT]** flesh out exact inscription)
- Surrounded by hydroponic feed lines, climate control
- Other Earth-genetic and Mars-engineered trees nearby for biodiversity
- Lit dramatically — a centerpiece installation
- **Cultural significance:** This is one of the most important things on Mars; people gather around it; it's a monument

**Inside the domes:**
- Bright sunlight filtered through glass panels — eerily harsh, not Earth-like
- Greenery in parks (Earth-imported and Mars-engineered species)
- Maintenance evidence: panels missing, caution tape, broken machinery
- Newer parts shiny/clean; older parts worn — the contrast is everywhere
- Screens: news, ads, AI-generated entertainment, civic information
- Hover drones with large fans (compensating for thin Mars atmosphere) moving everywhere

**Military base interior:**
- Drab, utilitarian, concrete
- No frills — function over form
- Heavy emphasis on screens, cameras, monitoring
- Drones with even bigger fans than civilian areas
- Hover pads constantly being scrubbed clean by dirt-removal robots
- Insignia, flags, U.S. military iconography throughout

### Auditory

**Constant background:**
- Turbines (large)
- Air handlers / wind pumps
- Ventilation systems
- Drone fans (always present, varying volume)
- Robot scrubbing/cleaning sounds
- AI voices (subtle, mostly in HUDs but occasionally public announcements)

**In the terminal:**
- Crowd murmur
- Announcement chimes
- Pressurization equipment
- Customs/processing equipment

**In the parks/civilian domes:**
- Voices, conversation
- Children sometimes
- Vendor sounds, food preparation
- Music in some spaces (commercial)

**Algae farm dome:**
- Bubbling, water circulation
- Industrial pumps
- Quieter than expected

**Animal-handling dome:**
- Animal sounds (chickens, goats, pigs faintly)
- Hay rustle
- Farmhand voices

**Military base:**
- Sharp, controlled
- Boots on concrete
- Radio chatter
- Drone command beeps
- Salutes — fabric-on-fabric, hand-to-temple

### Olfactory

This is critical to your worldbuilding — smells *carry* between domes through the air system.

- **Terminal:** sterile, antiseptic, cleaning chemicals, faint suit-rubber smell
- **Observation Dome:** filtered/clean, faint plant smell from the redwood and surrounding greenery
- **Commercial dome:** food smells (depending on what's being cooked) — coffee, frying, baking
- **Park dome:** soil, plant matter, hydroponic chemical hint
- **Algae dome:** distinctive — mineral, sea-like, slightly briny, vaguely fish-pond-but-cleaner
- **Animal dome:** hay, dust, dirt, livestock
- **Cross-dome bleed:** algae smell can reach the next dome over; animal smells linger in nearby corridors

### Tactile / Kinesthetic

- **Gravity:** Mars 0.38g — Jace's first time experiencing it. Steps feel too easy; he occasionally over-corrects and bounces.
- **Air pressure:** Slightly lower than Earth standard inside domes (saves on atmospheric losses). Jace may notice slight ear-popping.
- **Temperature:** Climate-controlled, comfortable. Outdoor surface (rover ride) is intensely cold but he's in a suit.
- **Suit weight:** The pressure suit Jace wears is lighter on Mars but still bulky. Helmet on/off rituals are physical.
- **Surfaces:** Concrete (military), composite paneling (civilian), regolith outside.

### Atmosphere/Mood

- **Terminal:** Tired, processional, herd-like
- **Observation Dome:** Awe-struck, marketing-grade dazzle
- **Civilian domes:** Lived-in, busy, real
- **Algae/Animal domes:** Industrial, working
- **Rover ride:** Brief moment of vulnerability — outside everything
- **Military base:** Sharp shift to discipline, hierarchy, professionalism

## Beat-by-Beat Choreography

### Beat 1: The *Pinta* Disembarkation (~03:00–04:00)

**Setting:** *Pinta* docking arm, Mars terminal entry

**Action:**
1. Jace, in a pressure suit (helmet on), shuffles forward in the disembarkation queue
2. Other passengers ahead and behind, all suited, all weary
3. The line moves slowly through the docking arm into the terminal
4. As Jace passes through the connection, his suit's HUD displays a "Welcome to Mars" overlay (military-issue, slightly more austere than civilian welcome screens)
5. Inside the terminal, helmets come off — the air is breathable
6. **Sensory shift:** suddenly the smells, sounds, voices of Mars hit him

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Stiff from voyage, recently woken, helmet-pressed hair, stubble
- Emotional: Mixed — tired, anxious, but underlying excitement
- Body language: Following the herd, doing what he's told

**[STORYBOT]** The exact moment Jace removes his helmet — make this a small ritual. First breath of Mars-recycled air.

### Beat 2: Terminal Processing (~04:00–07:30)

**Setting:** Mars terminal customs/medical processing area

**Action:**
1. Jace queues at customs
2. Identity verification via HUD — biometric scan, military ID
3. Customs officer (terse, professional) clears him
4. He's directed to medical processing
5. **The scanning machine moment:**
   - Jace enters the room-sized scanner
   - Stands in the marked position
   - Initial scan completes (~30 sec) — green lights
   - Then a deeper scan begins (~2 min)
   - During the deep scan, Jace notices: a screen briefly flashes red/amber, additional indicator lights activate, the technician's posture changes slightly
   - Jace feels a *twinge* of intuition — *something might not be right*
   - The technician makes a note, then says casually: "You're cleared, take your time."
   - Jace exits, shaking off the unease
6. Final processing: paperwork (largely electronic, HUD-driven), final stamp
7. Directed to the Observation Dome to meet his sponsor

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Increasingly tired (30+ hours awake)
- Emotional: Confident → uneasy (during scan) → relieved (after clearance) → forgets about it
- Body language: Military bearing despite fatigue

**[STORYBOT]** The exact dialogue the technician uses can be embellished. The key beat is the *twinge* — the reader needs to feel it without Jace fully registering it.

### Beat 3: Meeting Rich in the Observation Dome (08:00)

**Setting:** Observation Dome, facing the Mars surface through massive glass panels, the Redwood centerpiece nearby

**Action:**
1. Jace exits processing, finds himself in the Observation Dome
2. **Visual hit:** Mars surface stretching out beyond the glass — first real, unhurried look
3. Rich is waiting, in dress uniform, holding a tablet
4. Rich spots Jace, raises a hand, walks over
5. **First exchange:**
   - Rich: "Lieutenant Grant? I'm Rich Cullivan."
   - Handshake (informal, peer-to-peer)
   - Rich: "Welcome to Mars. How was the trip?"
   - Jace: [exhausted but trying to be friendly] "Long. Glad to be off the ship."
   - Rich: [grinning] "Yeah, the *Pinta* gets cramped. Come on, let me show you around before you collapse."
6. They begin walking — Rich points out the Redwood first
7. Rich explains: "Eighty years old. Came here as a sapling. We baby it. The whole city celebrates its birthday."

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Carrying his helmet, suit half-doffed, exhausted
- Emotional: Trying to project competence; genuinely struck by the Redwood
- Body language: Officer's posture despite fatigue; eyes flicking around taking in environment

**Rich's state:**
- Physical: Fresh, energetic, uniform crisp
- Emotional: Excited to meet his new peer, looking forward to friendship
- Body language: Open, gestural, smiling, leans in when speaking

**[STORYBOT]** Rich's verbal tics during this introduction can be developed organically. Suggestions: he says "yeah" a lot, gestures broadly, has a tendency to over-explain. See voice profile.

### Beat 4: The Walking Tour (~08:15–11:00)

**Setting:** Multiple connected domes, walking tour route

**Tour stops (with Rich's commentary):**

**4a. Commercial dome (~08:30):**
- Vendors, shops, food stalls
- Rich: "This is where everyone hangs out. There's good coffee at [shop name — **[STORYBOT]** invent]. You'll need it."
- **Sensory:** food smells, vendor voices, screens advertising products
- **Foreshadowing:** Jace will learn how expensive food is the next morning
- the WiFi/data-hunger exchange, indicating how much Jace missed having good data connectivity and how proud Rich is to keep the networking stable.

**4b. Park dome (~09:00):**
- Real grass (engineered for Mars), benches, walking paths, trees
- Rich: "People come here when they need to remember they're alive. The atmosphere helps with mental health."
- Rich gives Jace the sunscreen reminder: "By the way — you'll need sunblock anywhere there's direct sun through the panels. The UV gets through. People underestimate it. I've got some at my place if you don't have any."

**4c. Algae farm dome (~09:30):**
- Multiple connected agricultural domes visible through glass walls
- Bubbling tanks, harvest floors, processing equipment
- Rich: "This is where most of our food comes from. Multiple domes — redundancy. If one goes down, we still eat."
- **Sensory:** distinctive algae smell — Jace registers it as briny, mineral, not unpleasant but unfamiliar
- **Foreshadowing:** This is where Jace may end up working post-discharge

**4d. Animal-handling dome (~10:00):**
- Visible through observation windows (not entered directly)
- Pigs, chickens, goats; handlers in coveralls
- Rich: "Most of the meat. They're working on cattle but it's expensive. Goats and chickens scale better."
- **Sensory:** hay, dust, livestock — different but not bad
- **Foreshadowing:** Jace will work here briefly post-discharge, will adopt Cerberus from related stock

**4e. Mid-tour pause (~10:15):**
- Brief stop. Rich asks Jace about his family.
- Jace: mentions parents in Virginia, sister in college (Athena), older sister at Odysseus Station (Artemis)
- Rich: "Wow, Odysseus? That's the deep end. Smart family."
- Rich shares: "I've got three kids back on Earth. My wife handles them while I'm up here. I'll be cycling home in a few years."
- Jace: feels a small pang — Rich has a family; Jace is single. Notes Rich's pride.

**4f. Rover garage (~10:45):**
- Domed garage, multiple rovers tethered to charging stations
- Rich: "Time to suit up. Military base is a quick ride."

**Jace's state across this tour:**
- Physical: Increasingly tired but pushing through; sensory overload
- Emotional: Excited → overwhelmed → finding his rhythm with Rich → genuine curiosity
- Conversation pace: Quiet at first, opening up as Rich's chattiness encourages him

**Rich's state across this tour:**
- Physical: Tireless host
- Emotional: Excited to share, genuinely warming up to Jace, but also subtly *evaluating* him
- Behavior: Constant pointing, gesturing, naming places, telling stories

**[STORYBOT]** Specific stops can be reordered or expanded. The tour route is suggestive, not strict. Add specific shop names, vendor names, individual people Rich greets as they pass — this is a chance to populate the world.

Rich is consistently carrying his coffee canister throughout the tour. He drinks from it during pauses, between beats, when he wants to land something. The canister is part of his physical presence. When he gets in the rover next, he secures hist canister first before putting on safety harnesses. **[STORYBOT]** Background extras may notice and acknowledge it ("Morning, Rich — refill?" from a passing cafeteria staffer is a viable piece of color).

### Beat 5: The Rover Ride (~11:00–11:10)

**Setting:** Rover, Mars surface, traveling to military base

**Action:**
1. Helmets on. Pressurization check.
2. Rover exits the dome airlock — onto Mars surface for the first time
3. **Visual hit:** open Mars terrain, the dome cluster receding, military base ahead
4. Conversation in the rover (suit comms):
   - Rich: "First time outside?"
   - Jace: "Yeah."
   - Rich: "Don't get used to it. Most of us don't go outside much."
5. Rich points out the military base as it comes into view — concrete, hardened, unmistakably different in character from civilian domes
6. Rover docks at military base airlock

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Tired but adrenalized — outside Mars for the first time
- Emotional: Awe + slight fear (vulnerability of being outside)
- Body language: Pressed against the rover window, taking it in

**[STORYBOT]** The conversation can be expanded. Rich could mention specific Mars-surface features visible during the ride — a distant mountain, a crater, the curve of the horizon (closer than Earth's).

### Beat 6: Military Base Arrival (~11:10–12:00)

**Setting:** U.S. Air Force Mars Detachment

**Action:**
1. They cycle through the airlock
2. Helmets off
3. **Sensory shift:** drab, utilitarian, sharp discipline
4. Soldiers salute as Rich and Jace pass — Jace returns salutes (officer's bearing snaps back into place)
5. Rich introduces Jace to a few enlisted personnel: "Sergeant — this is Lt. Grant, our newest. He'll be running drone sweeps with us."
6. Brief friendly exchanges — the enlisted are excited to meet the new boss
7. Rich shows Jace key facilities: drone command center (briefly), officer's mess, comm rooms
8. **Glimpse — but no formal meeting:** Through a window, Jace sees a meeting room with the Colonel, an unfamiliar woman (Mei), and a nondescript man (Sylvester). He doesn't know them yet — they're a brief visual.
9. Rich notices Jace looking, says casually: "Intel folks. They're around sometimes. Hush-hush stuff."
10. Tour of military base wraps with Rich saying: "Okay, you're gonna fall over. Lunch, then I'll get you to your quarters."

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Running on fumes
- Emotional: Comfortable in the military environment — this is *his* world
- Body language: Tighter, more controlled — officer mode

**Rich's state:**
- Physical: Energized
- Emotional: Watching Jace integrate, pleased

**[STORYBOT]** Specific enlisted personnel Rich introduces can be invented. Useful to plant 1-2 names that recur later in the book.

### Beat 7: Lunch Together (~12:00–13:00)

**Setting:** Officer's mess (military base) OR a return to a commercial dome restaurant — TBD by author preference

**Action:**
1. They sit at a table, eat lunch
2. Casual conversation — Rich asks more about Jace, Jace asks about Rich's role
3. Rich establishes the schedule:
   - "Tomorrow at noon: medical evaluation."
   - "Then 14:00: formal welcome with the Colonel."
   - "After that, you start your real work."
4. **The HUD/spending conversation:**
   - Rich notices Jace's HUD configuration during the conversation (visible to other HUD users via shared overlay protocols)
   - Rich: "Nice rig. That's the new model?"
   - Jace: [proud] "Yeah. I upgraded right before deployment."
   - Rich: "Whoa, that's not cheap."
   - Jace: "I figure I'll pay it off with the Mars bonuses. It's basically my favorite thing about the military."
   - Rich: [internal pause — concerned but doesn't show it] "Wow. I've got kids back on Earth — I could never afford something like that."
   - Jace: "No problem. I volunteered for the six-year tour. Bonuses should pay off all my debts and college tuition."
   - Rich: "By then, an even newer model will be out."
   - Jace: "Yeah, but I'll be a Captain by then and can afford a nicer model."
   - Rich: [shakes his head, doesn't push it]
5. Rich pays for lunch — "First lunch on me. You'll see why everything's expensive soon enough."
6. Rich walks Jace to his quarters, gives him a key code, says: "Sleep. You need it. Tomorrow's a big day."

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Done. Ready to fall over.
- Emotional: Excited about tomorrow, blissfully naive about the financial conversation
- Body language: Slumping slightly despite trying to stay sharp

**Rich's state:**
- Physical: Still energetic
- Emotional: Genuinely fond of Jace already — mixed with growing concern about his naivete
- Body language: Watchful, friendly, slightly paternal

**[STORYBOT]** The HUD/spending conversation should land lightly — Jace is too tired to register Rich's concern. The reader should see what Jace doesn't.

### Beat 8: Jace at His Quarters (~13:30–14:00)

**Setting:** Jace's small apartment in residential building — bed, kitchen, bathroom

**Action:**
1. Jace enters, drops his bag
2. Looks around — small but his
3. Goes to the bed, sits — buzzing with excitement despite fatigue
4. Sets three or four alarms on his HUD (overachiever — he wants to be ready)

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Crashed

## Dialogue Style Markers

### Rich's Voice (this scene)
- Informal, peer-to-peer (no rank formality with Jace)

- "Yeah" as common opener/filler
