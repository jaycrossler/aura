# Scene — First Gym Session on Mars

## Purpose

Show Jace at peak physical confidence in the place he is most comfortable: a gym. Establish his daily exercise practice (which everyone on Mars maintains for bone and muscle reasons — and which, ironically, is what he will need *more* of after the discharge). Plant a small flirtation beat that confirms his self-image: he is attractive, he belongs, this place is his stage. The discharge will land harder for the reader having seen him here.

## Position in Sequence

After Jace settles into his quarters — possibly the same evening as arrival, more likely the morning of his medical checkout. A short scene; not a chapter, more like a hinge between the messages-home beat and the doctor scene.

## Setting

The U.S. Air Force Mars Detachment gymnasium. A real fitness facility — substantial, not improvised. Mars-built equipment: weighted resistance machines tuned for 0.38g (so the loads feel proportionally heavier than Earth-equivalent numbers suggest), a running track with adaptive treadmills, free weights, dedicated bone-loading platforms with pneumatic resistance, the works.

Crowded. Mars residents take exercise seriously because they have to. Mandatory daily PT for military; strong cultural pressure for civilians. The gym at any hour has people in it.

## Characters Present

- Jace Grant (POV)
- Background gym population: military, civilian contractors, Mars-born young adults, mixed
- A young woman exercising near him — flirtation moment, no name yet (could become a recurring background figure or remain a one-beat character)

## Emotional Beats

1. **The walk in.** Jace enters in PT gear, towel around neck. Notices himself being noticed — heads turning, the small social signal of a new arrival who looks the part. He is six feet tall, broad-shouldered, blond, fresh from Earth-gravity training. In a room full of Mars residents — many of them Mars-born and built for low gravity, taller and lankier than Earth-norm — he stands out as *substantial*.

2. **Settling in.** He claims a bench. Begins a familiar routine. The 0.38g feels strange for resistance work; he overloads his first set by reflex and has to recalibrate. Smooths it out within a few minutes.

3. **The looks.** He registers, with quiet pride, that several people are watching him work. He is not arrogant about it; he is just aware. The watching includes a young woman on a nearby machine.

4. **The flirtation.** Eye contact. A small smile from her. He returns it. She looks away first, smile lingering. He catches her glancing back a few minutes later. They do not speak. The exchange takes maybe forty seconds total. He thinks about her on and off through the rest of his workout.

5. **The Mars-born observation.** As he moves through his routine, he notices the bodies around him — taller, narrower, longer-limbed than the Earth-born he's used to. Some of the youngest people in the gym look like distance runners by build and aren't even trying to. He registers this with the curiosity of an anthropologist. He also registers, with a small private satisfaction, that *he* is the strong one in this room. He is wrong about how long this will be true, but right that it is true today.

6. **The HUD overlay.** His HUD provides workout data — heart rate, lactate estimate, recommended pacing. A new feature is real-time form analysis, which suggests a small adjustment to his squat depth. He tries it. The advice is correct. He grins to himself. *He loves this thing.*

7. **The shower and exit.** Showers in the locker room. Catches his own reflection in passing — pleased with what he sees, in the way a young man who has not yet been broken by anything is pleased with his reflection. Walks back to his quarters in good spirits.

8. **Closing.** Jace falls asleep that night thinking about the woman from the gym, the look she gave him, the day ahead. He sleeps well. The doctor's appointment is in the morning.

## Atmospheric Details to Hit

- **Mars gym culture.** Everyone exercises seriously. This is not vanity; it is medicine. The bone-loading platforms see constant use. Two-hour-plus daily routines are normal.
- **Body composition variance.** Earth-born and Mars-born bodies are visibly different. The Mars-born young adults are visibly distinct — taller, less dense. (This is exactly the population Jace's bone disease will eventually make him *more* like, except much worse.)
- **HUD as enhancement.** Form analysis overlay, biometric tracking, music routing through bone conduction — Jace is using his HUD for fitness in ways civilian athletes can't. He doesn't think about this advantage; he assumes it.
- **Background screens.** The gym has displays — Earth sports, Mars fitness influencer videos, news. Jace half-watches a soccer match from Earth that finished six hours ago.

## What This Scene Establishes

- **Jace's body as identity.** He is at peak fitness. He knows it. The reader needs to know it. The discharge takes this from him in ways he cannot foresee.
- **Mars exercise culture.** Two hours daily, mandatory in spirit if not in regulation. Bone density and muscle maintenance are universal concerns. (This makes the bone disease especially cruel: Jace is doing all the right things and it isn't enough.)
- **The Mars-born population as visible cohort.** Plants the eventual cultural-political dimension of Mars-born identity.
- **Jace's confidence in his attractiveness.** Quietly, not loudly. He is the kind of pretty that doesn't fully know it is pretty — he registers attention but doesn't wield it. The reader sees this and the gym-flirtation confirms it.
- **HUD-augmented life.** Subtle but constant — his world is informationally enhanced in ways that will feel like phantom limbs once it goes dark.

## What This Scene Foreshadows

- **The bone disease (cruel irony).** He is doing exactly the exercise everyone does to maintain bone density, and it is not enough. The disease is the kind that defeats good behavior.
- **The "Mars-okay, Earth-no" verdict.** The Mars-born bodies he is observing — taller, lighter-boned, adapted — are essentially what his future is going to look like. He will become more like them than them, in the worst version of this trade.
- **The HUD loss.** Every overlay he uses today is going dark in 24 hours.

## Open Questions

- Whether the woman becomes a recurring character or stays a one-scene flirtation. (Recommended: she could be one of the settlers on the *Falcon* later, which would be a small cruelty when Jace reconnects with her after losing everything else.)
- Specific exercises Jace runs through. (Squats, deadlifts, bone-loading pneumatics, treadmill work — needs research-level detail if we want the prose to feel authentic.)
- Whether the gym scene includes any subtle anomaly beat (dropped weight, momentary HUD glitch, an item briefly behaving oddly). Could plant the magic emergence very faintly.
