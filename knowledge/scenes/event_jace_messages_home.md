# Scene — Messages Home

## Purpose

Establish the Grant family as warm, supportive, and real before the discharge tears Jace away from them. Plant the Artemis communication problem as something off — not yet alarming, but noticeably wrong. Show Jace at his most genuine: in private, with people who love him, where the cocky golden-boy facade can drop a little.

## Position in Sequence

Evening of Mars arrival, after the Rich tour and before the doctor appointment. Could be the closing beat of Chapter 1 or a short opening of Chapter 2.

## Setting

Jace's small assigned quarters at the U.S. Air Force Mars Detachment. A single room: bunk, desk, terminal, locker, narrow window onto the dome interior or surface. He sits on the edge of the bunk with the terminal mounted on the wall. The HUD overlay can mirror the call to his vision; he prefers the wall screen tonight, eyes still adjusting.

## Characters Present

- Jace Grant (POV)
- Dawn Grant (mother, on call from Virginia)
- Chris Grant (father, on call from Virginia)
- Athena Grant (younger sister, on call from her university)
- Artemis Grant (older sister, attempted call to Odysseus Station — does not connect)

## Emotional Beats

### Part One — Parents

1. **The connection clicks through.** Mars-Earth latency is real but tolerable for asynchronous conversation — call is staged as recorded back-and-forth rather than synchronous video, with each side sending video messages and waiting for replies. Jace has prepared a short video to send first.

2. **The send.** He records: he made it, the trip was uncomfortable but fine, Mars is amazing, the base is everything he hoped, his sponsor is a good guy, his medical checkout is tomorrow and then he's on duty. He keeps it cheerful and slightly performative — they raised him to be a man who tells his mother good news first.

3. **The reply, minutes later.** Dawn and Chris in their living room in Virginia. Familiar furniture. Dawn is teary in the way that only registers if you know her. Chris is proud in the way that only registers if you know him. They congratulate him — *you finally made it, you're paying off that college debt at last*. Dawn asks if he's eating. Chris asks about the equipment. Both of them try not to mention how far away he is.

4. **A small private moment.** Dawn says, near the end, *"You sound tired, sweetheart. Sleep tonight."* Jace, who has been on his feet for nineteen hours, registers the small accuracy of being seen by his mother across two hundred million kilometers. He says he will.

### Part Two — Athena

5. **A separate call to his younger sister.** Athena is in her university dorm room — books visible, a poster, the scattered chaos of a student's life. She is younger and warmer than her older brother, and her affection has none of his self-consciousness.

6. **Sibling tone.** She teases him about the gravity making him stumble (he has not mentioned stumbling; she just knows). Asks if Mars is as red as the documentaries. Says she's proud of him in a way that lands harder than she meant.

7. **A small ordinary detail.** She mentions a class she's struggling with. He suggests resources. She rolls her eyes. He laughs. The reader sees him *being a brother*, in a register he won't get to occupy for very long.

8. **A goodbye.** *"Be safe up there."* *"It's literally just a base, Athena."* *"Be safe up there."*

### Part Three — Artemis (Failed)

9. **The third attempt.** Jace tries Artemis at Odysseus Station. He has been looking forward to this all day — Artemis is his hero, the eldest, the one who got out and made her own way. He records his message in advance because Odysseus is far enough that synchronous is impossible: forty-minute to an hour-long round trips on a good day.

10. **The send fails.** The terminal returns an error. Signal degradation. He retries. Second attempt: send appears to succeed but no delivery confirmation. Third attempt: connection drops mid-send.

11. **The first wrongness.** Jace frowns. Latency he expected. Outright failures he didn't. He checks his Aura — the connection diagnostics suggest a relay problem on the Odysseus side. His Aura recommends trying again in a few hours.

12. **He files it.** He doesn't know yet that this is the beginning of a months-long degradation that will cut him off from her almost completely. He just shrugs, makes a note in his Aura to try again tomorrow, and goes to bed.

13. **Closing image.** Jace in the dark of his quarters, supernova bright through the small window. He doesn't yet know the supernova matters. He doesn't yet know his sister has already started seeing things. He just knows he's tired, and he's on Mars, and tomorrow is the first day of the rest of his life.

## Atmospheric Details to Hit

- **Latency as texture.** Mars-Earth is a real round-trip lag (3-22 minutes one way depending on planetary alignment). At story start the alignment gives a moderate lag. The conversation pattern — record, send, wait, watch, reply — should feel natural and modern, not jarring.
- **Odysseus as another order of magnitude.** Mars-Odysseus is hours per round trip on a good day. The communication failure is not surprising in *kind*; it is surprising in *degree*.
- **The HUD on standby.** Jace runs the calls on the wall screen but his HUD provides quiet enhancement — overlay text of the conversation, biometric monitoring, Aura suggestions for follow-up topics. He turns off the suggestions; he wants to talk to his family without optimization.
- **Family home detail.** The Grant family living room in Virginia should feel real and specific. A photo on the mantelpiece. A sound from another room. The kind of domestic texture that makes the eventual blackout devastating.

## What This Scene Plants

- **The Grant family as anchor.** Warm, loving, real. The reader needs to feel this before the disease and discharge cut Jace from his future, and before the blackout cuts him from these people too.
- **Athena as the youngest.** Affection without performance. Sets up her later role as the one who stays.
- **Artemis as the absent eldest.** Mentioned, attempted, not reached. Plants the comms-degradation thread that the doctor will confirm and the blackout will complete.
- **Jace's private register.** Less performed than his Air Force self. The reader sees who he is when no one is grading him.
