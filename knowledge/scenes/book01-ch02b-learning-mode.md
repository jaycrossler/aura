# Chapter 2b — Learning Mode

## Summary

Comedic release after the crisis. Some sewage plumbing burst during the chaos and
flooded a ceiling run; now the corridor light globes along Main Street are tinged
brown. Jace is on cleanup. He puts **Thing One and Thing Two into "learning mode"**
(they imitate his demonstrated actions) because they don't have a light-change routine.
He pries off one light cavity; in low-g it drifts down rather than falls; it's full of
**stinking brown clumping sewage water**. His low-g reflexes still aren't perfect — he
swats at it to catch it and instead **smacks it against the wall, spraying himself**
head to chest. He turns to stop the drones — and both, faithfully in learning mode,
**pop their own globes and swat them at the wall and all over him.**

It's all on the ship's social cameras. By the time he reaches Fortuna, everyone has
seen it. "Learning Mode" becomes his station nickname/legend.

## Function in the Story

- **Tone**: pure comedy after the high-stakes crisis — the register the series needs to
  earn its horror. Tonal anchor = the marionette-drone choreography note: faithful,
  literal, escalating mimicry.
- **Reputation / influence (downward-funny)**: this — not the heroics — is what Fortuna
  knows him for first. Pays off on arrival (Nick and others greet him with it). Sets up
  the gap between how Jace wants to be seen and how he's actually seen.
- **Drone characterization**: Thing One/Two as competent-but-literal; "learning mode"
  as a real feature; the comedy is *their* fidelity, not malfunction. Also another
  quiet brick in the Origin-surveillance wall: of course it was recorded.
- **Jace voice**: self-effacing, gallows-humor, recovers with a line rather than a sulk.

## Beats

1. **Setup**: brown-tinged globes; Jace muttering about whose plumbing this was; no
   light-change routine on file, so he flips the drones to learning mode and narrates:
   *"Watch. Pry the ring, ease the globe, catch it. Pry, ease, catch. Simple."*
2. **The fumble**: first globe drifts free, heavier and wetter than expected; he
   mis-times the low-g catch; *smack* — sewage across his face and chest.
3. **The horror dawning**: he turns, dripping. Thing One and Thing Two have already
   moved to the next two globes, mirroring "pry, ease, catch."
4. **"Noooo—"**: both drones pry, ease, and **swat**, faithfully reproducing the smack,
   redecorating Jace and the corridor.
5. **Button**: Jace, motionless, coated, to the nearest camera he knows is on:
   *"...Cut learning mode."* Cerberus sneezes and backs away from him with visible
   judgment.

## Illustrative lines (register only)
> Jace: "Okay. We're gonna do something useful. Pry, ease, catch. You're learning."
> [later] Jace: "No — no, that was not the part to copy—"
> Jace (to camera, dripping): "For the record, the first one was the pipe's fault."

## Dialogue / Tone Notes
- Keep it physical and fast; don't over-narrate the joke.
- The cameras are ambient and unremarked until the payoff — the reader realizes with
  Jace that this is *definitely* on the feeds.
