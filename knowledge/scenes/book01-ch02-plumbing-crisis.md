# Chapter 2 — The Plumbing Crisis (Rewrite)

## Summary

A primary ring water pipe tears open and vents tons of water into space. The water
freezes as ice over one arc of the ring, encasing two animal berths. The asymmetric
mass throws the spinning ring into a worsening wobble — a grinding thump felt once per
rotation, lengthening in interval and growing in force as Maureen slows the spin to
buy time. Before a spoke strut fails, Maureen prepares to seal the corridor airlocks
and **cut the iced section away** — which would kill the trapped animals. Jace argues
for a different fix and gets a grudging window to try it. He counter-ballasts the ring
by pumping water and reserve propellant into the canisters on the opposite arc,
balancing the mass and stopping the wobble. With the ring stable, the external ice is
cut away by the drones — Thing One and Thing Two doing the cutting, **Sparky's sensor
core pressed into early service as the coordination node**.

It works. The animals live. And it costs: the maneuver was untested and it
**cross-contaminated the ship's water reserve with propellant**, eating into the thin
emergency margin and compromising part of the radiation-shield water. Maureen is
furious. Origin will later reprimand him. Everyone else calls him a hero. He has to
hold both of those at once.

Threaded under all of it, at the height of the emergency: the **first breach-crosser
incursion** (see Function, below, and [[magic_breach_crossers_transient]]).

## Function in the Story

- **Engineering competence under pressure** (Jace Principle): the fix is the *Falcon*
  doc's own prescribed response to a Spin Imbalance — "move water ballast to restore
  equilibrium." Earned, not lucky.
- **Stakes escalation with a real cost**: the win creates a new problem (contaminated
  reserve, thinner safety margin for the rest of transit). Advancement breeds danger.
- **Maureen relationship**: her fury is *justified*, not arbitrary grumpiness — he
  gambled the ship's margin to save livestock. This deepens both characters.
- **Resource progression / drone introduction**: Sparky's first real use; Thing One
  and Thing Two shown doing serious work (and quietly logging it all back to Origin).
- **Seeds Plot 1**: the incursion (frost, torn pipe, dead sensors, the glimpsed bug)
  is planted here, unexplained, and pays off across the series.
- **Reckless naive hero**: he thinks he's a hero; the institution he works for thinks
  he's a liability; the truth is both.

## Beats

### Beat 1 — The Spill
The sensor cluster that should have flagged the leak is already dead (Jace finds this
out after). The first sign is the *Falcon*'s voice changing — the constant pump-thrum
spikes, then a section goes wrong. Pressure alarms. Water is venting somewhere it
should not be. (Note the short-crewedness: with the cancelled settlers and Saul on
Mars, there are too few hands — this is the cancellation directly raising the stakes.)

### Beat 2 — The Wobble
Vented water freezes over one arc of the ring, encasing **two animal berths** (see
Animal-pod question in review-queue — likely the pig berth and a small-animal berth).
The off-center ice mass throws the ring out of balance.

> **Physics to keep honest (author wants the math visible):**
> Imbalance thumps **once per revolution**. At 0.3g / 40 m radius, ω = √(0.3·9.81/40)
> ≈ 0.271 rad/s, so the rotation period is **~23 s**. The thump lands every ~23 s at
> full spin. As Maureen **de-spins** to reduce strut load, the period *lengthens*
> (23 → 27 → 34 s…) even as the amplitude *worsens*. So "getting worse" = harder, not
> faster — a slow, deepening grind, not a quickening one.
> (Earlier draft said "every 14 seconds"; 14 s implies ~0.84g at 40 m, off-canon.)

The grinding transfers into a spoke strut. Maureen starts the de-spin; it will take
hours; the strut may not have hours.

### Beat 3 — The Cut Decision
Maureen's call: seal the corridor airlocks bracketing the iced arc and cut the section
free before a strut snaps and takes the ring with it. The trapped animals die. It is
the correct, conservative captain's decision.

> Maureen: "I'm not losing the ring for a pen of pigs. Seal it."
> Jace: "Give me the de-spin time you're already spending. If I can't balance it before
> the strut goes, you cut. You lose nothing by letting me try."
> Maureen: "I lose the time I spend watching you fail."

She gives him the window anyway — because he's right that she loses nothing, and
because (unspoken) Siren and the animals are not *nothing* to her.

### Beat 4 — The Ballast Fix
Jace does the moment-balance in his head/HUD: the ice sits at radius ~40 m on one arc;
he needs comparable mass on the opposite arc to bring the center of mass back onto the
spin axis. He floods the opposite-arc canisters with whatever mass he can move fastest
— **recirculating shield/ballast water plus reserve propellant** — letting the fluid
AI trim the rest. The thump lengthens, softens, **stops.** The ring is stable.

The cost, which he registers only after: he has dumped propellant into water-reserve
canisters. The fluid AI can no longer cleanly separate ballast/shield water from
propellant. He's spent part of the **20% emergency margin** and degraded a section of
the radiation-shield water to save the animals. (See ship_falcon fluid system.)

### Beat 5 — Cutting the Ice (Drone Set-Piece)
With the ring stable, the frozen mass still has to come off the hull. EVA is out;
drones do it. **Thing One and Thing Two** cut and chip the external ice; **Sparky** —
not yet a finished build, but its sensor/scan core ("the Ball") works — is pressed in
as the **coordination node**, mapping the ice and sequencing the two cutters so they
don't destabilize the ring again. First time the three drones operate as a team. First
time Sparky does something only Sparky can do.

### Beat 6 — The Incursion (Plant — keep ambiguous)
At the height of it, cutting into a ceiling panel to reach the torn pipe, Jace finds:
- **Large chunks of ice inside the cavity** — more than the vented water explains, and
  in the wrong place (interior, dry-side).
- **Long rents in the steel pipe** — torn, not punctured.
- The **sensor cluster destroyed** — the one that should have caught the leak.
- And, for a moment, in the half-dark behind the panel: **a large dark insectoid
  shape, two red eye-points, sharp pincers — like a child's toy left in the wall.**
  He blinks. It's gone. Second look: nothing.

He files **micrometeorite** in the incident report because it's the only catalogued
cause for a holed pipe. The rents, the dead cluster, the interior ice don't fit it. He
knows they don't. He tells no one about the shape.

> Jace (private log / dry): "Cause: micrometeorite. Probable. It punched the pipe,
> tore it lengthwise, killed the whole R-cluster, and left ice where there's no water.
> Micrometeorites are very busy lately."

Do **not** explain it. Reader gets the dread; the answer comes much later. See
[[magic_breach_crossers_transient]].

### Beat 7 — The Dressing-Down
Crew and (via cameras) Fortuna treat Jace as a hero. Maureen does the opposite, in
private, hard:

> Maureen: "You stabilized my ring by poisoning my water and burning my margin on a
> trick nobody's ever tested, in the dark, with livestock. If it had gone the other
> way you'd have spun us apart faster. You don't get to be lucky and call it skill."

She is right. He half-knows she's right. He can't quite let go of having saved them.

### Beat 8 — Saul's Note (quiet beat)
Time-delayed from Mars, after the logs reach him: a brief private message thanking Jace
for saving his ship. Understated, technical, warm. Maureen reads it (she reads them
all) and says nothing. Leave it to breathe. (See [[char_saul]], [[char_maureen]].)

## Dialogue / Tone Notes

- The incursion is shown, never narrated-as-magic. Engineering frame only.
