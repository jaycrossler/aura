# Scene Cluster — Assembling Artemis's List (The Smuggling Sprint)

## Overview

Jace has approximately six days (less than he thought — the message was three weeks old) to assemble the items on Artemis's list, get them to the Rat, and have them loaded onto an unmanned supply ship to Odysseus Station. He does this while also working his regular gig jobs, because he cannot afford not to. He is exhausted, broke, running on Aura-assisted logistics coordination, and increasingly aware that whatever is happening at Odysseus is worse than a supply problem.

This is the most active and outwardly directed week of his hard months — and the week he is most ashamed of, because part of it involves theft.

---

## Artemis's List — What She Needs

The list is divided into three tiers, based on how easy each item is to acquire on Mars:

### Tier 1 — Gray Market (Acquirable, But Not Through Normal Channels)
These items exist on Mars; they are just controlled, restricted, or artificially expensive due to corporate embargoes or export licenses:

- **Lead/bismuth ingot — high purity, 10kg.** Radiation shielding material. Not directly illegal but export-controlled; Origin and the other major shippers won't move it without a corporate account. Jace will find a gray-market broker through the Rat.
- **Analog medical supplies:** Manual blood-pressure cuff, physical scalpel set, silk sutures. These are being discontinued in favor of digital equivalents and hard to find; a black-market medical supplier has a cache. The Rat knows the supplier.
- **Mechanical watches, as many as Jace can get.** Old-style, non-digital, mechanical only. Preferably quality brands. The Rat suggests he check the pawnshop district — people brought Earth watches to Mars and have been selling them off as they run out of money. Jace spends two afternoons in pawn shops and comes away with six watches. Two are Seiko. One is a scratched vintage Omega with a broken crystal. He buys all of them.
- **Paper — not printable stock. Real, fibrous, acid-free paper.** For actual writing. Mars has almost none; there's no supply chain for it. The Rat finds a cache in a university supplies depot — someone ordered it for an art preservation project. Jace buys the entire stock; it fills a small box.
- **Gall ink and steel-nib pens.** Artemis's list is specific: *not digital ink, not gel, not ballpoint. Iron gall ink. Steel nibs.* This is the hardest Tier 1 item. Jace eventually locates a calligrapher in the civilian dome who makes her own gall ink and sells pens. She is suspicious of Jace until he pays well.

### Tier 2 — The Coffee (Special Category)
Artemis's note on coffee is emphatic: *"Send every Mars-grown coffee bean you can get. Real beans, whole, unprocessed. This is currency up here. People will trade almost anything for it. Get as much as you can possibly fit."*

Coffee is Jace's biggest acquisition challenge because Mars coffee is expensive and Jace is broke. His solutions:
- **Rich's connection at the Coffee Tree Dome.** Jace asks Rich — carefully, awkwardly — whether the cultivator connection Rich mentioned during the Day 1 tour is still available. Rich, who is exhausted and hasn't had a good night's sleep in three weeks, looks at Jace for a long moment, then makes a call. He gets Jace ten kilos of beans at cultivator-direct pricing. It is still expensive by Jace's standards. He buys all of it.
- **Civilian market.** He buys smaller amounts from three separate vendors to avoid flagging any single large purchase. Four more kilos.
- **Total:** Approximately fourteen kilos of whole Mars-grown coffee beans. They take up a significant fraction of the 2x2x2 meter crate.
- **His own supply:** He has a small personal supply of coffee beans he bought for himself during the early hard months before he realized he couldn't afford the habit. He adds his personal supply to the crate. He doesn't have coffee for the remaining weeks before departure. He feels the absence every morning.

### Tier 3 — The Chinese Military Warehouse
Several items on Artemis's list are not available through any civilian or gray-market channel that the Rat can locate. After two days of asking, the Rat comes back to Jace with a specific address and a specific problem:

> Rat: "There's a Chinese military logistics warehouse near the industrial dome cluster at the east perimeter. They've got spare electronic components for mining drone systems — the specific ones your sister wants. They also have some sealed mineral samples from their Jupiter-survey equipment. These have been sitting in a secondary annex for months. Not critical stock. Not watched every minute."
>
> Jace: "You want me to steal from a Chinese military warehouse."
>
> Rat: "I want you to recover some items that are sitting unused while your sister is in trouble. The framing is yours."
>
> Jace: [long pause] "Tell me about the security."

---

## The Chinese Warehouse Incident

This is not dangerous. It is something Jace is deeply ashamed of afterward and will not describe to people in detail.

**What actually happens:** The Rat has a contact who works in the warehouse's civilian support staff — cleaning and maintenance, rotating schedule. The contact leaves a secondary access door on a maintenance-access corridor unlocked during a specific two-hour window. Jace enters, locates the secondary annex by following the Rat's layout notes on his Aura, retrieves a sealed crate of electronics and one sealed sample container from a shelf that has not been touched in what the dust suggests is months, and exits. Total time inside: under twenty minutes. He sees no one.

**Why he's ashamed of it:** He is a former Air Force officer who swore an oath. He has never stolen anything. He is stealing from a military facility — not his own branch, but still. He feels like a criminal because he is, for twenty minutes, committing a crime. The fact that it is easy and no one catches him and no one is apparently hurt by it does not make him feel better. It makes him feel worse — like he was capable of this all along, and the only thing that kept him honest was having other options.

He doesn't tell Rich. He doesn't tell Lina (she has already left). He thinks about it for days.

> *In his personal log, that night: "I did something today I'm not going to write down. I'll know what I mean."*

## The Rat — First Real Meeting

The Rat has been a background presence at the Long Burn bar across several visits. This week is the first time they meet with purpose.

**Setting:** A private table at the Long Burn, or a neutral location the Rat prefers — a walkway booth, a food stall with enough ambient noise for privacy.

**First impression of the Rat:**
He is not what Jace expected. The name "the Rat" suggests something furtive. The man is not furtive. He is relaxed, specific, and clearly has done this many times. He dresses like a mid-level civilian contractor. He has an unremarkable face that Jace's HUD, trying to cross-reference against any database, comes back with nothing on. Either the Rat has scrubbed his presence from every accessible record, or he has several parallel identities of which this one is clean. Probably both.

He knows about Jace before Jace introduces himself:

> Rat: "Former Air Force. Drone ops. Discharged for a health issue three months ago. Sister on Odysseus. Needs a supply run." [pause] "The coffee request is smart, by the way. She knows what she's doing."
>
> Jace: "How do you know about the coffee?"
>
> Rat: "I know what's valuable out there. She knows what's valuable out there. You asked about the wrong thing first."

**The Rat's offer:**
He can get Jace a 2x2x2 meter slot on the next unmanned supply ship to Odysseus. The ship leaves in eight days from the Mars launch facility. The slot has to be loaded and sealed in six days. He takes twenty percent of declared cargo value, or a flat fee — Jace can choose. Given the coffee, the flat fee is better. The Rat names a number that is uncomfortable but survivable on Jace's current cash.

**The Rat's character in this meeting:**
He is the "heart of gold, pocket of more gold" version: he genuinely wants to help Jace get this to Artemis. He also is going to make his twenty percent. Both are real. Neither cancels the other. When Jace expresses concern about the warehouse item, the Rat doesn't lecture him about it — he gives Jace the layout, gives him the window, and says:

> Rat: "I've moved supplies to Odysseus twice before. Both times, the people who got the supplies are still alive because of it. Whatever's happening out there — this matters."

He doesn't explain what he knows about "whatever's happening out there." He doesn't need to. Jace hears it.

## What Gets Sent (Summary)

The crate that ships to Odysseus contains:
- 14kg of whole Mars-grown coffee beans (packed in sealed vacuum bags)
- Lead/bismuth ingot, 10kg
- Analog medical kit (manual BP cuff, scalpels, silk sutures)
- 6 mechanical watches (assorted)
- Acid-free paper, approximately 500 sheets
- Gall ink and steel-nib pens
- [TBD items from the Chinese warehouse — electronics and mineral samples]
- A handwritten note from Jace, on paper, in pen: *"Got most of it. The watches were the hardest. I drank your coffee allocation for three months. You owe me."* He pauses, then adds: *"Be careful. Everyone here thinks something's wrong out there but nobody will say what. I'll be there before the year is out. Stay put."*

He seals the note inside one of the coffee bags before vacuum-sealing it.

## Aftermath — What This Week Does to Jace

He assembled the list. He met the Rat. He did something he is ashamed of. He got his sister's supplies onto a ship.

And in the process of sourcing everything, running down every gray-market contact, navigating every restricted access and corporate log and shipping manifest — he discovered something he didn't expect: **he is very good at this.** His Air Force logistics training, his drone-ops pattern recognition, his HUD-integrated Aura coordination — all of it transferred to this completely different problem. He solved it in six days.

This is the week that plants the seed for what Jace becomes in the belt. He doesn't know it yet. He is mostly just tired and ashamed about the warehouse.

But he remembers, in a quiet moment after the crate is loaded: that he could do this. That when it mattered, he figured it out.

## What This Scene Leads Into

The Origin contract and Sylvester's recruitment follow within days of completing the list. The Rat, having seen Jace work, is the one who mentions the Origin opening. The connection was already there (Origin is in the Rat's network); the Rat just hadn't had a reason to bring Jace in until now.

The horse farm offer call (Scene 06f) arrives in this same window — either just before or just after the list is assembled. The timing makes Jace's refusal of the parental sacrifice more pointed: he already has a plan. He is not refusing his parents' offer from desperation; he is refusing it from the specific plan taking shape.
