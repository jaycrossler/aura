## Setting Layout (Spatial Map)

The scene moves through four distinct spaces.

### Space 1: Jace's Apartment
- **Type:** Small studio in residential building
- **Layout:** Bed, small kitchen, bathroom, single window (likely faces dome interior, not surface)
- **Size:** Compact — efficient Mars residential
- **Population:** Just Jace
- **Mood:** New, slightly impersonal — he hasn't made it his yet

### Space 2: Residential Building Common Areas
- **Type:** Honeycomb-pattern corridors connecting apartments
- **Function:** Transit between residential units, gym, food vendors, common spaces
- **Layout:** Disorienting on first navigation — multiple identical-looking corridors, multiple elevators
- **Population:** Other residents going about their morning
- **Defining feature:** A breakfast/coffee vendor area Jace stumbles upon

### Space 3: The Gym
- **Type:** Large multi-purpose fitness facility
- **Function:** Daily mandatory exercise for Mars residents (bone density, muscle mass)
- **Layout:** Multiple zones — treadmills, weight machines, free weights, "pulling tables" floor units, observation areas
- **Population:** Hundreds of people, peak morning capacity
- **Defining feature:** Observation windows looking out across the dome interior or onto Mars surface

### Space 4: Travel + Base Approach
- **Type:** Brief — corridor walk + rover ride to military base
- **Function:** Transition to medical appointment

- Wayfinding signs (some screens, some old-fashioned signage)

**Breakfast/coffee vendor area:**
- Small storefront-style food kiosk
- Menu boards (digital, with prices Jace finds shocking)
- Other patrons grabbing quick breakfast
- Coffee aroma very prominent
- **[STORYBOT]** Specific vendor name, menu items — invent

**The gym:**
- Massive open space
- Bright LED lighting throughout
- Hundreds of people in motion
- Treadmills in long rows
- Weight machines with visible counterweight gravity-compensation systems
- Observation windows showing Mars dome views or surface
- Screens *everywhere* — playing news, entertainment, ads, fitness leaderboards
- Color palette: institutional grays, accent colors for equipment, bright displays
- Mirrors throughout (allows people to watch others without direct looking)

**Mars-born vs Earth-born bodies (visible):**
- Mars-born: taller, slimmer, longer-limbed, less muscular (adapted to 0.38g)
- Earth-born (recently arrived): shorter, denser, more muscular by comparison
- Jace stands out — broad shoulders, dense Earth-built physique
- People notice this difference; it's a visible cultural marker

### Auditory

**Apartment:** Quiet, ventilation hum, distant building sounds
**Corridors:** Footsteps, voices, occasional elevator chimes
**Breakfast area:** Coffee machine sounds, cooking noises, conversation, vendor calls
**Gym:**
- Constant low rumble of treadmill motors
- Weight clanks (controlled — Mars residents have learned to lift carefully)
- Heavy breathing, occasional grunts
- Background music (low volume, motivational)
- Screen audio bleeding through (news anchors, entertainment)
- AI voices through HUDs (subtle, personalized to each user)
- Air handler systems louder here due to ventilation needs

### Olfactory

**Apartment:** New-occupant smell — slightly antiseptic, faint cleaning chemicals
**Corridors:** Nothing distinctive — well-ventilated
**Breakfast area:** Coffee, eggs, breakfast meats — strong, alluring
**Gym:**
- Sweat (concentrated, especially near treadmills)
- Disinfectant (constantly cleaned equipment)
- Rubber/composite (mat surfaces, equipment)
- Faint metal tang from weights
- Body warmth concentrated in dense crowd

### Tactile / Kinesthetic

**Gravity:** 0.38g — Jace is starting to adjust but still occasionally over-corrects
**Weight vests:** Most gym users wear them — adds 30-50% body weight to compensate for low gravity. Jace is offered or already wearing one. It's heavy and constraining.
**Treadmills:** Pull-down systems that increase effective gravity during use — Jace runs in something like 0.7-0.9g effective gravity. This feels familiar to him (close to Earth) but harder than walking around the dome.
**Sweat:** Builds up faster than expected — gym is well-ventilated but the workload is intense

### Atmosphere/Mood

- **Apartment:** Solitary, transitional
- **Corridors:** Disorienting (first navigation)
- **Breakfast:** Quick, efficient, mildly social
- **Gym:** Energetic, communal-but-individual — everyone working their own routine but all together
- **Travel to base:** Anticipatory

## Beat-by-Beat Choreography

### Beat 1: Waking Up (~08:00)

**Setting:** Jace's apartment

**Action:**
1. Three or four alarms go off in sequence on Jace's HUD
2. Jace wakes — first moment of disorientation (where am I?)
3. The dome window light, the room, the recognition: Mars
4. Stretches, sits up
5. Goes to bathroom, looks in mirror, registers his own face — fresh, rested, alive
6. Quick shave
7. Pulls on workout clothes (shorts, tech shirt, performance gear)
8. Heads out the door

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Rested for the first time in days, fresh, energized
- Emotional: Excited, confident — *today is the start of his life*
- Body language: Crisp, deliberate

**[STORYBOT]** The alarm sequence can be embellished — what does Jace's HUD look like first thing in the morning? What ads/notifications/news does it surface?

### Beat 2: Fumbling Through Honeycomb Corridors (~08:15–08:45)

**Setting:** Residential building common areas

**Action:**
1. Jace exits his apartment into a corridor that looks identical to ten others
2. Tries to navigate from memory — gets confused
3. Takes a wrong elevator, ends up in wrong section
4. **HUD intervention:** His Mars Aura instance offers navigation help
5. He accepts, follows directions
6. Passes other residents heading to morning routines — nods, polite
7. Eventually finds the food/coffee vendor area
8. Greets vendor:
   - Vendor: "Morning. What'll it be?"
   - Jace: "Coffee, eggs, anything quick."
   - Vendor: "[Quotes price — let's say 25 credits]"
   - Jace: [internal shock — that's expensive]
   - Jace: [externally] "Sounds good."
9. Pays via HUD-mediated transaction
10. Eats quickly, standing at a counter or small table
11. **Key foreshadowing moment:** The price registers in Jace's mind. He has no idea yet how much this will matter.

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Hungry, slightly disoriented, gradually centering
- Emotional: Mild frustration with navigation → sticker shock → adapting
- Body language: Officer trying not to look lost

**[STORYBOT]** The breakfast vendor's name, exact menu, and exact prices should be fleshed out. The sticker shock is the beat.

### Beat 3: Finding the Gym (~09:00–09:15)

**Setting:** Continued corridor navigation

**Action:**
1. With HUD navigation, finds the right elevator
2. Descends to gym level
3. Doors open — *the gym reveal*
4. Bright, massive, alive with activity
5. Jace pauses for a moment — *this is his happy place*
6. Smiles, walks in confidently

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Energized
- Emotional: Genuine joy — gyms are where he feels most himself
- Body language: Shoulders back, confident stride

### Beat 4: Settling Into the Workout (~09:15–09:45)

**Setting:** Gym floor, treadmill section

**Action:**
1. Jace surveys the equipment
2. Notices weight vests at a station — grabs one, puts it on (adjustment for his Earth-density body)
3. Walks to treadmills — long row, most occupied
4. Finds an open one
5. Programs his workout — running, fast, sustained
6. Begins running
7. Settles into rhythm — body remembers what to do
8. Notices the gravity adjustment via the treadmill — closer to Earth feel, comfortable
9. **Background awareness:** Other people on adjacent treadmills, including the two girls
10. Jace's HUD games begin (he opens his game during the run — see Beat 5)

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Warming up, sweat starting
- Emotional: Centering, comfortable
- Body language: Loose, in his element

### Beat 5: The HUD Game and the Girls (~09:45–10:00)

**Setting:** Gym treadmills, ongoing run

**Action:**
1. Jace's HUD game launches — a fully-immersive combat sim he plays regularly (or strategy game — **[STORYBOT]** select)
2. **POV split:** Jace appears to be staring straight ahead, but he's actually deeply engaged in the game inside his HUD
3. **Two girls on adjacent treadmills notice him:**
   - Girl 1 (to Girl 2): "Look at him. He must be from Earth."
   - Girl 2: "How do you know?"
   - Girl 1: "Look at his shoulders. Mars-born don't get like that."
   - Girl 2: "Use your AI. Find out who he is."
   - Girl 1: [pulls up info via HUD] "Public profile says newly arrived. Single. No military rank shown publicly."
   - Girl 2: "He's gorgeous. You should talk to him."
   - Girl 1: [giggling] "I can't just walk over there."
   - Girl 2: "Look — he's looking at you!"
   - Girl 1: [turns to look] "Oh my god, he is."
4. **Twist:** Jace appears to be looking at the girls but is actually deep in his HUD game. His eyes are tracking game elements, not them. He has no idea they're discussing him.
5. The girls keep glancing at him, hoping for engagement
6. Jace continues running, oblivious, occasionally making small expressions related to game events (a smirk at a kill, a frown at a miss)
7. The girls eventually give up and continue their own workouts, slightly disappointed

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: In the zone, sweat building
- Emotional: Engaged in game, mildly aware of bodies around him but not focused
- Body language: Deceptive — appears socially engaged but is not

**[STORYBOT]** The girls' specific dialogue can be embellished. They should have distinct personalities — perhaps one bolder, one shyer. They are not named characters; they are background but fully realized. Their reaction to Jace's obliviousness is part of the comedy.

### Beat 6: Family Video Messages (~10:00–10:30)

**Setting:** Same treadmill, continuing the run

**Action:**
1. Jace's HUD shows two messages from family — overlay notification
2. He pauses the game (or doesn't — he can multitask)
3. Opens the messages while still running
4. **Message 1:** From his mother Dawn — short video, 30 seconds. She's sitting in their Virginia kitchen. "Hi sweetheart, just checking in. We saw the *Pinta* landed safely. We're so proud of you. Dad says hi. Call us when you can." Jace smiles.
5. **Message 2:** From his sister Athena — also short. She's in her dorm room at college. "Apollo! How's Mars? Send pics! I want to see everything! Tell Artemis I miss her too — I haven't been able to reach her either." Jace's smile fades slightly at the Artemis mention.
6. Jace decides to record reply messages while running
7. **Records to Dawn:** "Hi Mom. Made it safely. Mars is incredible. The base is bigger than I expected. How's Jelly? Tell me she's still chasing the geese off the pasture. And how's Jinx? Dad still riding him at sunset? I miss you guys."
 **To Chris (father):** *"Dad. Made it. Base is great. I want to hear about the farm — how's Jinx doing? Send me a picture of him? I'll give you a real call this weekend."
8. **Records to Athena:** "Sis! Yeah, Mars is wild. Tell Mom I'm eating. Give Jelly a scratch behind the ears for me. Love you."
9. **Tries to record/send to Artemis:** Records a message — "Hey big sis. Just wanted to let you know I made it safely. Mars is amazing. Hope Odysseus is treating you well. Send me a message when you can — I want to hear about everything. Love you."
10. **The send fails:** HUD displays an error — "Message not delivered. Recipient unreachable. Retry?"
11. Jace tries again. Same error.
12. **Foreshadowing beat:** A flicker of unease — this isn't normal. Comms latency is one thing; outright failure is another.
13. He files it away — *try again later*

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: In rhythm with the run, sweat heavy
- Emotional: Warm with family love → unease at the failed Artemis message
- Body language: Soft micro-smiles during recording, then a brief frown at the error

**[STORYBOT]** The message contents are suggestive. Embellish based on character voices. The Artemis failure is the key beat.

### Beat 7: Back to Apartment + Grooming (~10:30–11:30)

**Setting:** Jace's apartment

**Action:**
1. Jace finishes workout, sweaty, exhausted in a satisfied way
2. Walks back to apartment (faster this time — he knows the route now)
2a. Replies come back from Dawn and Chris — Jelly is fine, sleeping in the barn, still pretending to be too old to chase the geese (she's not). Jinx is fine — slow on cold mornings but cantering well in the afternoon. Chris attached a short clip of Jinx in the south pasture. Jace watches it twice.
3. **Major grooming sequence:**
   - Long shower
   - Careful shave
   - Hair styled — *just right*
   - Dress blue uniform — pressed, perfect
   - Polished shoes
   - HUD calibration — making sure all his apps are organized and ready
   - Final mirror check — adjusts collar, straightens tie
4. He looks *good* — and he knows it (without being arrogant about it; he just wants to look his best for the Colonel)
5. Heads out

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Refreshed, clean, sharp
- Emotional: Confident, professionally focused
- Body language: Officer's pride — chest out, chin up, nothing slouched

**[STORYBOT]** The grooming sequence is character-revealing. Jace's perfectionism with his appearance shows his insecurity (the boy who needs to look perfect to feel okay). The StoryBot can extend this with internal monologue showing his thinking.

### Beat 8: Arriving at Base — The Sylvester/Mei Glimpse (~11:30–11:45)

**Setting:** Military base entrance, then interior corridors

**Action:**
1. Jace borrows a rover (he figured out the system the day before)
2. Drives himself to the military base — proud of figuring it out
3. Suit on for surface transit, then off at base airlock
4. Enters base, HUD passcode authentication
5. Walks through corridors toward medical wing
6. **Sees Rich:**
   - Rich: "Hey! Look at you, all dressed up."
   - Jace: "Big day."
   - Rich: "How'd you sleep?"
   - Jace: "Like the dead."
   - Rich: "Good. You look ready."
7. **The Sylvester/Mei glimpse:**
   - Through a conference room window, Jace sees three people: the Colonel, a Chinese-American woman (Mei), and a nondescript man (Sylvester)
   - They appear to be in serious conversation
   - There's something *off* about them — they don't fit the rest of the base
   - Jace asks Rich: "Who are they?"
   - Rich: [glances over, casual] "Special intel. Hush-hush. They show up sometimes for briefings. I don't ask questions."
   - Jace: [nods, files it away]
8. Rich points out the medical wing: "Doctor's office is down that hall. You've got fifteen minutes. I'll see you afterwards."
9. Jace heads down the hall

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Sharp, presentable, quietly proud
- Emotional: Confident, looking forward to the next steps
- Body language: Officer mode, military bearing
- **Curiosity flag:** The intel folks register as interesting

**Rich's state:**
- Physical: Same as previous day — uniform, comfortable
- Emotional: Genuinely glad to see Jace
- Body language: Welcoming, easy

**[STORYBOT]** The conversation can be embellished. Rich could share gossip about base life, mention something about the Colonel's mood, etc.

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Composed
