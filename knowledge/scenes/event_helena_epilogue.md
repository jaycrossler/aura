# Epilogue — Helena

## Scene Function

Paired bookend to the Prologue. Helena wakes, resurrected, with no idea what
has happened to her. She believes she is suffering the worst hangover of her
life. The reader — who by this point has lived through Arc 1's magic reveals
alongside Jace — understands what actually happened well before Helena does.
The scene plays the dramatic irony for both unease and dark comedy, then lands
the resurrection reveal in the final beat.

## Must Accomplish

1. Helena wakes cold, disoriented, with a massive headache
2. She is lying naked in her bed; the room is freezing
3. Her breathing mask is off — established as the first time in her life she's
   ever forgotten to wear it to sleep
4. She assumes she is enduring an extreme hangover
5. She tries to get water; the sync pipes seem frozen
6. She grows worried about a possible CO2 leak and reports it to {Penny}
7. She realizes she's nearly late to teach her yoga class and rushes
8. Her last clear memory is finishing yoga class and heading to the bar the
   night before — everything after that is gone; she assumes she simply drank
   too much, though she did not
9. Small environmental wrongness accumulates as she moves through the station:
   doors malfunctioning, a coffee machine failing twice before finally giving
   her a mocha on the third try
10. A crowd begins forming around her, staring, as she moves through the
    station — unexplained to her
11. She begins teaching her class as normal; someone screams — the
    resurrection is revealed to the reader (and to the station) in this beat

## Beats

### Beat 1 — Waking Wrong
Helena surfaces into consciousness cold and aching, worse than she's ever felt.
Naked, freezing, the room's environmental systems reading wrong. She reaches
for her breathing mask out of pure habit and it isn't there — a genuinely
alarming first, since she has never once forgotten it.

### Beat 2 — The Hangover Theory
She builds her own explanation in real time: she must have really gone for it
last night. It's the only theory that fits how she feels. She tries to get
water and the sync pipes seem to have frozen — odd, but station systems
hiccup sometimes. When the wrongness escalates (the cold, the mask, the
pipes), she gets genuinely worried about a CO2 leak and files a report with
{Penny} — practical, station-native instinct overriding the hangover theory
for a moment.

### Beat 3 — Late for Class
The CO2 worry gets shelved the second she realizes what time it is — she's
about to be late for her own yoga class, something she has never done. She
throws on clothes and runs.

### Beat 4 — Memory Gap
As she moves, she tries to reconstruct last night and can't get past finishing
yoga class and heading toward the bar. Nothing after that. She's mildly
embarrassed — must have really let loose at Sophia's party — but not alarmed.
This is her honest, mistaken read on what should terrify her.

### Beat 5 — The Station Is Wrong Around Her
Small failures stack up as she crosses the station: a door that won't open on
the first try, then the second; the coffee machine rejecting her twice before
finally producing a mocha. People are starting to stare. She reads it as her
own rough-morning self-consciousness, not as a station reacting to something
impossible.

### Beat 6 — The Scream
She arrives at the park, breathless, and starts the class like she always
does — settling into the familiar motions is a relief after the strange
morning. Partway in, someone in the gathering crowd screams. Cut.
