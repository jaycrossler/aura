# Scene — Rich's Comms Favor for Jace

## Summary

After Jace is discharged for the bone disease and stripped of his HUD and military comms access, Rich quietly arranges to meet him off-base. As the head of base IT and communications, Rich has access Jace no longer does. He uses it — carefully, within the limits of his own conscience and rank — to do Jace a single significant favor relating to communications. Rich does this because he feels genuinely terrible about what's happened to Jace and because Rich is the kind of person who acts on that feeling.

## Function in the Story

- **Establishes Rich's compassion in concrete action.** It's one thing to be warm and dryly funny on the tour. It's another to risk professional exposure to help a friend who can no longer help himself.
- **Sets up Rich as a recurring resource.** This is the favor; later, Jace may need (or be tempted to ask for) more. Whether Rich gives more is a real question.
- **Demonstrates the limits of military friendship.** Rich helps. He doesn't help unconditionally. He has a wife and three kids back on Earth and a career he is responsible for. The favor lands with weight precisely because it costs him something to give.
- **Plants the comms-filtering theme.** What Rich can do for Jace, and what he can't, exposes how thoroughly information is filtered in this world. (See [Communications Filtering and Censorship Architecture](../technology/tech_communications_filtering.md).)

## Open Questions / TBD

- **What specifically is the favor?** Possibilities to choose from or combine:
  1. Rich verifies whether Jace's outgoing messages to Artemis (sister at Odysseus Station) are actually being delivered, or being filtered/dropped — and confirms quietly that something is wrong with the comms pipe to Odysseus
  2. Rich helps Jace get one priority message through to family on Earth using base infrastructure (formally improper but not disastrous)
  3. Rich pulls a copy of Jace's own HUD personal-data archive before it's purged, so Jace at least has his memories/logs
  4. Rich runs a back-channel diagnostic on Jace's comms attempts and tells Jace exactly which of his messages are actually arriving where
- **Does the favor succeed cleanly, or does it complicate something later?** Strong recommendation: it complicates. Either Rich realizes something he was not supposed to see (intel-adjacent), or his fingerprint shows up where it shouldn't, or both. This is an opportunity to weave Rich's storyline into the larger comms-filtering arc.
- **Tone of the meeting:** Probably warm, slightly conspiratorial, edged with mutual sadness. Not a heist. Two friends in a cafe.

## Provisional Beats (Sketch)

### Beat 1: The Setup
Jace gets a brief, oblique message from Rich — civilian channel, nothing flagged. *"Coffee?"* Time and place attached.

### Beat 2: The Meeting
They sit. Rich is in civvies, off-shift. The smile is still there but it's quieter than it was on the tour. Small talk first — how Jace is doing (badly), how the kids are (good, growing), how Mars is treating Rich (the same as always).

### Beat 3: The Offer
Rich slides into the actual reason. He doesn't make a speech about it. He just says some version of:

> Rich: "Listen. I can do one thing for you. Maybe two if I'm careful. Not more — I've got the family to think about. But there's *one thing.*"

Jace, who has been asking nothing of anyone, breaks a little.

### Beat 4: The Favor Itself
[Specific favor, TBD — see Open Questions above. Probably enacted on the spot via Rich's personal device or arranged for the next morning at the base.]

### Beat 5: The Cost (For Rich)
Rich is calm but not casual. He acknowledges what he's doing. He doesn't ask Jace for thanks; he asks Jace for discretion.

> Rich: "If anyone asks where this came from — you got lucky. You don't know me anymore. Not for this."

Jace agrees. The reader sees that Rich means it kindly but firmly.

### Beat 6: The Goodbye
They stand. Brief embrace or shoulder-clap. Rich pays for the coffee (he always does). They walk out separately.

## Character States

### Jace
- **Entry:** Lower than he's been in the book so far. Discharged, broke, food-rationed, alone.
- **Exit:** Quietly stronger. Not because he got the favor — because someone showed up. Compassion as a structural support beam.

### Rich
- **Entry:** Heavy with the decision he's already made. He's not asking himself whether to do this; he's already past that.
- **Exit:** A little lighter. He helped. He still has limits. He's not a martyr; he's a husband and father who decided to spend a small amount of his risk budget on a friend.

## Dialogue Notes

- Rich uses Jace's first name. Always.
- Jace, by this point in the story, also uses Rich's first name comfortably. The "LT/Lieutenant" register is gone.
- No "sir" exchanges. They are no longer formally peers in the military sense (Jace is out), but the friendship is the relationship now.
- Rich's tone: practical, warm, bounded. He doesn't dramatize.
- Jace's tone: quieter than baseline. Grateful but also struggling to receive help.

## What This Scene Plants

- **Rich as a resource Jace has used, and may be tempted to use again.** This sets up future tension if Jace's path requires more help than Rich is willing to give.
- **Information about Jace's comms situation** that the favor reveals (depending on which option). Specifically: if the favor reveals filtered comms to Artemis, that thread becomes load-bearing later.
- **A model of compassion that costs something.** Counterpoint to the more transactional relationships in this part of the book (Origin, Sylvester, the Rat).
