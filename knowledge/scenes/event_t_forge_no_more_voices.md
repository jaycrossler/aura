# Scene Contract — "No More Voices"

## Must accomplish (testable)

1. **Maureen's AI aversion, shown in her own words.** Jace asks why the Falcon
   has no ship AI. Maureen: she already hates carrying {Alex}-adjacent systems
   when Origin crews board, has to load {Seek} for Chinese contract runs and
   {Gem} for AlphaCorp runs, and refuses one more voice on her ship. Introduces
   {Gem}/AlphaCorp and reinforces {Seek} without exposition dump — all in the
   register of a contractor complaining about mandatory software.
2. **The majordomo established.** Its flat, put-upon delivery gets at least one
   on-page line. Jace asks why it sounds so annoyed; Maureen: "It's a speaker."
   No curly braces — it is not an AI.
3. **The plate hack revealed.** Jace notices the daily 1-Token plate order in
   his print queue; Maureen collects the 20-Token facility fee per job.
   Establishes: Jace's job includes running the print/fulfillment queue; the
   forge network's facility-fee mechanism; Maureen as a many-grift operator.
   Jace's reaction: insulted, then taking notes.

## Should accomplish

- Seed Jace's automation instinct: he's already eyeing the queue for steps to
  remove (sets up the ArmBot beat).
- Warmth without softness: Maureen answers his questions because he's useful,
  not because she likes him. No acknowledgment of his repair work.

## Must NOT

- Maureen explaining herself apologetically or at length — she talks in
  invoices.
- Any AI name without braces; the majordomo with braces.
- Foreshadow the plumbing crisis directly.

## Emotional beat

Curiosity → small indignity (he *is* the plate-printer) → grudging admiration.
Reader leaves liking Maureen more and understanding Jace's job more, without
either being stated.
