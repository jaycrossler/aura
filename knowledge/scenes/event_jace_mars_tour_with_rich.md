# Scene — Mars Sponsor Tour with Lt. Rich Cullivan

## Purpose

Establish Mars as a setting through Jace's eyes. Introduce Lt. Rich Cullivan as a recurring presence and grounded counterpoint. Plant the HUD-cost / fiscal-naïveté beat that pays off catastrophically when Jace is discharged. Show Jace at peak golden-boy confidence so the fall lands harder.

## Position in Sequence

After Jace disembarks the shuttle on Mars; before his medical checkout. Probably interleaved with or adjacent to the drone control center tour. Could be split across two short scenes (initial pickup + later sightseeing) if needed for pacing.

## Setting

Multiple Mars locations as Rich drives Jace around in a pressurized rover:
- The shuttle landing pad and short transit tunnel to base
- The U.S. Mars Detachment perimeter (visible from the rover)
- One of the larger civilian dome districts (windows out to Mars surface, pedestrians, shops, screens displaying Earth news)
- Briefly: a public park area where Mars residents congregate around the few real plants
- Possibly: a glance toward the fabrication district, the big farm domes, or the consumer electronics district as background context

## Characters Present

- Jace Grant (POV — fresh off shuttle, slightly disoriented by Mars gravity, riding high)
- Lt. Rich Cullivan (sponsor — calm, friendly, observant)

## Emotional Beats

1. **Welcome.** Rich is waiting at the landing pad. Easy, professional handshake. "Welcome to Mars, LT." Jace is excited and a little dizzy from gravity adjustment; he plays it cool.

2. **The drive.** Rich points out landmarks. Jace sees Mars for the first time through a thick polycarbonate window — red sky, dust haze, tall thin people walking past the rover. The sky is wrong in a way he can't articulate. Rich notices him noticing.

> Jace: "God, the wifi here is incredible. I've got — I think I've got six months of game updates queued. The Pinta had pinprick bandwidth. They give you, what, half a meg per cabin?"
>
> Rich: [smiling, sips coffee] "Yeah, the carrier comms are brutal. You're going to love Mars on that front. Everything works here. Almost everything."
>
> Jace: "Almost?"
>
> Rich: "Belt's a different story. Latency goes up, reliability goes down. But here? You're golden. You can finally play with humans again."
>
> Jace: "I have like three hundred friends back home waiting for me to come online. Athena's been running raids without me for six months."
>
> Rich: "She's been carrying you?"
>
> Jace: "She's been bragging about it, yeah."
>
> Rich: "Sounds about right."

- Jace's hunger for **data and connection** after four months of low-bandwidth transit
- His genuine excitement about the WiFi/comms environment — **load-bearing irony** for the comms-degradation arc that drives the rest of his story
- Rich's first hint that **belt comms are different** (foreshadowing without naming the problem)
- Athena as a fellow gamer — a small character note that humanizes both siblings
- Rich's coffee canister mannerism (the sip before landing the line)


3. **The HUD question.** Rich asks how Jace is liking the new HUD. Jace lights up — calls it his favorite thing about the military. Rich shares that his own was activated about a year ago. Brief moment of camaraderie.

4. **The spending reveal.** Jace mentions, with the cheerful pride of someone who hasn't yet learned what money is for, that he has spent close to a year's wages on HUD upgrades, premium app subscriptions, exclusive courseware, and game packs.

5. **The skeptical pause.** Rich's tone shifts — not unfriendly, just *aware*. "Wow. I've got kids back on Earth. I could never afford something like that."

6. **The plan.** Jace explains his fiscal plan with bright confidence: six-year tour, bonuses pay off the debt and tuition. By the time he's a Captain he can afford a nicer model.

7. **Rich's quiet head-shake.** He doesn't argue. Doesn't lecture. Just glances at Jace, looks back at the road, and lets it sit. The reader sees what Jace doesn't.

Rich's tone shifts gently — not interrogating, just curious in the way an older brother might be:

> Rich: "So what's the plan, then? Just — work? Study?"
> Jace: "Yeah. Work, study, gym. Stay focused. Pay it down."
> Rich: "That's it? No entertainment? Maybe meeting a girl?"
>
> Jace blushes. He doesn't mean to. He looks out the rover window and tries to laugh it off.
>
> Jace: "I, uh — I'll figure that part out later."
>
> Rich files it away. Doesn't push.

8. **The park.** They pass — or briefly stop at — a public area where dozens of people are sitting on benches near a stand of small Earth-imported trees. Rich mentions, half-joking, that people miss plants more than they expect to and workers from Pangu come here to see the green. Jace doesn't quite track the comment.

They pass a small group of workers transferring through the dome — Chinese, judging by uniform patches, though most of the personalities of Mars are visibly multinational at the working level. Their coveralls are stained, slightly oily, and there is a faint smell about them that Jace cannot place.
>
> Jace: [quiet] "Is that — jet fuel?"
> Rich: [glancing] "Drone fuel, probably. They're rotating in from Pangu — the Chinese base. The atmosphere over there is rough. Industrial city. The fuel gets into everything — clothes, skin, the air itself. People say you can taste it after a few weeks. Whole base smells like a refinery."
> Jace: "That sounds awful."
> Rich: "It's where the work is, for them. They make a lot of what we burn." [pause] "Be glad you got Terminus."

9. **Coffee Tree Dome:**

Rich leads Jace through an airlock door into a dome that is noticeably warmer than the others — the temperature shifts about five degrees Celsius up, and the air feels heavier on the skin. Lower atmospheric pressure than typical Mars dome standard (or higher — **[STORYBOT]** confirm with author; the point is *different,* and Jace registers the change immediately in his ears or breathing).

Inside: rows and rows of coffee trees. Real, growing coffee trees. Hundreds of them, in tightly spaced cultivation rows under full-spectrum lighting. The smell is faint coffee-green — leafy, not yet roasted. Workers in light coveralls move among the rows, checking moisture and harvest readiness.

Rich's face changes. He smiles wider than he has all morning.
>
> Rich: "These are my favorite. I bring people here just to show them. Mars coffee — *real* Mars coffee, grown right here. The dome has its own atmospheric mix, different temperature, different pressure. The trees prefer it."
>
> Jace: [looking around, impressed] "It smells amazing."
>
> Rich: [holding up his canister, grinning] "Yeah. This is where it comes from. They sell the harvest at *unbelievable* markup, but if you know one of the cultivators, you can sometimes get a better deal."
>
> Jace: "And do you?"
>
> Rich: "I do."
>
[He sips with deep satisfaction.]

This beat plants:
- Mars coffee as a **prized, locally grown commodity** (already canonical in `location_mars_overview.md` — the timeslip + insomnia + coffee-as-essential thread)
- Rich's coffee canister habit deepened — he buys *good* coffee, has cultivator connections
- A specific dome with **different atmospheric conditions** — sets up the bird dome's even-more-different conditions next
- A small character intimacy — Rich showing Jace his favorite place, a low-stakes act of welcome

10. **The Bird Dome:**

They walk a connecting tunnel and arrive at another airlock — but this one has a fee gate. A small reader. A turnstile. A sign listing the entry fee.
>
> Jace: [reading] "A hundred credits *each*?"
>
> Rich: "Yeah. I know. Trust me, it's worth it. I'll get this one."
>
> Rich pays for both of them — two hundred credits, a small but real expense. Jace makes a mental note (*even Rich pays attention to a 200-credit transaction*) and follows him through.
>
> Inside the bird dome: **the air pressure is higher than any other dome they've been in.** Jace feels it immediately in his ears, his sinuses, the slight resistance against his chest when he breathes in. The dome is much taller than the others — built upward to give birds room to actually fly. Trees, real grass, a small fake lake at the center, full-spectrum lighting tuned to a long Earth day.
>
> Birds everywhere. Songbirds in the canopy. Ducks on the lake. **Geese** at the lake's edge, accompanied by a swarm of about a dozen tiny goslings — a recent hatch, still all yellow fluff.
>
> Rich: "There. *That's* what I wanted you to see. New goslings."
>
> Jace: [genuinely delighted] "How many?"
>
> Rich: "Twelve, last count. Hatched a few days ago. There's a full-time channel that just watches the eggs hatch — somebody's always streaming it. Couple million viewers across Mars and Earth, last I checked. Mars Goose Cam is genuinely a phenomenon."
>
> Jace: [laughing] "You're kidding."
>
> Rich: "I'm not. There's also a duck cam, a finch cam, and a peacock cam, but the goose cam is the one. You're going to see promotional clips for it everywhere. The dome makes a fortune off the streaming rights. That's part of why entry costs what it does — they're protecting the on-cam population from rowdy crowds."
>
> They watch the geese for a few minutes. Jace finds himself, briefly, almost *happy*. He hasn't seen this kind of life in months. It reminds him of Virginia and his parent's horse farm.
>
> Rich: [softly] "Yeah. Mars has its moments."

This beat plants:
- The **bird dome** as a real Mars location with engineering specifics (higher pressure for bird flight, taller dome, fake lake, full-spectrum lighting)
- Mars's **whimsical / humane / not-purely-utilitarian** side — the planet has aesthetic and emotional infrastructure, not just industrial
- The **information environment** at work — Mars Goose Cam as a real cultural phenomenon with millions of viewers (cross-references `tech_information_environment.md` nicely)
- The **expense of entertainment on Mars** — 100 credits per person for a bird dome visit; Jace's reaction confirms his cost-naïveté theme
- Rich as the **kind of friend who pays for the experience** without making a thing of it
- A genuine moment of joy for Jace before the discharge — sets up the loss harder


11. **Closing.** Rich drops Jace at his quarters. "Medical checkout tomorrow morning. After that, you're mine — I'll get you to drone command." Jace thanks him. The door closes. Jace is alone in his room on Mars for the first time. He looks out the window at the Martian sky and thinks about how *good* this is.

## Atmospheric Details to Hit

- **Gravity adjustment.** Jace stumbles a little on the way to the rover. Refuses to acknowledge it. Rich pretends not to see.
- **Background noise.** Constant ventilation hum. Jace will adjust to it within days; right now it is loud.
- **Earth screens.** Displays in public areas showing Earth news, Earth weather, Earth sports. People half-watch them the way Earth-bound people half-watch news from far places.
- **Plant scarcity.** People cluster wherever there are real plants. Jace doesn't yet feel the pull; he will.
- **Aura presence.** Rich's Aura quietly handles directions; Jace's Aura, still settling into Mars latency conditions, occasionally lags or surprises him.
- **HUD tutorial moments.** Jace's HUD overlays an info card on a building they pass; he hasn't asked for it. Slight nuisance. He's still learning what to suppress.

- Jace: "Yeah, but I'll be a Captain by then and can afford a nicer model."

## What This Scene Plants

- **Jace's relationship with money.** Establishes the spending pattern that makes the discharge financially catastrophic.
- **Jace's relationship with the HUD.** Establishes how much he loves it, which makes the deactivation a deeper wound than just "lost equipment."
- **Rich's character.** Recurring background presence; positions him to matter later if needed.
- **Mars as a setting.** First real look at the planet through the protagonist's eyes — gravity, sky, ventilation hum, plant-hunger, screens of Earth.
- **Foreshadowing the discharge.** The reader registers what Jace doesn't: this is going somewhere bad.
