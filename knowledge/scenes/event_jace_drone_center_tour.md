# Scene — Jace's Drone Control Center Tour

## Summary

Day 3 — Jace's first day formally reporting for duty at the joint U.S. Air Force / Space Force installation. Rich greets him, briefly asks about his acclimation day (geese and mines), introduces the earplug convention, and then takes him to the drone control center: six operators, HUD-integrated workstations, roughly 400 active drones and 900 robots, a wall of live flight-line feeds, and a passing line that plants the scale of corporate Mars presence for the first time. Through a secure window, Jace glimpses Mei and Sylvester with the base commander.

This is the scene that sells Jace on the job — and quietly previews how much he will lose when his MilX Military OS goes dark.

Rich is carrying his standard travel mug at this point. It is a normal mug. It is not yet the canister it will become.

## Function in the Story

- **Establishes the scope of Jace's future command.** Six direct reports. Hundreds of drones and fixed-wing vehicles. Large-scale robotic maintenance operations.
- **Plants the operators as recurring characters.** They meet their future LT here. When he is discharged before he ever formally leads them, the reader feels the loss.
- **Sells MilX-as-command-tool.** Watching the operators work — their MilX overlays layered on the screens — Jace sees what his own hardware is built to do. This deepens the wound when the Military OS goes dark.
- **Quiet foreshadowing of Mei and Sylvester.** Through a secure-area window, Jace sees them with the base commander. He files them away.
- **Corporate Mars present.** Rich's "almost a thousand drones, half corporate" line plants the reader's awareness that this planet is as much corporate as military.
- **Earplug beat.** The mine-tunnel callback is a small texture reward and a world-building landing.

---

## Setting

Low-light operations room inside the joint base. Operations-grade lighting tuned to keep MilX overlay colors readable. Six operator stations wrap in a half-arc facing a wall of screens. Physical screens plus individual MilX overlays per operator. Real-time flight-line feeds: hundreds of drones in various states, robot crews swarming over them, dust blowing across concrete pads. Other feeds show satellite tracks, orbital traffic windows, inter-dome logistics.

The room hums: ventilation, the murmur of operator voice traffic on throat mics, click of physical controls. It is professionally loud in a way the mine tunnels were not — purposeful noise, information-dense, not brutish.

## Beats

### Beat 0: The Day 3 Bridge (~09:30)

Rich meets Jace at the base entrance. He is carrying his standard travel mug — the one with the base emblem. He already had a sip on the walk over.

> Rich: "How was your day off?"
>
> Jace: "Good. Went back to see the geese in the morning. Then went down in the tunnels to see the mines. God, it's loud in there."
>
> Rich: [taps the small orange earplugs on a cord around his own neck] "Did you notice almost everyone wears earplugs?"
>
> Jace: "I did. I didn't have any."
>
> Rich: "Mars is all about fans and bumps and machines. The noise is everywhere — dome ventilation, conveyors, maintenance systems. The tunnels are worse but the surface domes aren't quiet either. Once you start noticing it you can't stop." [pops them in briefly as a demo, pulls them out] "You'll want a pair. Military covers the standard issue. Check your HUD."
>
> Jace: [checking his Military OS overlay] "...there's a whole noise-mitigation benefit schedule."
>
> Rich: "There's a whole everything-mitigation benefit schedule. Speaking of which — let me show you the drone cell."

They walk to the control center.

---

### Beat 1: Entry and Awe (~10:00)

Rich badges them through a secure door. Jace stops involuntarily — this is bigger than anything he saw at his Earth-side training or in Iran. Rich watches his face and grins over the rim of his travel mug. He remembers his own first look.

> Rich: "Yeah. Welcome to the actual job."
>
> Jace: "I used to fly a fleet of thirty in Iran. Thought I was pretty good at it."
>
> Rich: "Nice. The comms and networking is more my speed — I've got a similar setup downstairs tracking satellites and relays. It's been crazy busy lately with all the anomalies."
>
> Jace: "You mean the sunspots causing outages in the belt?"
>
> Rich: [a beat, a sip] "It's a lot more than sunspots. You'll see once you're read into the program."

He lets it sit. Jace files it away.

---

### Beat 2: The Six Operators

Six operators at their stations — four men, two women, mixed enlisted and junior officer. They notice the new LT entering. Most stand briefly, not full attention, acknowledgment. The sergeant nearest the door turns first.

> Sergeant: "Hey, LT! Welcome."
> Jace: [returns the nod] "Good to be here."

Rich walks Jace down the row, introducing each operator by name and station specialty:

- **Station 1:** Surface drone fleet (cargo, personnel transport)
- **Station 2:** Fixed-wing operations (long-range surveillance, atmospheric science)
- **Station 3:** Flight line robotics (maintenance and turnaround)
- **Station 4:** Base perimeter robotics (security, cleaning, dust management)
- **Station 5:** Inter-dome logistics drones
- **Station 6:** Reserve / training / shift handoff

### Beat 3: The Scale Reveal

At Station 3, Jace lingers. The wall feeds show the flight line — hundreds of drones, hundreds of robots, dust blowing across concrete.

> Jace: "How many machines are we running right now?"
> Operator: "Right now? About four hundred drones across all categories. Closer to nine hundred robots if you count the cleaners and the maintenance bots. Numbers spike during launch windows."
> Jace: [quiet] "Christ."

Rich, behind him, takes a sip from his travel mug and adds:

> Rich: "There are almost a thousand American drone aircraft on Mars total. Between the small drones and the big fixed-wing — your team probably flies half of them. The corporations fly the rest."

Jace turns.

> Jace: "Wait — the *corporations* fly half? On *our* base?"
>
> Rich: "On Mars. Not all on our base — Origin runs their own pads at the launch facility, Stellar has theirs near the fab district, handful of smaller players with leased slots. We share airspace, deconflict orbits, and mostly pretend to play nice."
>
> Jace: [quiet] "Half."
>
> Rich: [smiling, rueful] "Welcome to Mars. Half of everything is corporate. The half that isn't, we run."

---

### Beat 4: MilX Integration Reveal

One of the operators shows Jace how their MilX Military OS overlay layers on top of the physical screens — drone telemetry, robot health status, anomaly flags, predictive maintenance windows. Jace's own Military OS attempts to handshake with the room's systems.

With Level 2 access — granted at his Day 1 biometric scan — he gets a partial handshake: surface telemetry, basic system status, read-only. Not the full command integration of Level 3.

This moment lands for Jace: *his* hardware, the hardware that cost him weeks of pain and one week of blindness to install, was built to do exactly this. He is going to be doing this. He is going to be good at this.

The reader knows: he won't get the chance.

> Jace: [to himself, or almost to himself] "I need Level 3 for the full feed, right?"
>
> Operator: "Once you formally take command, yeah. Level 2 gives you read-only. Level 3 is the whole board."

Rich nods without comment. He knows Jace won't reach Level 3 in time. He doesn't know why yet.

---

### Beat 5: The Glimpse

Through a secure-area window on one side of the room, Jace sees a meeting room: the base commander (Space Force Colonel, black-and-silver uniform), an unfamiliar woman (Mei), and a nondescript man in civilian clothes (Sylvester). Seated, talking. The window's lower half is opaqued; he can see heads and shoulders.

Rich notices Jace looking:

> Rich: "Intel folks. They come and go. I don't ask questions."

Jace files it away. Doesn't think about it again that day.

---

### Beat 6: Wrap and Forward

Rich pulls Jace away from the feeds — Jace would stay all afternoon.

> Rich: "Come on. You'll have plenty of time. Commander welcome next, then we get you settled."

[Rich finishes the last of his travel mug on the walk to the door. He'll refill before the commander meeting.]

Jace looks back once at the wall of feeds before the door closes.

## Character States

### Jace (entry → exit)
- **Entry:** Fresh, officially on-duty mindset, still slightly awed by Mars, already forming the sense that the job is *real*
- **Exit:** Thrilled. This is what he wanted. He already knows which station he'd take if he were operating today. His MilX Military OS has been running at Level 2 all morning and it is *excellent*.

### Rich (entry → exit)
- **Entry:** Normal Rich; his standard travel mug is in hand; he is showing a new person around a place he is proud of
- **Exit:** Fond of Jace; quietly worried about how invested Jace already is; the medical news is the day after tomorrow
