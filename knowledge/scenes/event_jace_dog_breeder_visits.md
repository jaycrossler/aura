# Scene — Jace's Dog Breeder Visits

## Summary

After Jace is discharged and the cheap-food economy of his hard months sets in, he discovers a Mars dog breeder facility somewhere in the U.S. settlement and begins visiting it regularly. Free or near-free entry, and the dogs do not care that he is broke. He spends as much time as he can there over the weeks of his hard months and forms a real attachment to the dogs — specifically to a working-breed mother dog who is pregnant when he first visits, and to the litter she produces.

The mother dies during or shortly after giving birth.

Jace learns about it shortly after. He watches a litter grow motherless, cared for by the facility staff. He forms a quiet attachment to the male puppy in particular. He learns eventually that the female puppy — Siren — is scheduled to ship to Fortuna Station alone as breeding stock. The male will be sold separately on Mars.

Two orphaned puppies from a dead mother, being separated.

Jace does not let that stand. He pays for the male puppy himself. It costs real money he does not have. This is how Cerberus comes to be on the *Falcon*.

## Function in the Story

- **Foreshadowing.** Plants Jace as a dog person before the Cerberus purchase. The reader sees the bond forming over weeks; the negotiation moment then has weight rather than coincidence.
- **The mother's death as emotional driver.** This is not just sentiment about a puppy — it is Jace responding to orphaned siblings being separated after losing their mother. This is the kind of thing Jace cannot walk away from.
- **Character revelation.** Jace spends money he genuinely doesn't have on a dog. Some will think he wasted it. The reader will understand before Jace does how important Cerberus becomes.
- **Reveals depression.** Jace has nowhere else to go and no one who is happy to see him. The dogs are happy to see him. He keeps coming back.
- **Cost-of-Mars contrast.** He can't afford coffee, can't afford restaurants, can't afford much of anything. The dog breeder is free. His emotional life relocates to the place that doesn't charge admission.
- **Cerberus and Siren origin.** The mother's litter includes both puppies. By the time the Origin negotiation happens, Jace has weeks of relationship with both and understands what their situation is.

## Setting

A Mars dog breeder facility within the U.S. settlement's agricultural / animal-husbandry division. Pressurized dome with kennel runs, exercise areas, a small surgery/medical bay, climate control tuned for canine comfort, and socialization areas where staff and the occasional curious public can interact with the dogs.

The facility runs the Mars dog breeding program: roughly thirty years of selection, herding-breed × pug crosses, low-G-adapted physiology, calm temperament, low-shed coats, bred not to bark indiscriminately.

## The Mother's Death

The working-breed mother is pregnant when Jace first visits. The litter arrives over the weeks of Jace's visits. The mother does not survive.

**Timing options (author choice):**
- Jace is present when it happens — he is in the facility and the death occurs during or immediately after the birth. He witnesses it directly or is told by the handler within minutes.
- Jace arrives for a visit and the handler tells him what happened while he was away. The puppies are already being hand-raised by staff.

Either way: Jace knows. He was present for enough of the mother's life at the facility to feel this. He sits with the litter and the empty space where she was. He keeps coming back.

## Recurring Visit Structure

| Visit | Anchor | Beat |
|---|---|---|
| Visit 1 | ~week 2 post-discharge | Discovery of the facility; first encounter with the mother dog (visibly pregnant); staff handler's friendly tolerance; Jace returns |
| Visit 2 | ~week 3 | The litter arrives — and the mother dies. Either witnessed or learned immediately after. Jace stays. |
| Visit 3 | ~week 4–6 | Motherless litter being hand-raised; Jace begins to favor the male puppy |
| Visit 4 | ~week 6–8 | Jace learns from the handler: the female (Siren) is scheduled for the next Origin shipment to Fortuna as breeding stock; the male will be sold on Mars; the price is beyond Jace's stated reach — but not beyond what he could stretch to |

## Beats (Composite — for any single visit)

### Beat 1: Arrival
Jace shows up. The handler greets him with familiarity. By Visit 3, he is *the regular*. By Visit 5, the staff have stopped charging him.

### Beat 2: Time with the Puppies
Jace sits or kneels in the socialization area. The motherless puppies come to him — climbing on him, biting his sleeves, falling asleep on his chest. The male puppy has the most expressive face of the litter. Wrinkly. Big nostrils for his size. Already calm in a way the others are not. Jace doesn't realize he is being chosen. The reader does.

### Beat 3: The Conversation
Some visits include a brief conversation with the handler. The handler delivers the hard information:

> Handler: "The girl goes to Fortuna. Next Origin shipment. Breeding stock. She'll go alone — they don't have a handler slot on that run."
> Jace: [pause] "And the boy?"
> Handler: "Buy list. He'll sell."
> Jace: "How much?"
> Handler: [quotes a number that is real, significant, but not impossible for someone truly determined]
> Jace: [quiet for a moment] "...okay."

He doesn't argue. He doesn't ask for exceptions. He nods and stays another hour with the puppies.

### Beat 4: The Walk Home
Jace walks back through the dome corridors. Somewhere in these walks — across one visit or another — the name **Cerberus** lands in his head. He laughs once, quietly, alone in the corridor. He doesn't tell anyone. He's already decided, even if he hasn't said it to himself yet.

## Character States

### Jace (entry → exit, across the visit cluster)
- **Entry to first visit:** Depressed, broke, drifting. Looking for somewhere to be.
- **After the mother dies:** Quieter, more determined. This is the thing that locks it.
- **Exit from final visit:** Broke, committed, carrying a plan he hasn't spoken aloud. He is going to that Origin meeting to get two things: the contract and the dog.

### The Handler (consistent)
Kind. Patient. Has seen Earth-born settlers crash before. Does him small kindnesses without making a thing of it. May understand what Jace is deciding before Jace does.

## What This Scene Plants

- **Jace as a dog person, in advance of the purchase.** The Origin negotiation is the natural endpoint of weeks of attachment.
- **The mother's death as emotional driver.** Jace isn't buying a puppy on a whim. He's preventing two orphaned siblings from being permanently separated.
- **The pricing reality.** The cost is real. This establishes the "most expensive dog in human history" beat as something earned, not absurd.
- **Siren and Cerberus as a sibling pair** from the same tragic litter. Their later reunion at Fortuna carries this history.
