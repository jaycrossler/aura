# Scene — Jace's Dog Breeder Visits

## Summary

After Jace is discharged and the cheap-food economy of his hard months sets in, he discovers a Mars dog breeder facility somewhere in the U.S. settlement and begins visiting it regularly. Free entry, no transaction required, and the dogs do not care that he is broke. He spends as much time as he can there over the weeks of his hard months — sometimes daily, sometimes when other gig work permits — and forms a real attachment to the dogs and to one female dog in particular: a working-breed mother who is already pregnant when he first visits, and whose litter he watches arrive and grow.

This is the scene (or scene cluster) that **foreshadows Jace's eventual lifelong bond with dogs** and seeds his attachment to the specific puppy who will become Cerberus. It is also the place where, eventually, he learns that the mother dog and one female puppy are scheduled to be shipped to Fortuna Station as breeding stock — and that the boy puppy of the litter, the one Jace has been quietly favoring, is being held back to be sold separately on Mars. That news lands harder than Jace expects, and it sets up the Origin negotiation moment when he asks if he can take the puppy with him.

## Function in the Story

- **Foreshadowing.** Plants Jace as a dog person before the Cerberus negotiation. The reader sees the bond forming over weeks; the negotiation moment then has emotional weight rather than feeling like a coincidence.
- **Reveals depression.** Jace has nowhere else to go and no one else who is happy to see him. The dogs are happy to see him. He keeps coming back. The reader feels the loneliness without Jace having to articulate it.
- **Cost-of-Mars contrast.** Jace can't afford coffee, can't afford restaurants, can't afford much of anything. The dog breeder is free. His emotional life relocates to the place that doesn't charge admission.
- **Cerberus origin.** The mother dog Jace bonds with is Cerberus's mother. The litter he watches arrive includes Cerberus. The "boy puppy not being shipped" is Cerberus. By the time the Origin negotiation happens, Jace has already named the puppy in his head and has weeks of relationship with the family.
- **The Mars dog breeding program.** Visible to the reader through Jace's eyes — the scale, the seriousness, the science behind the breeding line. Reinforces the worldbuilding established in [Cerberus](../characters/char_cerberus.md).

## Setting

A Mars dog breeder facility within the U.S. settlement's agricultural / animal-husbandry division. Pressurized dome (or sub-section of a larger agricultural dome) with kennel runs, exercise areas, a small surgery/medical bay, climate control tuned for canine comfort, and viewing/socialization areas where staff and the occasional curious public can interact with the dogs.

The facility runs the Mars dog breeding program canonically established in Cerberus's character file: roughly thirty years of selection, herding-breed × pug crosses, low-G-adapted physiology, calm temperament, low-shed coats, bred not to bark indiscriminately. Jace doesn't know the science when he first arrives — he just knows the dogs are unusual-looking, calm, and wonderful.

Public visiting is allowed (and encouraged, since socialization with humans improves the dogs) but is not free in the strictest sense — there's a small token fee or a "volunteer hours" arrangement Jace works out with the staff. **[STORYBOT]** Decide which: token fee (a few credits, which Jace can manage) or volunteer hours (which becomes one of his gig-work threads). The volunteer-hours route makes for a richer beat — Jace cleaning kennels in exchange for time with the dogs, eventually picking up actual paid cleaning shifts as well.

## Recurring Visit Structure

This is a **recurring scene** across the hard months. Like the bar visits at The Long Burn, individual visits can be quick or extended depending on chapter pacing, and each one can plant a different beat.

| Visit | Anchor | Beat |
|---|---|---|
| Visit 1 | ~week 2 post-discharge | Discovery of the facility; first encounter with the mother dog (visibly pregnant); the staff handler's friendly tolerance; Jace returns the next day |
| Visit 2 | ~week 3 | The mother gives birth (off-page or witnessed — author choice); Jace sees the litter for the first time; mixed emotion |
| Visit 3 | ~week 4–6 | Regular visits; the puppies grow; Jace begins to favor the boy of the litter without fully realizing it |
| Visit 4 | ~week 6–8 | Jace learns from the handler that the mother and one female puppy are scheduled for the next Origin shipment to Fortuna; the boy puppy will be sold on Mars; the price ([STORYBOT]) is high enough that Jace cannot dream of buying him |
| Visit 5 | ~week 8–10 | Jace's relationship to the puppy crystallizes. He starts calling him *the boy* in his head. He doesn't tell the handler. |
| Visit 6 (final pre-Origin) | Just before the Origin negotiation | Quiet visit. Jace knows the puppy is leaving him soon. The handler senses something; says little. Jace says nothing. He goes from this visit straight to the Origin meeting. |

The naming of the puppy as **Cerberus** happens on the walk back from one of the later visits — possibly Visit 5 or 6 — half a joke, half an instinct Jace doesn't examine.

## Beats (Composite — for any single visit)

### Beat 1: Arrival
Jace shows up. The handler — a Mars-born or long-term-resident staffer, calm, used to the dogs — greets him with familiarity. By Visit 3, this is *the regular*. By Visit 5, the staff have stopped charging him.

### Beat 2: Time with the Dogs
Jace sits or kneels in the socialization area. The dogs come to him. The mother specifically — dignified, expressive face, big nostrils, short coat, almost no barking — leans her shoulder against his shin in greeting (the breed-line trait). Jace puts his hand on her head. She sits. He stays.

### Beat 3: The Boy Puppy
By Visit 3 onward: the puppies are mobile, climbing on Jace, biting his sleeves, falling asleep on his chest. The boy puppy — the one Jace is increasingly favoring — has the most expressive face of the litter. Wrinkly. Big nostrils for his size. Already calm in a way the others are not. Jace doesn't realize he is being chosen. The reader does.

### Beat 4: The Conversation
Some visits include a brief conversation with the handler. The handler, sensing Jace's attachment, eventually delivers the hard information:

> Handler: "She's going to Fortuna. Next Origin shipment. Her and the girl puppy. Breeding stock. The boy here goes on the buy list."
> Jace: [pause] "How much?"
> Handler: [quotes a number that is far beyond Jace's reach]
> Jace: "...okay."

He doesn't argue. He doesn't ask if there's any way. He nods and stays another hour with the dogs.

### Beat 5: The Walk Home
Jace walks back through the dome corridors. He doesn't have anywhere to be. He thinks about the puppy. He thinks about his sister Artemis at Odysseus, unreachable. He thinks about Jelly back on the farm in Virginia, half a planet and 200 million kilometers away. Somewhere in this walk — across one visit or another — the name **Cerberus** lands in his head. He laughs once, quietly, alone in the corridor. He doesn't tell anyone.

## Character States

### Jace (entry → exit, across the visit cluster)
- **Entry to first visit:** Depressed, broke, drifting. Looking for somewhere to be that doesn't cost money.
- **Exit from final visit:** Still depressed, still broke, but now with a specific attachment that will shape his Origin negotiation moments later. He has named the puppy. He believes he is going to lose him.

### The Handler (consistent)
- Kind. Patient. Has seen Earth-born settlers crash before. Doesn't ask Jace's story; doesn't need to. Does him small kindnesses (waiving the entry fee, letting him stay past closing) without making a thing of it.

## What This Scene Plants

- **Jace as a dog person, in advance of Cerberus.** The Origin negotiation moment is no longer a sudden impulse — it is the natural endpoint of weeks of attachment.
- **The Mars dog breeding program** as visible, real, populated by real animals.
- **Cerberus's mother and sister** as named (or at least faced) characters in the reader's mind before they ship to Fortuna.
- **The pricing reality** — even modest Mars dogs cost more than Jace can dream of post-discharge. The 200,000-credit "most expensive dog" gag at the Origin negotiation lands harder when the reader has seen Jace pricing the puppy directly.
- **The handler** as a minor recurring figure who could appear again later (Mars callback, or the figure who tells Jace where Cerberus's family ended up).
