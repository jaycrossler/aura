# Scene — The Bar (Rumors of Disaster)

## Purpose

Sketch the texture of Jace's hard months after the discharge. Plant the rumor field around belt anomalies — sabotage, piracy, disease, supply chain disruption — without resolving any of it. Show Jace beginning to believe something is genuinely wrong, not just inconvenient. Introduce the seedier register of Mars society he has not been exposed to before, in particular the ex-military and dock-worker types who will form the social ecosystem the Smuggler operates within.

This is less a single scene than a recurring location across several short scenes during the hard months. Could be one establishing scene plus one or two return visits as the rumors evolve.

## Position in Sequence

Begins a few days after the discharge. Jace has cycled through anger, denial, and is now trying to be useful. He has taken several menial gigs, is deep in a debt spiral, and is starting to grasp that going home is not a near-term option even financially. He needs cheap food and somewhere to be that isn't his rapidly-emptying base quarters.

## Setting

A working-class bar in the U.S. Mars settlement, near the launch facility and adjacent to the dock and warehouse districts. Not glamorous. Real food at reasonable prices. The kind of place where dock workers, contractor mechanics, off-duty enlisted, and retired military find each other. Decor: salvaged ship parts, an ancient airframe panel mounted as a backsplash, a real wooden bar imported at absurd cost decades ago, screens in every corner running news, sports, and the occasional contraband Earth broadcast. Background hum of ventilation. Smell of fryer oil, beer, and the specific dryness of Mars indoor air.

This bar will recur. Worth naming and giving a memorable feel. Suggested working name: **The Long Burn** (a reference to the nuclear ferries' departure burns; bartender's joke). Final name TBD.

## Characters Present

- Jace Grant (POV — out of uniform, beginning to look the part of a discharged man)
- Bartender (TBD — recurring background figure; the kind of person who has heard everything)
- A loose cast of dock workers, contractors, off-duty military, ex-military
- Possibly: an early sighting of the Smuggler / "the Rat" without yet being introduced (he is a fixture here too)
- Possibly: a manufacturer or merchant venting about supply chains — the kind of person whose business depends on belt shipments

## Emotional Beats

1. **Walking in.** Jace, in civilian clothes that fit slightly wrong because his frame is too military for them, takes a corner table. Orders the cheapest hot food on the menu. He has been on Mars two months and is in this kind of bar for the first time. Everyone notices him; nobody makes a thing of it.

2. **The screen above the bar.** A news ticker scrolls. *Belt shipment delays continue. Origin Industries reports unspecified operational issues at Odysseus Station. Commodity prices for platinum-group metals up 3.2% on the Mars exchange.* Jace half-watches it. Catches "Odysseus." Sits up.

3. **The overheard conversation.** A small group at the bar — looks like contractors, possibly ex-military by posture — is talking loudly enough to be heard. Topic: the Odysseus delay. Someone says they heard there were deaths. Someone else says they heard it's worse than that, the whole station's gone quiet. Someone else says it's sabotage, has to be — *the timing's too convenient, the Chinese are at Psyche, it's a metals war, it's been coming for years*. Someone else says no, it's a disease — workers going crazy, hallucinating, a whole shift quarantined. Someone laughs and says it's pirates. Everyone laughs. Then nobody does, because piracy in the belt isn't actually impossible, just ridiculous.

4. **A merchant venting.** Nearby, a different conversation. A woman in a contractor uniform — not military, not corporate, the kind of person who runs a fabrication shop — is venting to a friend that her quotas are about to be impossible. She had components scheduled to ship from Odysseus three months ago. They're not coming. Her customers are starting to switch suppliers. She is going to have to lay people off.

5. **Jace's quiet realization.** This is the first time he hears, from real people who actually depend on this stuff, that the belt situation is not a routine logistics hiccup. People's livelihoods are unraveling. The merchant is genuinely scared. The contractors at the bar are genuinely confused.

6. **The Artemis ping.** He thinks of his sister at Odysseus. He has not had a successful real-time conversation with her in three weeks. He has been telling himself it's latency. He is starting to not believe himself.

7. **A small interaction.** The bartender notices him noticing the conversations, comes over with a refill he didn't order. *"On the house. You look like you've had a week."* Jace nods. Doesn't explain. The bartender doesn't ask.

8. **A glimpse of the Rat (optional, foreshadow).** A small, ratty-looking man at the end of the bar has been quietly working the room — brief conversations with three different people, money or chits changing hands so smoothly that no one would notice if they weren't looking. Jace, in his current state, isn't looking. The reader can see him.

9. **Jace leaves.** The food was decent. The beer was cheap. He walks back to his quarters in the cold corridor light. The supernova is bright through the dome glass. He thinks about Artemis. He doesn't know what to do.

## Atmospheric Details to Hit

- **Mixed-class space.** Military in civilian clothes. Dock workers in gear they didn't bother to change out of. Contractors in branded jackets. Mars-born youngsters who treat this like neutral territory. The cross-cut population of working Mars.
- **Information density.** Multiple screens, multiple conversations, the constant low-grade dump of news, sports, advertising, social-media-feed video. The future doesn't lack information; it has too much.
- **Background censorship invisible-but-real.** A news segment about Odysseus cuts to a sponsor break suspiciously early. Jace doesn't notice. The reader, by the second time it happens, might.
- **Physical wear.** Jace has lost some weight. His exercise has dropped from 2-3 hours to whatever he can manage between gigs. He is not physically broken, but he is *thinner*. His clothes hang slightly. He registers this in a mirror behind the bar without quite admitting it.
- **The Rat in the background.** Foreshadowing. He should be a *visible-but-easy-to-miss* figure across two or three of these bar visits before Jace formally meets him at the launch facility.

## What This Scene Plants

- **The rumor field.** Sabotage, piracy, disease, Chinese conflict, deaths — the texture of belt anomalies as filtered through Mars working-class gossip. None confirmed; all plausible; all wrong in different ways.
- **Censorship begins to be felt.** The news cuts away. The merchant's shipments aren't coming. The pieces don't add up the way they should.
- **Origin's role.** The merchant's supply chain failures point at Origin Industries as the chokepoint. Foreshadows Jace's eventual contract.
- **The Smuggler / Rat.** Visible in his element, not yet introduced, but the reader can begin tracking him.
- **Jace's slow shift.** From confident officer to attentive observer of the system's underside. His naïveté is being chipped away, but slowly. He still believes the institutions mostly work.

## Recurring Visits

This scene is good across multiple visits. As Jace's hard months stretch, the bar becomes his low-cost dinner spot. Each visit can plant a different beat:

- **Visit 1:** Establishing scene. The rumors are still rumors.
- **Visit 2:** A piece of news lands harder — Odysseus comms officially classified as "intermittent." The merchant from before isn't there; her shop has closed.
- **Visit 3:** An ex-military man tells Jace — recognizing him as discharged — that the belt is "worse than they're saying" and that he should be glad he isn't going. Jace, by this point, is being recruited for exactly that.
- **Visit 4 (Smuggler proper):** Jace formally meets the Rat at the bar after the package run, and the working relationship begins.
