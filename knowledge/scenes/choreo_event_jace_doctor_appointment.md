## Beat-by-Beat Choreography

### Beat 1: Jace Enters the Office (12:00)

**Setting:** Doctor's office

**Action:**
1. Jace knocks at 12:00 sharp
2. Voice: "Come in."
3. Jace enters, posture sharp — dress uniform, confident
4. The doctor stands, extends hand: "Lieutenant Grant. I'm Dr. [Name]. Please, have a seat."
5. They shake hands — firm, professional
6. Jace sits in the visitor chair
7. The doctor sits at his desk, briefly looks at his tablet
8. **Casual small talk attempt by Jace:**
   - Jace: "How long have you been on Mars, Doctor?"
   - Doctor: [glances up, expression unreadable, brief moment of internal decision]
9. **The doctor decides to skip the small talk:**
   - Doctor: "Son, I've got some bad news."

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Composed, sharp
- Emotional: Confident, mildly curious, expecting routine
- Body language: Officer at attention-rest

**Doctor's state:**
- Physical: Steady, controlled
- Emotional: Reluctant — this is the part of his job he hates
- Body language: Slightly leaning forward, hands clasped, eye contact

### Beat 2: The Activation (12:00–12:02)

**Setting:** Doctor's office, the room transforming

**Action:**
1. The doctor makes a hand gesture — fingers spread, drawing in the air
2. **Information explodes outward:**
   - Wall screens activate, displaying medical scans
   - Jace's HUD floods with overlays — diagrams, statistics, medical journal references
   - The room itself becomes a 3D data environment
3. Floating in the air: 3D model of Jace's skeleton, with regions highlighted in red — femur, tibia, vertebrae
4. **Doctor speaks while the visuals support him:**
   - Doctor: "I'd like to walk you through what we found this morning."
   - Doctor: "We have a brand new scanning machine, the latest generation. It detects things older scanners couldn't."
   - Doctor: "We've identified a bone density disorder in your scans. It's a recently classified condition."
   - Doctor: "Jace, you have what we're calling [medical term — **[STORYBOT]** invent a plausible-sounding name]. It's a degenerative bone disease."
5. **Jace's reaction in real-time:**
   - First: confusion (this can't be about him)
   - Then: dawning recognition (the scan delay yesterday — he remembers)
   - Then: shock (frozen, not breathing)

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Frozen in place
- Emotional: Confused → recognition → shock — within 30 seconds
- Body language: Hands gripping chair arms, jaw slack, eyes wide

**[STORYBOT]** The exact medical name of the disease — invent something that sounds plausible. Examples: "Osteopenic Cavitation Syndrome," "Progressive Bone Density Disorder," "Tannenbaum-Reyes Syndrome" (named after fictional discoverers). Should sound real-medical.

### Beat 3: The Information Overwhelm (12:02–12:15)

**Setting:** Doctor's office, full information environment

**Action:**
1. The doctor walks Jace through the diagnosis
2. **Key information delivered:**
   - The disease is genetic (likely inherited)
   - It's progressive — bones become brittle over time
   - Currently, medicine can *halt* the progression but cannot *cure* it
   - Treatment requires a low-gravity environment for the rest of his life
   - High-gravity environments (Earth) would accelerate the disease catastrophically
   - Returning to Earth permanently is no longer medically advised
3. **Visualizations support each point:**
   - Animation showing bone structure under high vs. low gravity
   - Statistical projections of disease progression
   - Comparison: untreated vs. treated outcomes
4. **The implication lands:**
   - Doctor: "Jace, this disease unfortunately means we can't keep you in active service."
   - Doctor: "The military requires personnel deployable to high-gravity environments. With your condition, that's not possible."
   - Doctor: "I'm so sorry. We'll have to process you out."
5. **Jace's processing:**
   - First wave: numb shock, can't speak
   - Second wave: questions begin forming
6. The doctor pauses, gives Jace space

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Frozen → starting to thaw, body trembling slightly
- Emotional: Shock → growing horror as implications cascade
- Body language: Clenched fists, jaw working, swallowing repeatedly

**[STORYBOT]** The visualization details are key. The story should *show* the transition from confidence to devastation through Jace's body language as the information mounts.

### Beat 4: Jace's Questions (12:15–12:35)

**Setting:** Same — information environment continuing

**Action:**
1. Jace finds his voice — small, controlled
2. **Jace: "How long?"** (trying to ask about life expectancy)
3. The doctor and AI together: "On Mars, with treatment, you can live a normal full life. Many decades."
4. **Jace: "And on Earth?"**
5. AI overlay: "Without treatment in Earth gravity, progression is severe within 5-10 years. With treatment, manageable but lifestyle-restrictive."
6. **Jace: "Is there any way..."** (his voice cracks, he stops)
7. The doctor: "We can stabilize you here. But active military service requires deployability we can't certify."
8. **Jace tries to negotiate:**
   - Jace: "Can I stay on Mars? Just Mars duty?"
   - Doctor: "I asked the same question. Mars duty rotates. Soldiers cycle home. It's the policy. Even Mars-stationed personnel must be Earth-capable."
   - Jace: "What if I sign a waiver?"
   - Doctor: "There's no waiver for this."
9. **Jace: "What do I do?"** (small voice, lost)
10. The doctor's tone softens
11. Doctor: "You have options. You can stay on Mars as a civilian. Many former military do. There's work here — base contracts, civilian jobs. The colony needs people."
12. AI overlay: Information about transition resources, civilian opportunities, support services
13. **Jace processes:** can't go home, can't be a soldier, has to start over from nothing

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Trembling, stomach in knots, eyes burning (holding back tears)
- Emotional: Shock → sadness → flickers of anger → bargaining → despair
- Body language: Hunched, hands gripping each other, occasionally looking down to hide his face

**[STORYBOT]** Jace's questions can be expanded. He may ask about his sisters (genetic implications), his family (what to tell them), specific financial questions. The conversation should feel like a man in shock trying to find footing.

### Beat 5: The Cut Away (12:35–12:55)

**Setting:** Same office, but pacing decision

**Action:**
1. Show the conversation in fragments rather than fully
2. The doctor explains practical matters: paperwork, discharge timeline, civilian transition
3. Jace's responses become shorter, more mechanical
4. The information overwhelms — he stops registering details
5. **Final moment in the office:**
   - Doctor: "I'll have my office reach out with the full transition packet. You don't need to decide anything today."
   - Doctor: "I'm so sorry, Jace. Take some time. Talk to people who care about you."
6. Jace stands, mechanically
7. They shake hands again — Jace's grip is weaker now
8. Jace walks to the door
9. Pauses, looks back
10. Doctor: "The military will help you transition. You're not alone in this."
11. Jace nods, exits

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Numb, body moving on autopilot
- Emotional: Shut down, processing
- Body language: Diminished — uniform suddenly too big, posture collapsed

**[STORYBOT]** This beat should feel like a montage — fragmented, time-distorted, showing Jace receding from the world. The exact dialogue can be sparse.

### Beat 6: The Hallway — Rich and the Colonel (~12:55–13:00)

**Setting:** Military medical wing hallway

**Action:**
1. Jace exits the office
2. The doctor's office door closes behind him
3. **Hallway scene:**
   - Rich and the Colonel are standing nearby, in dress uniforms
   - They were waiting to formally welcome Jace
   - Both are in conversation, smiling — *they don't know yet*
4. Rich notices Jace, smiles, waves him over
5. Rich's smile *fades* as he registers Jace's expression
6. **Rich's reaction:**
   - Rich: "Jace? You okay?"
   - Jace: [tries to speak, can't]
   - Rich: [understanding dawning, looks at the Colonel]
7. **The Colonel realizes something is wrong:**
   - Colonel: "Lieutenant?"
   - Rich: "Sir, may I be excused? I think I should — "
   - Colonel: [sees Jace's face, nods immediately] "Of course. Take care of him. We'll handle the welcome later."
   - Rich: "Thank you, sir. I'll brief you afterwards."
   - Colonel: [nods, gives Jace a respectful glance] "Lieutenant, whatever it is — we're here. Take whatever time you need."
8. The Colonel withdraws, giving them privacy

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Frozen at the door of the doctor's office
- Emotional: Cracking — Rich's kindness is breaking through
- Body language: Shoulders shaking, eyes filling

**Rich's state:**
- Physical: Sharpening into protective mode
- Emotional: Worried, attentive
- Body language: Stepping toward Jace, hand on his shoulder

**Colonel's state:**
- Physical: Calm authority
- Emotional: Sympathetic without intruding
- Body language: Respectful step back, brief acknowledging look

**[STORYBOT]** The Colonel's specific words can be embellished. He should feel like a senior officer who has seen this before — not cold, but professional. He doesn't ask questions; he gives space.

### Beat 7: Private Room — Off-Page (13:05–13:30)

**Setting:** Small private room (recommend: junior officer's lounge or empty briefing room)

**Action:**
- This beat is **off-page** — implied but not depicted in detail
- Rich takes Jace to a private room
- Jace tells him what happened
- Rich listens
- Jace possibly cries here, where it's private
- Rich offers genuine support — friend, peer, no agenda
- Brief — a compressed scene rendered in the prose with minimal dialogue or fully cut

**[STORYBOT]** The author indicated the reader's imagination can fill in this beat. Recommendation: a single paragraph or short scene fragment. Rich's presence, his lack of false comfort, his "I've got you" energy.

### Beat 8: The Drive Home (~13:30 onward)

**Setting:** Rover, returning to residential dome

**Action:**
1. Rich drives — Jace doesn't have it in him
2. Mostly silent
3. **Rich speaks once:**
   - Rich: "I'll come check on you tomorrow. You don't have to be anywhere. Take the day."
   - Jace: [nods, can't speak]
4. They reach the residential dome
5. Rich walks Jace to his apartment door
6. Rich: "Anything you need, you call me. Day or night."
7. Jace: "Thanks, Rich."
8. Rich pauses, then hugs Jace briefly — peer hug, awkward but genuine
9. Jace enters his apartment
10. Door closes
11. **Final image:** Jace stands in the small apartment that is suddenly no longer his — military housing he must vacate within 30 days. He looks at the bed where he slept the deep sleep of the rested 90 minutes ago, when his life still made sense.

**Jace's state:**
- Physical: Empty, hollow
- Emotional: Devastated, alone
- Body language: Slack, drained

**Rich's state:**
- Physical: Steady
- Emotional: Mourning for his friend
- Body language: Protective

**Scene ends:** Cut. Next scene picks up days/weeks later.
