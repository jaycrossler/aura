# Chapter 1 — Morning Round (Draft)

Most men dream of being the only guy on a spaceship with fifty-eight women.

It was slowly driving Jace Grant insane.

He peeled off his sleep mask first — the rebreather seal releasing with a soft hiss, recycled air giving way to the *Falcon*'s ambient atmosphere, which smelled like animals and someone else's yesterday. Standard. He set the mask on its cradle, watched the scrubber indicator blink from amber to green as it recharged, and noted that it had cycled his CO2 four times overnight. Good mask. Better than the alternative, which was waking up with a splitting headache in a pool of his own exhaled carbon dioxide, which had happened exactly once on his first night aboard and not since.

He reached down and petted the warm belly sprawled across his legs. Cerberus twitched without waking.

The ship breathed around him — the deep, constant surge of the fluid system cycling through the hull, liquid mass shifting through three dozen tank zones as the spin demanded balance. Thunk of a pressure valve. Groan of a pipe under load somewhere aft. He'd stopped noticing it around week three. Now it was just the ship's groaning voice, low and continuous, the sound of the better part of a thousand tons of water doing its job.

HUD came up: 0614, atmo nominal, bathroom clear, Maureen's cabin AWAKE / DO NOT DISTURB. He tagged Thing One and Thing Two in his peripheral and pulled their overnight logs. Ring segment seven, hull panel R7-04: patched, sealed, polymer cure nominal. Both drones back in their cradles at 0340.

There was also a voice note from Saul. Twelve minutes old — the lag was improving as they closed on the belt, but it still arrived like a letter rather than a conversation.

*Good cure on R7-04. You torqued the corner anchor a little hot but it'll hold. Watch the adjacent section — the polymer gets brittle when the temp cycles hard. Leave it another day before you load it.*

Jace listened to it twice. Then he tagged the adjacent panel for inspection and filed the note without responding. Saul's replies would arrive after the moment had passed anyway. That was fine. He'd started leaving his own notes on completed jobs — narrating the work slightly more than strictly necessary. He would deny this if asked.

Maureen still hadn't linked his drones to the *Falcon*'s majordomo. Something about certification. In practice: her ship, her rules, his problem. Every job was a manual tag.

"Up, boy. Time to potty."

Cerberus executed a flip — comatose cockroach to alert predator — low gravity making it look slightly too easy. He stared at Jace with the focused intensity of a dog who had been awake for some time and was simply waiting for permission to exist.

They squeezed out of what the manifest called a stateroom and found the bathroom empty. Jace had rigged a dog waste station in the corner. He felt genuinely proud that both dogs used it. Mostly used it. There was a substantial turd next to the grate with the confident placement of something that had aimed and simply declined.

"Looks like your sister."

Cerberus sniffed it, then shivered.

"Yeah."

He pulled up the fabrication queue in his HUD, tagged a 90mm circulation fan — toilet module — and assigned Thing Two to collect it once printed. Forty minutes to fab. He sent it anyway, unlinked from the majordomo, invisible to the ship's systems, living only in his head and Thing Two's task list.

---

They passed a cluster of maintenance drones clicking through the corridor and found Mei in the galley, feeding a scrap of egg to Siren from her plate. Cerberus barked and hurled himself at his sister. Siren stepped aside. His puppy hit the counter with a yelp and an expression of profound personal betrayal. The siblings began snarling and wrestling in their way — all theater, no damage.

"Morning. Everything good?"

Mei didn't look up. "The pigs are up to something."

Jace nodded. The pigs were always up to something. He'd gotten a message from Nick two nights ago — *Screwdriver figured out the feeder latch again, I watched her do it, what is she* — which meant the pig-cam had beaten him to his own morning report. Again.

"Take Siren on the run?"

He answered with a mouthful of coffee. Mei turned and walked aft. Eleven words. One more than yesterday.

He glanced down the corridor toward Lanchee's cabin. Door closed, the faint sound of a voice — hers, low and professional — carrying through. Already in consultation. He'd passed her exactly twice this week, once at the coffee machine at 0500 and once in the corridor when they'd both stepped aside politely and said nothing. She knew things about Fortuna Station's sick bay that he was fairly sure Fortuna's own staff didn't know yet.

He reached to the highest drawer and pulled out two treats. Both dogs locked on with the preternatural focus of animals who have decided this is the most important moment in recorded history.

They ran. Four loops through Main Street was a kilometer, floor curving ahead and behind - after a while you expected to see yourself running in front of you. Every few seconds, they passed a door on the right, then left - three dozen doorways into the attached pods, some open, some closed and airlocks engaged.  Each of the livestock ones he passed had their outter door open, with snouts and eyes peering out through windows that could glimpse into the corridor - howls and grunts and pleas for food following their passage. Siren ran with efficiency. Cerberus ran with faith, dodging through the periodic maintenance robots and boxes or crates cluttering the hallway.

He finished the loops, soaked. Dropped Siren in her bed. She was asleep before he set her down.

Cerberus padded after him as he changed into his ranching overalls, panting and grinning a goofy puppy grin.

## Animal Rounds

He pulled up the livestock queue. His HUD painted the ring in stacked transparent layers — airflow, waste loops, feed levels, humidity, the soft amber glow of animals requiring human attention. Which was all of them. Always.

The sheep went first. Bessie detached from the flock and bleated at him accusatorially.

"I know. Twelve hours without food. Tragic."

He entered slow, feed cart ahead, tether clipped. The pellets dispensed. The flock became believers. One lamb startled at the touch of another's ear, leapt sideways, floated briefly, and registered the physics as a personal insult.

Cerberus pressed against the outer barrier, vibrating.

"No."

He sat. Vibrated differently.

Jace scanned tags, checked feet, replaced a mineral block, logged a minor stool concern on Sheep Nine. "We need to talk about your contribution to the data environment," he told her. She chewed at him with horizontal pupils and the calm menace of an animal that knew he cleaned her floor.

---

The goose module announced itself before the HUD did.

*HONK.*

Cerberus stopped.

*HONK.*

Eight long necks. Eight orange bills. Jurisdiction opened her wings at his chest.

"Crewman Grant, scheduled water service. All paperwork in order."

She honked again.

"Denied. Understood."

He slid the water tray through the side port before entering. The geese descended on it like a committee discovering budget surplus. He serviced the overflow channel, checked Jurisdiction's left foot. She was busy trying to bite the scanner beam.

"That's light."

She bit it again.

Cerberus lay outside with his chin on his paws, watching with the expression of someone learning from an enemy.

"Don't take notes," Jace told him.

---

The duck module was worse. Always worse. Forty-three straight days of excessive floor moisture, surviving four splash guard redesigns and one memo he'd titled *Waterfowl Surface Interaction Mitigation Plan* that he deeply regretted naming that way.

Duck Four stood at the viewport, pecking condensation off the panel. It looked cosmic. She wanted the droplets.

He cleared a pellet jam. One duck flapped — brief and terrible aviation, rose half a meter, rotated without a plan, landed in the head-wash pan. Splash. Cheek.

His HUD said: *FACE CONTAMINATION DETECTED.*

"I was going to age into it naturally."

---

The pigs had been up to something. Specifically: 312 low-force contact events on the waste access panel over four hours and twenty-two minutes. Screwdriver stood with a gasket strip hanging from her mouth and the innocent expression of a suspect with representation.

He pointed through the glass. "Spit out the spacecraft."

She chewed thoughtfully.

"I went to engineering school."

He entered, patched the cover, flagged it for replacement, dispensed enrichment blocks. The gilts attacked them with delighted violence. Somewhere on Fortuna, he knew, people were watching this.

---

The calves took longest because affection and obstruction were the same moral category. Mabel fogged the viewport as he approached.

"Morning, Auditor."

She licked the panel.

Inside, she placed her head against his chest and leaned. At 0.3g she weighed less. Her mass remained committed to Newton. He slid back half a meter.

"We've discussed momentum."

She licked his sleeve from wrist to elbow.

"Thank you. I was saving that salt."

Dot performed a happy kick, floated, landed on all four feet in the wrong order, and looked astonished by the accusation of physics.

Cerberus's ears went flat, then forward.

"Majestic and dumb," Jace said. "Respect both."

---

He ended with the rabbits, because after pigs and calves a man deserved one module where nobody tried to eat the ship or lovingly crush his ribs. Ledger emerged, sniffed, vanished. Receipts stayed out and stared.

One doe thumped.

His HUD flagged a micro-fan speed adjustment in the ventilation duct. He looked at her. "Your objection is entered into the minutes."

Three minutes of peace.

Then: *GOAT MODULE 01: UNAPPROVED ELEVATION DETECTED.*

He closed his eyes.

Cerberus lifted his head.

"Don't."

He looked excited.

---

He stood in the livestock spine and looked through the goat module window at Juniper, who had returned to the supply shelf and was calmly eating the warning label.

He opened the voice log. "Livestock deck operational. Ducks wet. Sheep suspicious. Pigs technically innocent. Chickens in session. Geese hostile to due process. Rabbits correct. Calves heavy in the soul. Goats remain goats."

His HUD said: *SOME VALUES NOT SUPPORTED.*

"Then support better values."

He went to retrieve the goat.
