# Carlos

## Summary

Carlos is a drone operations specialist at Fortuna Station — one of Jace's future
coworkers, and the person who meets him on arrival day and walks him around the
station. He is one of the longer-tenured residents, old enough to have been here
through multiple settler waves and comfortable enough with the place to know which
details actually matter.

## Physical Appearance

Late fifties, possibly older. He wears a cardigan sweater and a tie. Every day.
On a station where everyone else is in utilitarian workwear, this is notable. It is
clearly a deliberate choice and he does not explain it. No one asks.

## Manner

Unhurried. Knowledgeable. Fond of Fortuna without being sentimental about it — he
has watched the station grow and has opinions about what it's becoming, but he doesn't
proselytize. His tour of the station on Jace's arrival day is good precisely because
he knows what to linger on and what to walk past.

He is the kind of person who has been somewhere long enough that his presence is part
of the furniture — not powerful, not flashy, but there in every scene that needs
someone reliable.

## Relationship to Jace

He meets Jace on arrival day as a future coworker with a mandate to show him around.
The relationship that develops is warm and practical — Carlos respects competence,
and Jace is competent; that's most of what they need.

## Relationship to Erin's Inn

Carlos has maintained a comfortable distance from the chaos of the second transit inn
for however long he's been on the station. He is fond of Erin's energy from that
distance. He tells Jace he probably doesn't want to stay there with the affection of
someone who has made the correct choice and is at peace with it.
