# Jinx — Grant Family Horse

## Summary

Jinx is the Grant family's primary horse — Chris Grant's riding mount and the horse Jace and his sisters learned to ride on as kids. He is older now (about fifteen years old at story start), still working but slowing down. The Grants own and operate a horse farm in Virginia; Jinx is one of several horses there but is the *family* horse — the one named, the one mentioned in Jace's messages home, the one that represents the farm as a place rather than as a business.

Jace asks about Jinx in his video messages from Mars. His parents' replies — that Jinx is fine, that he's still slow on cold mornings but cantering well in the afternoons — anchor Jace to the Virginia farm in a way that simple facts about his parents don't quite manage. The horse is a load-bearing detail.

## Why He Matters

Jinx, alongside Jelly the family dog, represents the continuity of home. They are two of the small certainties Jace clings to as his Mars situation deteriorates and as his ability to reach Artemis fails. *The farm is fine. Jinx is fine. Jelly is fine.* These small reports become important.

More crucially, Jinx is *the horse the farm exists for*. The Grant horse farm in Virginia is the family business — Chris and Dawn's livelihood, the equity in their lives. When Jace's parents later offer to **sell the farm** to pay off his million-credit debt (see `/knowledge/scenes/event_jace_horse_farm_offer.md`), Jinx is the embodied stake of that offer. Selling the farm means selling Jinx. Jace cannot bear that thought, and his refusal of the offer becomes the driving factor that pushes him to accept the Origin Industries contract and ship to the asteroid belt.

Jinx is the horse Jace cannot ask his parents to give up.

## Background

Chris bought Jinx as a four-year-old when Jace was about thirteen. Jinx became Jace's riding horse during high school summers — Jace was the first kid in the family responsible enough to be trusted on him. Athena learned to ride on Jinx too. Artemis was already off to the Academy by then.

Jinx is part of the working livestock at the Grant farm but has never been a "rental horse" or boarder — he is family. The farm operates as a working horse farm (boarding, training, breeding adjacent to the main house) but Jinx is kept off the commercial operations. He is the one the kids ride. He is the one Chris rides at sunset. He is the one Dawn talks to when she goes out to feed in the morning.

## How He Appears in the Story

Jinx never appears on-page in Book 1. He is mentioned in:
- Jace's video messages from the Mars gym (asking about him, reassurance from parents)
- Reference in `/knowledge/scenes/event_jace_horse_farm_offer.md` — the offer to sell the farm implicitly proposes selling Jinx; this is part of why Jace cannot accept
- Off-page references throughout the post-discharge arc as Jace thinks about home
