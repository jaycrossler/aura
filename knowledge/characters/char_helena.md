# Helena

## Summary

Helena is Fortuna Station's first native daughter — the first child born on
the station, now 25, who grew up knowing everyone on it. Her parents left
her their earnings when they died, and the station has since added to that
fortune through informal gifting, making her the wealthiest person on
Fortuna. She spends her days teaching a beloved daily low-g yoga class in
the park and wandering the station on self-appointed small jobs — checking
pressure seals, feeding animals, helping wherever help is needed. She
**disappears overnight in the final week of the *Falcon*'s transit**,
before Jace arrives.

Her disappearance lands very hard. She was beautiful, warm, and visibly woven into every part of station life; her yoga classes were one of the few communal pleasures on a working station. Her loss puts a damper on Fortuna right as leadership is trying to project confidence to an incoming settler-and-investor wave.

**The true cause (reveal-later, GM-only at story start):** Helena had latent magical sensitivity and crossed into the Astral in her sleep — an accidental full crossing (see [[spec_crossing_mechanics]]). She did not survive it. She is, so far as anyone yet knows, the first Fortuna disappearance that *cannot be quietly suppressed* — a beloved public figure rather than an anonymous miner.

## What the Investigation Finds (Physical-Space Facts)

This is the forensic signature of an accidental crossing, presented to the characters as an impossibility (see the "{Aura} goes dark" section of [[spec_crossing_mechanics]]):

- Her pod was **never opened** after she retired for the night. Hatch logs show her entering; nothing shows her leaving.
- **No hull breach, no EVA record, no movement-sensor track** leaving the pod.
- Her personal **{Aura} device connected to the network as normal, then stopped transmitting at ~01:00 local** — abruptly, mid-record, with no fault code. After that: nothing. No location, no biometrics, no reconnection.
- No video or data evidence of another person present. No struggle. No mess.
- Multiple casual partners exist; all are interviewed; all are cleared by the simple fact that **no one entered or left the pod.**

To investigators with no framework for crossing, this reads as a locked-room impossibility. It "demands explanation" and gets none.

### Died in the dark

Helena wore her CO2-scrubbing rebreather mask (which is normal safety gear for sleeping in the belt). This bought a few hours of air on the far side after she crossed into the Astral in her sleep. However, as an untrained, terrified first-crosser in the -20°C dark, she never managed the panic-return that saves experienced practitioners. 

Her room is locked, AI records shows she went in and no one else opened doors.  Jin was seen closely with her at the party, and was nearby when she dissapeared.  Her room has only her comms bracelet lying on her rumpled bed, and her breathing gear is missing.

The exact cause of her death post-crossing remains open: she either eventually suffocated once her mask's air expired or fell victim to an attack by Astral fauna.

Either way, the physical-space evidence is identical: empty pod, {Aura} flatlining at 01:00, no breach.

### The True Mechanism (Reveal-Later — Author/GM Only)

Helena is an untrained latent sensitive. During the transcendent peak of her
daily yoga class on the day of her death, she unknowingly triggers an
**accidental Self-Template store** — a raw, unintentional vault copy, made
under the duress and disorientation of an emerging first crossing, with no
training, awareness, or intent. See the addendum in
[[magic_will_and_templates]] for the mechanic.

That night, she undergoes the accidental full sleep-crossing described above.
Disoriented and inexperienced, she loses her bearings in the Astral and steps
outside the station's Astral-equivalent footprint. She dies of exposure. She
was never in physical space at the time of death — this is what preserves the
locked-room quality of the investigation (no breach, no EVA record, {Aura}
flatline at ~01:00 local).

Her accidental vault copy is what later enables her resurrection (see
[[event_helena_epilogue]] and [[sheet_helena_epilogue_resurrection]]). Upon
resurrection, per standard Template-vault rules, she retains nothing past the
moment of the store — her last clear memory is finishing yoga class and
heading toward the bar to meet friends for Sophia's birthday party. She wakes
believing she is suffering an extreme hangover and has no idea a resurrection
has occurred.

### Framing Role — Prologue and Epilogue

Helena bookends Book 1. The **Prologue** (see [[event_helena_prologue]])
follows her POV through an ordinary last day — yoga class, informal rounds
checking seals and feeding animals, anticipation for Sophia's 30th birthday
party that night, and her plan to finally flirt properly with Jin. It ends
with a plain statement that she is dead; no death scene is shown. The
**Epilogue** (see [[event_helena_epilogue]]) follows her POV waking after
resurrection, confused and cold, misreading her state as a hangover, up
through the moment someone screams upon recognizing her.

## Story Function

- **The red-herring mystery.** The human theory (jealous partner, foul play) is *wrong*; the true answer is metaphysical. The reader and Jace won't get the real explanation for a long time. Mystery survives explanation.
- **Stakes before understanding.** Helena establishes — viscerally, with a sympathetic victim — that the Astral kills people who touch it accidentally, long before anyone can name what the Astral is. It pre-loads dread under Jace's own later crossing arc.
- **Introduces Suzi.** Her case is how the station police chief enters the story and how she comes to trust Jace (see [[char_suzi]]).
- **Thematic resonance.** A *yoga instructor* — stillness as her practice — crosses over in stillness, asleep. This fits the canon principle that *magic comes to people in stillness, not in action.*
- **Couples to the Cryptid.** A frost residue at the scene (if introduced — see review-queue) could later let Lanchee or Sophia connect Helena's death to the *Falcon* incursion and the broader flash-freeze signature.

## Voice and Presence (Posthumous)

Helena appears mostly through other people's accounts: her students, her partners, Suzi's case file, Nick's network logs. She should feel like a real, specific person who is gone — not a plot device. A few concrete textures (her class playlist still queued, a half-finished tea, the mat still rolled out) do more than eulogy.
