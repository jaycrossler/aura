# Jelly — Grant Family Dog

## Summary

Jelly is the Grant family's dog at the horse farm in Virginia. She is older — roughly nine years old when Jace arrives on Mars — and has been part of the household since Jace's late teens. She doesn't appear on-page in Book 1; she exists as a small, persistent presence in Jace's family video messages and as a touchstone for what "home" means.

When Jace records messages home from the Mars gym on his second day, one of his recurring questions is *"How's Jelly?"* The answers — that she's fine, sleeping in her usual spot, still chasing the geese off the pasture even though she pretends to be old — are quietly important to him in ways he doesn't articulate.

## Why She Matters

Jelly is one of the small anchors to Earth that the story occasionally checks in on. As Jace's ability to reach his family degrades (failed Artemis sends, then the broader comms-filtering problem, then the eventual blackout), the small certainties — *Jelly is fine. Jinx is fine. The farm is fine.* — become increasingly load-bearing for Jace emotionally. They are the things he can still believe in even when much of the rest of his world is dissolving.

She also serves a quiet structural purpose: when Jace eventually adopts Cerberus, the reader is meant to feel that Jace is replacing something he lost (immediate access to Jelly) with something he chose (a Mars-bred puppy who will go with him to the belt). The two dogs are not equivalent, but they rhyme.

## Background

The Grants have always had a dog at the farm. Jelly is the current one. She came into the household when Athena was still in middle school — Jace was already at the Air Force Academy by then. Her name was Athena's choice. ([STORYBOT] reason for the name is open — consistent with a teenager naming a dog something cute.)

She is calm around horses, which is a non-negotiable trait at the Grant farm. She does not chase Jinx. She and Jinx have a working understanding that involves Jelly sleeping in the barn during summer thunderstorms.
