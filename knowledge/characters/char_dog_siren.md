# Siren — The Station's Dog

## Summary

Siren is Cerberus's littermate — his sister from the same litter, the same Mars breeding program, the same kennel. Their mother died during or shortly after giving birth. Siren was shipped to Fortuna Station alone as breeding stock on a prior Origin shipment, arriving before Jace and Cerberus. Cerberus traveled separately on the *Falcon* because Jace paid to bring him, specifically not wanting the two orphaned siblings separated permanently.

Where Cerberus bonds primarily with Jace, Siren bonds with everyone. She becomes the *Falcon* crew's collective dog (she had weeks at Fortuna before Jace arrived, but her bond with the crew that handles her en route is still real) and, after settling at the station, Fortuna's station-wide pet. She is goofier than Cerberus, more demonstrative, more willing to make a fool of herself for a laugh, and beloved across faction lines on the station.

She is narratively protected. The author commits to the dogs' safety.

## The Mother's Death

The litter's mother died during or shortly after giving birth. Siren and Cerberus are orphaned puppies, raised by the facility staff from very early on. This is the fact that drove Jace to spend real money buying Cerberus — he couldn't keep both puppies together permanently, but he could keep them together through the transit, and he could make sure Cerberus was cared for by someone who loved him rather than sold to a stranger.

At Fortuna, Siren and Cerberus are the only surviving members of their litter. Their reunion — when Jace and Cerberus arrive — is a small warm beat in an otherwise hard arc.

## Differentiation From Cerberus

It is important the two dogs not blur together on the page:

- **Bond pattern.** Cerberus is Jace's. Siren is everyone's.
- **Energy.** Cerberus is reserved and observant. Siren is exuberant and clownish.
- **Body language.** Cerberus leans; Siren bumps and occasionally jumps (she knows she shouldn't).
- **Vocalization.** Cerberus is quiet (bred trait). Siren has a full vocabulary of huffs, whines, play-growls, and dramatic sighs.
- **Talents.** Cerberus is precise. Siren is athletic — the better acrobat in the rotating cylinder.
- **Coat color.** Cerberus is darker; Siren is paler (cream or pale brindle). Helps readers keep them straight.

## Arrival at Fortuna

Siren arrived at Fortuna weeks before the *Falcon*. She is already familiar to the station's livestock staff when Jace and Cerberus arrive. The sibling reunion is warm and immediate — the two dogs recognize each other without hesitation.

By the time Cerberus arrives, Siren has already begun her work as a station mascot. She does the rounds, knows which crew members carry treats, has established the sock-stealing habit. She is working.

## Aboard the Station

Siren is allowed in nearly every common area, knows nearly every adult resident by smell, and has personal relationships with several station leaders. She is one of the small things that holds the station together when the blackout deepens and the magical anomalies escalate.

She works with the senior livestock supervisor, who becomes her primary handler post-arrival. Like her brother, she is Will-sensitive — animals perceive flux changes early — and her behavior becomes part of the station's informal warning system.

## Series Role

Siren is in every book. Where Cerberus is Jace's, Siren is the station's. Together they represent two different forms of bond — one intimate, one communal — both essential to the kind of community the belt has to become to survive.

## Voice and Mannerisms

- **Body language:** Tail-up always (unless something is genuinely wrong — the only time her tail goes down). Body slams as greeting; flops onto her back at the slightest invitation.
- **Vocalization:** Wide range. The crew accuses her of speaking. She has a specific complaining whine for "I am not being included" that is impossible to resist.
- **Quirks:** Steals socks. Has a known stash. Will not give them back; will let you find the stash.
