# Lieutenant Rich Cullivan

## Summary

Lt. Rich Cullivan is the Air Force officer assigned as Jace's sponsor when Jace arrives on Mars. He is one of only a handful of LTs stationed at the U.S. Air Force / Space Force joint installation at Terminus, and he is the lead officer for the base's communications and IT infrastructure — satellites, deep-space relays, signal networks. He is married to a woman who teaches school in their hometown, and they have **three children** — all young, all growing up while he is away. He is approximately two years ahead of Jace on the same career track, with two years left on his Mars tour.

He picks Jace up at the shuttle terminal, drives him around Mars, and serves as his window into this world before the routine of duty takes over. He is the grounded counterpoint to Jace's golden-boy enthusiasm. Where Jace is single, debt-loaded, and confidently spending future bonuses on present luxuries, Rich is married, fiscally careful, and quietly aware of how much of his career goes to supporting people he rarely sees. He likes Jace. He also sees through him.

---

## Background

Rich came up through ROTC, took a Mars-track posting, and arrived approximately two years before the story opens. His own MilX HUD was activated during his first months on Mars — he remembers the rush of it, the first time the overlay surprised him with information he hadn't asked for. He is now a competent user, no longer awed by the technology, and beginning to notice its limits.

He is married to a woman who teaches school in their hometown, and they have **three children** — all young, all growing up while he is away. The Mars tour is the financial cornerstone of their family plan. He is acutely aware of every dollar.

His role at the base is the **lead officer for communications and IT** — server infrastructure, encrypted military comms, network operations, equipment maintenance. This access makes him one of the most operationally important people on the Air Force side of the installation, and it becomes story-critical post-Jace's-discharge when Rich uses that access to do Jace a careful, quietly rule-bending favor.

## Role in the Story

**The arrival and the biometric scan.** Rich meets Jace at the shuttle terminal on Day 1 carrying a small hardened box alongside his standard-issue travel mug of coffee. Before the tour begins, he has Jace place his hand on the box — which scans his DNA, palm print, and capillary patterns. Jace's MilX HUD flashes: *Level 2 Access Granted. Connection to Local Military Network Established.* He is officially recognized as arriving at the Mars military installation. Rich grins: *"I guess it's really you."*

> Jace: "What — do you ever have people faking it?"
> Rich: "You hear stories. Anyway, after you officially take command you'll get Level 3 access and then the real work begins."

**Sponsor duties.** When new officers arrive at the Mars Detachment, an established LT is assigned to handle their first weeks — pickup at the shuttle, base orientation, billet assignment, introductions, the practical small things. Rich does this for Jace and does it well. Through Rich, the reader sees the Mars installation as a working place, not a glamour destination.

**The HUD/fiscal conversation.** During the rover tour, Rich asks how Jace is liking the new MilX HUD. Jace is enthusiastic — calls it his favorite thing about the military, mentions he's spent close to a year's wages on upgrades, game subscriptions, and courseware on the civilian partition. Rich is quietly appalled.

> Rich: "Wow. I've got kids back on Earth. I could never afford something like that."
> Jace: "No problem — I volunteered for the six-year tour. The bonuses should pay off all my debts."
> Rich: [glances at Jace, looks back at the road, says nothing]

**Post-discharge support.** After Jace is discharged and stripped of his military network access, Rich quietly meets him off-base and does him one significant favor using his IT/comms access. The favor establishes Rich as a model of compassion-with-limits: he helps, but not unconditionally. He has a wife and three kids back on Earth and a career he is responsible for.

## Personality

**Surface:** Friendly, professional, dryly funny. Comfortable with new arrivals because he has been one. Does not condescend, but does not perform enthusiasm either.

**Compassion as default.** Rich is genuinely warm. He smiles often — not performatively, but as his baseline expression around people he likes. New arrivals notice this immediately. When the people around him are struggling, Rich notices, and Rich acts — within the limits he has set for himself.

**Underneath:** Tired in the specific way of a parent serving far from home. Quietly worries about the time he is missing. Sees more than he says. As the outbreak deepens and his comms operations center becomes the center of unexplained crisis management, this tiredness accumulates visibly. He does not talk about what he is managing. His coffee canister grows.

**Contradictions:** Believes in the mission while resenting some of its costs. Genuinely likes the tech he works with but does not romanticize it. Hopes the people he mentors will avoid the mistakes he has watched others make, while knowing they mostly will not.

## The Coffee Canister — Progression

Rich's coffee is the single most visible external signal of his internal state across the entire hard-months arc. **It starts small. It grows.**

| When | Canister | State |
|---|---|---|
| **Day 1 arrival** | Standard-size travel mug — a nice one, well-used, has a base emblem on it. Sipped occasionally. | Warm, rested, happy to welcome someone new |
| **Day 3 (duty reporting, drone center tour)** | Same travel mug, refilled once during the tour | Normal operational energy; slightly more alert than Day 1 |
| **First post-discharge meeting (bar)** | Slightly larger insulated bottle — upgrade from the mug | Still mostly himself; starting to feel the weeks |
| **Second meeting** | The bottle again; he refills it mid-conversation | References "a long week" without specifics |
| **Third meeting / comms favor** | A full large canister — the kind with a belt-clip holster, noticeably larger than anything before | Visibly tired; takes longer to smile; the canister is new |
| **Final pre-departure meeting** | The large canister, now dented slightly, with an extra insulated sleeve | Hasn't shaved in two days; still smiles; the tiredness is a permanent feature now |

The growth of the canister is never commented on directly in the text. The reader tracks it. Jace eventually says something like *"Nice canister"* and Rich says *"Yeah"* and that's the whole conversation.

The canister is well-used: insulated metal, develops dents over time. It may have something personal on it — a unit patch, something one of his kids drew — but this is never announced. Storybot can extrapolate.

## Voice and Mannerisms

- **Speech:** Plainspoken, slightly older-brother-ish. Uses fewer acronyms than Jace; talks like an officer who has been around long enough to know when not to.
- **Body language:** Relaxed posture, but always aware of his MilX overlay. Glances aside reflexively to check messages — a tic Jace doesn't yet have.
- **The pause-and-sip.** The canister is part of his speech rhythm. When Rich wants to land something, he takes a sip first. When he wants to deflect, he takes a sip and changes the subject. As the crisis deepens, the sips come more frequently.
- **With Jace:** Patient. Doesn't lecture. Lets Jace's overconfidence speak for itself and trusts the universe to handle the rest. Calls Jace by his first name — always. Never "LT."

---

## After the Discharge

Rich is on Mars for the duration of Jace's collapse. He continues to acknowledge Jace after the discharge — a nod across the mess hall, a "you good?" in passing, and eventually the comms favor that kicks off the hard-months active plot. Each time Jace sees him, the canister is larger and Rich looks more tired.

Whether Rich becomes a more significant story figure depends on later books. He is well-positioned to matter if the comms-blackout / signals-intelligence plotline expands.
