# Kim

## Summary

Kim works Drone Ops alongside Jace and Carlos at Fortuna Station. She's around
45, African American, and carries a near-permanent scowl that reads, to Jace and
most newcomers, as active dislike. It isn't. She is dealing with something no one
around her knows about, and dealing with it quietly enough that it takes most of
Arc 1 for the truth to surface — to Jace, and only because of what he can now see.

## First Impressions (A-03)

Jace's first real exposure to Kim is overhearing her tell Carlos, flatly, that
Jace has only a mediocre level of skill. He'd thought he did well. He's
embarrassed, tries harder every shift after, and reads her scowl as personal —
proof she doesn't rate him. This is Jace's read, not necessarily the truth: Kim's
assessment of his work may be entirely accurate and professionally meant: the
scowl is not commentary on him specifically.

## Kim and Cerberus

Kim does not like dogs. This is established on arrival day, when most of the
station is charmed by Cerberus (many residents have never seen a dog in person)
and Kim is visibly not one of them. It continues as a running friction once Jace
starts bringing Cerberus to Drone Ops — Cerberus stays in a bed by Jace's feet
during shifts, and Kim complains regularly about dog hair and the smell.

**This should read as a separate, genuine dislike — not a mask for or an
explanation of her scowl toward Jace.** The two frictions (dogs, and whatever's
actually behind the scowl) run in parallel without one explaining the other. Don't
let the reader collapse them into a single grievance.

## The Falcon Connection

Kim was a passenger aboard the *Falcon* roughly ten years before Arc 1 — long
before Maureen's current run, on an earlier leg of the ship's working life. She
and Maureen have been friends since. Jace privately theorizes that whatever
happened during that voyage is the reason Kim is cold to him now — some
transferred association, some old grudge he's inherited by association with the
ship.

**This is presented as Jace's theory, not confirmed canon.** Per the project's
ambiguity principle (see Jin Lóng's construction from surveillance data as the
template), this should not be silently resolved. The real, observable cause of
Kim's affect — chronic migraines — is established separately and is sufficient
on its own to explain the scowl without needing the Falcon theory to be true.
Whether there's *also* something real in Jace's guess is open for a future arc,
not Arc 1.

## The Reveal

Once Jace's Astral perception has matured enough to read more than glow and
signature-color in people — likely after `X-02` establishes ambient Will-glow,
and reinforced by whatever slots into the proposed `L-08` — he can see that Kim
is in real, sustained pain. Migraines, severe and constant, that she has never
mentioned to anyone at work and has been quietly working through the entire time
Jace has known her.

He feels genuinely bad. Not because his assessment of her competence changes —
she was right that his early work was mediocre — but because he'd spent months
reading her pain as hostility, and she never once let it affect the job.

**Recommended placement:** the proposed open `L-08` slot in
`arc_01_thread_L_life_texture.md`, timed to land in the Dread/Fracture stretch
of the emotional arc as a warmth counterweight — not yet author-approved.

## Arc 1 Close

Kim's migraines are not resolved in Arc 1. Per author notes, she is present in
the swarm battle (S-01) — attacked while at her post in Drone Ops, her migraine
"suddenly stops" when she absorbs a Will orb mid-fight, and she's able to slice
through a group of cryptids at range, saving herself and Carlos. This is a
proposed battle beat currently living in the unintegrated "Many undecided
chapters" notes in `arc_01_thread_S_swarm.md` — not yet formalized as a scene
contract.

**Full resolution — her migraines tied to a genuine Will manifestation — is
explicitly deferred to a future arc**, not Arc 1.

## Relationship with Maureen

Old friends, dating back at least ten years to Kim's own time aboard the
*Falcon*. Not otherwise detailed yet — how they met, what the friendship looks
like day-to-day, whether Maureen knows about the migraines (plausible, given the
history — worth a decision).
