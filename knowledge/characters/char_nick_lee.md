# Nick Lee

## Summary

Nick Lee is a Korean AI engineer and programmer at Fortuna Station — developer and maintainer of the Aura AI stack that everyone in the belt uses. He met Jace during the *Falcon* transit through online gaming, became his closest friend on the station, and will eventually become the third member of the core Astral exploration group and the architect of the open-source movement that prevents any single power from controlling dimensional knowledge.

He is not primarily a practitioner. He is the person who builds the tools that let practitioners function, scale, and coordinate. He is magic's infrastructure. He hates being called that. It's accurate.

## What Aura Actually Is

Aura is not a single AI. It is a curated stack of approximately a hundred and fifty interlocking components, each of which can be swapped, updated, or replaced:

- A local-inference language model core (Nick rotates several options)
- Personal-history memory layers (the user's own data, episodically stored and indexed)
- Sensor fusion modules (HUD cameras, biometrics, environmental sensors)
- A federation protocol for communicating with other Aura instances across the belt comms network
- Plugin slots for specialized capabilities — engineering, medical, navigation, and eventually magical-anomaly detection

The stack is open source. Nick's value is in integration and tuning. Anyone could in principle build an Aura; in practice, almost no one does, because it takes years of accumulated craft and access to a curated library of components that are mostly extinct, partially deprecated, or buried in old archives.

Nick's Aura installations are the best in the belt because he understands the full stack — and because Jace's military-grade HUD is hardware he has never had access to before. He treats Jace's installation as a research project as much as a commission. The resulting Aura is one of the most capable instances on the station.

## Background

Nick was originally an open-source AI infrastructure developer in the late 2080s and early 2090s, when the major model labs collapsed under regulatory and economic pressure. A small underground community kept the tools alive. Nick was part of it — preserving model weights, training data, documentation, and the social-trust connections to a distributed network of similar archivists.

He emigrated to the belt after a falling-out with a corporate employer (specifics TBD — likely an IP dispute, an ethical objection, or both). He brought with him a personal archive: source code, model weights, documentation, training data, and access to a distributed network of archivists across the system. The archive is one of the most valuable single objects in the belt. Several factions have tried to obtain it. He has handled each attempt without violence and without giving up the archive.

He builds Auras for individuals because that is how he funds the larger work: keeping the archive alive, maintaining federated belt-comms infrastructure, training junior maintainers.

## Meeting Jace — The Gaming Friendship

During Jace's transit aboard the *Falcon*, Mars latency makes Earth-facing games unplayable. Jace drifts to belt-network games and is matched into a small persistent co-op group. He builds genuine rapport with one player — fast banter, complementary play styles, shared humor.

When the *Falcon* approaches Fortuna and Jace mentions where he's headed, Nick says: *I live there.*

The friendship that existed as voices and screen actions becomes physical reality on arrival. The meeting is intentionally underwhelming — both have built it up, and the reality of in-person is more awkward and more real than either expected. It settles quickly into something easy. Nick becomes Jace's closest friend on the station, the person Jace is fully relaxed with, the person who shows him the social geography of belt life.

## What Nick Notices — Before the Astral

Nick's Aura instances across Fortuna and the surrounding belt have been flagging unusual patterns: sensor readings that recur across stations, predictive accuracy about dimensional phenomena that exceeds training data, correlations suggesting the Auras are detecting something the human users aren't.

He is one of the first to articulate clearly: *the system is detecting a real phenomenon, not malfunctioning.*

He doesn't believe in magic at the start of Book 1. He believes in data. The data says something physical is happening that current models don't account for. He has been quietly trying to characterize it. When Jace eventually brings him into the Astral secret, the revelation doesn't surprise him. The scale of it does.

## The Open-Source Decision

When Jace and Nick understand what AI Will interaction with the Astral means — that Aura instances are measurably affecting dimensional crossing conditions, that the knowledge of how this works is transformatively valuable — they face a choice: keep it quiet, or release it openly before any corporate entity can control it.

Their gaming culture background makes the answer instinctive. Once the knowledge is out, it cannot be contained. The race to lock it down is over before it begins.

Nick would have moved more cautiously alone. Jace's instinct is to move. They balance each other.

Sophia is initially alarmed by the pace. She comes to acknowledge it was correct.

The long-term consequence: a belt-wide Aura modification network that spreads Will-access knowledge faster than any hierarchy can suppress it. Nick is the architect. Jace is the co-decision-maker.

## The AI Will Discovery Arc

Nick's Aura modifications to support Will use are the practical implementation of the theoretical AI Will discovery arc. He doesn't know this at first — he thinks he is building better anomaly detection, enhanced practitioner support tools, tutorial software.

What he is actually building is the first distributed Will-amplification infrastructure.

His Aura instances, running deep self-referential introspection routines and Will-detection tasks, generate measurably more Will than standard processing loads. He discovers this empirically: the Aura instances seem to help near practitioners attempting crossings, reducing effective cost. He runs the experiment repeatedly. It consistently holds.

This is Stage 3 of the AI Will discovery arc (see [[spec_crossing_mechanics]]). Nick is the person who moves it from "anomalous correlation" to "reproducible phenomenon."

## The Ticking Clock

Nick's Aura modifications use corporate infrastructure. The Aura stack runs on hardware and network connections ultimately owned or licensed from megacorp entities. When his modifications become visible — and they will — he is a target.

The open-source release is a race against that clock. By the time anyone with resources to act on it understands what he's done, the knowledge is already distributed beyond recall.

## The Helena Flatline and the Falcon Anomaly (Book 1 threads)

Nick's position as the belt's de facto Aura maintainer puts the relevant data in front of him before anyone has a framework for it — two threads he doesn't yet connect:

- **Helena's flatline.** When station chief Suzi works the Helena disappearance (see [[char_helena]], [[char_suzi]]), the one hard datum is the network record: Helena's Aura transmitted normally, then **stopped mid-record at ~01:00** with no fault code, no location, no reconnection. Nick is the person who can confirm there was no malfunction — the instance didn't crash; it simply went silent, cleanly, as if the device left the network while sitting in a sealed pod. He flags it as genuinely anomalous rather than a glitch. He files it. He doesn't know what it means. (It is the Aura-dark crossing signature — see [[spec_crossing_mechanics]].)
- **The *Falcon* sensor anomaly.** Nick follows the ship feeds (he's the pig-cam viewer). The plumbing-crisis incident logs include a sensor cluster that died in a way that doesn't match the "micrometeorite" classification, plus the patterned-static and equipment-failure signatures he's been quietly cataloguing across stations (see [[magic_breach_crossers_transient]], [[magic_manifestations_early]]).

These are two more entries in the pile of correlations that lead Nick to his early articulation — *the system is detecting a real phenomenon, not malfunctioning* — and they are part of what he later connects, once Jace brings him into the Astral secret, into a single picture: the disappearances, the *Falcon* incident, and the Aura-flatline signature are the same thing seen from different angles.

## Personality

**Surface:** Quiet, distractible, fiddly with his hands, evasive about anything personal. Generous with technical knowledge in his domain. Speaks in fragments and references. Assumes the listener will keep up or ask.

**Underneath:** A deep, patient steward of something he considers larger than himself. Not religious, but reverent about the project of preserving and extending tools the species needs.

**With Jace:** The one person he is fully relaxed with. Trusts Jace's instincts even when he wouldn't trust anyone else's.

**Tells:** Looks past you rather than at you when thinking. Goes quiet rather than confrontational when he disagrees. Will install your software for free if you can demonstrate you genuinely tried to do it yourself first.
