# Saga (working name) — Lead Dog of the Belt

## Summary

Saga is one of the first two domestic dogs ever transported from Mars to the asteroid belt. She and her littermate (see `/characters/char_dog_beta.md`) are products of a roughly thirty-year Mars breeding program that crossed working pugs, terriers, and several small herding breeds to produce a compact, low-G-adapted dog with strong bones, efficient lungs, calm temperament around livestock, and modest caloric requirements.

She bonds with Jace Grant during the *Falcon* transit and remains his companion across the entire series. She is comic relief, emotional anchor, and a recurring symbol of life persisting in places it was never meant to live. She is not in regular danger. The narrative protects her, and so do the characters.

## Background

The Mars dog program operates out of the U.S. settlement's agricultural and animal-husbandry division. After three decades of selective breeding under 0.38g, the program has produced a stable hybrid line:

- **Skeletal density** above the original parent breeds, achieved through both selection and dietary supplementation.
- **Respiratory efficiency** comparable to high-altitude working breeds despite the small body size — important because ferry and station atmospheres run at slightly lower pressure than Earth norm.
- **Caloric efficiency** — the line eats roughly 60% of what an equivalent-mass Earth dog would require, due to lower base metabolic rate and better feed conversion.
- **Temperament** — selected for calm, livestock-tolerant, human-bonded behavior. Aggression and excessive prey drive bred out.
- **Reproductive** — both dogs in this shipment are spayed for ferry transport per standard policy. Future breeding pairs ship separately, with strict protocols.

Saga and her sister are the first two dogs cleared for belt transport. The clearance was contested for years — every kilogram on a ferry is fought over, and the political question of "can we afford pets" was hotly debated on multiple stations before approval. The argument that finally carried: dogs improve livestock handling efficiency in ways that pay for their mass within months.

## Aboard the Falcon

Saga ships on the *Falcon* with her sister, Jace, and the rest of the animal complement. Within the first week she has selected Jace. She follows him through the rotating cylinder, sleeps in his bunk when permitted (often), and accompanies him through every shift in the livestock canisters. The pigs and cows acclimate to her quickly; she handles the introduction calmly and the senior animal handler approves.

Crew members not predisposed to dogs gradually warm. Two never do (one is allergic; one simply dislikes fur in life support). The tension this produces is a recurring low-key plot element — Saga represents both the *coziness* of frontier life and the *cost* of accommodation to the people who'd rather not.

She learns to flip and turn in the rotating cylinder's variable gravity in ways no Earth dog could — chasing a thrown ball through the long fifty-meter run, she launches off one wall, twists, catches the ball mid-rotation, lands lightly, and returns. The crew films her doing this. The footage circulates on the belt comms net within a week of arrival at Fortuna and becomes one of the early viral pieces of belt-original media.

## Aboard Fortuna Station

Saga and her sister are received with the rest of the animal complement. They become station-wide celebrities almost immediately. Children (the few there are) imprint hard. Adults split: most love her; a vocal minority resent the precedent. The station debates a "pet quota" within the first six months and ultimately tables it because Saga has demonstrably reduced incidents in the livestock pens by an order of magnitude.

She becomes Jace's grounding presence as the magical anomalies escalate. She is a sensitive in her own right — animals appear to perceive flux changes earlier and more reliably than humans — and her behavior becomes one of Jace's most trusted instruments long before he understands what he is reading.

## Series Role

Saga is in every book. She ages over the series — slowly, under low-G she may live measurably longer than an Earth-bound equivalent — and she changes as the world changes around her. She does not develop magical capabilities of her own (an open question; the working answer is *no*) but she perceives them with consistent clarity.

She is never killed for plot. She is never seriously injured for plot. The author commits to this at the outset. Her stable presence is part of what makes the series livable.

## Voice and Mannerisms (Yes, Even For a Dog)

- **Body language:** Forward, attentive, slightly bouncy. Tail high and slow when curious; high and fast when excited; low and still when something is wrong.
- **Vocalization:** Reserved. She does not bark indiscriminately. When she barks, the crew listens.
- **Quirks:** Greets Jace by leaning her shoulder against his shin rather than jumping. Always sleeps where she can see the door. Refuses certain foods that humans assume she should like.
