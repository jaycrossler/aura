# Mira

## Summary

Mira is ten years old. She was born on Fortuna Station and has never left it. She
does not speak. This is not a diagnosed medical condition — the station doctor's
file says "selective mutism (presumed); no organic cause identified; no intervention
recommended at this time." This has been the file's content for several years.

She follows Jace.

Not aggressively or frighteningly by intent. She finds him interesting and expresses
this the way she expresses everything: with patient, hollow-eyed, sustained attention,
conducted from distances just slightly too far to require acknowledgment.

---

## Physical Appearance

Small for ten. The particular thinness of a child who forgets to eat when she is
interested in something. Dark hair, usually not quite combed. Eyes that are
biologically normal and produce an effect that is not — not because of their color
or shape, but because of what they are doing. They are tracking. Whatever she is
looking at, she is really looking at it.

Standard-issue Fortuna children's station clothes. Clean enough. She has a small
pouch on her belt for carrying small things. The pouch always has plant matter in it.
Some of it is not plant matter anymore, technically. It has become something else.

---

## Behavior

Mira does not make sounds. She does not sign. She communicates what she needs to her
parents through gesture and, when necessary, writing on her forearm screen. She is
literate, possibly above average. Her school attendance is irregular. Her teachers
have various theories. None are correct.

She watches Jace from:
- The end of a corridor as he passes through
- The far end of the common area during meals
- A maintenance level above the drone bay, where there are gaps in the paneling
  she has learned to use
- Once, from inside a supply closet with the door open exactly the width of one eye

She is not hiding. If asked, she would not understand why her position constitutes
hiding. She is watching. These are different things.

---

## The Plant Matter and Creations (Arc 1 Progression)

Mira's [Astral Creation] ability expresses through whatever she is handling when
she concentrates. Her AI tutor — a {Penny} educational instance — has been
enthusiastically supporting her interest in biology, providing access to a complete
human anatomy database, botanical taxonomy references, and an invertebrate zoology
module she found independently. She makes things. She does not know the things she
makes are unusual.

**Early Fortuna:** Pressed leaves, fused stems, shapes that shouldn't be possible
without tools. Wrong but simple.

**Mid-arc:** Something that appears to be a leaf except one side is vascular tissue —
wet, red-brown, the attempt at a specific biological structure. {Aura} describes it
as "consistent with attempts to replicate vascular plant anatomy, though the
organization suggests a learning process."

**Later:** A small heart. Three centimeters. Four chambers, approximately. Not
beating. But not exactly not doing something. A wing — joint geometry copied from a
diagram missing the wrist-equivalent bone. Found near Hayes's table.

**Pre-swarm:** Full arrangement on the drone bay threshold. Roughly circular,
plate-sized. Plant-matter weavings around the outer edge. Anatomical constructs
within. At the center: something roughly spherical, translucent, with an internal
structure Jace cannot identify. He looks at it for two full minutes.

Cerberus refuses to cross the threshold until Jace moves the arrangement. Jace moves
it with gloves. The sphere is warm.

{Penny}'s educational instance has sent Mira's parents a positive progress report.
Her parents are relieved and have not looked closely at what "three-dimensional
modeling" means in this context.

---

## Cerberus's Response

Cerberus will not approach Mira. Not aggression — specific avoidance. The same dog
that approaches Threshold Hounds at breach zones will not come within three meters
of a ten-year-old girl.

When Jace tries to walk him toward Mira, Cerberus puts his body behind Jace's leg
and stays there until they've passed.

After Yáo's death during the swarm fight, Cerberus finds Mira and sits beside her.
The avoidance ends here. Whether something resolved in that moment, or whether
proximity to something true (the sacrifice, the cost) created a new context —
Cerberus does not avoid Mira again.

## What Is Actually Happening

Mira began manifesting [Astral Creation] approximately two years ago, at age eight,
without context, framework, or anyone to notice. The ability had nowhere to go and
no language to be contained in, so it went sideways — expressing through whatever
she was handling when she was concentrating on something.

She does not know she is doing it. She knows that sometimes when she holds plants
and thinks very hard, they feel different afterward. She thinks this is something
everyone experiences and that the social convention is not to discuss it.

**Because she was so young when this began, she developed none of the Sol human
Mental Fortification architecture** that adult practitioners build as a side effect
of long-term Will-with-nowhere-to-go. Her Will is entirely forward-facing — outward
expression with no internal buffer. This makes her:

- Simultaneously more capable of direct creation than any adult practitioner who
  developed skills in the normal sequence
- More vulnerable to Resonance-based influence than any adult human on the station
- Completely unprotected in ways that will become urgent as threats escalate in
  later arcs

**Why she follows Jace:** She does not know. She is ten. If pressed, she might write:
*he is bright.* Not in personality terms. In the literal sense of what she perceives
when she looks at him. She has been watching the glows on the station for two years.
Jace's is different — active, layered, threaded through with something that changes
between sessions. She is interested in why it changes.

She found Hayes interesting for the same reason. She watched him from across the
common area. After Hayes's accident, she stopped watching that direction.

They love Mira in the way people love a child who is more than they feel equipped
to handle. They are glad when she has something to occupy herself. They check in at
