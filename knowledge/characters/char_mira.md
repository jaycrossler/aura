# Mira

## Summary

Mira is ten years old. She was born on Fortuna Station and has never left it.
She does not speak. This is not a medical condition that has been diagnosed — the
station doctor's file on her says "selective mutism (presumed); no organic cause
identified; no intervention recommended at this time." This has been the file's
content for several years. No one has updated it.

She follows Jace.

This is not aggressive or frightening by intent. She simply finds him interesting
and expresses this the way she expresses everything: with patient, hollow-eyed,
sustained attention, conducted from distances that are just slightly too far to
require acknowledgment.

---

## Physical Appearance

Small for ten. The particular thinness of a child who forgets to eat when she is
interested in something. Dark hair that is usually not quite combed. Eyes that are
biologically normal and produce an effect that is not normal — not because of their
color or shape, but because of what they are doing. They are tracking. Whatever she
is looking at, she is really looking at it.

She wears the standard-issue children's station clothes that every Fortuna kid wears.
They are clean enough. She has a small pouch on her belt that her parents gave her
for carrying small things. The pouch always has plant matter in it. Some of it is
not plant matter anymore, technically. It has become something else.

---

## Behavior

Mira does not make sounds. She does not sign. She communicates what she needs to her
parents through gesture and, when necessary, writing on her forearm screen. She is
literate, possibly above average. Her school attendance is irregular. Her teachers
have various theories about her. None of the theories are correct.

She watches Jace from:
- The end of a corridor as he passes through
- The far end of the common area during meals
- A maintenance level above the drone bay, where there are gaps in the paneling
  she has learned to use
- Once, from inside a supply closet with the door open exactly the width of one eye

She is not hiding. If asked, she would not understand why her position constitutes
hiding. She is watching. These are different things.

---

## The Plant Matter

Fortuna Station has communal planters through the residential corridors —
low-maintenance species, morale improvement, part of the original ring design.
Mira is often near them. This is not unusual; children are often near planters.

What is unusual: the arrangements she leaves behind.

They are not crafts. They are not decorations. They are small collections of stems
and leaves that have been:
- Pressed into shapes that don't correspond to how plants press
- Fused at points where fusion isn't possible without tools, heat, or something else
- Warped so that the cellular structure of the plant, if examined, would show
  stress patterns inconsistent with the force required to produce the visible result

They are not threatening. They are wrong. As if someone tried to reproduce a
thing they had seen from a very long distance and gotten the *shape* but not the
*nature* of what they were copying.

Jace finds them in:
- The corridor outside his room (twice)
- The floor of the common area near where he usually sits (once)
- The drone bay threshold (once — this one was more elaborate and Jace looked at
  it for a long time before stepping around it)

He does not report them. He doesn't know what he would report.

## Cerberus's Response

Cerberus will not approach Mira.

This is specific and deliberate. Cerberus approaches strangers freely — she is a
sociable dog who has been well-handled. She approaches adults, children, machinery,
animals. She has approached the Threshold Hounds that congregate near the breach
zones, which are creatures that would make most dogs reconsider.

She will not go within three meters of Mira.

When Jace tries to walk her toward Mira, Cerberus puts her body behind Jace's leg
and stays there. Not aggression — avoidance. She looks up at Jace with an expression
he has come to read as *you should know about this but I can't tell you what to do
with it.*

After they've passed, Cerberus walks normally again. She does not look back.

## What Is Actually Happening

Mira began manifesting [Astral Creation] approximately two years ago, without
context, framework, or anyone to notice what she was doing. She was eight. Children
who grow up in the Scar's clearing zone sometimes manifest earlier than adults.
The ability had nowhere to go and no language to be contained in, so it went
sideways — expressing through whatever she was handling, reshaping matter she
touched when she was concentrating on something.

She does not know she is doing it. She knows that sometimes when she holds plants
and thinks very hard, they feel different afterward. She thinks this is something
everyone experiences and that the social convention is not to discuss it.

Because she was so young when this began, she developed none of the Sol human
Mental Fortification architecture that adult practitioners build as a side effect
of long-term Will-with-nowhere-to-go. Her Will is entirely forward-facing —
outward expression with no internal buffer. This makes her simultaneously more
capable of direct creation and more vulnerable to Resonance-based influence than
any adult human on the station.

Kael will notice her immediately upon arriving at Fortuna. This is a late Arc 1 /
early Arc 2 beat.

## Why She Follows Jace

She does not know. She is ten. When pressed — if she were ever pressed — she might
write: *he is bright.* Not in personality terms. In the literal sense of what she
perceives when she looks at him.

She has been watching the glows on the station for two years. Most people have
small, warm, steady glows. Jace's is different — active, layered, threaded through
with something that changes between sessions. She is interested in why it changes.
She is not going to stop watching until she understands.

She finds Hayes interesting too, for the same reason. She never sat near Hayes at
the table — she watched from across the common area. After Hayes's accident, she
stopped watching that direction.

## Parents

Her parents (names TBD — father works in manufacturing, mother works in station
administration) are Fortuna residents who are doing their best. They love Mira
in the way people love a child who is more than they feel equipped to handle.
They are glad when she has something to occupy herself. They check in at meals.
They sleep when she is occupied with her watching.

They will need to be part of the story when Kael's assessment of Mira becomes
clear. This is Arc 2 territory.

## Future Arc Role

Mira is a significant character in later arcs. Her specific trajectory:

- Arc 1: The strange child. Background wrongness. Cerberus's refusal.
  Plant-matter arrangements. Nothing explained.
- Arc 2 (early): Kael's assessment — Kael sees Mira and her reaction is the
  reader's first understanding of how unusual Mira actually is
- Later arcs: [Astral Creation] developed with guidance; the question of her
  lack of Mental Fortification becomes urgent as threats escalate; she is
  potentially the most powerful creator-type practitioner in the Sol system
