# [TBD] — The Friend Through the Net

## Summary

Jace's best friend on Fortuna Station is the person who pulls him into the social and magical underground of the belt — and they meet entirely by accident, through the games Jace plays during the *Falcon* transit. The friendship begins online, deepens through hours of conversation across the comm latency, survives the move from gaming partners to in-person crewmates, and becomes the most important non-romantic relationship of Jace's adult life.

This character is the **bridge**: between Jace and the Aura engineer, between Jace and the broader Synthesis circle, between Jace's American outsider position and the station's cultural communities. They are not Jace; they are not Mei; they are the person Jace becomes himself with.

## How They Meet

During transit, Jace's online gaming options shrink as Mars latency makes Mars-based games unplayable. He drifts to local belt-network games, where he is matched into a small persistent group. Over hours and weeks of co-op, he builds a real rapport with one player in particular — fast banter, complementary play styles, shared sense of humor.

When the *Falcon* approaches Fortuna, Jace mentions where he's headed. The friend laughs: they live there. They will be on the same station. They might even end up on the same mining ship. The friendship that has existed entirely as voices and coordinated screen actions becomes physical reality on arrival.

## Background (TBD, but with constraints)

The character is **not American** — they bring a real cultural tradition that Jace doesn't have, which becomes one of the things they teach each other. Working possibilities:

- **A second-generation Mars-born** with European cultural inheritance (Icelandic? Greek? German?) carrying fragmentary Skaldic, Hellenic, or Oath-Bound knowledge.
- **A Chinese belter** with partial Inscribed Path knowledge — adds political complexity given the Mars-Earth Chinese-tension narrative.
- **An Indian belter** with disciplined Vedic-path practice — narratively powerful but possibly too on-the-nose.

The recommended direction is **Icelandic-descended**, second-generation Mars-born, Skaldic Path practitioner with strong gaming and engineering skills. This keeps them culturally distinct, narratively useful, and structurally interesting (their tradition is the proto-programming-language one, which fits their gamer/engineer disposition).

## Role in Book One

- **Online during transit.** A voice and a screen presence; a relationship that grows by accretion of small shared moments.
- **In-person on arrival.** They meet Jace at the airlock or shortly after; the moment is intentionally underplayed — both have built up the meeting in their heads, and they handle the awkwardness of it differently than they expected.
- **Tour guide.** Shows Jace the station's actual social geography (which mess hall has the best bread, which corridor the engineers haunt, which faction not to insult).
- **Introduction.** Brings Jace to the Aura engineer (see [Identity](char_aura_engineer.md)). This is how Jace's HUD ends up running a personal Aura instance.
- **Crewmate.** They end up on the same mining ship for early Book 2 expeditions. The bond deepens.
- **Magical mirror.** They are also a sensitive, emerging at the same time. They learn alongside Jace, from a different cultural starting point. Their conversations become a primary mechanism by which the reader learns about the Returning Arts.

## Why They Matter

This character does narrative work that no one else can do:
- They give Jace a friendship that isn't transactional. Mei is an asset relationship. The smuggler is a transactional relationship. The dogs are companions but not peers. Jace needs a peer.
- They normalize the magic. Where Jace agonizes, this character explores. Different responses to the same phenomena make the phenomena legible to the reader.
- They introduce the network. One person can only be so many things; this character is the door to the rest of the cast.
