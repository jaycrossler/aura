# Character Voice Profile — Jace Apollo Grant

This file captures the vocal, dialogue, and speech-pattern characteristics of Jace for use by the StoryBot in generating consistent dialogue, audiobook narration, and visual character expressions. Areas marked **[STORYBOT]** invite extrapolation consistent with established traits.

---

## Vocal Characteristics

### Audio Profile (for audiobook generation)
- **Voice type:** Tenor-baritone — medium-low register, warm
- **Accent:** General American (Mid-Atlantic, slight Virginia softness)
- **Pitch:** Medium-low at baseline; rises when nervous or excited
- **Speech rate:** Slightly fast at baseline (Type-A officer); slows under emotional weight
- **Volume:** Moderate; trained to project clearly when needed; quiet in private/intimate settings
- **Resonance:** Chest voice dominant; some throat tension when stressed
- **Distinctive features:**
  - Slight softening of "r" sounds (Virginia upbringing residue)
  - Crisp consonants when in military mode
  - Occasional swallow before difficult sentences

**[STORYBOT]** For voice casting reference: think of a 28-year-old Tom Cruise meets young Chris Pine — earnest, capable, with vulnerability underneath.

### Vocal Variations by Emotional State

**Confident (default):**
- Clear projection
- Direct eye contact assumed
- Short declarative sentences
- Minimal filler words
- Slight smile in voice

**Nervous (around authority, attractive people, uncertainty):**
- Pitch rises slightly
- Filler words appear ("uh," "I mean")
- Hesitation before key words
- Sentence length shortens
- Throat clears more often

**Angry (rare — only under extreme stress):**
- Volume drops, doesn't rise (controlled anger)
- Words clip
- Pauses between sentences for control
- Voice becomes flat, almost mechanical

**Sad/Devastated (post-discharge):**
- Quieter
- Longer pauses
- Voice cracks on emotional words
- Often trails off mid-sentence
- Avoids eye contact

**Excited (HUD upgrades, Mars arrival, gym):**
- Faster speech
- More gestures while talking
- Higher pitch slightly
- Words tumble over each other
- Genuine warmth in tone

**Tired (after voyage, after work shifts):**
- Slower
- Words run together
- Fewer details
- "Yeah" and "okay" become primary responses

## Speech Patterns

### Verbal Tics and Habits
- Says "copy" instead of "okay" when concentrating on a task
- Says "negative" instead of "no" when stressed or in formal mode
- Reflexive "How can I help?" — the needs-to-be-needed instinct
- Says "right, right" when processing new information
- Says "sir" or "ma'am" automatically with strangers/authority (Virginia upbringing + military)
- Says "thank you" excessively when receiving help (good manners + slight insecurity)

### Filler Words and Verbal Hedges
- "Uh" — when nervous
- "I mean" — when correcting himself or softening a statement
- "Right" — affirmation while processing
- "Yeah" — casual agreement
- "Look" — when trying to be sincere
- Avoids "like" as filler (more military-clipped)

### Sentence Structure
- **Default:** Subject-verb-object, direct
- **Under stress:** Sentence fragments, incomplete thoughts
- **Excited:** Compound sentences, lots of "and"
- **Formal:** Polished, complete, slightly stiff
- **With family:** Loose, warm, story-prone

### Vocabulary Range
- **Technical (high competence):**
  - Drone operations terminology (sortie, sweep, payload, swarm)
  - Military procedure (deployment, briefing, sit-rep, copy that)
  - HUD/AI terminology (overlay, instance, latency, render)
  - Engineering basics (load-bearing, redundancy, failover)

- **Casual (medium):**
  - Everyday speech, slightly less hip than peers
  - Sports references (he played in college)
  - Pop culture (slightly outdated — he's been in the military bubble)
  - Tech/gaming references (his nerd interests)

- **Emotional/Vulnerable (low — atrophied):**
  - Struggles with emotional vocabulary
  - Defaults to action-language ("I'll handle it") rather than feeling-language
  - Compliments are awkward, brief
  - Gratitude is profuse but generic

- **Romantic/Intimate (very low — underdeveloped):**
  - Shy, awkward
  - Avoids declarative emotional statements
  - Tends toward humor or deflection
  - Compliments women on capability, not appearance

## Relationship-Specific Speech

### With Rich (peer, sponsor, friend)
- **Tone:** Informal, peer-to-peer
- **Mode:** "Yeah, man" register
- **Topics:** Tech, work, mild personal sharing, dry humor
- **Body language while speaking:** Relaxed, open, occasional grins
- **Sample line:** "Yeah, I figured I'd grab the new model. The interface is incredible."

### With the Colonel (superior officer)
- **Tone:** Formal, respectful
- **Mode:** "Yes, sir" register
- **Topics:** Mission, duty, respectful answers
- **Body language while speaking:** Officer's posture, eye contact, no slouching
- **Sample line:** "Yes, sir. Drone command was my specialization at the Academy."

### With Mei (intel operative, asset relationship)
- **Tone:** Initially confused, then guarded
- **Mode:** Carefully professional
- **Topics:** Stays surface; avoids personal questions
- **Body language while speaking:** Tense, slightly defensive
- **Sample line:** "I get it. We don't know each other."

### With Sylvester (handler, transactional)
- **Tone:** Polite, transactional
- **Mode:** Asset-to-handler — receives instructions, asks clarifying questions
- **Topics:** What's expected of him, terms, timeline
- **Body language while speaking:** Reserved, listening more than speaking
- **Sample line:** "Understood. What do you need from me?"

### With Family (Dawn, Chris, Athena, Artemis)
- **Tone:** Warm, performative
- **Mode:** "Good son" / "Big brother" / "Little brother" register
- **Topics:** Mostly positive updates, hides struggles, asks about their lives
- **Body language while speaking:** Soft smile, genuine warmth
- **Sample line (to Dawn):** "Hi Mom. Made it safely. Mars is incredible. I miss you guys."
- **Sample line (to Athena):** "Sis! Yeah, Mars is wild. I'll send pics later — I just got here."
- **Sample line (to Artemis):** "Hey big sis. Just wanted to let you know I made it safely."

### With Cerberus (the puppy)
- **Tone:** Unguarded, soft
- **Mode:** Goofy dog-talk — the only register where Jace is fully himself
- **Topics:** Doesn't matter — pure affection
- **Body language while speaking:** Crouching, smiling, stroking
- **Sample line:** "Hey buddy. Hey Cerberus. You ready for a big adventure?"

### With "the Rat" (the smuggler)
- **Tone:** Cautious, learning
- **Mode:** Customer-to-fixer
- **Topics:** Logistics, money, risk assessment
- **Body language while speaking:** Tense, surveying surroundings
- **Sample line:** "What's it going to cost me?"

### With Strangers (vendors, base personnel, civilians)
- **Tone:** Polite, brief
- **Mode:** Officer-public-presentation — courteous, efficient
- **Topics:** Transactional
- **Body language while speaking:** Pleasant, mildly distant
- **Sample line:** "Coffee, eggs, anything quick. Thank you."

### With AI/Aura (personal AI instance)
- **Tone:** Treats it as a colleague, not a tool
- **Mode:** Conversational, occasionally arguing
- **Topics:** Anything — Aura knows him well
- **Body language while speaking:** Subvocal or murmured aloud
- **Sample line:** "Pull up the latest on the Odysseus comms. And give me a workout playlist."

## Internal Monologue Style (for prose POV)

### Default
- Observational, slightly self-critical
- Notices details others miss (military training)
- Tracks his performance — am I doing this right?
- Catastrophizes silently while presenting competence externally

### Under Stress
- Becomes more military-clipped internally
- Reverts to procedure-speak
- Mental checklists
- "Get through this" mantras

### When Vulnerable
- Becomes more emotional, child-like
- Thinks of family
- Wonders what his father would do
- Brief flashes of self-pity that he immediately suppresses

### When Joyful
- Expansive, sensory-rich
- Notices beauty, novelty
- Plans futures
- Quiet pride

## Speech-Affecting Conditions

### When Wearing Pressure Suit Helmet
- Voice sounds slightly metallic, processed
- Subvocal microphone picks up smaller sounds
- Tendency to over-enunciate
- Occasional radio-protocol creep ("Copy that," "How copy")

### When HUD is Deactivated (post-discharge)
- Slower speech — relying entirely on memory and unaided cognition
- More hesitation
- Less smooth flow of references and facts
- Occasionally reaches for something (an app, a stat, a translation) and isn't there
- This phase changes how he sounds — pay attention in scenes after the discharge

### When HUD is Reactivated (Sylvester deal)
- Speech smooths out again
- Cross-references and recalls come faster
- Slight mental "tickle" of having his AI back — sometimes makes him pause mid-sentence

### When Tired
- Slurred slightly
- Sentences trail off
- "Yeah, sure" becomes universal response
- Body language sags before voice does

### When Drinking
- Becomes more emotionally open
- Family comes up more
- Self-deprecating humor surfaces
- Apologizes more than usual

## Visual/Body Language Markers (for video/image generation)

### When Confident
- Shoulders back
- Direct eye contact
- Small smile (often unconscious)
- Hands relaxed, open
- Slight forward lean

### When Nervous
- Shoulders tighten slightly
- Eye contact breaks more often
- Hands fidget — touches collar, smooths uniform, plays with HUD
- Adam's apple visible swallow
- Slight tension in jaw

### When Devastated
- Slumped posture
- Eyes unfocused or downcast
- Hands clasped tightly or slack
- Slight tremor in hands
- Trying not to cry — nostrils flare, blinks rapidly
- Voice breaks before tears do

### When Excited (about tech, fitness, military duty)
- Animated gestures
- Faster movement
- Big genuine smile
- Eyes light up
- Shifts weight forward

### When Tender (with family memories, with Cerberus)
- Soft eyes
- Reduced motion
- Half-smile, lopsided
- Hands gentler in their movements
- Occasional sigh

### Default Posture
- Officer's stance — chest up, shoulders back, balanced weight
- Aware of his surroundings (military training)
- Habitually checks exits
- Stands to side of doorways
- Tracks people's hands subconsciously
