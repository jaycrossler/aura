# Cerberus

## Summary

Cerberus is Jace Grant's dog — a young, compact Mars-adapted working breed he acquired as a puppy four months before arriving at Fortuna Station. She is four to five months old at story start. She is the first animal to survive repeated Astral crossings with purpose-built equipment. She has already saved Jace's life.

## Background and Bond with Jace

Cerberus was born at a Mars livestock facility. Jace first encountered her through gig work cleaning the pens — she was part of a litter, and he visited repeatedly, forming a quiet attachment over weeks. When he negotiated his Origin Industries contract, he included Cerberus on the manifest as a condition of signing. The Origin representative agreed. Jace walked out feeling he had held his ground. The reader knows what he doesn't. (See [[char_jace_apollo]] — the "most expensive dog in human history" beat.)

The four-month voyage from Mars to Fortuna aboard the *Falcon* bonded them completely. Jace raised her through puppyhood in transit. She has known no adult life without him. She sleeps pressed against him every night. He talks to her plainly, without the careful self-presentation he maintains with people. She gets the truest version of him.

She is still young — full of energy, occasionally uncoordinated, deeply loyal. She is also, without anyone knowing yet, Will-sensitive in ways that are about to matter enormously.

## The First Crossing — Cerberus Pulls the Mask

Jace's first full crossing is accidental, happens in his sleep, and would have killed him within ninety seconds if not for Cerberus.

### The Sequence

Jace is in deep meditative sleep. He has been having recurring dreams of glowing crystal tunnels for weeks — glimpses of the Astral bleeding into his sleep perception. His Template, sensitized by high-flux exposure, crosses fully. Cerberus — sleeping pressed against him as always — crosses with him. Her Template has enough natural Will-affinity that his crossing carries her through.

She wakes first.

Cold. Wrong-smell. Wrong-air. Jace not breathing properly.

She doesn't understand dimensions. She doesn't understand what has happened. She understands: Jace is suffocating. The mask — the familiar object he always sleeps with, the thing that fixes breathing — is on the other side. She reaches for it.

What follows is an act of pure instinct that happens to be a dimensional tear-pull — the same technique Sophia used to rescue her respirator during her first crossing. Cerberus has no training, no framework, no understanding of what she is doing. She is a four-month-old puppy acting on the most fundamental drive she has.

The mask resists. The plastic and rubber flow through faster; the battery catches and requires repeated tugs. She is spending Will she doesn't have. By the time the mask is fully through, her Astral glow is close to guttered. She has nearly killed herself.

Jace wakes gasping — CO2 poisoning, barely functional. He sees Cerberus, exhausted, with the mask. He takes it. Puts it on. Breathes. Then gives it back — she needs air too. They share the mask, back and forth, taking turns, until Jace recovers enough Will and enough clarity to pull a second mask through himself. Massive effort. His first deliberate dimensional act.

They survive together.

### After the Crossing — Exploration and the Meeting

With two masks and breathing stabilized, Jace doesn't immediately panic back. He looks around. The station walls are semi-transparent. He can see into adjacent rooms, see rocks and ships outside in the dark. He explores his quarters, then the corridors. Cerberus leads him, following familiar smells, toward the station's central areas.

Hours pass. They are cold and disoriented but alive, and Jace is an engineer — he is observing, processing, trying to understand what he is seeing.

In the mess hall, they find Sophia. She is exhausted and focused on a micro-pull, trying to bring some food through. She doesn't notice them until Cerberus barks.

There are no insects in the immediate area. The station's interior Astral layer is not yet heavily infested, and they've had hours of safe exploration before the meeting. The encounter is cold and disorienting and deeply human — two people relieved beyond words to find each other.

## After the First Crossing — Early Warning System

Cerberus has been somewhere. She knows it. After returning to physical space, she recognizes the dimensional conditions that preceded the crossing. When flux conditions rise high enough to risk another accidental crossing, she wakes Jace before it can happen — standing on him, refusing to let him settle, becoming intensely anxious.

He thinks she needs the bathroom. She is keeping him alive.

The Threshold Hound pack near Fortuna Station treats her differently after the first crossing. She smells like the other side. They give her a respectful distance they do not extend to other animals.

## Cerberus's Kit

After Sophia and Jace formalize their partnership and begin regular expeditions, they engineer proper equipment for Cerberus: a sealed breathing mask adapted from station veterinary equipment, a light thermal layer fitted to her compact Mars-bred frame.

She accepts the mask immediately. She has watched Jace wear masks her entire life. This is simply what you wear to go to the important place.

With proper breathing support, her operational time extends from 60-90 seconds of ambient bleed to the same 3-6 hour range as her human companions. She is the first animal with purpose-built Astral crossing equipment, and she wears it with complete unselfconsciousness.

## In the Astral

With her kit on, Cerberus is alert rather than anxious — tracking, sensing, oriented toward things before her human companions notice them. Her instincts make her a genuine advance warning system.

Her glow in the Astral, documented carefully by Sophia, is developing additional structural complexity with each crossing. Animal minds glow more simply than human minds: single-center, less fractal, minimal color variation. Cerberus's glow, after repeated crossings, is beginning to develop structure that shouldn't be there by baseline expectations — a faint echo of the fractal complexity of human minds.

She is accumulating something. Whether it is Template development, Will-sensitivity, or something without a name yet, Sophia is watching it closely.

The Astral fauna give her a wider berth than they give the humans.

## Long Arc

Over repeated crossings, Cerberus becomes progressively better at sensing dimensional conditions, more attuned to practitioner activity, and notably calm in situations that panic other animals. Station residents notice she often orients toward events before they happen. Nobody has an explanation.

When Jace crosses deliberately and leaves her behind, she scratches at the air where he disappeared for the full duration. When he returns, she checks him over methodically — sniffing, assessing, like checking for injury. He has not been injured. She knows the smell of where he went, and she is not happy about it. She does not stop trying to follow him.
