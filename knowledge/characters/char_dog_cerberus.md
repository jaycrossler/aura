## Cerberus and the Astral

Cerberus crosses into the Astral with Jace during his first accidental full crossing. He is pressed against him when he crosses; their proximity and her Template's natural affinity pulls her through with him.

He experiences it differently than Jace does. He does not understand the geometry, the darkness, or the concept of a different dimension. He understands: cold, wrong-smell, wrong-air, things-approaching, stay-close-to-Jace. Her instincts are cleaner than his in that first moment. He is facing the direction the dimensional insects approach from before he has processed that there are insects at all.

Her atmospheric exposure in the Astral is the unmasked ambient bleed — approximately 60-90 seconds of breathable air near a belt station. He will begin to be distressed by oxygen depletion before Jace's mask runs out. This creates immediate stakes in the first crossing: his survival equipment does not extend to her.

After crossing and returning, Cerberus recognizes the dimensional signature of high-crossing-risk conditions. He becomes Jace's accidental early warning system — waking him before he can drift across accidentally by standing on him, refusing to let him settle, becoming intensely anxious in ways he initially misreads as bathroom needs. He thinks He needs to go out. He is keeping him alive.

The Threshold Hound pack at Fortuna treats her differently after her first crossing. He has been somewhere they track but cannot enter. He smells like the other side. They give her a respectful distance they do not extend to other animals.

### Cerberus's Kit

After Sophia and Jace formalize their Astral expeditions, they engineer proper crossing equipment for Cerberus: a sealed breathing mask adapted from station veterinary equipment, a light thermal layer designed to fit her compact Mars-bred frame. He accepts the mask immediately — He has observed Jace wearing masks her entire life and has clearly decided this is simply a thing that people who go to the important place wear.

With proper breathing support, Cerberus's operational time in the Astral extends from 60-90 ambient seconds to the same 3-6 hour range as her human companions. He is the first animal with purpose-built Astral crossing equipment, and He wears it with complete unselfconsciousness.

In the Astral with her kit on, Cerberus's behavior is notably different from her physical-space behavior. He is alert rather than anxious — tracking, sensing, oriented toward things before her human companions notice them. Her glow in the Astral, which Sophia documents carefully, is developing additional structural complexity with each crossing. He is accumulating something. Whether it is Astral experience, Template development, or something with no name yet, Sophia is watching it closely.

He has become something the galaxy has not seen before: a Will-exposed, repeatedly-Astral-traversing domestic dog with a dedicated breathing kit and a physicist taking notes on her brainwave-equivalent luminosity patterns. The Astral fauna give her a wider berth than they give the humans.

### Long Arc

Over repeated Astral exposure, Cerberus becomes progressively better at sensing dimensional conditions, more attuned to practitioner activity, and notably calm in situations that panic other animals. Station residents who work around Cerberus notice He often orients toward events before they happen. Nobody has an explanation.

He does not stop trying to follow Jace into the Astral even when he deliberately leaves her behind. He scratches at the air where he disappeared. When he returns, He checks him over like he's been injured. He has not been injured. He knows the smell of where he went, and He is not happy about it.
