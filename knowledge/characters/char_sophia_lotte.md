# Dr. Sophia Lotte

## Summary

Sophia Lotte is a German physicist stationed in the outer asteroid belt who became, without intending to and without anyone else knowing, the first human being to deliberately cross into the Astral in three thousand years.

She has been going back every night for a month. She named it the Never-Never — after Baba Yaga's domain in Slavic folklore, not Peter Pan. The distinction matters. She is not thinking about lost boys or escapism. She is thinking about the hut beyond the deep forest where dangerous knowledge lives, where you go knowing it will cost you something, where the boundary between the living world and whatever lies beyond it becomes thin. That framing tells you everything about how Sophia approaches what she has found.

## Background

Sophia was posted to the outer belt as part of a research station studying the anomalous flux phenomena that have been accumulating in the region. Her speciality is high-energy particle physics, specifically the exotic radiation signatures associated with the Vela Junior supernova remnant. She was trying to understand why the flux produces the anomalies it does — the equipment interference, the strange biological effects on fauna, the sensor readings that don't fit any existing physical model.

She wasn't looking for the Astral. She was doing physics.

The first crossing was an accident — a partial crossing during a high-flux measurement event that briefly overlapped the physical and Astral spaces in her immediate vicinity. She saw through the membrane. She made the mistake of reaching toward what she saw.

She survived by approximately three seconds. Her respirator was on the shelf above her workspace. She had exactly enough time to grab it and pull it through with her before the crossing completed.

She landed in the dark, in the dead landscape, with a respirator she had yanked through at the cost of what felt like pulling her arm out of its socket — the first tear-pull she ever performed, entirely instinctive, entirely without understanding what she'd done. She had thirty seconds of held breath in her before she got the mask on.

She stayed for eleven minutes before finding her way back. She went home. She sat in her bunk for three hours staring at nothing. She wrote six pages of notes. She went back the next night.

A month later, she has:
- A reliable crossing routine (approximately 35 minutes for her, suited lightly, with practice)
- A small cache of supplies on the Astral side — food, water in sealed containers, a laptop, a dedicated Aura AI device, additional measurement equipment
- Seventeen notebooks (physical) of observations
- No idea that anyone else is crossing
- Complete scientific isolation — she has told no one, because she has no framework for how to tell anyone

When Jace and Cerberus crash through the dimensional boundary in her vicinity — disoriented, panicking, a confused dog and a man in his sleepwear stumbling into her careful research space — she is the most relieved she has been since she arrived.

Someone else. Someone real. She is not imagining this.

## The Meeting

Sophia finds them within seconds of their crossing. She has learned to navigate the Astral well enough to move quickly in low-will conditions. She reaches Jace before the insect swarm finds them in force.

Her introduction is characteristically direct. She does not ask "who are you" or "how did you get here." She says, in slightly accented English:

*"You need to move away from that position. Stay close. Don't use any active ability — it attracts the insects. I have supplies. Follow me."*

She is carrying a light thermal suit and mask. She has a spare. She was not expecting to need it, but she has it anyway. She is a physicist. She plans for contingencies.

Cerberus approaches her cautiously, then decides she is acceptable. Sophia has, without understanding the mechanism, been cultivating a relationship with the astral algae in her regular crossing spot that seems to make the insects less aggressive in that immediate area. She is not sure why. She is documenting it.

## Sophia's Scientific Framework

Sophia is approaching the Astral entirely as a physicist confronting a new set of physical laws. She does not have a magical tradition. She does not think in terms of Will, Templates, or any of the framework that galactic tradition would provide. She thinks in terms of:

- **Dimensional geometry** — she has worked out the dist² compression relationship empirically, from timing her traversals and triangulating against known stellar positions visible in the Astral. She doesn't have the terminology yet, but her numbers are right.
- **The missing dimension** — she has extensive notes on the perceptual experience of the 4th spatial dimension as expressed in the Astral. Her physics background gives her better tools than most to describe what she's observing, even though her brain can't fully process it any more than anyone else's can.
- **Will as a physical force** — she is documenting what she calls "luminosity gradients" — the brightness differentials in the Astral that she can now reliably navigate by. She doesn't know yet that she's mapping Will. She knows it has pressure and directionality and that it responds to conscious intention in ways that standard physics doesn't account for.
- **The fauna** — catalogued, observed, documented. She has named the dimensional insects "motes" in her notes (independent of whatever others will call them). She has working hypotheses about the Memory Leeches she has observed but not yet encountered directly.
- **The algae** — she has been scraping samples of the astral algae and attempting to document its growth patterns. She suspects it is responsive to something she's doing but hasn't identified the variable. When Jace burns a patch away for the first time and she observes the station crew's mood shift, she immediately connects it to her sample data. This is the moment she understands the algae is feeding on Will.

## The Baba Yaga Name

Sophia named the Astral "the Never-Never" on her first night back, in her notebook, in German.

The reference is not to J.M. Barrie. It is to a Russian expression — *za tridevyat' zemel'*, "beyond the thrice-nine lands" — and to the space beyond the deep forest where Baba Yaga lives: the place you go when you need dangerous knowledge, the liminal territory between the world of the living and what lies beyond. You go there knowing it will cost you something. You go there because nowhere else has what you need.

Sophia went there knowing it would cost her something. She went back because nowhere else has what she needs.

When Jace later adopts the term and splits it into "the Astral" and "Hyperspace" for the two regimes, she accepts the new nomenclature practically — more precise, easier to communicate — while privately keeping the original name for the whole system in her own notes. The Never-Never is what it is. The Astral is what humans can reach. The distinction feels important to her.
