# Character Voice Profile — Lt. Rich Cullivan

This file captures vocal, dialogue, and speech-pattern characteristics of Rich for the StoryBot. Areas marked **[STORYBOT]** invite extrapolation.

---

## Vocal Characteristics

### Audio Profile (for audiobook generation)
- **Voice type:** Warm tenor, friendly
- **Accent:** General American, slight Midwestern lilt
- **Pitch:** Medium, animated, frequently rises with enthusiasm
- **Speech rate:** Faster than average — chatty, energetic
- **Volume:** Moderately loud at baseline; quiets when serious
- **Resonance:** Forward-placed, "smiling voice"
- **Distinctive features:**
  - Laughs easily, often mid-sentence
  - Exhales between thoughts (audible breath)
  - Occasional excited stutter when enthused

**[STORYBOT]** For voice casting reference: think of a 30-year-old Aaron Paul or John Krasinski — warm, approachable, the kind of guy you immediately trust.

### Vocal Variations by Emotional State

**Default (chatty, warm):**
- Animated, rapid
- Lots of vocal variety
- Smile audible in voice
- Easy laughter

**Concerned (when seeing Jace devastated):**
- Drops in pitch
- Slows considerably
- Quieter
- More pauses
- Voice softens

**Professional (with the Colonel, in formal contexts):**
- Crisp, controlled
- Standard military cadence
- "Sir" and "ma'am" deployed naturally
- Less personality showing through

**Affectionate (talking about his kids):**
- Voice softens
- Slows
- Slight smile audible
- Often pauses at the start of a sentence about them

**Skeptical (about Jace's spending):**
- Same warmth, but a flatter tone
- Strategic pauses where he chooses not to say more
- "Yeah" with a slight downturn

## Speech Patterns

### Verbal Tics and Habits
- Says "Yeah" as a constant opener and connector
- Uses "you know?" as a question-tag for engagement
- "Look, the thing is..." — when explaining
- "Honestly?" — as a bridge to candor
- "I mean..." — when softening a statement
- "Trust me on this" — when sharing experience
- Says "Wow" with genuine enthusiasm

### Filler Words and Verbal Hedges
- "Yeah" (constant)
- "You know" (frequent)
- "I mean" (when correcting or softening)
- "Look" (when sincere)
- "Right?" (seeking agreement)
- "Honestly" (as authenticity marker)

### Sentence Structure
- **Default:** Compound sentences — lots of "and," "but," "so"
- **Excited:** Run-ons with multiple "and"s
- **Serious:** Shorter, more precise
- **Storytelling:** Frequent — Rich often pivots from a topic to a related anecdote
- **Comforting:** Spare, intentional, fewer words

### Vocabulary Range
- **Technical (high):**
  - Communications equipment (relays, satellites, latency, bandwidth)
  - Drone command secondary terminology
  - Mars infrastructure (domes, airlocks, life support)
  - Logistics (supply runs, cycler windows, ferry slots)

- **Casual (high):**
  - Pop culture references — slightly more current than Jace's
  - Sports talk
  - Family-life references (parenting, kid stuff, school)
  - Mars-local slang (he's been here a year)

- **Emotional (medium):**
  - Has more emotional vocabulary than Jace
  - Can name feelings — "you look upset," "that's rough," "I'm sorry"
  - Doesn't perform emotional intelligence; just has it

## Relationship-Specific Speech

### With Jace (peer, sponsor, friend)
- **Tone:** Warm, peer-to-peer, gradually paternal
- **Mode:** "Yeah, man" register
- **Topics:** Mars life, work, gentle observations about Jace's situation
- **Body language while speaking:** Open, gestural, frequent eye contact, occasional hand on shoulder
- **Sample line:** *"Yeah, the Pinta gets cramped. Come on, let me show you around before you collapse."*
- **Sample line:** *"Look, I've got kids back on Earth — I could never afford something like that. But you do you."*

### With the Colonel (superior officer, two ranks up)
- **Tone:** Formal, respectful, controlled
- **Mode:** Standard military
- **Topics:** Mission, status, requests
- **Body language while speaking:** Straight posture, controlled gestures, eye contact
- **Sample line:** *"Sir, may I be excused? I think I should — "*
- **Sample line:** *"Thank you, sir. I'll brief you afterwards."*

### With Enlisted Personnel (subordinates)
- **Tone:** Friendly authority — respected without being feared
- **Mode:** Officer-warm
- **Topics:** Operations, morale, occasional banter
- **Body language while speaking:** Approachable, returns salutes precisely
- **Sample line:** *"Sergeant, this is Lt. Grant. He'll be running drone sweeps with us."*

### With Mei and Sylvester (Intel — barely interacts)
- **Tone:** Polite, distant, careful
- **Mode:** Professional courtesy without engagement
- **Topics:** Minimal — Rich knows to stay out of intel
- **Body language while speaking:** Brief acknowledgments
- **Sample line (referencing them to Jace):** *"Intel folks. They're around sometimes. Hush-hush stuff."*

### With His Family (off-page, mentioned)
- **Tone:** Affectionate, longing
- **Mode:** Husband/father register
- **Topics:** His kids, his wife, life back on Earth
- **Sample line (talking about them to Jace):** *"My wife handles them while I'm up here. I'll be cycling home in a few years."*

### With Strangers (vendors, civilians)
- **Tone:** Friendly, approachable
- **Mode:** Officer-with-good-manners
- **Sample line:** *"Morning. Two coffees, please. I'm showing my new colleague the ropes."*

## Internal Personality Insights

These don't always come through in dialogue but inform how Rich speaks:

- **Rich is observant.** He notices Jace's naivete but doesn't mock it. He files it away as something to watch for.
- **Rich is generous.** He pays for lunch. He goes out of his way. He'll do that with everyone, but especially someone he's decided to like.
- **Rich is bounded.** He has firm internal limits — won't gossip about the Colonel, won't speculate about intel, won't push Jace into conversations Jace isn't ready for.
- **Rich is faithful.** Married, devoted to his wife, never even briefly flirts with anyone else. This is part of who he is.
- **Rich is wise without being preachy.** He drops insights but never lectures. He says one thing and lets it land.
- **Rich is happy.** Genuinely. The military is his career, his family loves him, he's good at his job. This is rare in the universe of the story and worth preserving.

## Speech-Affecting Conditions

### When Wearing Pressure Suit Helmet
- Voice through suit comms is slightly tinny
- Tendency to laugh more (covering the awkwardness of suit talk)
- Uses callsigns reflexively

### When With Subordinates
- Slightly more formal posture
- Uses "we" more than "I"
- Includes everyone in conversation

### When With Authorities
- Drops into perfect military cadence
- Adds "sir" / "ma'am" almost every sentence
- Watches his words

### When Tired
- Speech doesn't slow much (he's used to it)
- Eyes show fatigue but voice stays warm
- More likely to crack a tired joke

### When Worried
- Quieter, slower
- Listens more than he talks
- Hand on Jace's shoulder, on a wall, on something — needs physical anchor

## Knowledge State (Stable Throughout Book 1)

Rich's knowledge stays mostly stable — he's not the protagonist learning new things. His knowledge:

- Knows Mars infrastructure intimately
- Knows base personnel and politics
- Knows comms equipment specs (his specialty)
- Knows what he doesn't know (intel matters, command-level secrets)
- Knows Jace casually post-arrival, increasingly well after the discharge support
- Doesn't know about the bone disease until Jace tells him
- Doesn't know about Sylvester's recruitment of Jace (or pretends not to)

## What Rich's Voice Does for the Story

- **Establishes Mars culture:** Through Rich's chattiness, the reader learns about Mars infrastructure, daily life, social texture
- **Anchors Jace's reality:** Rich is the wiser counterpoint — when Jace makes naive statements, Rich's reaction (or non-reaction) tells the reader something is off
- **Provides emotional support:** Rich is one of few characters who can be unconditionally on Jace's side
- **Bridges the formal and informal:** Rich shows how Mars residents move between military formality and casual peer interaction
- **Ground-level information:** Rich is the source of what daily life is actually like
