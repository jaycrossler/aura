# Dawn and Chris Grant

## Summary

Dawn and Chris Grant are Jace's parents — retired, living in Virginia, and genuinely proud of the son who made it to Mars. They are loving without being naive, supportive without being blind to reality. When Jace's military career collapses, they offer everything they have, including the house. Jace refuses. This refusal is one of the first moments he chooses his own path over the family's instinct to catch him.

They appear primarily in video call scenes — increasingly delayed as Jace moves further from Earth, eventually cut off entirely by the communications blackout. Their loss of contact is not dramatic. It is slow, then sudden.

## Dawn Grant

Background TBD. Likely had a career in education, healthcare, or public service — something that put her in daily contact with people and gave her a practical emotional intelligence. She is the one who noticed Jace's tone in the call after his discharge, even through the communication lag, and said "you sound like your father when he's decided something." She is right.

She does not push. She asks one good question per conversation and waits.

## Chris Grant

Background TBD. Possibly former military, possibly engineering or trades background — something that gave Jace his affinity for systems. He is proud in the quiet way of fathers who don't know how to say so directly. When Jace calls after the discharge, Chris is the one who says "well, let's figure this out" before anyone mentions selling the house.

He is practical, not sentimental — except about his children, where the sentimentality is total and well-disguised.

## The House

The family home in Virginia is a recurring detail. It is not large or particularly remarkable, but it is paid for and it is theirs. Offering to sell it is the clearest expression of what Jace's parents would sacrifice for him. He cannot let them do it. This is character-defining.

By the time the communications blackout cuts his connection to them entirely, Jace has not fully processed how much he will miss even the lagged calls.

## Story Function

Dawn and Chris serve primarily as emotional ballast. They are what Jace is protecting in the abstract — the people back home who cannot follow, who will age and change without him present, who represent a life he chose to leave and cannot easily return to. The communications blackout makes this concrete: one day the calls simply stop getting through.
