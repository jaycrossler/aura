# Erin

## Summary

Erin runs the second transit inn at Fortuna Station — ten rooms for new arrivals
waiting on permanent pod assignments. She is red-haired. Her inn is always in the
middle of something. This is not a management failure; it is the natural state of
a place run by someone who finds chaos genuinely interesting rather than tiring.

## The Inn

Ten rooms, same as the other transit inn. The difference is atmosphere. Something
is always happening — a dispute, an improvised repair, a social event that started
as one thing and became another. Erin mediates, facilitates, or occasionally
accelerates these situations with equal energy. Outcomes are generally fine and
rarely logical.

New arrivals who don't know Fortuna yet sometimes want to stay there because it
looks lively. Residents who know Fortuna generally advise against it, with affection.

## Kael's Two Weeks (NEW — Arc 2 material)

At the start of Arc 2, once the station starts reconstructing Kael's actual
timeline via station footage, it comes out that she talked her way into a room
at Erin's inn almost two weeks before the swarm attack — beat up, exhausted,
starving, and not smelling great. Erin genuinely doesn't fully remember how she
was talked into it; her best account is that she remembers being charmed and
somewhat steamrolled, which, for Erin, barely registers as unusual. This is
played for comedy rather than suspicion — Erin is not complicit or careless, she
is simply operating at her normal baseline of chaos tolerance, and a starving,
haggard, non-English-speaking guest who pays somehow and keeps to her room for
several days sick with what looks like a cold-then-flu doesn't clear her bar for
"suspicious."

See `arc_02_addendum_kael_two_weeks.md` for the full proposed scene.

## Physical Appearance

Red-haired. (The other innkeeper also has red hair. This has not been explained and
may not need to be.)

## Manner

Genuinely engaged. Whatever is happening in front of her, she is in it. She addresses
situations with the energy of someone who considers them interesting rather than
problems to be solved. Outcomes tend to resolve in ways that satisfy everyone while
making no obvious logical sense.
