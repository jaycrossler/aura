# Hayes

## Summary

Hayes is an old man who sits at the small table outside Erin's inn every morning
with coffee and a biscuit. He has been at Fortuna Station since before Ring Two
existed. He watched the rings go up and can tell you, in the voice of the structural
alarm that fired when they lost a spar on Ring Three's first assembly attempt, exactly
what that sounded like.

He has a cat named Josephine. He says this in a way that communicates, without
stating, that Josephine is no longer alive. He says her name every morning, the same
way, with the same weight — not grief performance, just the habit of a man who has
not found a reason to stop saying it.

He is not a harmless old man. He is a man who has been noticing things for a long
time and has been very careful not to say so to anyone.

---

## Physical Appearance

Ethnically indeterminate — the belt has been sorting people for seventy years and
Hayes has the face of someone whose grandparents came from several directions. Compact
frame. The specific leanness of a person who spent decades doing hard physical work
and has not entirely lost the shape of it. Age is difficult to place: could be sixty,
could be seventy-five. His hands are the most informative thing about him — the
knuckles are large, the fingers are scarred in the particular way that suggests
a long relationship with equipment that sometimes pushed back.

After the medbay incident: his left arm is gone from the shoulder. He does not make
more of this than he has to. He learns to give Cerberus her corner of biscuit
left-handed, and that is the whole adjustment he performs in public.

---

## Voice

Hayes's defining characteristic, before and after the accident, is his voice.

He uses it as an instrument. He can make it sound like:
- A drill press approaching a seam change
- A woman in a specific kind of argument (the kind where she has been right for
  forty-five minutes and the other person is beginning to understand this)
- The mechanical shriek of a drill bit contacting unexpected rock composition
- The particular hollow boom of a structural spar settling into its mount
- The specific timbre of the Ring Three structural alarm — the one that fired on
  the day they lost a spar and Petrov died

He uses this constantly, mid-story, without announcing it. He does not say "and then
the alarm went off." He becomes the alarm for three seconds. Then he becomes himself
again and continues the sentence.

Jace finds this impressive. Cerberus finds it fascinating. The inn's other residents
have heard these stories enough times that they stop hearing the voices.

## Story Role

### The Daily Ritual

Jace encounters Hayes every morning from A-01 forward. The ritual:
1. Hayes is already at the table when Jace comes out.
2. Hayes feeds Cerberus the corner of his biscuit before Jace says anything.
   Cerberus accepts this as if it has been the established order of the world.
3. Hayes tells a story. The stories are about Fortuna's construction — the real
   history, told by someone who was there, not the plaque-version.
4. Jace listens with half his attention. He thinks he is absorbing color. He is
   actually learning the station's physical structure from the inside — chokepoints,
   old decisions, corridors that don't make sense until you know what they replaced.

### What Cerberus Glow Means

Hayes says, once, that Cerberus glows. Not as a metaphor. As an observation: "Your
dog glows sometimes. In the morning especially." He says this with the matter-of-fact
tone of someone reporting a measurement.

Jace thinks he is being poetic. He says something noncommittal. Hayes does not press
it. He gives Cerberus her biscuit corner.

Hayes has been noticing glows for years. He noticed his own hands, sometimes, in
certain lights. He never told anyone. He is old enough that he knows what people
do when an old man starts saying he sees things.

### The Wrongness — Medbay Beat

Approximately at the X-04/X-05 junction of the arc, Hayes does not appear at his
table. Cerberus sits down in front of his empty chair and looks at Jace.

{Alex} reports that Hayes is in the medical bay. This information is delivered
cheerfully and accompanied by an offer to purchase a premium care-package for
hospital visitors (discounted for regular customers).

Lanchee's account (delivered directly, because Jace asks directly): Hayes is
unconscious. Stable. His left arm — from the shoulder — is gone. Not amputated.
*Pulped* is the word Lanchee uses. She says it carefully, the way she has been
saying it to herself since she first looked at the wound.

The tissue is structurally intact but the arrangement is wrong. The inside of the
arm is outside. Not turned — inverted, precisely, as if the geometry of the limb
briefly existed in a space where inside and outside were different coordinates and
then snapped back with the contents in the wrong position.

Lanchee has filed the wound as "traumatic crush injury, mechanism unknown." She
has not filed it as what it looks like. She is not sure what it looks like.

What happened: Hayes was in his pod, alone, attempting something he had been
attempting for months in private. He had been noticing his hands for years. He had
been trying, slowly, carefully, to do the thing he could feel was possible. He was
not careful enough. He nearly died. He would have died from blood loss in his pod if
the automated med-alert had not triggered on his biometrics.

None of this is in the report.

### Recovery

Hayes is released from the medbay weeks later. He comes back to his table. He is
quieter. One-armed. The stories are the same stories but he tells fewer of them at
once.

On the morning he returns, Cerberus puts her head on his knee. He gives her the
corner of a biscuit with his remaining hand. He doesn't say anything for a while.

He eventually says that he misses Josephine.

He does not say anything about his arm. Jace does not ask.

## Knowledge Encoded in His Stories

Hayes's morning stories are, cumulatively, a construction history of Fortuna
Station. The things Jace absorbs without realizing it:

- Ring One was the whole station once. The geometry was simpler. The decisions made
  then constrain everything built since.
- The Fortuna landing zone dust-clearing took three weeks longer than planned because
  the mitigation drones kept losing orientation in the irregular gravity. They did it
  by hand at the end. Hayes was one of the people who did it by hand.
- A man named Petrov died getting a structural spar into position during Ring Three's
  first assembly. They named a corridor after him for six months and then it got
  renumbered in a reorganization. It's 6-West now. Hayes says this without inflection.
  The inflection is in the silence after.
- The station has soft spots — not structural failures, just places where the asteroid
  substrate has voids the scans didn't catch. Hayes knows where several of them are.
  He says "oh, and watch yourself in the 7-junction, there's a spot that sounds wrong
  when you tap it" in the same tone he uses for everything else.

The 7-junction void information pays off during the swarm.

---

## Relationship to Jace

Hayes asks Jace nothing personal and volunteers nothing about himself except through
stories. He is one of the few people on the station who treats Jace with
uncomplicated friendliness through the arc's decline phase — not because he doesn't
notice the changes, but because he is old enough to know what he doesn't know.

After the accident, when Jace comes to the medbay: Hayes is unconscious. Jace sits
for a few minutes and then leaves. He does not know what else to do.

This is, quietly, the moment when Jace begins to understand that there are people
on the station who could get hurt by what he and Sophia are exploring — not from
the fauna, not from the swarm, but from the thing itself. The Astral rewards
blundering in ways that are also punishments.
