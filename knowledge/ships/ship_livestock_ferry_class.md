# Livestock-Primary Ferry (Class Overview)

## Current Status

There is currently **one operational vessel** in this category: the *Falcon*. She is not a production class ship. She is a 30-year-old one-of-a-kind Omphalos-configuration vessel that has been continuously modified, patched, and extended since her original launch.

Newer Omphalos-class designs exist on paper and in prototype stages. None are operational. Until a successor vessel is commissioned, the *Falcon* is the sole means of moving large quantities of breeding livestock and agricultural cargo to the asteroid belt.

## The Omphalos Configuration

The *Falcon* uses what engineers call an Omphalos configuration — colloquially "the Merry-Go-Round." Key features:

- **100-meter rotating outer cylinder** providing 0.3g via centrifugal force
- **Stationary central core** (the Candle) housing propulsion and sensitive equipment
- **Conical forward debris shield** of layered fiber composite
- **Fluid shielding and dampening system** — thousands of tons of liquid lining the hull, managed by localized AI to maintain spin balance and provide radiation shielding
- **External modular cargo pod system** — hundreds of 6m³ pods studded along the outer hull
- **Exterior drone maintenance swarm** — continuous hull repair

## Why This Configuration

Moving live animals through deep space requires:
- Sustained artificial gravity (0.3g is the proven minimum for most livestock)
- Radiation shielding beyond standard hull armor (animals are more radiation-sensitive than electronics)
- Dynamic mass balancing (animals move; ships must compensate)
- Large enclosed volume for species-isolated enclosures, feed storage, and waste management

The Omphalos configuration addresses all of these. The fluid system handles both radiation and balance in a single integrated (if temperamental) solution.

## Newer Designs

Next-generation Omphalos-class vessels are in development at Mars shipyards. Design goals include:
- Modular fluid system with cleaner pipe routing (the *Falcon*'s 30 years of add-on plumbing is a known failure risk)
- Larger diameter (proposed 150m) for increased cargo capacity
- Improved majordomo integration across all ship systems
- Reduced crew requirement through better automation

None are commissioned. The belt's agricultural supply chain remains entirely dependent on the *Falcon* until they are.
