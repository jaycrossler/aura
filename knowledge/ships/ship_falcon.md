# Falcon ("Barnyard 1" / "The Chicken Coop")

## Summary

The *Falcon* is a nuclear livestock-primary ferry running the Mars-to-Fortuna-Station corridor. She is the vessel that carries Jace Grant, Mei, and approximately twenty-eight settlers on the four-and-a-half month transit to the asteroid belt. She is not glamorous. She smells like animals within the first week. Her crew refer to her with affectionate contempt. By the end of the transit, most passengers have a complicated love for her that they will not admit.

Her official name is *Falcon*. Her unofficial name — *Barnyard 1* or simply the Chicken Coop — predates this particular voyage; it is a class nickname for livestock-primary ferries generally, applied specifically to this one by crew and eventually adopted by Fortuna Station, which begins calling incoming animal shipments "the Barnyard run."

## The Live Feed System

The *Falcon*'s livestock canisters are equipped with observation cameras originally intended for veterinary monitoring — allowing handlers to check on animals without entering the canister for every inspection. At some point on a prior voyage (or this one, the specific origin is TBD), someone routed those feeds to the common area screens.

The result: pig-cam, goat-cam, cattle-cam, and poultry-cam running continuously in the mess and common areas. The animals become incidental entertainment. Passengers check them first thing in the morning. The pigs, in particular, develop followings.

When the *Falcon* arrives at Fortuna and the communications blackout is either imposed or tightening, the first thing the station does is request a live feed patch to the station's own screens. Watching the new animals settling in — uncertain, exploring, occasionally doing something funny — becomes prime entertainment in an information-starved environment. The pig-cams outlast most other media for months.

## The Four-Month World

The *Falcon* is a closed world for the transit. Seven crew, twenty-eight settlers, several hundred animals. Everyone learns everyone's business within three weeks. The settlers form social structures. The crew have already formed theirs. The animals have their own social dynamics visible on camera.

Jace's experience of the *Falcon*: first weeks are overwhelming — learning the animal routines, learning the ship's systems, trying to figure out how to function in civilian context after the structure of the Air Force. Middle weeks are the settling-in, where he starts to become genuinely competent and genuinely bonded with specific animals. Late weeks are when the anomalies start.

Mei's experience of the *Falcon*: she is listed as a settler, but she is working the whole time — observing, developing her cover, monitoring Jace's adjustment, communicating with Sylvester during the communication windows before the blackout tightens. She is also, quietly, watching the animals behave in ways she cannot explain.

## The Anomalies Begin

By the second month of transit, the *Falcon*'s Aura instance has logged fourteen "unmodelable sensor events." The captain is aware. The engineer has checked every system twice. The events are real but not catastrophic — readings that don't correlate with physical state, brief disagreements between instruments. The livestock anomalies are stranger: animals responding to stimuli that don't exist on any sensor. The senior animal handler notices and says nothing publicly. Jace notices the senior handler noticing.

By the third month, the anomalies are occurring daily. The *Falcon*'s Aura has started flagging a new category in its internal logs: "resonance interference, confidence: low." No one has asked it what it means by that yet.
