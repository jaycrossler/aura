# Santa María

## Summary

The *Santa María* is the youngest, largest, and most modern of the three cyclers. She entered service in the 2090s and reflects three decades of design improvement over the original *Niña*. She is the preferred ride for corporate executives, government delegations, and those who can pay for premium passage. Standard colonist passage on *Santa María* is also better than on her sisters — more comfortable cabins, larger common spaces, more recent food and entertainment options.

## Design Philosophy

Where *Niña* prioritizes endurance and *Pinta* prioritizes operational efficiency, *Santa María* prioritizes integration and experience. Her systems are more thoroughly modeled and coordinated. Her Aura integration is the most advanced — passengers and crew can run their personal Auras with deep ship-system access not available on the older vessels. Her medical bay is closer to a small hospital. Her research facilities allow corporate clients to run experiments during the long transit stretches.

She is also the most expensive to operate. Her efficiency advantages do not fully offset her higher operating cost.

## History

(*Outline.*) Commissioned in the 2090s. No refit yet at story start. Several notable corporate and political figures have ridden her; she is the cycler most likely to appear in news photographs.

## Crew Culture

*Santa María* crew are professional and slightly polished. They lack *Niña*'s deep traditions and *Pinta*'s pragmatic earthiness. Crew turnover is higher; many crew use *Santa María* as a stepping stone to other careers rather than a lifelong vocation. The captain (TBD) is competent and politic, with strong relationships in both Earth and Mars governments.

## Capabilities and Limitations

- **Strengths:** Most modern systems, best passenger experience, largest capacity, most flexibility for unusual missions.
- **Weaknesses:** Higher operating cost, less crew continuity, some passengers and crew find her "corporate" feeling.
- **Edge cases:** A crisis requiring deep cycler-tradition knowledge would find *Santa María* less prepared than her sisters.

## Voice and Personality

The shipboard AI (name TBD) is the most polished of the three — articulate, accommodating, and noticeably more present than her sisters'. Some crew find this charming; others find it slightly artificial.
