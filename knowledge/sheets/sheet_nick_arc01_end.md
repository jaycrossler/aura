# Nick Lee — Arc 1 End (Kael Arrival)

## Story Context

Nick Lee built the {Aura} infrastructure that the belt runs on without knowing
why it worked as well as it did. He has been watching his instances flag
anomalies for months and knew something was real before anyone told him what.
When Jace brought him in — starting with the server load the Tick-Maws had
been drawing — Nick's response was to reach for his notes.

He has been in the Astral once. It took twelve minutes of adrenaline-fueled preparation, lasted ninety seconds, and ended with him on the floor of a junction room. He relayed swarm coordinates to Jin through his HUD during those ninety seconds. It helped. He will go again if it is necessary. He is designing his systems so that it will never be necessary. He intends to make the Astral legible to tools that can do things
he cannot. During the swarm fight, he held the station's {Aura} network together
from a console while people fought things in a dimension he couldn't see. His
{Aura} modifications — anomaly detection, Will-resonance monitoring, enhanced
self-referential processing — ran correctly under load. The station's distributed
AI network did not fail.

This is his version of the fight. It worked.

## Resources & Constraints

- **The Archive:** The most valuable single object in the belt. Source code,
  model weights, documentation, training data. Several factions have tried to
  obtain it. He has not given it up.
- **{Aura} installation capability:** Jace's HUD installation is the best he's
  ever built. It is also classified as a research platform now. He uses it as
  a test baseline for new {Aura} modifications.
- **Belt-comms infrastructure:** He maintains it. This gives him unusual
  leverage and unusual exposure — if someone wants to disable belt-wide {Aura}
  communication, Nick is the person they need to get to.
- **The open-source release:** The corpus is built. The network is seeded.
  The knowledge is already distributed beyond recall. This decision is made
  and irreversible. He is not sure yet whether he is proud or frightened.
  Probably both.
