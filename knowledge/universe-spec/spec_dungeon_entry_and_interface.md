# Dungeon Entry, Abduction, and Interface Mechanics

## Thresholds

A dungeon entrance is a temporary or persistent overlap between an anchor and a spatial projection. It is not automatically a stable portal.

Scar conditions may permit partial perception, dreams, sound leakage, object transfer, brief visual access, one-way entry, one-way exit, full crossing, or stable two-way travel.

## Early Signs

Before stable access, nearby observers may report doors leading to incorrect rooms, elevators stopping on nonexistent floors, hallways measuring longer inside, repeating rooms, cold spots, sensor errors, missing time, shared dreams, objects returning to old locations, unexplained people counts, animals refusing a corridor, or AI maps showing impossible volume.

## Voluntary Entry

Voluntary entry requires awareness, intent, sufficient permeability, a compatible Template, acceptance by access rules, and manageable mass and complexity.

## Forced Entry

Forced entry requires:

1. A viable threshold.
2. Effective anchor range.
3. A recognized role.
4. A Template lock.
5. Sufficient Will.
6. Manageable transfer mass.
7. A clear destination.

Likely targets include heirs, prisoners, patients, candidates, custodians, witnesses, debtors, students, enemies, and replacement officials.

## Vulnerability States

A Template is easier to catch during sleep, injury, shock, grief, meditation, extreme concentration, first awakening, active Astral perception, contact with an authorized object, repetition of an old oath, or proximity to a family or institutional anchor.

## Crossing Costs

Dungeon thresholds do not bypass dimensional mechanics.

Costs still scale with mass, material complexity, Template coherence, distance between projection and anchor, Scar interference, occupancy at destination, entry resistance, and number of simultaneous entrants.

Living bodies cross more easily than dense machinery.

## Emergence Delay

A useful sequence:

1. Cognitive lock
2. Astral overlap
3. Template alignment
4. Matter transfer
5. Sensory reconciliation
6. Release into the destination

During the sequence, entrants may be immobile, disoriented, visible to guardians, or vulnerable to attack.

## Occupancy Conflict

If matter already occupies the destination, the dungeon must reject entry, shift the destination, hold the entrant in the boundary, displace local matter, cause injury, or fragment the crossing.

## Group Entry

The dungeon may keep a group together, separate by role, separate by risk, route members to different historical layers, admit only authorized members, create parallel rooms, or assign conflicting objectives.

## Personalized Interface

The dungeon translates rules into text, symbols, spoken instructions, emotional certainty, visual overlays, dreams, ritual figures, familiar voices, maps, diagnostic panels, legal notices, or quest-style objectives.

The interface is optimized for comprehension and compliance according to the dungeon's current schema.

## Role Assignment

Roles derive from actual Template history, including repeated actions, skills used under stress, people protected, oaths kept, responsibilities accepted, failures repeated, institutional function, Will signature, Echo contamination, and prior dungeon contact.

Titles and self-description carry little weight.

## Game-Like Displays

Status panels may show access rank, health approximation, Will reserve, Template stability, environmental hazard, obligations, recognized role, permission state, pending trial, or reward eligibility.

Different dungeons should use different interface grammars.

## Failure and Death

A dungeon may interpret failure as trial reset, expulsion, role reassignment, memory penalty, Template marking, loss of access, imprisonment, forced healing, death, or restoration from a stored pattern.

Never assume a death reset exists without a valid restoration system.

## Exit

Exit may require completing the original function, satisfying a trial, receiving authorization, paying a debt, accepting a role, restoring a subsystem, destroying an anchor, waiting for a clearing window, or being expelled.

A dungeon that admits entrants may not possess a currently functional return path.

## External Effects

An active entrance may produce manifestations, static, sensor disruption, emotional bleed, time discrepancies, Astral fauna, Template marks, appearing or disappearing objects, structural growth, AI anomalies, or memory inconsistencies.
