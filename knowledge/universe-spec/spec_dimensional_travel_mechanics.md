# Dimensional Travel — Complete Mechanics Reference

This document is the authoritative single-source summary of how dimensional travel
works in *The Aura Chronicles*. It consolidates and updates content from multiple
prior spec files. Where this document conflicts with older files, this document is
correct. Flag discrepancies rather than silently resolving them.

---

## 1. The Three Layers

Reality has three spatially distinct layers. Most humans at story start know only one.

### Physical Space (Kata Zero)

Normal three-dimensional reality. No distance compression. No dimensional hazards.
The baseline.

### The Astral (dist² layer — 4th dimension)

The fourth-dimensional layer overlapping physical space within gravity wells. Distance
compresses at approximately **distance-squared** relative to physical space.

- Accessible anywhere within a significant gravity well (for the Sun: roughly out to
  the outer asteroid belt, ~3–4 AU)
- Temperature near Fortuna: approximately **-20°C** — cold but survivable, not vacuum
- Contains fauna, dimensional algae, and Scar-blighted terrain in varying degrees
- Fortuna → Métis: ~80 million km physical; **~9,000 km Astral**

### Hyperspace (dist³ layer — 5th dimension)

The fifth-dimensional layer, accessible only **beyond** gravity wells (roughly outer
asteroid belt and beyond). Distance compresses at **distance-cubed**.

- Accessible: exit the gravity well first, then shift deeper
- The shift is perceptible — landscape changes, compression deepens, gravitational
  anchor disappears. Practitioners describe it as stepping off a cliff into open sea.
- Dramatically more dangerous than the Astral: worse fauna, more extreme geometry,
  higher atomic tension over distance
- Fortuna → Métis: ~80 million km physical; **~450 km Hyperspace**
- Kael's hoverboard lives permanently in Hyperspace — pulling it to the Astral would
  take many hours of Will expenditure

**Distance table:**

| Journey                         | Physical       | Astral (dist²) | Hyperspace (dist³) |
|---------------------------------|----------------|----------------|--------------------|
| Fortuna → Métis                 | ~80 million km | ~9,000 km      | ~450 km            |
| Sun → outer planets             | ~30–50 AU      | ~1 month foot  | — (gravity well)   |
| Solar system → Proxima Centauri | 4.2 ly         | —              | ~1 week foot       |
| Solar system → Scar boundary    | ~800+ ly       | —              | ~1 month foot      |

## 2. What the Layers Are Made Of — Stellar Efflux and the Substrate

The Astral and Hyperspace are not empty spaces. They have a **substrate** — a
dimensional medium with physical properties that shape everything that happens in them.

### Stellar Efflux

Stars are trans-dimensional engines. Fusion cores, magnetospheres, and stellar winds
operate across all dimensional layers simultaneously. The energy a star releases into
physical space is matched by a corresponding injection of **stellar efflux** into the
higher layers — processed, ordered dimensional material that integrates into the
substrate.

Stellar efflux is the source of:
- The ambient illumination of healthy dimensional terrain (the faint glow of the
  wider Never-Never is accumulated efflux from billions of years of stellar output)
- The warm directional background that experienced navigators use as an orientation
  reference (the solar efflux gradient is detectable throughout the asteroid belt Astral)
- The substrate topology of Hyperspace (stellar masses press upward through the
  dimensional layers, shaping the terrain; stellar efflux fills and enriches the
  substrate around them)

**Prior supernovae as feedstock:** The resonance flux that supernovae release into the
dimensional layers is not waste — it is feedstock for the stellar exchange cycle.
Surrounding stars ingest it, process it, and inject it back as efflux. The light
filling healthy dimensional terrain across the galaxy is, in the deepest sense, the
processed output of every supernova that has ever occurred. This is the normal galactic
ecosystem. The Scar was catastrophic not because the resonance flood is inherently
destructive, but because the accompanying shockwave destroyed the civilizational
infrastructure needed to navigate the flood period. See: `universe-spec/cosmology.md`.

### Black Holes as Deep Injectors

Black holes ingest matter from physical space and inject the processed output almost
entirely into the higher dimensional layers. Their physical-space signatures (Hawking
radiation, accretion disk emissions) are the fraction that leaks back. The bulk goes
into the Astral and Hyperspace as a concentrated, continuous efflux stream — qualitatively
denser and more energetic than stellar output.

**Dimensional brightness:** A stellar-mass black hole is vastly brighter in the
dimensional layers than in physical space relative to surrounding stars. Supermassive
black holes at galactic cores are dimensional weather systems — their efflux streams
create currents, terrain features, and navigational hazards at galactic scales.

**Navigation:** The galactic core's supermassive black hole produces the strongest
dimensional currents in this region. Experienced Hyperspace navigators feel it as a
directional tide. Pre-Scar road networks were partly engineered around these currents,
using them the way sailors used trade winds.

**In the Scar:** Black holes within the Scar's affected volume continued their efflux
injection throughout the three thousand years of the resonance flood. Their directed
streams cut persistent channels through the mist — the most stable thin patches in the
Scar. Kael reads and follows these channels without fully understanding their source.

### Trace Will from Non-Conscious Sources

Will is generated by any system processing information against entropy — consciousness
is the densest form, but not the only form.

| Source               | Will output (relative)         | Notes                                      |
|----------------------|--------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Human practitioner   | 1.0 (baseline)                 | Dense, directed, emotionally variable      |
| AI instance ({Aura}) | ~1/1000 per instance           | Scalable, continuous, architecturally cool |
| Living ecosystem     | Trace (aggregate meaningful)   | Metabolism counts; forests generate Will   |
| Star (fusion/magneto)| Tiny per unit, vast in total   | Primary source of substrate illumination   |
| Gas giant (Jupiter)  | Significant secondary source   | Clears local Astral faster than expected   |
| Dead rock/asteroid   | Near-zero                      | Slow geochemistry; effectively invisible   |

**Practical navigation consequence:** A practitioner can always orient by:
- Earth's Will gradient: strong, directional, unmistakable — the lighthouse
- The Sun's efflux warmth: faint omnipresent background, like ambient light
- Jupiter: subtle presence, detectable with attention
- Dead asteroids: invisible in Will terms, but their mass creates substrate topology

## 3. Directional Forces in the Layers — Gravity vs. Will Pressure vs. Substrate

The higher layers have no conventional gravity. What they have instead are three
distinct directional forces that practitioners learn to read and navigate by.

### Will Pressure Gradient (Astral primary)

In the Astral (dist²), the dominant directional force is **Will pressure** — the
dimensional weight of conscious beings generating Will in physical space, producing
detectable pressure in the Astral.

- **Earth** (billions of minds): enormous Will-pressure gradient, felt throughout the
  belt Astral. The strongest orientation reference available. Always present. Always
  directional. Practitioners learn to navigate using it the way sailors use a compass.
- **Mars** (~millions of people): much weaker gradient, perhaps 1/2000th of Earth's
  pressure from the belt.
- **Belt stations**: small local gradients; near a station, its occupants are the
  dominant local source.
- **The Sun**: the solar efflux gradient provides a faint but omnipresent warmth and
  directionality independent of conscious Will.

Will pressure is not gravity. It does not pull you down — it pushes directionally, like
a current in water. A practitioner who doesn't orient against it will drift toward the
pressure source. A practitioner who pushes deliberately against it can use it for
propulsion — effectively flying, at speeds depending on Will capacity.

**Near masses without conscious occupants** (dead asteroids, uninhabited moons): the
Will pressure gradient is negligible. Movement is effectively free-floating 3D. Faint
solar efflux provides orientation but no meaningful push.

### Substrate Topology (Hyperspace primary)

In Hyperspace (dist³), outside gravity wells, Will pressure from physical-space
conscious masses becomes negligible — too distant, too attenuated. The dominant
navigational medium is the **substrate topology** — the dimensional terrain shaped by
billions of years of stellar efflux and mass-shadow from stellar bodies.

Stellar systems appear in Hyperspace as elevated terrain — massive, stable features.
The voids between stars are valleys. The ancient roads follow the ridgelines and passes:
the optimal paths through this terrain that pre-Scar navigators mapped over millennia.

Vehicles traverse Hyperspace terrain the way boats traverse water — the substrate
provides purchase and resistance, not conventional gravity. Kael's hoverboard rides
against the substrate. Jace's engineered vehicles will eventually do the same.

**Losing frame anchoring in Hyperspace:** In the Astral, gravity wells provide a
reference frame (see Section 4). In Hyperspace, that anchor is gone. Navigators must
maintain active positional awareness using celestial mechanics — where stellar masses
are relative to current position — or become lost in featureless terrain. This is a
primary reason Hyperspace is more dangerous for the inexperienced.

### Stellar Echo (residual in both layers)

A very faint directional pull toward stellar masses exists in both layers — not
meaningful for movement, but sufficient orientation information that a traveler near
a stellar system always knows which way the star is. Not gravity. The substrate-level
shadow of a massive body's dimensional presence. Useful as a navigation cue; irrelevant
for physics calculations.

## 4. Tau — The Anchor Mechanic

**Tau** is a traveler's dimensional anchor point. It is the origin from which their
movement-squared (or cubed) distance is calculated, and the physical-space location
where they would emerge if they crossed back right now.

### The Fundamental Rule

Tau is not a stored history. There is no "entry point" that is preserved. There is only
the **current tau** — which advances as the traveler moves and snaps when movement
lapses. The origin point of a previous tau is gone once it has been superseded.

### Setting and Advancing Tau

Tau begins at the physical-space location corresponding to where the traveler entered
the dimensional layer. It advances by two mechanisms:

**Continuous drift:** As a traveler moves through the Astral or Hyperspace, their tau
slowly drifts toward their current dimensional position. Movement keeps tau behind the
traveler — there is always a gap between where they are and where their tau sits.
Crossing back while moving returns the traveler to their tau's current position, not
their leading edge.

**The Snap:** When a traveler stops exerting directed Will-driven movement — when they
cease conscious directed locomotion — tau snaps sharply forward to their current
dimensional position. The snap is physical and unmistakable: a warping sensation, a
slap of dimensional pressure across the body, slightly painful. The traveler always
knows when it happens.

After a snap, tau is fully updated. Crossing back now deposits the traveler at the
physical-space location corresponding to their current dimensional position.

**Snap trigger — directed Will, not velocity:** The snap is triggered by the lapse of
*conscious directed movement*, not merely by a drop in velocity. A practitioner sitting
still but consciously holding their dimensional position does not snap. A practitioner
who falls asleep snaps, because conscious direction of Will has lapsed. A practitioner
drifting on a moving vehicle does not snap — the vehicle's movement vector carries them.
This means:
- Deliberate rest without losing conscious hold: no snap
- Sleep: always snaps, because conscious Will cannot be maintained unconsciously
- Riding a moving vehicle while unconscious: no snap if the vehicle is moving

**Snap timers (as orientation, not hard rules):**
- **Astral:** A few minutes of lapsed directed movement before snap
- **Hyperspace:** Approximately 10 minutes — then snaps down to the Astral layer at
  current position, where Astral tau anchoring begins

Deliberate snapping is how travelers **bank progress**. Stop, accept the snap, tau
advances. Crossing back now deposits you further along your route.

### The Gravity Well Reference Frame

Gravity wells anchor the Astral's reference frame. When a traveler is inside a gravity
well's Astral zone, motion *relative to the central body* — orbital velocity, rotation —
does **not** register as movement for tau purposes. The traveler moves with the frame,
not through it.

Only deliberate traversal *through* the Astral registers as movement.

**Practical implication:** Fortuna orbits the Sun at ~17 km/s. A practitioner standing
still in the Astral near Fortuna is not moving at 17 km/s relative to the Astral
substrate. They are stationary in Fortuna's reference frame. Tau does not advance from
orbital motion. Their tau snaps to where they are standing relative to Fortuna's
neighborhood, not to some arbitrary point in the solar system.

**Nested frames:** The frame hierarchy is Sun → planet → moon/station/asteroid → ship.
Each frame is contained within the larger. A practitioner at Fortuna is in Fortuna's
frame, which is in the Sun's frame. Orbital motion at every level is transparent.

**Hyperspace loses frame anchoring:** Beyond the gravity well boundary, the reference
frame disappears. Motion, stillness, and tau all revert to absolute terms. This is one
reason Hyperspace is harder to navigate — there is no frame to orient within.

### Boarding Rotating Structures

A rotating station presents a frame-matching challenge. The station's interior is
spinning; approaching from the Astral requires matching frames from the outside in:

1. Navigate to the station's *neighborhood* in the Astral (mass is easy to find)
2. Match the station's orbital frame — approach its position and snap tau there
3. Approach the **central spire / rotation axis** — the only point where the rotational
   frame delta approaches zero. Stepping onto anything off-axis means matching rotation
   speed, which costs Will and is technically difficult
4. From the axis, ride inward as the rotation carries you to the relevant section

**The spire as universal docking point:** This is a direct mechanical consequence of
rotation frame physics, not an architectural choice. Experienced designers will
discover it empirically. Less experienced practitioners bounce off the wrong frame and
drift away. Astral predators face the same challenge: a spinning ship is nearly
impossible to board anywhere except the axis. The spire is the chokepoint.

**Skilled practitioners:** Advanced navigators can perceive movement vectors directly
and jump to a matching frame without the step-by-step approach. Most cannot. Most want
the easy access point.

### Dropped Objects and Lost Equipment

Objects obey the same tau mechanics as travelers. An object's tau is carried by its
movement vector. When an object stops moving — when its directed momentum drops below
threshold — its tau snaps to wherever it stopped.

**Dropped equipment is usually lost** — not lost in the Astral, but lost in physical
space. The object is now dimensionally anchored to a specific Astral position. When
it crosses back, it emerges at whatever physical-space location corresponds to where
it stopped. This may be anywhere in the asteroid belt.

If the object was bound to a moving vector (see Section 5), it travels with that vector
indefinitely and can in principle be retrieved if the vector is known. This creates a
category of lost objects that are recoverable through skilled navigation — salvage
economics, treasure hunting, and accidental weapons delivery.

**Tethering is not optional.** Nothing floats free on serious expeditions.

## 5. Vector Anchoring and Movement

### The Movement Vector

An object's tau is carried by its **movement vector** — the aggregate motion of all
atoms and molecular structures moving together as a cohesive unit. Objects that share
strong bonds and are moving together share a single movement vector. Their tau advances
and snaps as a unit.

### Will-Anchoring Objects to Vectors

A practitioner can perceive a movement vector (with practice — it is a learned skill)
and exert Will to **bind** an object's tau to that vector. The bound object then behaves
as if it belongs to that frame — its tau advances with the vector rather than its own
stillness.

**Cost scales with vector delta:** Binding an object to a vector nearly identical to
your own costs almost nothing. Binding to a wildly different vector (latching equipment
to a passing asteroid's orbital frame while you remain stationary) costs significant
Will, proportional to the difference in velocity and direction. Binding across frame
hierarchies is expensive and requires sustained attention.

**Anchor decay:** Vector anchors require ongoing Will trickle to maintain. They are not
set-and-forget. If the practitioner runs out of Will, loses consciousness, or stops
paying attention, anchors decay and bound objects drift back toward their natural
movement state. The decay rate scales with the vector delta — anchors on nearly-matching
vectors are cheap and slow to decay; anchors on wildly different vectors are expensive
and decay quickly.

**Before sleeping:** A practitioner converts Will-anchors to physical tethers wherever
possible. Anything that cannot be physically tethered may be lost if Will lapses
mid-sleep. The pre-sleep equipment check is not optional on serious expeditions.

### Vector Merging

Two objects that come into contact and exert sustained force on each other begin merging
their movement vectors. The merge follows the **center of mass rule**: the combined
system's tau advances from the combined center of mass. The larger mass dominates, but
does not immediately erase the smaller vector.

**Merge timescales:**
- Brief contact (a strike, a momentary grab): minimal tau effect, recoverable
- Sustained contact (holding hands, grappling, being carried): vectors merge toward
  shared center of mass over the duration of contact
- Separation: vectors begin re-diverging; if the merge was brief, they may fully
  separate; if sustained, they may remain partially merged

**Vector collapse costs Will:** Vectors naturally collapse toward equilibrium over time,
consuming Will from the involved parties to resolve the merge. The greater the initial
difference between vectors, the more Will the collapse requires. Long Astral trips have
a natural Will drain from this: every contact with terrain, fauna, or other travelers
creates small vector-merge events consuming Will to resolve.

**Combat implication:** A trained practitioner who understands vector mechanics can
resist a grappler at a dimensional level — preventing tau merge by maintaining their own
vector integrity through Will. This is a learnable skill distinct from physical
fighting strength.

**Kael carrying Jace:** Kael provides sustained movement force; Jace is held against
her. Their vectors merge, Kael dominant. She navigates for both. This is physically
expensive because she is fighting the tendency for Jace's vector to reassert its
independent position whenever his grip slackens or he loses conscious coordination.

## 6. Crossing In and Out

Entering or exiting a dimensional layer requires **Will** proportional to the
dimensional resistance of everything being moved.

### The Fundamental Rule

Matter with a living **Template** — a higher-dimensional pattern of its own — crosses
with relative ease. The Template aligns with the higher layer. Inert matter without a
Template must be pushed across by brute Will.

### Crossing Cost Scale

| What                                         | Approximate cost                               | Notes                                             |
|----------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Living human, unclothed                      | ~20 min meditation                             | Floor. Template provides natural affinity.        |
| Human + light organic clothing               | ~25–30 min                                     | Organic fibers add minimal resistance.            |
| Human + standard clothing and personal items | ~45 min                                        | Metal clasps, small electronics accumulate.       |
| Full spacesuit                               | ~1 hour                                        | First major practical threshold.                  |
| Electronics-heavy pack                       | Hours                                          | Every chip and processor adds cost.               |
| Simple printed/forged metal equipment        | Hours to a day                                 | No tight interfaces — cheaper than complex parts. |
| Small powered vehicle                        | Days, multiple practitioners                   | Practical limit for solo effort.                  |
| Small scout vessel                           | ~1 week, concerted group effort                | Alloys + electronics combined.                    |
| Warship or industrial vessel                 | Effectively impossible (biological Will alone) | See: AI Will.                                     |

### Atomic Weight Rule

Crossing cost scales with **atomic weight squared**. Hydrogen (Z=1) costs almost
nothing. Aluminum (Z=13) is manageable. Iron (Z=26) is expensive. Copper (Z=29) is near
the practical ceiling for routine use. Anything heavier becomes prohibitively costly.

This is why Jace converges on hydrogen fuel and aluminum frames — dimensional economics,
not merely engineering preference.

### AI Will Exception

AI systems generate small but real Will through complex self-referential processing.
Dense AI arrays measurably reduce crossing costs for nearby objects. This is humanity's
hidden asymmetric strategic advantage — not yet understood at story start, discoverable
through careful observation.

## 7. Gravity and Physical Forces Within the Layers

### No Conventional Gravity

The Astral and Hyperspace do not have conventional gravity. Physical-space gravity is
a property of Kata Zero spacetime geometry and does not propagate directly into the
higher layers. What exists instead:

- **Will pressure gradient** (Astral): directional force from conscious mass, described
  in Section 3. Strongest near populated bodies. Enables directed movement without
  mechanical thrust.
- **Substrate topology** (Hyperspace): terrain-like surface that provides purchase for
  vehicles and travelers. Not gravity — more like buoyancy and friction combined.
- **Stellar echo**: very faint orientation cue toward stellar masses, present in both
  layers. Not mechanically significant; navigationally useful.

### Momentum Damping

Momentum in the Astral and Hyperspace bleeds off over time. A thrown object gradually
slows and stops without sustained force. A traveler who stops exerting effort coasts to
rest. This is a property of the dimensional layers — motion has a natural entropy that
physical space lacks.

**Practical consequence:** You cannot coast. Sustained travel requires sustained effort.
Vehicles operating in the Astral or Hyperspace must provide continuous thrust, not
merely impulse. This shapes all dimensional vehicle design.

## 8. Dimensional Perception

### N-1 Perception

Human brains cannot process a fourth or fifth spatial dimension. They cope by projecting
a three-dimensional representation of whatever they encounter. This is technically
accurate but systematically incomplete.

**What this produces:**
- Objects and terrain from physical space appear flat, transparent, or geometrically
  impossible — seen from the outside in cross-section simultaneously
- Parts that appear connected in 3D may not be; parts that appear separate may be
- Machinery looks like a diagram of itself more than a working object
- Experienced navigators develop crude awareness of the extra-dimensional direction —
  described as a seventh direction they can attend to but not name

### Looking Into Physical Space

From the Astral, a practitioner can see into physical-space objects at any range. An
asteroid appears as a translucent mineral map showing internal composition. A station
appears as all its floors and corridors simultaneously. Invaluable for prospecting,
navigation, and intelligence gathering.

Physical-space walls can be bypassed by navigating in the dimensional direction they
don't extend into. The wall is not penetrated; it is walked around in a direction it
doesn't have.

### Mind-Glows

Conscious beings emit Will as visible light in the dimensional layers.
- Human minds: fractal, complex, warm-colored
- Animal minds: simpler, fewer centers, less color variation
- AI systems: crystalline geometric patterns — striking, distinctive, not immediately
  recognizable as minds to observers without context
- Stars: a vast, steady warmth that experienced travelers learn to orient by
- Black holes: intensely bright in the dimensional layers, with directional streams of
  efflux visible as luminous currents

### The Density Trap

Dense elements — lead, tungsten, refined heavy metals — create visual fog and lensing
distortion in N-1 perception. Useful as shielding against Astral observation; expensive
to cross.

## 9. Atomic Tension Over Distance

Moving through a higher-dimensional layer exerts **dimensional gradient stress** on all
matter. The traveler and everything they carry is being pulled across geometries that
physical-space matter was not built for.

### In Living Beings

Experienced as a deep stretching ache that builds with distance. Starts as discomfort.
Progresses to genuine pain. At extremes, becomes incapacitating. Biological tissue is
the most resilient — Template-aligned, somewhat self-repairing. Hyperspace makes this
significantly worse.

**Dimensional Shear:** Moving too fast through compressed geometry creates
micro-hemorrhaging in biological tissue — a cellular speed limit on transit.
Experienced navigators know the safe speed range.

### In Equipment

Inorganic manufactured objects accumulate **dimensional stress** as structural damage.
The stress targets crystalline lattices, bonded interfaces, and close-tolerance parts.
This damage is permanent and cumulative in anything without a Template.

**Failure hierarchy** (what breaks first):
1. Precision electronics / chips — crystalline lattice structures. Fail earliest.
2. Precision machined metal — close-tolerance parts, threaded fasteners. Warps, seizes.
3. Simple printed or forged metal — no tight interfaces. Significantly more resilient.
4. Simple organics — rope, wood, fabric. Very resilient, no critical tolerances.
5. Living biological tissue — most resilient, continuously self-repairing toward Template.

### Template-Based Repair

Objects with a **Template** can partially or fully repair dimensional stress damage when
their Template is energized with Will. The physical object's atomic arrangement tends
back toward the Template's stable pattern.

**Emergent Template development:** Jace's HUD implant — partially organic circuits —
may develop a Template over time through extended dimensional exposure, enabling
Will-based repair. A vehicle deliberately constructed with biological or organic elements
integrated into its structure could develop a partial Template over repeated dimensional
use. This is the seed of **Titan-wrought** design philosophy (Kael's term): integrating
organic and magical elements into manufactured objects to give them Template affinity.

## 10. Long-Distance Travel — Operational Constraints

### The Sleep Problem

Sleep lapses conscious Will. Will-anchors on equipment begin to decay. The traveler
does not snap (if they are on a moving vehicle or being carried) but they lose the
ability to maintain anchors or react to changing conditions.

**If the traveler is stationary when they fall asleep:** Tau snaps. In Hyperspace, the
snap drops them to the Astral layer at their current position.

**If the traveler is moving (carried, on a vehicle):** They continue moving. Their tau
advances with the vehicle's vector. They wake up somewhere further along the route —
in worse shape from unmanaged atomic tension, with decayed Will-anchors, but not
dropped out.

The critical concern during sleep is **equipment management**: physical tethers for
everything important, Will-anchors converted to physical attachment wherever possible.

### Sustained Long-Distance Travel

Long-distance dimensional travel requires either:
- **Continuous movement by a conscious navigator** — grueling, unsustainable alone over
  multiple days
- **A team** — when one navigator sleeps, others maintain the movement vector. The
  sleeping person's tau merges with the carrier's dominant vector.

Solo long-distance travel over multiple days is essentially impossible for biological
beings without extraordinary Will support or vehicle infrastructure.

**Kael and Jace to Métis:** Kael provides movement force for two people while managing
her own atomic tension. Jace experiences atomic tension without the Will capacity to
manage it. Two days of this is genuinely brutal for both.

### Gear Discipline

Every piece of equipment that matters must be physically held, worn, or tethered.
Nothing floats free. The dimensional layer's low directional force means dropped items
float near where they stopped — but their tau snaps and they become unretrievable
without precise navigation back to their exact dimensional position.

Standard dimensional travel discipline: tether everything, check tethers before
resting, never let go of anything you cannot afford to lose.
