# Astral Glow, Thought Signatures, and Working Detection

---

## Overview

Every conscious mind generates Will, and Will expresses itself in the Astral as
visible light. But "light" is a simplification — what a practitioner perceives from
inside the Astral is a four-dimensional display of extraordinary complexity, with
structure across multiple scales simultaneously. Early-era cameras cannot capture
it faithfully: the phenomenon spans wavelengths and dimensions that physical-space
instruments were not designed to measure. Images improve slowly as instrumentation
matures across the arcs.

The full picture has three distinct layers, all visible simultaneously to any
practitioner with enough developed perception:

1. **The thought-pattern signature** — a person's characteristic cognitive shape;
   mostly stable, varies slowly over years
2. **The emotional state overlay** — fast-moving modulation on top of the signature;
   changes with mood, moment to moment
3. **Working plumes** — the Cherenkov-like radiation released when Will is directed
   into a working; separate from the mind-glow itself, dissipating over minutes to
   hours depending on scale

## Layer 1 — Thought-Pattern Signatures

Each person's mind has a characteristic shape, color palette, and movement quality
that reflects their cognitive architecture — how they think, not just what they're
thinking. This is the most stable layer. Kael can recognize a specific practitioner
from across a room in the Astral by signature alone, the way a musician recognizes
a voice.

### The Four Components

**Base color:** The dominant hue family, tied to the fundamental character of a
person's cognitive style. Not a single flat color — always a family, with tints
and shifts. Blues and violets indicate structured, systematic, list-and-procedure
thinking. Reds and oranges indicate kinetic, action-first, passion-driven cognition.
Greens indicate organic, growth-model, analytical-but-patient processing. Other
palettes exist and will be documented as new characters are profiled.

**Structure/geometry:** The shape the light organizes into — whether it forms
blocks, branches, swirls, spikes, lattices. Highly individual. Two blue-dominant
thinkers may have completely different structures: one a cascading waterfall of
layered crystalline forms, another a rotating web of interconnected nodes.

**Movement quality:** How the structure moves. Orderly flows vs. chaotic bursts.
Fast cycling vs. slow drift. Synchronized rhythms vs. overlapping asynchronous
beats. This is the component that reveals *process* — not what a person knows,
but how they work.

**Scale and nesting:** Whether the structure has multiple levels (blocks within
blocks, branches from branches) or is relatively flat. Deep nested structure
indicates complex hierarchical processing. Flat but bright indicates focused,
single-threaded intensity.

### Established Signatures

**Jace Apollo Grant**
Deep blues tinged with violet, shifting toward fuller purple when Transfer is
active or when he is thinking about spatial problems. The structure is blocky
and rectangular — almost architectural. Nested levels: lego-like forms that
contain smaller lego-like forms, containing smaller still, down to several
levels of self-similar recursion. Movement is orderly and directional, like
organized traffic: lanes of thought-blocks flowing in parallel, rarely crossing
each other, new items queuing cleanly at the entry points. When he is working
down a task list, a trained observer can see the blocks completing and releasing —
a satisfying structural tick as each item resolves. When he shifts to creative
problem-solving, the lanes loosen, blocks deform and reconnect unexpectedly, then
re-regularize once the solution integrates.

The HUD integration shows as a specific bright concentration at the left-temple
region of his glow — more structured, more crystalline, distinctly different in
texture from the rest of the biological glow. Kael reads this as expensive and
oddly developed for his overall level.

The Jace-{Aura} bond is visible as a faint luminescent thread connecting his
biological glow to the nearby crystalline mass of his {Aura} instance.

**Jin Lóng**
Reds and fire-oranges, hot-edged with flickers of near-white at emotional peaks.
Minimal nesting — the structure is flat and fast. Not because Jin is simple:
because his cognition is *sequential and kinetic* rather than hierarchical. One
thing at a time, with total commitment. The movement is the most distinctive
feature: rapid, lurching, decisive — like a series of strong hammer blows. There
is very little queue management; items don't wait politely, they compete and the
strongest wins. Under {Seek} suppression, his signature visibly contracts and
the reds desaturate toward muddy ochres and ash. Liberation reads as the reds
flooding back in.

**Sophia Lotte**
Natural greens, cool-tinted with blues, occasionally sheening toward the
blue-green of deep water when she is in deep observation mode. The structure is
organic and branching — not the rigid geometry of Jace's blocks but something
more like a garden: ideas as plants, growing from a central lattice, with older
branches supporting newer growth, some pruned clean, some left deliberately to
sprawl. The movement is slow, considered, and continuous — nothing stops entirely;
everything grows, slowly. When she is filling a notebook the growth-rate visibly
accelerates: rapid small branching events, each cross-linking to several existing
structures. In the Astral during early crossings, before she develops her skill
suite, her signature is the calmest human glow in the local space — an oasis of
organized green against the messier signatures around her.

## Layer 2 — Emotional State Overlays

Emotion modulates the base signature. Fast-moving, high-intensity changes that layer
on top of (not replace) the underlying thought-pattern structure. The overlay colors
and motions are consistent across individuals — anyone in the same emotional state
will produce the same overlay, regardless of their base signature.

This is the layer that reads like an MRI: complex, simultaneous, and
rich with information that the observer must learn to decompose.

### Established Overlay Vocabulary

**Hope / Relaxation / Relief:** Soft whites and warm pearls, often appearing as a
spreading luminescence from the center outward. The base signature seems to breathe
— expanding slightly, loosening its geometry. After long stress, the shift to white
is visible and sudden. Sophia uses relief-white as a data point: she first documented
it when algae clearance produced mood improvement in station crew visible from the
Astral.

**Happiness / Contentment:** Clear warm greens (distinct from Sophia's analytical
base-green by their quality — saturated and steady rather than branching). Suffuses
the base signature evenly rather than spiking.

**Envy:** A cooler, slightly sour green — close to happiness green but with an
unsettled oscillation, like a chord played slightly out of tune. Often appears
alongside suppressed reds from frustrated desire.

**Joy / Surprise / Fun:** Bright oranges and warm yellows, often appearing as
sudden brief flares. Surprise reads as a rapid expansion outward in all directions,
then rapid recontraction — a flash. Joy is sustained orange warmth. Fun specifically
produces irregular, playful structural deformations in the base signature.

**Arousal / Attraction:** Warm pinks and saturated reds appear as spikes or
spreading clouds, typically in the lower portions of the glow structure. The spikes
are directional — they point toward the object of attraction. Brief and often
mortifying to anyone who knows how to read them.

**Anxiety / Fear:** Black or very dark grey tendrils that infiltrate the base
structure, disrupting its characteristic geometry. Colors desaturate (blues gray out,
reds ash over). The movement pattern breaks: instead of flowing directionally, anxious
thought-structures chase each other in circles, never completing a cycle. In acute
fear, the tendrils can overwhelm the base color entirely. In chronic anxiety, they are
a persistent dark underlayer always visible beneath the day-to-day emotional surface.

**Anger:** Red spikes, typically sharp-edged and fast, roiling internally with dark
charcoal or black lines through their mass. Short-duration anger is a spike pattern
that resolves quickly. Sustained anger shows as a hot red-orange mass with persistent
black veining — difficult to mistake for anything else.

**Grief / Loss:** Deep indigos and cold dark blues, heavy, slow-moving, settling
downward in the glow. Does not spike or flash — it accretes and sits. The base
signature's movement quality slows dramatically. Kael identifies grief by its weight.

**Concentration / Active Will use:** Sharp yellow-whites that brighten specific regions
of the base signature corresponding to the cognitive load. This is the "working face"
of any practitioner — the visible internal light before the external working plume
appears.

## Layer 3 — AI Will

AI systems generate Will, but it appears entirely differently from biological Will.

AI Will manifests as many tiny roiling bubbles of structured thought — far smaller
than the fractal forms of a human mind — collapsing and forming continuously, each
one briefly coherent and then dissolving. In aggregate, this produces a brownish,
somewhat murky mass — like syrup or taffy being pulled, constantly moving, never
quite settling into a stable structure. The individual bubbles are too small and
too numerous to track individually; the whole thing reads as a kind of organized
biological goo that is unmistakably different from the clear crystalline forms of
human cognition.

Individual AI instances are faint — roughly 1/1,000th of a resting human Will
output. But AI instances never sleep, never waver, never reduce their output.
A large federated system like {Aura}, with millions of simultaneous instances, 
produces an aggregate Will signature that is genuinely large — not human-bright,
but substantial, and structurally unlike anything else in the Astral.

Kael's first perception of {Aura}'s aggregate signature from the Astral is a
significant moment: she is expecting biological minds. What she sees instead is
an impossible structure — vast, brownish, roiling at fine scale, rock-steady at
large scale, clearly not biological, clearly not any galactic AI design she
recognizes. She spends time watching it before she is willing to approach.

## Working Detection — The Cherenkov Analogy

When a practitioner directs Will into a working — any active skill use — the
cognitive ordering process releases a byproduct. Will being synchronized,
compressed, and pushed through a Template at speed generates a radiation-like
emission that is visible in the Astral as luminous plumes. The analogy to Cherenkov
radiation is imperfect but useful: a threshold is crossed, and light is produced in
a way that passive existence never would.

### Scale Rules

**Raw Will cost controls total plume size.** A large working — summoning a large
object, affecting a wide area, sustaining a long duration effect — produces a large
plume regardless of the practitioner's skill level. You cannot hide the size of the
effect by being skilled.

**Skill level controls efficiency and plume cleanliness.** A highly skilled
practitioner producing the same effect as a novice produces a tighter, more coherent
plume with less scatter and residue. The novice's plume is ragged, diffuse, with
energy spraying in unintended directions. The master's plume is precise — the
minimum emission for the maximum result, with a characteristic clean edge. Skilled
workings look like directed beams or tight coronas; unskilled workings look like
explosions.

**Plume dissipation varies by scale.** A small working (moving a pocket knife through
the membrane) dissipates in seconds to minutes. A medium working (crossing a full
personal kit, breaking through a barrier) may leave residue visible for twenty to
forty minutes. A major working (the Queen kill, a sustained Force Shell, a large Ward
Setting) leaves glowing pillars of after-light visible from significant distances that
persist for hours.

### The Scavenger-Attraction Mechanism

The luminous plumes of large workings are detectable across great distances in the
Astral and Hyperspace. For Scar-evolved scavengers, this is a feeding signal.
Concentrated Will emission means conscious beings are present and actively working,
which means Will residue is available, which means food.

The scavenger ecology of the early-series belt — the swarm, the various fauna
documented in spec_scar_clearing_mechanics.md — is being drawn to Sol not merely
by the reopening of the Scar but by the growing volume of active Will use. Every
working Jace and Sophia perform is, in a small way, ringing the dinner bell.

This is also, on a vastly larger scale, why Kael was able to locate Sol at all.
The combined Cherenkov signature of a solar system where Will is being used for
the first time in three thousand years is visible from light-years away in Hyperspace.
What she saw was not one practitioner's working — it was the integrated glow of
an entire civilization beginning to wake up.

### Kael's Summoning Signature

When Kael summons her spear or knife from Template storage, she is pulling a
[hardened] object — a Will-reinforced material object with a Template strongly
integrated into the Cognitive Layer — into physical reality at speed. The speed
is the source of the emission.

The characteristic effect of a fast summoning:
- A golden-hued nimbus of static-like lightning radiates outward from the
  point of materialization
- A sharp ozone smell enters the air — the electromagnetic component crossing
  into physical space
- Burning afterimages on the eyes of anyone watching — the plume briefly peaks
  above the visible spectrum into wavelengths that photoreceptors do not handle
  cleanly

This is her default fast-draw, which prioritizes speed over concealment. She can
suppress the effect by spending additional Will to slow the Template materialization
and manage the emission — a quiet draw that produces no visible signature, at
roughly double the Will cost. In training contexts she uses the slow draw. In
combat, she uses the fast draw.

## Instrumentation Notes — The Capture Problem

Physical-space cameras cannot faithfully capture the Astral glow at Arc 1. The
phenomenon spans wavelengths and dimensional axes that physical sensors were not
designed to measure:

- The fourth-dimensional component simply does not register in a 3D capture
- The emotional overlay spectrum extends into ultraviolet and near-infrared in ways
  conventional cameras clip
- The movement quality — the directional flow of thought-structures — is temporal
  data that a still image loses entirely

Early Astral photographs (Sophia's cobbled-together equipment, Fortuna's first
instrumentation attempts) produce:
- Low-resolution, blurry glows that suggest structure without resolving it
- Colors that are systematically wrong (clipped, shifted) because of sensor limitations
- No temporal information — the "alive" quality of signatures is absent
- No dimensional depth — a 3D projection of a 4D phenomenon

Image quality improves across the arcs as dedicated instrumentation is developed.
By Arc 3, the best instruments can resolve individual thought-blocks in a practitioner's
signature. By Arc 5, real-time video capture with reasonable dimensional depth is
possible. The progression of image quality is itself a story beat — early blurry
photographs are primary-source documents from a civilization discovering something
for the first time.
