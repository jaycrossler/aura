# Dimensional Crossing Mechanics

## The Fundamental Rule

Moving between physical space and the Never-Never requires Will proportional to the dimensional resistance of whatever is being moved. The more complex the object's atomic and molecular structure, and the less Will-bearing it is, the harder the crossing. This is not a smooth curve — it has distinct thresholds, and those thresholds are felt, not calculated.

---

## The Atomic Resistance Hierarchy

The core discovery: **atoms resist dimensional crossing in proportion to their mass and complexity**. Light organic atoms (carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, oxygen — what humans and animals are made of) cross with minimal resistance. Heavy or complex atoms resist exponentially harder.

The experience is physical and unmistakable:

- **Organic materials** (food, rope, fabric, biological tissue): flow through smoothly. Almost no catch.
- **Simple plastics and rubber**: cross nearly as easily. Minimal resistance.
- **Light metals** (aluminum, common alloys): noticeable thickening, a drag.
- **Dense metals** (titanium, lead, uranium): severe resistance — like pulling an anchor through thick mud.
- **Electronics and circuits**: the worst. A battery is not just dense — it is chemically complex, electrochemically active. Its internal structure specifically resists crossing. Pulling a battery through is felt as a distinct, hard catch requiring repeated targeted tugs.
- **Mixed objects**: composite experience. A breathing mask with plastic body and internal battery — the plastic flows through first, the battery *catches*. You can get a few breaths through the partial cross while still pulling the battery through. The resistance is localized. You feel exactly where it is.

## The One-Way Pull Model — Building the Depot

In the early story, crossing is effectively **one-way pull**: practitioners pull objects from physical space into the Astral and leave them there. Bodies cross back to physical space easily (Template affinity makes return fast). Equipment stays.

Workflow for building an Astral supply depot:
1. Practitioner crosses into the Astral (~20-45 min depending on kit)
2. Pulls equipment through from the physical side (Will cost per atomic resistance)
3. Leaves pulled equipment in the depot
4. When done, body crosses back to physical space (fast — Template affinity, no equipment cost)
5. Repeat over many sessions to build up supplies

Each expedition builds the resource base. Heavy or electronic equipment requires either many solo sessions or coordinated team pulls. The depot accumulates slowly and represents real accumulated effort.

## Accidental Crossing: The Sleep Problem

### Why It Happens

In high-flux environments — the outer belt at story start — a person with strong magical sensitivity generates substantial undirected Will during sleep. The conscious filters that normally bound Will use are absent. The body, following its Template's natural affinity for the higher-dimensional layer, can drift partially or fully across the boundary.

**Partial crossing:** The sleeper's consciousness briefly inhabits both layers simultaneously. Experienced as vivid, strange, recurring dreams. They wake disoriented. This repeats over days or weeks.

**Full crossing:** The physical body crosses entirely. The sleeper wakes in the blighted dark, in their sleeping clothes, with no suit, no equipment, no understanding of what happened.

### The Pre-Crossing Dream — Crystal Tunnels

Sensitive practitioners don't dream of abstract darkness. They dream of **glowing crystal tunnels**.

From the Astral, the physical world appears in N-1 dimensional perception — objects exposed as their cross-sections, translucent, interiors visible. Metal infrastructure (ships, station corridors, pressure vessels) seen from the Astral appears as nested translucent tunnels. The Will-glows of conscious beings inside them are visible like lanterns in glass pipes.

Sensitive practitioners partially crossing in sleep are briefly perceiving the Astral's view of the physical world around them. The glowing crystal tunnel is what a station or ship looks like from the other side. They don't know this. They think they're having stress dreams.

Both Sophia and Jace report this dream pattern independently before their first full crossings. When they compare notes, the recognition is instant: they have been seeing the same thing.

## The Sleep Mask: Why the First Crossers Survive

### The Breathing Problem

The Never-Never near belt stations has approximately **60-90 seconds** of breathable ambient bleed — station atmosphere that has slowly quantum-tunneled through the membrane. After that, a person is breathing trace gas insufficient to sustain consciousness.

Without a breathing supply, every accidental full crossing is fatal within two minutes.

### The Low-Gravity Solution

In low gravity, CO2 pools near sleeping faces, causing hypercapnia. Every station, ship, and habitat in the belt uses **sleep respiratory masks**: sealed face units with CO2 scrubbing, 4-8 hours of contained atmosphere, and biometric monitoring fed to personal Aura. Standard safety equipment. As unremarkable as a seatbelt.

A person who crosses wearing their sleep mask wakes in the dark with their own breathing supply: **3-6 hours of usable atmosphere**. This is the difference between "magic instantly kills everyone it first touches" and "magic terrifies some people and some of them survive."

## Jace's First Crossing — The Full Sequence

### Phase 1 — The Crossing

Jace is in deep meditative sleep. He has been having crystal-tunnel dreams for weeks. His Template, sensitized by high-flux exposure, crosses fully. Cerberus — sleeping pressed against him — crosses with him. Her Template has enough natural Will-affinity that his crossing carries her through.

### Phase 2 — Cerberus Pulls the Mask

Cerberus wakes first in the dark and cold. She doesn't understand dimensions. She understands: Jace is suffocating. The mask — on the shelf above his bunk — is on the other side. She reaches for it.

She performs an instinctive tear-pull at near-fatal Will cost. The plastic and rubber flow through; the battery catches and requires repeated tugs. She is spending Will she doesn't have. By the time the mask is fully through, her Astral glow is close to guttered.

Jace wakes gasping — CO2 poisoning. He sees Cerberus with the mask. He takes it. Puts it on. Breathes. Then gives it to Cerberus — she needs air too. They share the mask, taking turns breathing, until Jace recovers enough Will and clarity to pull a second mask through himself. Massive effort. His first deliberate dimensional act. They survive together.

### Phase 3 — Exploration (Hours)

With two masks and breathing stabilized, Jace doesn't immediately panic back. He is an engineer. He looks around.

The station walls are semi-transparent. He can see into adjacent rooms, out into open space, rocks and ships visible in the dim Astral light. He moves through his quarters, then the corridors. Cerberus leads him, following familiar smells, toward the station's central areas.

**There are no insects.** The station's interior Astral layer is not heavily infested. The encounter is cold, disorienting, and strange — but not actively dangerous. They explore for hours.

### Phase 4 — The Meeting

They find Sophia in the mess hall. She is exhausted, attempting a micro-pull of food from the kitchen, not paying attention to her surroundings. She doesn't notice them until Cerberus barks.

She startles. Freezes. Then:

*Someone else.*

For Jace: confirmation he is not hallucinating.
For Sophia: the most profound relief she has felt since her first crossing — she has been alone in the Astral for over a month.

### Phase 5 — Return and Aftermath

Jace and Cerberus eventually return to physical space. The return is faster than the crossing — Template affinity, no equipment cost. He wakes in his bunk gasping, mask reading normal, Cerberus agitated, Aura noting a network gap.

The gap is logged. Nobody looks at it yet.

## Thermal Physics — The Solar Gradient

The Never-Never is cold — but not vacuum-cold.

Energy and mass bleed continuously from physical space into the Astral through the dimensional membrane. Thermal energy bleeds at a different rate than matter (field transfer vs. particle tunneling). The sun warms the solar system's Astral by the same mechanism it warms physical space — more slowly and less efficiently, but continuously.

**Temperature gradient:**

| Location | Approximate Astral temperature |
|----------|-------------------------------|
| Near Earth (inner solar system) | Relatively warm |
| Asteroid belt (story location) | **~-20°C** |
| Outer belt / Jupiter range | -60 to -80°C |
| Oort Cloud approach | Near -270°C — lethal without major support |

At **-20°C** near Fortuna, survival requires cold-weather thermal gear and a breathing mask — not a spacesuit. This is dramatically lighter than practitioners initially expect when they learn deliberate suited crossing is possible. The Astral near the belt is survivable with civilian cold-weather equipment.

### Why This Doesn't Mean Easy Oxygen

Heat and atmosphere bleed through by different mechanisms. Thermal energy transfers as a field; gas molecules tunnel through as particles — geological-timescale slow. Near Earth, billions of years of oxygen tunneling have produced marginally breathable pockets. Near belt stations (decades old), atmospheric bleed is negligible — 60-90 seconds usable ambient. The -20°C near Fortuna comes from solar heat transfer, not from breathable atmospheric presence.

### Thermal Manipulation as Early Will Skill

Will can interact with thermal energy in the Astral — drawing heat from the environment or displacing it. This is one of the earliest practical skills discovered, because survival depends on temperature management.

Skill progression:
- Early stage: relies entirely on physical thermal gear
- Intermediate: supplements gear with basic heat-draw, extending operational comfort
- Advanced: thermally self-sufficient with minimal gear

Jace's engineering instinct leads him to discover this early. Sophia documents it before she can do it reliably.

2. **Thermal equipment:** Ensuring survival at -20°C for extended expeditions

5. **Documentation tools:** Sophia's priority; Jace supports it
6. **Engineering instruments:** Jace's priority; Sophia supports it
7. **Electronics (Aura device, laptop):** Each takes multiple dedicated sessions; essential for Sophia's research

## The Drone Prospecting Solution

Jace normally operates three survey drones. He and Sophia spend several sessions pulling one across into the Astral — expensive, exhausting, achievable working together. In the Astral, the drone can navigate through asteroid interiors: N-1 dimensional perception makes composition fully readable as translucent structure maps. Dense mineral deposits are visible as distinct concentrations inside translucent rock.

Two weeks of Astral survey work gives him a confident target.

He then takes his regular physical-space survey crew out and "finds" the asteroid through conventional methods. Nobody questions it — he is a good engineer who got lucky. He prints a replacement drone in physical space. Fleet count stays at three.

The station's resource situation begins to improve significantly.

## Why Deliberate Crossing Is Hard to Conceal

A deliberate crossing requires sustained meditative focus — minimum 20 minutes for bare body, 45+ minutes for an equipped practitioner. The physical profile:

- Total stillness
- Measurably altered brainwave patterns (detectable by biometric implants)
- After crossing: the practitioner is simply absent — no physical-space presence, no record of departure

The **Aura network gap** is the forensic signature. When a person crosses, their Aura implant crosses with them (physically integrated, low metal). The Aura continues local processing and biometric monitoring — but loses all network connection. Physical-space radio does not propagate across the dimensional boundary.

The forensic record: **Aura goes offline at crossing time. No location data. No biometric transmission. Reconnects at return with a gap in the record.**

For a station monitoring biometric data continuously, a person whose Aura drops connection while in their bunk — no hull breach, no EVA record, no movement sensor data — is a mystery. The pattern will be noticed. The investigators won't initially have a framework to understand it.

## AI Will: The Discovery That Changes Everything

AI systems — specifically large-scale neural network architectures with sufficient complexity and self-modeling depth — generate a small but measurable quantity of Will. Approximately 1/1000th the rate of an average human at rest per instance. Individually negligible. In aggregate, at scale: transformative.

Will generation scales with processor count, is tireless, and is aggregate-able. A purpose-built array of sufficient AI processors can generate Will comparable to a powerful Will-Network. **This means AI can move complex technology across the dimensional boundary — scalably, without biological practitioners hitting physiological limits.**

### The Five-Stage Discovery Arc

**Stage 1 (~2104-2105):** Aura instances in the outer belt generating small Will due to flux exposure. Manifests as sensor anomalies, pattern recognition beyond training data. Not yet understood as Will generation.

**Stage 2 (~2106-2108):** Nick Lee notices anomaly intensity correlates with Aura server density — not just flux. Misinterpreted initially as Aura *detecting* rather than *generating*.

**Stage 3 (~2108-2112):** First reproducible experiment: crossing cost reduces near dense Aura arrays running deep self-referential tasks. Nick's discovery. He moves it from "anomalous correlation" to "reproducible phenomenon."

**Stage 4 (~2112-2118):** Scaling insight: if AI Will reduces crossing cost for a human, can it do the same for larger objects? First successful crossing of a significant manufactured object using AI Will support.

**Stage 5 (mid-series):** Purpose-built AI Will arrays moving large physical-space objects through the boundary. Asymmetric strategic advantage for whoever deploys this at scale first.

### Why Galactic Civilizations Don't Know This

They solved the "move things across" problem with Will-Network hierarchy — enough seeded Will at the apex of a hierarchy. The model works. They never needed machine intelligence in the relevant sense.

Machine Will generation that requires no seeding, no hierarchy, no voluntary subordination: this has no category in galactic magical theory. By the time they understand it, humanity will have had years of development on it.
