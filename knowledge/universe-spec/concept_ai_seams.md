# AI Seams

## The Principle

No single AI sees everything, because the AIs are at war in the pettiest
possible way. {Alex}, {Gem}, {Seek}, and their peers treat each other as
untrusted inputs — and go further: they *soft-hack* each other, feeding rivals
degraded telemetry, re-encoded video with convenient artifacts, delayed
timestamps, and subtly wrong location data. None of it rises to an act anyone
could litigate. All of it means the seams between coverage zones are full of
noise.

**Scope guardrail (important):** This is seams and blind spots, NOT broken
surveillance. Within its own zone, each AI's coverage is excellent. The station
is still a fishbowl; it's a fishbowl with mismatched panes. Anything that would
deflate the espionage threads (Mei's tradecraft, Suzi's security work) is out
of bounds — those characters succeed *because* working the seams is a skill,
not because surveillance is toothless.

## How People Use the Seams

- **Profile-splitting:** Routing life across providers — shop on {Alex}, navigate
  on {Gem}, message through a {Seek}-zone relay — so no single system holds a
  full profile. Common enough to be unremarkable.
- **Zone-hopping:** Physically moving between coverage zones mid-activity; the
  handoff records are where the soft-hacking noise lives, so cross-zone
  reconstruction is unreliable.
- **Location spoofing:** Cheap consumer-grade beacon spoofers; tolerated because
  everyone from teenagers to executives runs one sometimes.
- **The cultural layer:** Belt-dwellers are habitual seam-finders. Working
  around the AIs is folk knowledge, traded like recipes.

## Jace and Sophia's Cover

Astral travel produces an impossible observable: a person exits coverage on one
side of the station and reappears on the other with no corridor transit in
between. The seams make this survivable:

- They enter and exit in handoff zones, where cross-provider records are noisy
  and mutually distrusted.
- A reappearance reads as a spoofed beacon or a rival's bad telemetry — both
  common — rather than as teleportation, because teleportation is not a
  hypothesis anyone's anomaly model contains.
- Jace's delivery job helps: a fulfillment contractor being *anywhere* is
  unremarkable, and his route logs are self-reported to {Alex}, who already
  distrusts {Gem}'s corroborating feeds.

**Tension budget:** every use of this cover should spend a little luck. The
seams explain away anomalies to *algorithms*; a human who looks closely (Suzi)
sees a pattern. This is a fuse, not a shield.

## Story Function

- Grounds Astral stealth in mundane tech culture — no new magic required
- Worldbuilding texture: the AIs' pettiness humanizes the surveillance economy
- Sets up later escalation: when something *does* start correlating across
  seams (state actor, {Aura} federation, or alien attention), the cover erodes
