# Dungeon Anchor Mechanics

## Core Principle

A dungeon does not occupy one ordinary place. Its Cognitive Program persists independently of any single room, but it requires anchors to interact with spatial reality.

Anchors determine where the dungeon can project entrances, who it recognizes, which populations feed it Will, and how it maintains continuity across time.

## Anchor Classes

### Territorial Anchors

Territorial anchors bind the dungeon to a city, harbor, palace district, mountain, river crossing, battlefield, temple complex, political boundary, or major road junction.

They are usually stable but may shift locally as cities move, rivers change, buildings collapse, or political centers migrate. The central question is not exact coordinates. It is what the dungeon recognizes as the continuing place.

### Dynastic Anchors

Dynastic anchors bind the dungeon to a family, House, bloodline, household, inherited title, or Will-seeding lineage.

They may follow genetic descent, legal inheritance, adoption, marriage, household identity, a dynastic relic, a recurring oath, or whoever carries the strongest compatible Template.

A dynastic dungeon can migrate across continents. It may recognize a forgotten branch, adopted heir, servant family, corporate successor, or legal claimant rather than the person modern genealogy expects.

### Institutional Anchors

Institutional anchors attach to a persistent function such as a government, military office, temple administration, bank, university, guild, court, shipping network, civil-service office, or hospital tradition.

These anchors survive personnel changes but create succession disputes. A dungeon must decide whether a modern republic succeeds an ancient kingdom, a central bank succeeds a royal treasury, or a space force succeeds an old military order.

### Portable Seed Anchors

Portable seed anchors establish new access points. They may take the form of heirlooms, seals, coins, weapons, tablets, jewelry, Template fragments, bound Echoes, oath objects, or embedded Cognitive addresses.

A seed does not contain the full dungeon. It carries enough identity and access logic to establish a branch when local conditions permit.

This provides the cleanest mechanism for dungeon access on Mars, the Moon, orbital habitats, or asteroid stations.

## Primary Cores and Branches

### Primary Core

The oldest and most authoritative Cognitive structure. It contains the original Purpose Kernel, root authority definitions, oldest Template reservoir, contract identity, primary safeguards, and original historical record.

### Branch Anchor

A later projection linked to the primary core. It may contain a local entrance, cached Templates, partial authority logic, regional records, local guardians, and limited autonomy.

### Severed Branch

A branch cut off long enough may develop its own interpretation of purpose. It may believe the core was destroyed, that it is the legitimate successor, or that other branches are corrupted.

## Migration Rules

A branch can follow a family or institution when:

1. The recognized anchor leaves the old region.
2. The relationship remains active.
3. The dungeon retains enough Will to maintain the link.
4. A compatible location exists at the destination.
5. The Scar or local dimensional terrain allows projection.

Migration may take decades or centuries.

## Will Collection Range

Will collection depends upon more than distance.

**Effective intake = proximity x resonance x attention x continuity x permeability**

Nearby populations contribute more strongly. People emotionally or institutionally aligned with the dungeon's purpose contribute more. Repeated focus on the anchor increases contribution. Recognized members, heirs, citizens, employees, patients, or users contribute efficiently. Scar interference and dimensional damage modify all collection.

## Entrance Formation

Entrances form where an anchor intersects a viable boundary.

Common forms include existing doorways, elevators, tunnels, closets, temple ruins, secure vaults, service corridors, airlocks, maintenance hatches, mirrors, pools, transit platforms, family safes, and abandoned infrastructure.

The dungeon usually reuses culturally plausible thresholds.

## Entrance Stability

Entrances progress through stages:

- Trace
- Flicker
- Intermittent
- Conditional
- Stable
- Networked

## Forced Entry

Forced entry costs substantially more Will than voluntary entry. The dungeon cannot arbitrarily seize anyone on Earth.

## Anchor Damage

Destroying a physical entrance rarely destroys the dungeon. It may close local access, reduce Will intake, isolate a branch, destabilize rooms, or mark the destruction as an attack.

## Re-Anchoring

A dungeon may be re-anchored through legal succession, Template recognition, Will-seeding consent, recovery of a root key, restoration of an authority Echo, installation of a new administrator, negotiated contract transfer, or Titan-Forge modification.

## Off-World Propagation

Off-world branches should remain rare and traceable.

A valid chain requires a portable seed or authorized person, sustained population, viable local Will, Scar clearing, a spatial threshold, and recognition that the destination falls within the dungeon's purpose.
