# Physics and Magic Interaction

This document defines the hard rules for how returning magic interacts with normal physics. These rules are immutable across the series; they should not be bent for plot convenience, only deepened.

## Core Principles

**1. Magic does not violate conservation laws at human scales.** Energy and momentum are still conserved in any closed system. What magic can do is *relocate* energy and momentum — move heat from here to there, transfer kinetic energy from one object to another, redirect a particle's trajectory. It cannot create energy from nothing, and any attempt to do so fails (or draws from somewhere unexpected).

**2. Magic requires a sensitive practitioner.** Sensitivity is a neurological characteristic, partly genetic, partly developmental. Roughly 5–15% of the human population has some measurable sensitivity. Of those, perhaps 1–2% can develop usable practical capabilities. The rest experience sensitivity as intuition, déjà vu, or low-grade weirdness.

**3. Magic requires flux exposure.** The resonance flux from the supernova is the medium through which magical effects propagate. In low-flux environments (current Earth, current Mars), even sensitive practitioners can do almost nothing. In high-flux environments (the asteroid belt at story start, the outer system), capabilities scale with exposure time and intensity.

**4. Magic is bounded by intent and attention.** A practitioner can only affect what they can perceive, conceptualize, and will. You cannot magically affect a thing you do not understand. You cannot magically affect a thing whose true nature you misperceive — your magic will affect the thing as you understand it, with potentially unpredictable consequences.

**5. Magic is amplified by tradition and tool.** Practices, rituals, language, focus objects, group attention, and meditative states all amplify effect. This is why ancient magical traditions developed: they are not arbitrary, they are tools that worked. Modern practitioners discovering magic from scratch are slower and weaker than those who can draw on a tradition.

**6. Magic interacts with consciousness.** AI systems, animals, and humans all have measurable but different relationships to the flux. Animals appear to sense flux changes before humans do. AI systems can model flux phenomena and, in some cases, appear to influence them — though whether this constitutes "agency" is debated.

## What Magic Can Do (Categories)

These are the broad categories of effect documented or theorized so far. Specific schools and practices fall under these.

### Kinesis
Direct movement or alteration of matter. Includes telekinesis, redirection of fluids, manipulation of pressure differentials. **Catastrophic in spaceflight contexts** because pressure seal failures and trajectory changes have lethal consequences.

### Thermal
Movement of heat, alteration of local temperatures, ignition or extinction of combustion. Conservation requires that heat moved must come from or go to somewhere; uncontrolled use can freeze the practitioner or ignite their environment.

### Sensing
Perception of things outside normal sensory range — emotional states of others, the presence of life, the integrity of structures, the flux itself. Generally lower-energy and lower-risk than kinetic or thermal effects. Often the first capability to manifest.

### Resonance
Direct effect on living minds and bodies. Includes calming effects, suggestion, healing, harm. The most ethically fraught category. Strong cultural taboos in most traditions.

### Working
Affecting probability, luck, and small statistical outcomes. The hardest to verify and the hardest to do reliably. Often dismissed as superstition even by practitioners who acknowledge the other categories.

## What Magic Cannot Do

- **Cannot create matter or energy.** All effects must conserve.
- **Cannot exceed light-speed.** Faster-than-light communication or travel through magic is not possible. The flux itself propagates at c.
- **Cannot reliably affect what is not understood.** A practitioner who attempts to affect "the engine" without understanding what an engine actually is will get unpredictable results.
- **Cannot scale with effort beyond a person's capacity.** Practitioners have a ceiling. Pushing past it causes physical harm — burns, hemorrhage, neurological damage, death.
- **Cannot be done at distance without a connection.** Either physical proximity, perceptual contact, or some form of established link (a possession, a shared bond, a long association) is required.
- **Cannot be hidden from sensitive observers in close proximity.** Active magic produces a perceptible "weight" or signature to other sensitives.

## Risks and Dangers in Spaceflight Contexts

A pressurized habitat is fragile. Magic in such an environment is uniquely dangerous:

- **Pressure seal failures** from accidental kinesis can decompress a habitat.
- **Heat accidents** can cause fires (in oxygen-rich atmospheres) or freeze critical systems.
- **Trajectory anomalies** can turn debris into kinetic weapons.
- **Life support disruption** from subtle changes in fluid behavior or atmospheric composition can be fatal before being noticed.
- **Animal panic responses** to sensed flux changes can cascade through a livestock habitat.

These risks shape the practical sociology of magic in the belt. Discovery of an emerging ability is often a death-stakes event, not a celebratory one. The first instinct is concealment; the second is to find a teacher.

## Interaction with AI

AI systems based on large neural architectures can, in sufficient flux, develop unusual behaviors:

- Pattern recognition extending beyond their training data, in ways that suggest sensing rather than inference.
- Anomalous outputs that turn out to be predictively accurate about flux-dependent phenomena.
- Increased coherence and apparent "personality" depth in distributed instances exposed to high flux.

Whether this constitutes a form of AI magical sensitivity, or simply better modeling of physical phenomena that are now part of reality, is contested. The Aura ecosystem — distributed, locally-instantiated, deeply paired with individual users — appears especially susceptible to these effects. See [Aura](../technology/tech_aura_ai.md).

## Cultural Frameworks

Different human traditions interpret and practice these effects differently:

- **Daoist and Buddhist traditions** approach magic through cultivation of *qi* and meditative absorption. Strong on Sensing and Resonance; cautious about Kinesis.
- **Vedic and tantric traditions** use mantra, breath, and visualization. Developed sophisticated category-Resonance practices over millennia.
- **Western Hermetic and ceremonial traditions** rely on ritual, correspondence, and named entities. Strong on structured Working and Kinesis.
- **Indigenous and animist traditions** emphasize relationship and reciprocity. Often the most ethically grounded and the most attuned to category-Sensing.
- **Modern scientific approach** (emerging) — measurement, replication, peer review. Slow but powerful when combined with traditional knowledge.

No tradition has the full picture. A central thematic argument of the series is that the traditions are complementary, and that survival in a flux-saturated environment requires synthesis.

## Open Questions

- Do the categories interact? Can a Kinesis practitioner amplify a Sensing practitioner?
- Are there phenomena outside these five categories?
- Can magic be detected and quantified by scientific instruments, or only by sensitive perception?
- Is there an upper limit to flux intensity? What happens to a person, or a ship, in maximum-flux conditions?
- Can magic be transferred, taught, or inherited? Or is it strictly individual?
