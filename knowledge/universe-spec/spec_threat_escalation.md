# Astral Threat Escalation — The Leveling Arc

## Overview

As the Scar clears and human practitioners begin entering the Astral, they are not entering an empty space. They are entering a landscape that has been without rich food sources for three thousand years. The same flux clearing that enables practitioners also enables the fauna to move inward. Human practitioners, radiating Will brightly and unconsciously, are the first rich prey this territory has seen since the Scar formed.

The threat level escalates in proportion to human capability. The threats arrive *just faster* than humans can handle them. Every improvement in human capability makes the next set of threats accessible. Getting better at magic makes the universe more dangerous. This is the right shape for narrative tension across the series.

---

## Tier 1 — Environmental (Early Book 1)

The dangers are the environment itself. No active predators. Just the conditions.

- **Cold:** -20°C near Fortuna — manageable with gear, lethal in sleepwear over time
- **Darkness:** No ambient light source except mind-glows and distant stars
- **Disorientation:** The extra dimension, N-1 perception, low-gravity float movement
- **Breathable air window:** 60-90 seconds ambient; masks extend to 3-6 hours — hard operational limit
- **The learning curve itself:** Getting navigation, equipment, or timing wrong kills you

**Character response:** Sophia and Jace are solving engineering and physics problems. The danger is real but impersonal.

**Note on early crossings:** The station's interior Astral layer is not yet heavily infested. Jace and Cerberus's first crossing — hours of exploration through station corridors — encounters no active fauna. The environment is cold and strange and disorienting, but not dangerous beyond the environmental threats. This changes as more practitioners enter and the Will-signature of human activity attracts attention.

## Tier 2 — Passive Fauna (Mid Book 1)

Will-draining organisms that don't actively hunt practitioners — but feed on them opportunistically.

### Dimensional Insects (Motes)

Small, numerous, quasi-physical. Individually harmless. In swarms — and they swarm whenever Will is detected — they drain enough to cause significant exhaustion, broken concentration, and in extreme cases dangerous incapacity.

Three thousand years of near-starvation has optimized them for detecting the faintest Will signature at long range. A practitioner entering higher-flux or more remote Astral zones is immediately visible to every mote within a substantial radius as a blazing food source in a famine landscape.

**In station interiors (early story):** Not yet a significant problem. The station's Astral layer near Fortuna is relatively low-fauna density at story start. As more practitioners enter and stay longer, mote populations build around human activity.

**In deeper or more remote Astral zones:** More active. The further from the station, the higher the ambient mote density. Extended expeditions into open belt Astral will encounter them.

**Story function:** The ambient threat of extended or deeper expeditions. Sophia has learned to keep them at bay near her regular crossing point through inadvertent algae cultivation (the algae suppresses mote activity). Jace and Cerberus's first crossing in the station interior doesn't encounter them in meaningful numbers.

### Astral Algae

Passive Will drain, colony-based. Not dangerous to practitioners directly — but suppresses mood and Will-generation of the station population, creating a persistent fog. Jace clears a patch and the effect on the crew is immediate and observable.

## Tier 3 — Active Fauna (Late Book 1 / Early Book 2)

Organisms that actively hunt practitioners.

### Memory Leeches

Soft-bodied predators approximately the size of a large hand. Flatworm-jellyfish hybrid. Semi-translucent, found by touch before sight — they absorb most wavelengths.

**What they do:** Attach to a practitioner's Template and siphon memory and cognitive capacity. Victims in physical space present with unexplained memory gaps, personality changes, vivid dreams, progressive cognitive deterioration. No pathogen identified. Three deaths from Template shock documented at Odysseus before anyone understands what's happening.

**Story function:** First fauna that threatens identity, not just survival. The horror is not pain — it is forgetting. Coming back from an expedition and not being sure who you were when you left.

### Threshold Hounds

Medium-sized, canid body plan. Physical-space organisms that sense breach zones from outside without fully crossing. Multiple clusters of fine filaments along the spine for dimensional detection. Eyes with pupils that dilate into non-biological shapes.

**Story function:** Not directly dangerous but reliable markers of significant breach activity. A station overrun with Threshold Hounds is a station on a major breach zone. They treat Cerberus differently after her first crossing — she smells like the other side, and they give her respectful distance. By late Book 1, the Fortuna pack is informally named and regarded as mascot and warning system.

### Drift Coral

Sessile colonial organisms forming large branching structures in the Never-Never, anchored to dimensional substrate. In physical space, they manifest as crystalline formations growing from walls, metal, and manufactured objects.

**The danger:** Drift coral stores preserved experiences — emotional echoes from the Scar-era collapse of civilizations. A drift coral formation releasing its stored residue creates an *emotional bleed event*: overwhelming waves of preserved experience from civilizations dying. The experiences are not the practitioner's own. They are thousands of years old.

Researchers who survive a drift coral bleed event often develop magical sensitivity. The trauma seems to open something.

### Scar Wolves

Template-hunters. Large, predatory, appearing in breach zones as the Scar clears. They track practitioners specifically — a sensitive worker doing active magical work is immediately visible to a Scar Wolf as a distinct Template signature.

**What they do:** Attack the Template directly, not the physical body. Victims present with injuries that have no physical cause: severe neurological and psychological collapse, extreme identity drift, in fatal cases sudden Template dissociation. Physical-space medicine cannot explain what it sees.

**The predator paradox:** Scar Wolves are apex predators in an ecosystem that had no sapient prey for three thousand years. They were surviving lean, feeding on other organisms and Echoes. The awakening human practitioners are the first rich prey this territory has seen since the Scar formed. They are not evil. They are hungry. The distinction matters for later story developments.

**Non-practitioner safety:** Non-practitioners are largely invisible to Scar Wolves — no Template reach, nothing to track. The safest people in a Scar Wolf encounter are those with no magical sensitivity. The most experienced practitioners are the most attractive targets.

## Tier 4 — Sentient Threats (Later Books)

As the Scar continues clearing, the threats become intelligent.

**Returning pre-Scar entities:** Beings of enormous accumulated Will who survived the Sundering in Template Vaults or dimensional refuges. They have been waiting for three thousand years. They arrive to find humanity radiating Will brightly, unnetworked, ungoverned, and very interesting to various parties with very different agendas.

**Galactic civilizations:** The surviving Will-Network hierarchies outside the Scar begin sending scouts, then delegations, then interests. Their political structures have no category for what humanity is. The encounter is not necessarily hostile. It is enormously dangerous regardless.

**Intelligence within Tier 3:** Some of the active fauna show behavior patterns in later encounters that suggest coordination — particularly Memory Leeches. This will not be comfortable to think about.

## Tier 4 — Outsider Entities

Sentient beings from beyond the Scar boundary operating under their own protocols.
Two distinct factions are established in Arc 2 with fundamentally different threat
profiles.

### Tier 4A — The Woven (Kael's Civilization)
**Threat profile:** Observational, protocol-driven, not immediately hostile. Standard
protocol for unnetworked populations is assessment followed by absorption negotiation.
Will negotiate, at least nominally. The nominal negotiation can become absorption
pressure. Time works against humanity in this framework.

**Key distinction from Tier 4B:** The Woven's protocol includes a contact and
communication phase. This creates the possibility of relationship, influence, and
the kind of misrepresentation Kael engages in on humanity's behalf.

**Representative individual:** Kael (Arc 2).

### Tier 4B — The Bloom
**Threat profile:** Classification-and-absorption doctrine with no negotiation phase.
An unnetworked population of this size is, in their framework, unclaimed resource.
Standard protocol proceeds directly from classification to integration. They have
done this before. It has worked before.

**Physical threat level:** Very high. Force-discipline Will use. Very hard to kill
(bark-armor, regrowth). Do not sleep. Can operate in vacuum with personal environmental
generation. Hate fire — this is exploitable.

**Key distinction from Tier 4A:** The Bloom do not have a negotiation phase. Contact
means classification, which means absorption has begun. Every interaction with them is
happening inside their protocol, not outside it.

**Representative group:** The Bloom scouts who kill Kael and take Jace prisoner in
Arc 2.

**Critical vulnerability:** They have no concept of recording technology. {Aura}
recording everything during imprisonment is not in their threat model.

## The Escalation Rule

Threat level scales with:
1. **Density of human Will in the Astral** — more practitioners = more attractive target overall
2. **Skill level of practitioners** — better practitioners = more visible, more interesting targets
3. **Depth of Scar clearing** — deeper clearing = more fauna can cross in from outside
4. **Ambition of expeditions** — deeper exploration = contact with worse things

The universe rewards competence by sending harder problems.
