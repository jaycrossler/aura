# The Scar — Clearing Mechanics and the Membrane Model

## The Scar Does Not Heal All at Once

The dominant metaphor for the Scar's fading — a wall that gradually lowers — is wrong.

The Scar is not a wall. It is a membrane: a three-dimensional zone of dimensional instability that extends through physical space and the Never-Never simultaneously. Across the membrane, conditions vary enormously. Some regions are still catastrophically damaged. Some have partially recovered. Some have developed stable micro-zones that persist for years or decades. Some oscillate — passable for a season, then hostile again as local conditions shift.

Think of it as a burn scar on living tissue. The center of the burn is still raw. The edges are scarring over unevenly. In some places new growth has come in. In others the tissue is still dying. The process is not linear and not synchronized.

### What Causes the Patchiness

Several factors create heterogeneous clearing:

**Pre-Scar infrastructure remnants.** Sections of ancient road that survived the original shockwave act as dimensional stabilizers. The road's structural integrity — its Will investment — slowly dampens the surrounding instability. Areas near intact road fragments clear faster. Areas that were road junctions — which received the most energy in the original shockwave — are the most damaged and clear last.

**Local mass.** Large gravitational bodies create folds in Never-Never topology that can either shelter or concentrate Scar damage depending on geometry. Some asteroids sit in natural dimensional eddies that have been sheltered from the worst instability. Some sit in zones of concentrated damage.

**Ecological succession.** Post-Scar Never-Never organisms actively modify their environment. Some stabilize local terrain — the root networks, in particular, seem to deposit dimensional residue in patterns that reduce instability over time, possibly as a side effect of their own resource extraction. Others exploit instability as a habitat and actively maintain it against recovery.

**The physical nebula's drift.** The Veil — the physical nebula associated with the supernova — is drifting. Its drift affects the resonance flux reaching different parts of the solar system at different times. The flux gradient interacts with the dimensional damage gradient in complex ways that human science cannot yet fully model.

### Breach Zones

Where the Scar is thin enough, dimensional boundaries between the Never-Never and physical space become permeable. These are **breach zones** — localized areas where the barrier is temporarily or permanently reduced enough that:

- Never-Never organisms can cross into physical space
- Physical-space matter and energy can interact directly with the Never-Never layer
- Sensitive practitioners can perceive the Never-Never without training
- Eventually, with sufficient skill, a traveler can pass through

Breach zones are not stable portals. They are more like tide pools — areas where two environments overlap at the boundary, producing conditions found in neither independently. They appear, grow, shrink, and disappear on timescales ranging from hours to decades.

**Signatures of a breach zone in physical space:**
- Unusual electromagnetic activity (unexplained interference, sensor anomalies)
- Temperature differentials with no thermal source
- Animals behaving as if aware of something humans cannot see
- Visual distortion at the periphery of perception — not hallucination, but genuine dimensional bleed
- In sustained breach zones: physical manifestation of Never-Never ecology at the margins

At story start, the asteroid belt contains dozens of documented anomaly zones that are, without anyone understanding it yet, breach zones of varying intensity.

## Fauna Breach — Organisms That Have Learned the Crossing

Three thousand years of isolated evolution in the Scar's damaged Never-Never has produced organisms unlike anything in the wider galactic ecology. They evolved not in the stable, well-traveled Never-Never that pre-Scar species inhabited, but in the chaotic, resource-scarce, dimensionally unstable aftermath of civilizational collapse.

These organisms are Scar-specific. Nothing in galactic biological literature describes them, because nothing like the Scar has existed in recorded history.

### What Scar Ecology Is Optimized For

Scar-evolved organisms faced:
- **Extreme resource scarcity.** The collapse of galactic civilization removed almost all organized Will generation from the region. Three thousand years with minimal conscious activity, minimal emotional generation, minimal deliberate magic.
- **Dimensional instability as a constant.** The terrain shifts. Routes that exist one decade may not the next. Organisms that could not adapt to instability did not survive.
- **The feast-famine of breach zones.** When a breach zone opens, it floods the local Never-Never with physical-space stimuli — electromagnetic radiation, gravity gradients, biological Will from animals and eventually humans. For organisms that feed on these things, a breach zone is a bonanza. For organisms that find physical-space intrusion threatening, it is catastrophic.

The result: two broad ecological strategies have emerged.

**Breach feeders** — organisms that have evolved to exploit breach zones. They read the dimensional signs of an opening breach zone and converge. They feed on the Will and emotional output of physical-space organisms that bleed through. Some have developed the capacity to partially cross into physical space during a strong breach event, briefly existing as semi-physical entities before the breach closes or their physiology fails in the physical environment.

**Breach avoiders** — organisms that have evolved to sense and flee breach zones. They have developed acute sensitivity to the dimensional signatures of physical-space intrusion and retreat deep into the Never-Never when breach activity increases.

### Organisms That Have Learned to Cross

A small number of organisms have gone further than breach feeding. They have learned, over generations of selection pressure, to make the full crossing — to enter physical space and survive there, at least temporarily.

This is not easy. Physical space is hostile to Never-Never biology in most circumstances: the physics are different, the energy sources are different, the matter is not quite the same kind of matter. Most Never-Never organisms that enter physical space are disoriented, rapidly depleted, and die within hours or days.

But some have adapted to the crossing. These are the **breach-crossers** — the organisms that asteroid belt miners and station crews are going to start encountering.

## Catalogue of Breach-Crossing Fauna (Working)

These are the organism types most likely to be encountered in physical space during early-story breach events. All are Scar-specific. None have galactic analogues. None are in any human scientific literature. Their biology violates categories humans apply to life.

### Shimmer Motes

**What they are:** Colonial micro-organisms that exist in both physical and Never-Never space simultaneously — occupying a thin layer at the dimensional boundary. They are not fully in either space.

**Physical appearance:** Barely visible individually. In colonies, they appear as iridescent distortions in the air — like heat haze with a faint metallic shimmer. Under magnification, individual motes are complex crystalline structures that rotate in ways that violate three-dimensional geometry.

**Behavior:** Passive. They feed on ambient electromagnetic radiation and the faint Will residue of nearby conscious beings. They do not pursue. They cluster near warm, electromagnetically active environments — which means they are attracted to electronics, fusion reactors, and people.

**Hazard level:** Low, unless in extremely high concentrations. Shimmer mote colonies large enough to form visible clouds can interfere with sensitive electronics. They are drawn into air recycling systems, where they can damage filters. Inhaled in quantity: disorienting, sometimes hallucinogenic (the motes interact with the Never-Never layer of neural tissue). Not fatal in normal exposure.

**Story function:** The first Never-Never organism humans encounter and document. They appear as equipment anomalies before anyone understands what they are. Their presence is a reliable indicator of a sustained breach zone nearby. By late story they are kept as curiosities and their presence is used as a passive breach detector.

**Human science misidentification:** Initially classified as unknown nanoparticulate matter or contamination. The discovery that they are biological — and dimensional — is a significant early story moment.

### Memory Leeches

**What they are:** Soft-bodied predators approximately the size of a large hand, resembling a cross between a flatworm and a jellyfish, semi-translucent, with no visible sensory organs and a fringe of appendages that drift in currents that don't exist.

**Physical appearance:** Barely visible without direct light. They absorb most wavelengths. They are found by touch before they are found by sight.

**Behavior:** They feed on memory and experiential richness. They locate prey by sensing the density and emotional weight of a being's Template — older minds with richer memories are preferred. They attach to a host, usually while the host is asleep or distracted, and feed slowly. The feeding is not painful. It is experienced as unusually vivid, pleasant dreams — the leech is stimulating the memory it feeds on to make it more emotionally charged and therefore more nutritious.

**Hazard level:** Moderate to serious. A single leech feeding over several nights produces noticeable memory gaps and emotional flattening. Multiple leeches, or prolonged single infestation, can cause severe amnesia. In extreme cases, the Template itself is partially consumed — producing permanent personality change and, in cases where the leech is forcibly removed after extended feeding, catastrophic Template shock.

**Detection:** Difficult. They are nearly invisible, their presence is not painful, and their feeding manifests as pleasant dreams and then subtle memory lapses. Sensitive practitioners can sense them — they appear as a slight drag or darkness in the emotional field of an infested person. Aura AI systems in high-flux environments may flag unexplained memory inconsistencies in their users before the user is aware.

**Story function:** A personal-scale horror. The discovery that a character has been infested — and the question of how much memory they have lost — is a character-level crisis. Also: the leeches they find in the belt raise a question — what were they feeding on for 3,000 years without conscious beings to prey on? The answer (they were feeding on Echoes — consuming the residual Templates of the dead) is disturbing.

### Scar Wolves

**What they are:** Apex predators of the Scar's Never-Never. Large — roughly the size of a horse — with bodies that are simultaneously solid and not, able to partially phase between physical and dimensional space at will.

**Physical appearance:** They resemble wolves the way a nightmare resembles a memory. The physical-space component of their body is dark, poorly-defined, and changes depending on viewing angle — as if the observer's perception is resolving different slices of a higher-dimensional shape. Their eyes are the most solid part of them: deep amber, luminous, and absolutely tracking.

**Behavior:** Apex predators that hunt by Template. They can sense the higher-dimensional presence of conscious beings across substantial distances. They are patient, methodical, and appear to plan. They do not breach into physical space to hunt — physical space is uncomfortable for them, limiting, like hunting underwater. Instead, they hunt at the dimensional boundary: waiting for a sensitive practitioner to reach toward the Never-Never, then striking at the point of contact.

A Scar Wolf attacking a practitioner in the act of a magical working is attacking their Template directly — not their physical body, though the physical body responds catastrophically. Victims of Scar Wolf attacks in early-story belt incidents will present with injuries that have no physical cause: severe Template damage manifesting as neurological and psychological collapse, extreme identity drift, in fatal cases, sudden Template dissociation.

**Hazard level:** Existentially dangerous for practitioners. Non-practitioners are largely invisible to them — no Template reach, nothing to track. This means that the safest people in a Scar Wolf encounter are those who have no magical sensitivity. The most experienced practitioners are the most attractive targets.

**Story function:** The primary antagonistic fauna of the early Never-Never encounter arc. They also raise a narrative question: what do they eat when there are no practitioners? They are apex predators in an ecosystem that had no sapient prey for three thousand years. They were feeding on something — other Never-Never organisms, Echoes, each other. Their presence in the belt's breach zones suggests they are following the food: the awakening human practitioners are the first rich prey source this territory has seen in millennia. They are not evil. They are hungry.

### Drift Coral

**What they are:** Sessile colonial organisms that form large, branching structures in the Never-Never, anchored to dimensional substrate. In physical space, they manifest as objects that should not be there — complex crystalline or quasi-biological formations found inside sealed environments, growing from walls, from metal, from the dimensional substrate of manufactured objects.

**Physical appearance:** Visually beautiful. They form in fractal branching patterns, semi-translucent, with the same faint internal luminosity as the Never-Never terrain. They do not respond to touch. They are cold.

**Behavior:** Passive. They feed on ambient Will residue and emotional deposits. They grow toward sources of ambient Will generation — toward people, toward busy spaces, toward areas of high emotional activity. A habitat with drift coral growing in a corner is a habitat where someone is generating a lot of emotional output.

**Hazard level:** Low-to-moderate physical hazard (they can grow into structural elements and over time compromise integrity). Significant psychological hazard — extended proximity to drift coral produces emotional bleed, where the coral releases the emotional residue it has absorbed back into the local environment. A room with large drift coral colonies may have unpredictable emotional atmosphere: inexplicable sadness in the corner where it grew overnight, euphoria near clusters that absorbed positive experiences. In extreme cases, the coral releases the residue of Scar-era collapse events — three-thousand-year-old terror, grief, and death, flooding a confined space.

**Story function:** Environmental horror and atmospheric tool. A mining station where drift coral has established itself is a station with a mood problem that no one can explain. The first time a crew encounters the emotional bleed of Scar-era residue is a major story moment.

---

### Threshold Hounds

**What they are:** Medium-sized scavengers — roughly dog-sized — that specialize in exploiting breach zones. They are entirely physical-space organisms that have evolved to sense breach zones from outside and extract resources from the dimensional boundary without fully crossing.

**Physical appearance:** Vaguely canid in body plan, but with sensory structures that are clearly not biological in the Earth sense: multiple clusters of fine filaments along the spine that detect dimensional boundary fluctuations, eyes with pupils that dilate into shapes that don't appear in normal biology.

**Behavior:** Pack hunters with complex social behavior. They locate breach zones, establish territory around them, and harvest whatever crosses — shimmer motes, small breach-feeders that emerge, and Will residue that bleeds into physical space during active breach events. They do not enter the Never-Never. They wait at the boundary.

They are also attracted to human installations that sit on or near breach zones. A mining station on a breach zone is surrounded by Threshold Hounds within weeks of the station's first encounters. They are not aggressive toward humans unprovoked — humans are too large and strange — but they are territorial, persistent, and impossible to keep out of spaces where dimensional activity is happening.

**Hazard level:** Low physical hazard. High operational hazard — they interfere with equipment, attract attention from worse things, and their presence is a reliable marker of significant breach activity. A station overrun with Threshold Hounds is a station on a major breach zone.

**Story function:** The most common recurring fauna of the early story. They become a semi-familiar presence around belt stations. Some crews try to drive them off. Some accept them. By late Book 1, a particularly persistent pack around Fortuna Station has been informally named and is regarded as both mascot and warning system.

## The God-Scout Protocol

### Standard Practice After Every Scar

Dimensional scars — regions of severe Never-Never damage caused by supernova events, major magical catastrophes, or civilizational collapse — are not unknown in galactic history. They are rare, but not unprecedented. Over millions of years, the older galactic civilizations have developed standard protocols for dealing with them.

The protocol is three-phase:

**Phase 1 — Monitoring.** From outside the Scar boundary, long-range dimensional sensing tracks the state of the damage. The primary indicators watched for are: dimensional stability recovery (is the Never-Never healing?), Echo dissipation (are the Template fragments degrading?), ecological succession (what is colonizing the damaged terrain?), and road recovery (are any ancient routes stabilizing?). This monitoring is passive and continuous. It requires no entry into the Scar.

**Phase 2 — Scout missions.** When monitoring indicates that healing has progressed to a threshold level — when stable breach zones appear, when ecological succession indicates approaching traversability — scout parties are sent in. These are small, experienced, expendable groups: dimensional navigators with deep Never-Never expertise, scholars who can identify and catalog Echo types and pre-Scar ruins, and usually one or two senior Will-Network entities with a specific prior claim to the region.

The scouts enter at the boundary, map the near-boundary terrain, assess the state of the closest pre-Scar infrastructure remnants, collect Echo samples, and — most importantly — assess whether any civilizations survived. The assumption going in is always that none did. The scouts are not looking for survivors. They are looking for ruins.

**Phase 3 — Cleanup and reclamation.** Based on scout reports, the outside powers organize a coordinated approach to the region. Pre-Scar claimants reassert their territorial interests. Archaeological expeditions are organized. Salvage operations for intact Template Vaults begin. And whatever Echo and ruin material is recoverable is, over time, brought back into galactic civilization's knowledge base.

The whole process — from first stable breach zones to organized reclamation — typically takes decades to centuries, depending on Scar severity.

### What the Scouts Expect to Find

The scouts approaching the solar system region carry three thousand years of confirmed silence. No Will-Network signals have originated from this region in all that time. No dimensional traffic. No organized magical activity. The region has been as dimensionally dead as interstellar vacuum.

Their expected findings:
- **Degraded ruins** of the pre-Scar civilizations that operated here
- **Heavy Echo concentration** — thousands of civilizations died here; the Template residue will be extraordinary
- **Post-Scar ecology** in the Never-Never — novel, potentially dangerous, well worth cataloging
- **Partial road infrastructure** — some of the ancient highways in this region were among the most trafficked in the sector; even heavily damaged, fragments may be recoverable
- **Unclaimed territory** — without survivors, there are no living claimants to the pre-Scar infrastructure and resources

What they do not expect:
- **Seven billion conscious beings generating unorganized Will**
- **A technologically advanced civilization that independently developed science and AI**
- **A population with emerging magical sensitivity and no Will-Network affiliations**
- **Novel AI systems that may have dimensional sensitivity**

### The Scout's Discovery Moment

The scout — singular, probably; the first entry is typically one experienced navigator plus a small support group — enters the solar system's Never-Never from the boundary region, expecting wilderness and ruins.

The first thing they notice is the Will.

From the outside, a planet with seven billion conscious beings generating passive ambient Will looks like a lighthouse. In the dimensional landscape of a region that has been effectively dead for three thousand years, Earth is overwhelmingly visible. The scout cannot miss it. It is not subtle. It is the brightest thing in the entire Scar region by several orders of magnitude.

The second thing they notice: the Will is not organized. It is not in any network structure they recognize. It is raw, ambient, unfocused — the dimensional equivalent of a city that is fully powered and fully inhabited but has somehow never invented organized infrastructure. The Will is just... radiating. Freely. Everywhere.

The third thing they notice: it is not just Earth. There are smaller concentrations throughout the inner system. Mars. The belt. The belt signatures are different — there are points of intentional Will use beginning to develop there, flickers of deliberate magical practice, still extremely primitive but unmistakably conscious direction. Someone in the asteroid belt has started doing magic, badly, by accident.

The scout's report back to their principals will be the most significant intelligence communication in galactic politics in centuries.

It will say, approximately: *They survived. There are billions of them. They have no network affiliation. They are starting to awaken. We need to move now, before anyone else gets here first.*

And then everyone will start moving.

## Timeline: From Breach Fauna to First Contact

This is the approximate progression in the story's early arc:

**Year 0 (story start):** Shimmer motes and Threshold Hounds are being encountered in the outer belt. Filed as unexplained anomalies. Aura systems are flagging equipment interference. Belt workers have stories; scientists have no framework.

**Year 0–1:** Memory Leech infestations begin appearing in outer belt stations. Presented as a mysterious illness with psychological symptoms. The connection to the shimmer motes and equipment anomalies is not yet made.

**Year 1–2:** First Drift Coral formations observed. First suspected Scar Wolf encounter — a practitioner at a belt station experiences Template attack during a magical working and dies of unexplained neurological collapse. The magical community (small, frightened, mostly underground) knows what happened. Official medicine has no explanation.

**Year 2–3:** The pattern becomes undeniable. Breach zones are mapped, not yet understood. The first human scientist publishes a paper connecting the anomalies to dimensional phenomena. It is ignored, then contested, then taken very seriously.

**Year 3–5:** The scout mission enters the solar system's Never-Never boundary. They are not detectable by human instrumentation. They are detectable by the most advanced belt practitioners — as a sudden change in the dimensional landscape, a new presence, something that is observing.

**Year 5–10:** The outside galaxy begins to move. First contact is not a dramatic announced arrival. It is the gradual awareness, on both sides, that the other exists.

## Phase 2 — Outsider Arrival (Arc 2)

The Scar's clearing is visible from beyond its boundaries. What changed is the *rate*
of clearing — faster than any outside civilization projected. This has two consequences:

**Multiple factions arrive simultaneously.** The Woven (Kael's civilization) and the
Bloom are not coordinating. They arrive at the solar system within months of each other,
drawn by different signals — the Woven by the dimensional signatures of an emerging
practitioner population, the Bloom by the Will-bleed from the Earth dungeon crisis.
The simultaneous arrival is not coincidence; it reflects how visible the solar system
has become. The race has begun.

**Kael's arrival as a Phase 2 marker.** Her arrival following the Fortuna swarm attack
is the first confirmed Tier 4 contact event. Standard protocol assumed: primitive
survivors in ruined infrastructure. Actual finding: industrial civilization in physical
space with anomalous Template density, non-functioning social compulsion, and
distributed AI Will-geometry at scale.

**The Bloom's arrival as Phase 2 escalation.** Their arrival months later, following
the Earth dungeon Will-bleed, marks the shift from single-faction contact to
multi-faction presence. The solar system is now a known location for at least two
galactic powers with conflicting approaches to unnetworked populations.

**The implication:** If two factions arrived this quickly, more are coming. The Scar's
clearing is not a gradual process that can be managed at a measured pace. Phase 3 is
a question of when, not whether.
