*   **Status:** staged — author-approved 2026-07-15

## Transit (Mars/Falcon) Track

### Chapter 1: Departure

*   **Status:** staged — author-approved 2026-07-15
*   **Source:** Jace Grant, transit logs
*   **Content:**
    > Most people don't know this, but you can hear a spaceship breathe. A thousand tonnes of water moving through the walls to balance every human footstep. I spent four months falling asleep inside a very large animal that was politely pretending not to notice me.

### Chapter 2: Morning Rounds

*   **Status:** staged — author-approved 2026-07-15
*   **Source:** Jace Grant, transit logs
*   **Content:**
    > Most people don't know this, but the most sophisticated piece of equipment on a livestock ferry is the pig. We had six. They knew it.

### Chapter 3: Afternoon Workshop (Book 1, Chapter 1 / Workshop Chapter)

*   **Status:** confirmed — assigned in spec_chapter_epigraphs.md
*   **Source:** Jace Grant, *Falcon* transit logs
*   **Content:**
    > Nobody warns you how much video games suck across long distances. When it takes 12 seconds for your shot to land, first-person shooters don't work. Light-speed lag doesn't care about your reflexes; you learn to love turn-based strategy, or you learn to lose to physics.

### Chapter 4: Plumbing Crisis

*   **Status:** staged — author-approved 2026-07-15
*   **Source:** Jace Grant, transit logs
*   **Content:**
    > Most people don't know this, but a spinning ship is just a very large washing machine, and the entire job of everyone aboard is to keep the load balanced. You find this out the day somebody throws a boot in.

### Chapter 5: Morning-After Debrief

*   **Status:** staged — author-approved 2026-07-15
*   **Source:** Jace Grant, transit logs
*   **Content:**
    > Most people don't know this, but corporate AIs are incapable of malice. I want to be clear that this is not a compliment.

### Chapter 6: Learning Mode

*   **Status:** staged — author-approved 2026-07-15
*   **Source:** Jace Grant, transit logs
*   **Content:**
    > Most people don't know this, but my reputation across the entire asteroid belt was established eleven weeks before I arrived, in nine seconds of security footage, none of which were my fault. Approximately four of which were my fault.

### Chapter 7: Artemis Call / Canister

*   **Status:** staged — author-approved 2026-07-15
*   **Source:** Jace Grant, transit logs
*   **Content:**
    > Most people don't know this, but the loneliest distance in the solar system is the last one percent. Six years, I couldn't reach my sister. Now she was three weeks away, and I still couldn't reach my sister.
