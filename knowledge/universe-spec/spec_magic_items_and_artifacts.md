# Magic Items and Artifacts — System Specification

---

## Foundational Principle

Every magical item is a **Will-and-Template interaction made permanent or semi-permanent**.
There is no "magic" that exists independently of Will and Templates. An artifact is
not a different kind of thing from a practitioner's skill — it is a stored configuration
that a practitioner's Will built, sustained, or sealed into an object at construction.

This has three implications:

1. **Every artifact's visual signature is consistent with the glow system.** The same
   color/structure/movement rules that govern mind-glows and working plumes apply to
   artifact light. A golden-colored [hardened] weapon glows with warm golden light when
   active. A bracer channeling [Force Shield] emits the same visual register as the
   practitioner skill it embeds. See `spec_astral_glow_and_signatures.md`.

2. **The atomic weight rules still apply.** [Hardened] items cross the dimensional
   membrane more easily — that is what [hardened] does — but they cannot make lead as
   cheap to cross as aluminum. Hardening reduces the cost differential; it does not
   eliminate it. Material choice in artifact construction is still a dimensional economics
   decision.

3. **Artifacts degrade when Will isn't feeding them.** Sealed artifacts maintain
   themselves through stored Will, but that store is finite unless continuously
   refilled. Template-locked items are self-sustaining through the practitioner's
   own Template maintenance. Non-locked items need periodic recharging.

## The Three Artifact Classes

### Class 1 — [Hardened] Objects

The most common form of magical item. A practitioner imprints a strong, optimized
Template onto an object using [Object Template], then reinforces that Template with
compressed Will until the object's dimensional affinity is substantially improved.

**What [hardened] does:**
- Reduces the dimensional crossing cost for that object dramatically — a [hardened]
  aluminum spear crosses as easily as unhardened fabric; a [hardened] copper object
  crosses approximately as easily as unhardened aluminum
- Dramatically increases physical durability — the object's Template resists
  deformation, cracking, and wear by continuously pulling itself back toward its
  ideal configuration
- Makes the object repairable through Template reinforcement rather than physical
  replacement — a broken [hardened] item can be restored by recharging its Template
- Gives the object a faint, persistent Astral glow — the stored Will is visible as
  a low-level luminescence in the Astral at all times, not just during active use

**What [hardened] does NOT do:**
- Does not give the object active powers or skills — a [hardened] knife is just an
  extremely durable, dimensionally light knife
- Does not prevent physical destruction by sufficient force — [hardened] improves
  durability, it does not make items indestructible
- Does not make the item self-aware, autonomous, or bonded

**Visual signature:** In physical space, a [hardened] item appears ordinary to most
observers except for unusual quality — the edge that never dulls, the surface that
never scuffs. In the Astral, it glows with a low steady light in a color consistent
with its Will affinity at the time of hardening. Kael's weapons glow warm gold
(reflecting her Will-color at hardening time). The glow is constant and passive,
unlike the burst-and-fade of active workings.

**Summoning from Template storage:** When a [hardened] item is called from Cognitive
Layer storage, it materializes at the practitioner's location. The speed of materialization
determines the visual signature:

- *Fast summoning:* Will pours into the Template rapidly, flooding the physical
  space with a burst of Cherenkov-like radiation. For Kael's weapons: a golden-hued
  corona of static lightning, sharp ozone smell, burning afterimages. This is the
  combat-default — prioritizes speed over concealment.
- *Slow summoning:* Will feeds the Template gradually, giving matter time to organize
  quietly. No visible plume. No smell. The item simply becomes present. Costs
  approximately double the Will of a fast summoning. Used when concealment matters.

### Class 2 — Template-Stored Items (Non-Locked)

Objects stored as pure Cognitive Layer Templates but not permanently bonded to a
practitioner. The item does not physically exist between uses — only its Template
does. When needed, the practitioner spends Will to read the Template out, manifesting
the physical object.

**Properties:**
- The item exists only when summoned; it occupies no physical space when stored
- Any practitioner with the Template can summon it, given sufficient Will
- The stored Template can be copied or transferred (unlike Template-locked items)
- If the practitioner storing the Template dies, the Template may degrade and the item
  may be unrecoverable unless the Template was backed up externally
- Repeated summoning and dismissal does not degrade the item's quality, because each
  manifestation reads from the same pristine Template

**Use case:** Practitioners who travel through the Astral and Hyperspace regularly
keep their most important equipment as Template-stored items to eliminate crossing costs
on the outbound trip. They carry only the Template in their Cognitive Layer; the item
exists only at the destination.

**Kael's weapons** are Template-stored (non-locked). She can give the Template to
another practitioner. She has not done so in three hundred years.

### Class 3 — Template-Locked Artifacts (Titan-Wrought and Equivalents)

The highest artifact class. A Template-locked artifact is permanently bound to
a specific practitioner's Template — it is not merely stored or carried, it is
*part of the practitioner's dimensional signature*.

**Properties:**
- The artifact's Template is interwoven with the practitioner's own Template at the
  Cognitive Layer — they share dimensional affinity
- If removed from the practitioner's body, it returns to them when they will it to
- The artifact maintains itself through the practitioner's own Will-maintenance
  cycle, not a separate store — it effectively becomes a body part for Template purposes
- It can be lent: the practitioner can intentionally suppress the return-pull,
  allowing another person to use it. But it will eventually return when the practitioner
  wants it back, regardless of where it is
- It cannot be copied, duplicated, or transferred permanently without destroying
  the practitioner's Template (and likely the practitioner)
- The artifact does not degrade or need recharging as long as the practitioner lives
  and maintains their own Template

**Construction:** Creating a Template-locked artifact requires [Object Template] at
high levels (L3+ for a simple item; L4+ for a complex powered artifact) combined
with a Cognitive Layer bonding working that permanently merges the item Template with
the practitioner's own. The Titan-Forge are the acknowledged masters of this
construction; their artifacts are known as **Titan-Wrought** and represent the
highest quality Template-locked construction available in the galaxy.

**Non-Titan-Wrought locked artifacts exist** but are rarer and often have
construction flaws — imperfect merges that cause edge-case behavior under stress.

**Visual signature:** A Template-locked artifact does not appear as a separate
Astral light source. It is part of the practitioner's own glow. Looking at Kael's
Astral signature, the bracer does not light up separately — it is seamlessly
integrated into her overall Template glow, visible as a particular density or quality
at her left forearm when the bracer's powers are active.

## Kael's Known Artifact Inventory

### The Bracer (Template-Locked, Titan-Wrought)

The most powerful single item carried by any practitioner in the series through Arc 1.

**Physical description:** Left forearm bracer, leather-matched brown with gold tints,
set with a single blue crystal at center. Unremarkable in appearance — it reads as
a decorative piece to an uninformed observer. The crystal activates visibly (deepens
in color, flickers or pulses) when a power is invoked.

**Construction:** Titan-Wrought Template-locked artifact. Bonded to Kael's Template.
Will return to her arm when she wills it, regardless of where it is. Can be lent;
returns on demand.

**Powers:**

`[Force Shield]` — Projects a directional barrier from point source. Scalable from
a few inches in diameter (deflecting a thrown object) to approximately two feet
(covering the upper body against multiple simultaneous projectiles). The shield
matches the momentum vector of any impacting object to Kael's own movement, effectively
absorbing force rather than deflecting it — a sword strike does not ricochet
dangerously; it stops. Extremely difficult to overwhelm through raw force within the
two-foot scale; determined simultaneous pressure from multiple heavy objects can
saturate it. Will cost scales with the momentum absorbed.

Visual signature when active: the shield face glows with a faint blue-white clarity
— the crystal's color extended into space. Objects stopped by the shield briefly
show the same blue-white tint as their momentum is absorbed. In the Astral, the
shield appears as a crisp disc of light with clean geometric edges (reflecting
Titan-Wrought construction precision).

`[Atmosphere Bubble]` — Generates a heated, breathable atmosphere around Kael. Bubble
diameter is approximately two to three meters — sufficient for Kael, Kai, and two
additional human-sized people with modest personal space. Composition is adjustable:
O₂/N₂ ratio tunable; CO₂ scrubbing built in. The bubble's edge is not visible to
the naked eye in atmosphere; in vacuum, it is faintly visible as a subtle distortion
of starlight at its boundary.

Capacity: not enough for more than approximately four human-sized beings without
becoming stuffy and CO₂-rich over extended periods. Five is a hard operational
limit for anything longer than a few hours.

`[Temperature Control]` — Warms or cools the atmosphere inside the bubble. Range:
from just above freezing (keeping the bubble from icing over in the Astral's -20°C
near Fortuna) to near-boiling. Precision is high — Kael can hold a specific
temperature within a degree or two. Will cost scales with the differential between
ambient and target temperatures.

Note: The three powers (`[Force Shield]`, `[Atmosphere Bubble]`, `[Temperature Control]`)
can be run simultaneously, but this triples the Will cost. Kael typically runs
`[Atmosphere Bubble]` and `[Temperature Control]` continuously during Astral/Hyperspace
travel and deploys `[Force Shield]` reactively.

### The Spear (Template-Stored, Non-Locked, [Hardened])

Primary combat weapon. Stored as a Cognitive Layer Template between uses; manifested
on demand.

**Physical description:** Standard spear proportions, scaled to Kael's 6'0" height.
Warm golden color throughout — not metallic gold, but a treated/hardened material
whose Will-saturation produces this hue. The edge and tip are unnaturally precise —
[hardened] sharpness maintains itself through Template pull.

**[Hardened] properties:** Crossing cost dramatically reduced. Physical durability
extreme — the spear will not chip, bend, or break under normal combat stresses.
Can be repaired by Template reinforcement if damaged.

**Summoning signature (fast draw):** Golden lightning-static corona radiates from
the point of manifestation. Sharp ozone scent enters physical space (electromagnetic
component bleeding through). Burning afterimages on any observers. Plume scale:
moderate (object is relatively simple and low-mass).

**Summoning signature (slow draw):** No visible effect. The spear simply becomes
present in her hand over two to three seconds. Double Will cost.

---

### The Knife (Template-Stored, Non-Locked, [Hardened])

Secondary close-quarters weapon. Same construction class as the spear.

**Physical description:** Shorter than a combat knife by conventional standards —
closer to a long dagger. Same warm golden color as the spear. Edge is preternaturally
sharp and self-maintaining; the blade will slice through materials a conventional
knife would not.

**Summoning signature:** Same as the spear but proportionally smaller plume. Faster
to manifest (less mass).

**Tactical use:** First draw in close quarters where the spear is impractical.
Held in the same hand as the spear when both are deployed (right hand; she is
ambidextrous but defaults right for weapons, left for the bracer).

---

### Food and Drink Templates (Template-Stored)

Kael maintains a library of several dozen food and drink Templates in Cognitive Layer
storage. These are not artifacts in the physical sense — they are pure Cognitive Layer
patterns for specific molecular configurations.

**Manifest cost:** Mass-proportional Will. A cup of water costs roughly the same
as any other same-mass summoning. A full meal requires meaningful Will investment.
Emergency water summoning in a depleted state is survivable but leaves her noticeably
drained.

**Quality:** Nutritionally complete and molecularly accurate, but practitioners
consistently describe Will-manifested food as tasting slightly wrong — not unpleasant,
but missing the provenance-quality of food grown, caught, or cooked. Kael has decided
opinions on this. She does not talk about it with humans because explaining the
difference between her grandmother's olive oil and Will-manifested olive oil would
require more Greek than {Aura} can currently translate nuance across.

**Visual signature:** Brief flash of golden light as food manifests (her Will-color
at time of storage). In the Astral, the manifested food item has a faint warm glow
for several minutes before it becomes purely physical. The glow is subtle enough
that Jace notices it before he understands what it means.
