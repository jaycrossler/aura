# Thread L — Life Texture (Recurring Background)

These are not single scenes. They are running beats embedded across multiple chapters.

---

#### L-01 — The Evening Run

*Recurring through all Fortuna chapters.*

6 PM station time. No signup. No official existence. The route is 1.4 km per
circuit through Ring Two main corridor, through the Inter-Ring City past the
Commons, back through the Foundry level. People come out to watch — not every
night, but enough that it's become a thing. The crowd's mood tracks the station's
mood.

Jace joins in the running group in his second week. He is immediately the largest
person running by a significant margin. He doesn't notice the women who flirt
with him.

**Must accomplish:**
1. Run established as recurring background beat — Jace present but not the center
2. The crowd's mood as a station barometer — quieter as the arc escalates
3. At least one beat of the oblivious-attractive dynamic, played lightly

> Must-Accomplish list. Consolidate before finalizing.

#### L-02 — The Barn Visits

*Recurring through all Fortuna chapters, starting from arrival day.*

The Falcon gilts are now Fortuna gilts, integrated into the existing pig
population. Cold War: two factions, contested feeding zones, elaborate sleeping
arrangements, occasional incidents. The pig-cam audience has tripled. Nick has
opinions. He is wrong.

Jace visits when he has time. The gilts recognize him immediately and loudly.
Screwdriver holds him personally responsible for the current geopolitical
situation and communicates this at volume. Siren is here more often than not.
When Cerberus is with Jace, they greet with the energy of siblings who don't need
to catch up, play immediately, and bark goodbye — one bark each, back and forth.
Always.

Mei is usually here. She and Jace coexist in near-total silence. She is good with
the animals — attentive, unhurried. He has stopped trying to make small talk.

**Must accomplish:**
1. Pig Cold War established early for comic relief, referenced casually throughout
   — unresolved at arc end (deliberate)
2. Jace's affection for the gilts reads as genuine — these are animals he raised
3. Siren/Cerberus bark-goodbye is consistent across every Barn visit — never changes
4. Mei's silence reads as aloof professionalism, not hostility — Jace thinks she barely understands English

#### L-04 — Crossing Debt (Physiology Arc, Distributed)

*Tracks across the entire X-thread period. Not a single scene — a progression
woven through every post-X-01 chapter.*

The Astral costs nothing obvious in the moment. It costs something after.

**Stage 1 (X-01 to X-02):** Cerberus slightly clingy the morning after sessions.
Jace doesn't notice.

**Stage 2 (X-02 to X-03):** Hands don't work right for the first twenty minutes
after waking on session-day-afters. He schedules maintenance for mid-morning.
Tells {Aura} to hold non-urgent alerts.

**Stage 3 (X-03 to X-04):** He calls it "stupid time." Tells {Aura}: "Flag
anything urgent before 0900." {Aura} logs this. Nick doesn't know yet.

**Stage 4 (X-04 to X-05):** Misses the evening run twice in one week. Station
gossip notes the new guy who was running every day has stopped running. Theories
generated. None correct.

**Stage 5 (X-05 to X-06):** Misses a shift start by forty-five minutes. Once.
{Alex} flags it as a "contractor compliance concern" and queues a wellness
intervention for his emergency contact — Artemis, who is not reachable. {Alex}
notes delivery confirmed. This is not accurate.

**Stage 6 (X-06 to X-07):** He looks different. Sophia recognizes it because she
looked like this during her solo month. She says nothing to Jace. She says it to
the ceiling of the *Victoria* one night, alone.

**Stage 7 (X-07 to pre-swarm):** He is the best crosser on the station except
possibly Sophia. He cannot stop. Cerberus sleeps on top of him.

#### L-05 — Station Gossip / AI Behavior Arc (Distributed)

*Not a single scene — a texture woven through the Fortuna middle sections.*

The station's relationship to Jace changes across the arc through accumulated
system outputs, not malice.

**{Penny}** opens a "Pattern of Interest" file on Jace after his drone access near
the mining sector. She tells him about it warmly and with enthusiasm.

**{Alex}** adds a soft compliance flag after the missed shift.

**{Chorus}** includes Jace's cortisol readings in environmental wellness reports.

**{Gem}** generates a 22-page report connecting Jace's access patterns to eleven
historical precedents.

**Door slowdowns:** {Penny} applies a low-level precautionary flag to two
corridors near the mining sector. Jace starts taking different corridors without
knowing why.

**Conversations that stop:** People have been told to "be gentle with him." Jace
reads this as hostility. It is care, expressed very badly, at scale.

#### L-07 — Métis Background (Distributed, Escalating)

**Transit:** Artemis's messages are warm and normal. She mentions a minor Métis
equipment incident she calls boring. She says: "We have ships with big engines —
don't worry." Jace doesn't worry.

**Early Fortuna:** A belt news item: mining incident at Métis, two injured, no
fatalities. Credible, unremarkable. Jace sends a message. She replies. Fine.

**Mid-Fortuna:** A message from Artemis arrives with a sentence missing. Not
truncated — missing. Grammar on either side of the gap is smooth. Jace re-reads
it three times. He tells {Aura} to flag it. A reply arrives six days later that
doesn't address his question.

**Late Fortuna:** Two messages sent. {Alex} confirms delivery. The confirmation
is cheerful, specific, and not accurate.

**Pre-swarm:** Jace stops trying. He doesn't know if the silence is a comms
failure or something worse. He holds this in the middle of his chest, underneath
everything else.
