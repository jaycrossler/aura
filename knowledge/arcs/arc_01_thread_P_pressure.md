#### P-01a — The Bar, Suzi (Friendship Beat)

**Summary:** Suzi finds Jace at the Long Burn Bar. She is good company — the kind
of person who asks questions because she finds people interesting, not because
she is working. Cerberus approves immediately. They stay too long. Jace walks back
to the inn feeling, for the first time since Mars, like someone on the station
actually knows he is there.

No plot function. No Helena. The bar is warm and someone is doing something
inadvisable with the good whiskey and Cerberus gets pieces of pretzel from three
different tables.

**Must accomplish:**
1. Suzi established as genuinely warm — the friendship is real before it becomes useful
2. The bar established as the social space where station gossip lives
3. Jace feels sad after his first mediocre day at work then less alone; worth showing he is lonely

#### P-01b — Suzi's Ask / Helena / The Miner's Wound

**Summary:** Later, Suzi tells Jace that Jin Lóng is the person of interest in
Helena's disappearance — nothing rises to evidence, the case is technically
closed. She is precise about what she has and doesn't have. She is not satisfied.

Then, quietly: Jace has access she doesn't. Drone eyes go places station security
cameras don't. She is not asking him to surveil Jin. She is asking him to tell her
if he notices anything unusual. "One new resident to another. You see things
differently when you haven't been here long enough to stop seeing them."

Jace says yes without fully understanding what he's agreed to.

The bar produces, incidentally: a miner at a nearby table mentions an injury to a
colleague. Massive wound. Suit intact. No logged failure. The table moves on. Jace
doesn't.

That night, back in his room, Cerberus warm against his feet and the stuffed pig
under her chin, he is still enough for once. And then he dreams he sees a dead
girl floating in space.

**Must accomplish:**
1. Helena close-out delivered professionally — precise about what she has
2. Suzi's ask explicit: Jace has access she doesn't; he agrees without knowing the
   full implication
3. Miner's wound planted and left — the bar moves on; Jace doesn't

**Must NOT do:**
- Make Suzi manipulative — she genuinely thinks Jace is the right person to ask
- Show Jin as guilty — this is Suzi's working theory, not the text's verdict
- Explain how Jace sees things in the Astral — he doesn't know that's what is happening
- Forget Cerberus — make him part of the experience
- Play Jace as a heavy drinker — he's lonely and excited to meet new people

#### P-04 — The Quiet War (Jin's Pre-Swarm Fighting Thread)

**Summary:** Three months before the main swarm attack, Jin's crew found their
first nest in the deepest mining tunnel — something the size of a large dog with
too many legs. They killed it with drill bits and heat. {Seek} logged the thermal
data. Beijing did not respond. Jin and his crew were already too afraid of {Seek}
to file an honest report, and an honest report would have said his hands were on
fire and he didn't know why.

So they kept finding nests. Eleven total, cleared quietly, systematically. By the
time of the main swarm attack, Jin's crew are the most combat-experienced Astral
fighters on the station — by a significant margin. Three crew members have
developed modest force abilities alongside Jin. They have improvised weapons:
drill lances adapted for close work, thermal cutters rewired for directed heat
output, and Jin's hands.

This thread runs as background through the later Fortuna section. The reader
learns about it in retrospect — Jace learns during the fight itself.

#### P-05 — {Seek} Flags Jace / Yáo Confrontation

**Summary:** {Seek} has been monitoring Jace's access patterns since he arrived.
The data is not ambiguous from a compliance AI's perspective: US military
veteran, prior drone operations specialist, station access above the security
team's level, drones observed near the Chinese mining sector three times in eight
days. {Seek} flags this to the crew. HUD translation approximately: "Pattern of
access by US-affiliated contractor Grant consistent with reconnaissance activity.
Recommend heightened situational awareness." Technically accurate. Devastating to
the crew's threat model.

{Seek} has been feeding a Bloated Tick-Maw for months. Its threat-assessment
model is subtly miscalibrated — more paranoid than baseline. This does not make
the flag inaccurate. It makes it more paranoid than it would otherwise be.

Yáo corners Jace in a maintenance corridor. No weapon needed. He has been
clearing Astral fauna in tunnels for three months with improvised equipment. The
accusation is flat. Jace is confused, defensive, baffled that legitimate access
has been read this way. He tries to explain. Yáo does not find explanation
relevant. He walks away.

Jace tells Suzi. Suzi assumes Jin ordered it. Jace isn't sure. Neither should the
reader be.

Sophia, when Jace tells her: *"You keep coming back to Jin. I think the data you
have might not be about what you think it's about."* Jace has no good answer.

**Must accomplish:**
1. {Seek}'s flag is technically accurate — Jace IS doing reconnaissance (Suzi
   asked him to); the AI is right about the behavior and wrong about the intent
2. Yáo is capable and scarred, not merely hostile — he is right that Jace is a
   potential threat to his crew; he is wrong about the motive
3. Jin is absent from this scene entirely — whether he ordered it is genuinely
   ambiguous and must remain so
4. Sophia's calibration lands — she is questioning Jace's certainty, not his data

**Must NOT do:**
- Show Jin giving any instruction about this confrontation
- Make Yáo a simple villain

#### P-08 — Jin and Jace, "I Know"

**Summary:** The scene where Jin and Jace have their first real human exchange is
not dramatic. It is incidental. They are both in the Long Burn Bar at an unusual
hour — late, after most people have gone to sleep. Jace is there because he is
avoiding his room. Jin is there because he is carrying something and the bar is
empty.

They don't talk about the fire. They don't talk about the corridors. They talk
about something small — the quality of the recycled water in Ring Three (worse
than Ring One, a consistent grievance).

At some point Jin says: "You should be careful in the maintenance corridors near
the server section." Flat. Testing whether Jace understands what kind of warning
it is. Jace says: "I know." Not: I'll avoid it. Not: thank you. Just: I know.

Jin looks at him for a moment and then looks away. They finish their drinks and
leave separately.

This is the beginning of something. Not yet friendship — two people who have been
circling each other deciding, independently, that the other one is real. The
swarm fight and Yáo's sacrifice build on this. Arc 2 builds on this.

**Must accomplish:**
1. No confrontation, no revelation, no explanation — a real human exchange with
   the subtext entirely submerged
2. Jin's warning is ambiguous: could mean "I know you were there" or "that area is
   dangerous, I know from experience" — Jace doesn't resolve it
3. The register shift is felt, not stated
4. This scene earns the reader's investment in Yáo's sacrifice

**Must NOT do:**
- Resolve any of the Jin/Jace tension — that belongs to Arc 2
- Have either character say anything direct about fire, walls, or the Bloated Maw
