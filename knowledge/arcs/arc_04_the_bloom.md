# Arc 4 — Galactic Prisoners

## Opening Condition

Kael's Template regenerates at her vault anchor. The Bloom tracked the regeneration thread during Arc 3's closing events. They are already there when she arrives.

Jace, Jin, Cerberus, Sparky, and Kai are in Bloom custody, in transit. The Bloom move between solar systems in extended Hyperspace hikes, forcing their prisoners to travel with them.

## Closing Condition

During a Bloom encounter with a dangerous predator pack near a Scar-adjacent system, Jace, Jin, Kael (now rejoined), and the alien prisoners overwhelm their captors and escape. They navigate back to Sol as a mixed group. They arrive at Fortuna as something new: the first humans to have been in galactic space and returned with friends.

At arc close, a Titan-Forge arrival signature is detected in the outer system. The debt-collection call is incoming.

## Background Threads

**Thread A — Sol System Without Jace**
During the months of transit, Sol continues. Sophia is the primary point of contact for Earth and Mars institutions. Nick is running the {Aura} distributed network. Fortuna is rebuilding from the Bloom ransack. The Kael Will message is in transit toward the Woven; ~year one of two. Nobody knows where Jace is except in outline.

**Thread B — Eugene's Framework Advances**
Eugene is at Metis. He has comms access to Fortuna when the relay is working. He is watching the data from Jace's pre-departure {Aura} syncs remotely, via the Metis-Fortuna relay. His theoretical framework is advancing. He is also, separately, working on `WHAT IS A SHIP FOR (REVISED)`. These two projects are converging in ways he has not stated aloud.

**Thread C — The Dungeon Seed Consumption**
The Bloom consume the dungeon seed's stored Will during the transit. From their perspective this is resource gathering — the Will is unowned, therefore available. From Kael's perspective (observed from imprisonment), this is desecration. The process takes time. Its completion is a story beat in itself.

**Thread D — Kael's Captivity (Separate from Jace)**
Kael is held separately from Jace and Jin — the Bloom understand that the high-Will entity should not be co-located with the humans she has been directing. Her separation means she does not know what Jace is doing. He does not know how she is. Their reunion is the arc's emotional midpoint.

## Scene Contracts

### Scene C-01 — Kael Regenerates

**The physical specifics:**
Kael's Template reconstitutes at the vault anchor — the weapon she left on the distant asteroid. She emerges from nothing into cold vacuum in a body that is technically complete but has not yet developed the full coherence of her trained state. She is for the first seconds genuinely disoriented — regeneration is not waking from sleep; it is closer to being assembled.

The Bloom are already there. They tracked the regeneration signature from the Arc 3 killing field.

The roots begin before she has fully oriented. The process is biological and deliberate — not aggressive in the way predation is aggressive, but inexorable in the way that a plant is inexorable. The root structure entwines and the pressure is real.

The demand, communicated through chromatophore patterns she can partially read: cede your Will into the root network, or be drained until Template continuity ends permanently.

She does not cede immediately. She is not built for immediate submission. She holds for as long as she can. She is in a body that is still assembling. She is in vacuum. She is alone.

She cedes enough to survive. Not fully. Not everything. The Bloom accept this as the beginning of a negotiation that will continue.

**Must accomplish:**
1. The regeneration experience is specific and not comfortable — she is not reborn, she is reassembled; the difference matters
2. The root-capture is shown as biological and patient, not aggressive; the Bloom are not being cruel; they are harvesting; the horror comes from their indifference
3. Her partial cession is shown as a tactical decision, not a defeat — she is managing the situation until a better situation exists

**Must NOT do:**
- Make the regeneration clean or triumphant — it is the worst possible context for waking up
- Make the Bloom villainous — they are following process

### Scene C-02 — Jace and Jin in Transit (Distributed, Multiple Scenes)

**The mechanics:**
The Bloom travel in extended Hyperspace hikes. Their distance limits are vastly greater than any Sol human's. Jace and Jin have not significantly extended their limits yet — their useful transit range is measured in AU, not light-years.

When they hit their distance limits, the atomic tension becomes unbearable — the crossing cost accumulates against biological tolerance. The Bloom's response is not medical care. It is Will feeding: they push Will into the prisoners' systems to extend the tolerable range, then require them to continue.

This is genuinely painful. It is also genuinely effective. Over two months of this treatment, Jace and Jin's distance limits expand significantly. The Bloom are not trying to hurt them; they are solving a logistics problem. The fact that the solution involves sustained pain is not a consideration.

**Must accomplish:**
1. At least two scenes where the distance limit hits and is pushed through — specific, physical, not summarized
2. The Will feeding is shown as alien care: the Bloom are maintaining the health of their cargo; the cargo's comfort is not a variable they measure
3. Jace and Jin's bond deepens under this pressure — they are the only people who speak the same language; the relationship that develops is its own character arc

**Must NOT do:**
- Make this a montage — the progression has to be felt
- Make the Bloom sadistic — they are indifferent; that is worse

### Scene C-03 — The Other Prisoners

**The prisoner group:**
The Bloom have other prisoners from nearby species captured during their current resource-gathering circuit. They are held in separate air bubbles. The exact species composition is TBD (flagged for dedicated development), but the following structural facts apply:
- At least three distinct species/groups
- At least one of them has been in Bloom captivity significantly longer than Jace and Jin
- At least one of them initially mistrusts or fears humans (humans are a new species to them; new species mean new threats)
- Kai and Cerberus are the social breakthrough mechanism — they cross the species gap before the humanoids can

**Must accomplish:**
1. The first contact with the other prisoners is established as a genuine communication challenge — no common language, no common reference points; {Aura} has nothing to work with yet
2. The mistrust is specific and understandable — from the other prisoners' perspective, these humans showed up with a high-Will entity and are now here; what does that mean?
3. The animal bridge: Cerberus approaches; Cerberus is recognized; the recognition opens something

### Scene C-04 — The Dungeon Seed Consumed

**Must accomplish:**
1. Kael observes the consumption from her separate captivity — she can see the Will signature diminishing
2. Her reaction is the scene: she knows what that seed was, what its age represented, what was stored in it; the reader has enough context from Arc 3 to understand why this matters
3. The consumption is complete. The Will runs out. The Bloom move toward the next source in their circuit. This is what interstellar Bloom transit means.

### Scene C-05 — Reunion

**Must accomplish:**
1. Kael is brought to the main prisoner group at a point in the transit — the Bloom's logistics reasoning for this is not explained, but is derivable from their behavior patterns
2. Jace's reading of Kael's condition is the scene's core beat — he can see something is different; she is technically intact but something about her movement has changed; the partial cession cost something
3. Neither of them says much — the language situation is still partial, and emotional expression is not how either of them operates under stress

**Must NOT do:**
- Make this a reunion scene in the conventional sense — they are both in captivity; the reunion is not relief, it is tactical information
- Skip the visible difference in Kael — the partial cession should be legible

### Scene C-06 — The Plan Develops

**The planning challenge:**
Communication across species, incomplete language, hostile environment, captors who do not sleep, a recording that Jace knows exists but cannot access conventionally.

{Aura}'s recordings from Arc 2/3 contain the Bloom patrol patterns, the response times to disturbances, the weak positions in their formation, the chromatophore patterns associated with different operational states. Jace has been building this picture during transit. He cannot transmit the recording — but he can describe what it shows.

Kael's contribution is tactical: she knows the Bloom well enough to know that a predator encounter is the scenario where their formation weakens most. They focus on the threat. Their prisoner management degrades.

Jin's contribution: fire. Lots of it. Against beings that hate fire, coordinated fire is the best possible weapon.

The alien prisoners' contribution: their specific capabilities. TBD in dedicated session, but at minimum one of the prisoner groups has abilities that complement the humans.

**Must accomplish:**
1. The planning process is shown as genuinely difficult — not a clever montage but a series of partial communications that slowly become a coherent plan
2. Each contributor's role is specific — the plan works because of what each person/species can do, not because of general heroism
3. The predator encounter as the trigger condition is established clearly enough that when it happens the reader understands the timing is not luck

---

### Scene C-07 — The Escape

**The mechanics:**
The predator attack on the Bloom formation is real and dangerous — the Bloom engage it seriously. This is the window.

Jin's fire capabilities at full capacity are the opening move — coordinated fire at multiple Bloom simultaneously, targeting the ones holding specific prisoners. The fire does not kill them, but it disrupts their attention and degrades their cohesion.

Hydrogen is the fuel source Jace has been building toward — small amounts, improvised delivery, combined with the fire already established. The explosions are not massive. They do not need to be. They need to be disorienting to beings who hate fire and are already managing a predator attack.

The shadow tactic from the recording: blocking the Bloom's light sources simultaneously as a disorientation multiplier. They are fighting the predators, the fire, the explosions, and suddenly the light is wrong. Their chromatophore communication becomes unreliable.

The escape window is short. They take it.

**Must accomplish:**
1. The chaos is specific and not glamorous — people get hurt; the plan has at least one failure that has to be improvised around
2. Jin's fire is the primary weapon; his arc from "emerging abilities" to "this is what he can do" closes here
3. Kael's contribution is navigation — she gets them into Hyperspace fast, before the Bloom can regroup

**Must NOT do:**
- Make the Bloom into monsters they are not — they are overwhelmed by a combination of circumstances they did not anticipate; this is their failure, not their villainy
- Clean it up — escapes are messy; not everyone has what they need; not everything works

### Scene C-10 — The Signal

**Short scene. End of arc.**

Eugene's monitoring equipment picks up a Hyperspace approach signature. He brings it to Nick. Nick brings it to Jace. Jace brings it to Kael.

Kael looks at the signature for a long moment. Then she says, in fully fluent {Aura}-translated Woven: *"That is very old."*

She does not say what it is yet. The reader, if they have been paying attention, knows.
