# Arc 1 — The Falcon and Fortuna

## Opening Condition

Jace Grant boards the *Falcon* at Mars as a newly-discharged civilian contractor — identity stripped, carrying a HUD debt, a dog he negotiated for, and a covert intelligence arrangement he doesn't fully understand. He is heading to the asteroid belt because there is nowhere else to go.

## Closing Condition

A swarm attack on Fortuna Station is underway or just repelled. Jace and others have been fighting it with improvised means. A figure arrives — Kael — tailing the swarm, expecting ruins and primitive survivors. She finds humans in physical space with no magic. She attempts dominance. It fails. She finds Cerberus. Arc 1 closes. Arc 2 opens.

## Background Threads (Parallel — Reader Tracks, Protagonist Cannot)

**Thread A — Sophia's Prior Weeks**
Sophia has been crossing alone for a month before Jace arrives. The reader gets fragments — her exhaustion, her absence from normal station life, her crewmates' worry. The mystery of what she's doing has been building in the station before Jace gets there.

**Thread B — Helena**
Fortuna's first un-suppressible disappearance happened before Jace arrived. Suzi has been investigating. The official story is incomplete. The China withdrawal is being misread as sabotage. The station is already under stress before the swarm.

**Thread C — Comms Degradation**
The communications filtering architecture is tightening. Jace's attempts to reach Artemis at Odysseus keep failing in small ways — delayed messages, strange gaps, one message that arrives with a sentence missing. The reader knows something is wrong before Jace does.

**Thread D — The Pig-Cam**
Nick Lee on Fortuna follows the *Falcon*'s pig-cam like a nature documentary. He and Jace are already friends. The comms lag is shrinking. The connection that will become central to the open-source decision is already being built, casually, through multiplayer gaming and animal antics.

## Scene Contracts

### Scene 1-01 — Mars Departure Sequence (Pre-Transit)

**Must accomplish:**
1. Jace's identity loss is shown, not described — what he had, what it cost, what it's left him with
2. Cerberus is established as a negotiated-for companion, not a given — the reader understands what he represents
3. The intelligence arrangement with Sylvester is established as vague and uneasy — Jace doesn't know what he agreed to

**Must NOT do:**
- Explain the intelligence arrangement — Jace doesn't understand it and neither should the reader yet
- Make Jace's losses purely self-pitying — the hard months are about scrambling, not grieving

---

### Scene 1-02 — The Falcon Transit (Distributed — Multiple Scenes)

**Must accomplish:**
1. Four humans on an 80-meter ring with 54 animals and two dogs — the confinement is real and the relationships develop through friction and routine, not dramatic events
2. Jace's engineer identity is rebuilt through competence: drone work, maintenance, the arrangement with Maureen
3. Saul's remote presence is felt through voice notes and Maureen's private readings of them

**Must NOT do:**
- Rush the transit — this is where the characters are established
- Make Maureen openly warm — her warmth is private and deniable
- Forget that Mei is always slightly observing

---

### Scene 1-03 — The Plumbing Crisis

**Must accomplish:**
1. The engineering solution is the *Falcon*'s own prescribed response — earned, not lucky
2. The win has a real cost: contaminated water reserve, thinner safety margin, Origin reprimand
3. Maureen's fury is justified, not arbitrary — she is right that he gambled the ship's margin

**Must NOT do:**
- Resolve the tension between Jace and Maureen — it deepens
- Skip the cost — the contamination matters for the rest of transit

---

### Scene 1-04 — The Crystal Tunnel Dreams

**Must accomplish:**
1. The dream is specific: vast cold cave, faceted walls lit from within, blue-white, humming below hearing
2. Cerberus on his chest when he wakes — the dog knows something is happening
3. No explanation — the reader registers it before Jace does

---

### Scene 1-05 — Arrival at Fortuna

**Must accomplish:**
1. The station is already stressed: China withdrawal fallout, the Falcon sabotage misread, Helena's disappearance, Suzi's investigation
2. Nick Lee is there as an established friend, not a stranger — the comms-lag friendship is real
3. The "Learning Mode" sewage footage is already station legend — Jace arrives into his own reputation

---

### Scene 1-06 — Meeting Sophia (First Crossing)

**Must accomplish:**
1. Cerberus pulls the mask — not Jace's decision; the dog initiates
2. Hours of solo exploration before finding Sophia in the mess hall — no instant meeting
3. Sophia is startled by Cerberus, not by Jace — the dog's barking breaks the silence
4. Sophia is exhausted, gaunt, intensely alive — a month of solo crossings has cost her something real

**Must NOT do:**
- Make the meeting romantic or dramatically charged — it is two scientists discovering they are not alone
- Explain the Astral to the reader through dialogue — show it

---

### Scene 1-07 — Partnership and Discovery

**Must accomplish:**
1. Sophia's existing framework (seventeen notebooks, empirical measurements) meets Jace's engineering instinct
2. At least one discovery that neither could have made alone — the temperature probe, the drone prospecting, the open-space exploration
3. Nick is brought in when the drone data requires outside analysis — the circle expands with purpose

---

### Scene 1-08 — The Open-Source Decision

**Must accomplish:**
1. The decision is genuine and has stakes — not distributing the Astral data means it stays controlled; distributing it means it spreads beyond anyone's control
2. Nick and Jace make it together — Nick's reasoning is practical and political; Jace's is instinctive
3. The reader understands this is the moment that changes what the belt is

**Must NOT do:**
- Make the decision feel inevitable — it is a choice with an alternative

---

### Scene 1-09 — Fortuna Under Pressure (Pre-Swarm)

**Must accomplish:**
1. The station's two-face dynamic: public containment of the Astral truth vs. the private reality
2. Jin Lóng established as a Fortuna presence — his abilities emerging
3. The comms blackout beginning to tighten — Jace's attempts to reach Artemis failing in small ways

---

### Scene 1-10 — The Swarm Attack

**Must accomplish:**
1. The swarm is genuinely dangerous — this is not a drill
2. The improvised defense shows what Fortuna has developed: rust-guns, analog pivot, engineering solutions
3. Jin Lóng fighting is significant — the reader sees what developing practitioners can do
4. The scale is survivable but barely — the station is not fine

**Must NOT do:**
- Resolve cleanly — the arc closes on the edge, not on victory

---

### Scene 1-11 — Kael Arrives (Arc Close)

**Must accomplish:**
1. Kael's arrival reads as something categorically different from the swarm — a presence, not a threat (yet)
2. The failed dominance attempt lands without explanation — humans just don't respond
3. Cerberus and Kai find each other — the arc closes on this image
