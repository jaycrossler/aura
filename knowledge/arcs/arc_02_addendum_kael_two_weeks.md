# Proposed Scene — "The Two Weeks"

**Recommended placement:** After Arc 2's existing `A-01 (First Contact at Fortuna)`
and `A-02 (The Language Project)`, before or interleaved with `A-02a` (the
dungeon-crisis distributed scenes). This is a retrospective/investigative beat,
not a forward-plot beat — it recontextualizes what "first contact" actually meant
once people start asking the obvious question.

**Why here:** `S-02b` (Arc 1) already establishes Kael was in the planning session
"under occultation" before anyone knew she was there. This scene is where that
gets its full weight — she wasn't hiding for an hour. She was hiding for two weeks,
living among them.

## Summary

Once the dust settles and the station starts asking questions about who Kael
actually is and how long she's been around, Nick — predictably — goes to the
footage. He starts pulling station camera records to build a timeline of her
movements, expecting to find her arrival correlated with the swarm.

Instead, he finds her almost two weeks earlier than that.

She looks rough in the earliest footage — beat up, haggard, clearly exhausted
from however she got here, hollow with hunger, and (this should read as
genuinely funny once the horror settles) she smells. Somehow, in that state, she
talked her way into a room at Erin's inn. Nobody knows how — Erin doesn't fully
know how, when asked; she just remembers being charmed and slightly steamrolled,
which is Erin's normal experience of running the inn, so it didn't register as
strange at the time.

For the first several days of footage, Kael barely leaves the room — sleeping,
eating everything Erin brings her, visibly unwell. She catches what looks like a
cold, then something that looks like the flu. Whatever she is, she is not immune
to Sol-human pathogens, and nobody was watching closely enough to notice an alien
running a fever in a rented room.

After a few days she recovers, and the footage changes character. She starts
exploring — corridors, the Commons, the Barn, Drone Ops, the bar — moving like
someone conducting a survey rather than someone sightseeing. Throughout, she's
muttering. In ancient Greek. To herself, mostly, occasionally at people who have
no idea they're being addressed.

**The comedy beat:** once Nick (and whoever else gets pulled into the translation
project — this scene can overlap with `A-02`) starts running the audio through
partial translation, some of what surfaces is unexpectedly rude, or unexpectedly
funny, or both — a 300-year-old scout's unfiltered commentary on Sol humans,
caught on camera, translated after the fact by people who now have to sit with
the fact that she's been narrating her opinion of them for two weeks.

## Must Accomplish

1. The footage discovery should land as unsettling before it lands as funny — the
   "she was here the whole time, we just didn't know" beat needs its full weight
   before the comedy defuses it
2. Erin's unwitting hospitality is played straight — she genuinely didn't clock
   anything wrong, which says more about Erin's baseline chaos tolerance than
   about Kael's disguise
3. Kael's illness (cold, then flu-like) is a small but real worldbuilding beat:
   whatever she is, she is not immune to Sol pathogens — worth a line acknowledging
   this is new information
4. The Greek muttering is the seed for Nick's translation project (`A-02`) —
   this scene can supply some of the raw audio material he starts working from
5. At least one or two translated lines should land as genuinely funny — rude,
   dry, or blunt commentary rather than anything grand or prophetic

## Must NOT Do

- Explain why Kael chose to hide rather than announce herself — that belongs to
  the Protocol Crisis material already drafted in `A-03`
- Make Erin complicit or suspicious in any way — the comedy depends on her not
  having known
- Over-translate the Greek — partial, contested translations are funnier and
  more realistic than clean subtitles

## Open Questions

- Exact translated lines — needs a dedicated brainstorm session; author's call
  on tone (dry? imperious? exhausted?)
- Whether Erin gets a scene of her own reacting to the footage, or whether this
  stays entirely Nick's investigation
- Whether any of what Kael explored/observed during the two weeks becomes
  plot-relevant later (she had two weeks of unsupervised access to the station —
  does she know something as a result that no one accounts for?)
