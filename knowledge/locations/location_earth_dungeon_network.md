# Earth Dungeon Network

## Overview

Earth was not governed by one magical dynasty before the Scar.

It contained multiple regional powers, Houses, temples, governments, trade systems, military orders, and patron networks. Each practiced Will and Template engineering differently.

Across the ancient world, these groups commissioned protected facilities for governance, storage, continuity, training, healing, travel, and private use.

The surviving network is not one unified system. It is a field of unrelated or partially related Cognitive programs layered beneath modern civilization.

## Scale

Working estimate:

- 60-80 major original dungeon cores
- 150-300 minor annexes and protected rooms
- 20-30 cores destroyed or dissolved
- 20-40 dormant or inaccessible
- 15-25 entering intermittent reopening
- 5-10 migrated or branched far from their original region
- 2-4 with off-Earth branches before full galactic reconnection

These figures remain uncertain because one dungeon may have multiple entrances, while several apparently distinct sites may be branches of the same core.

## Original Distribution

Major cores likely clustered near ancient population and power centers across the eastern Mediterranean, Nile Valley, Mesopotamia, Iranian plateau, South Asia, China, Mesoamerica, the Andes, West Africa, Central Asian routes, and northern European sacred or political centers.

Ancient religious memory preserves distorted fragments of real Will-network leaders, institutions, visitors, and magical events.

## Fixed Sites

Some dungeons remain attached to continuing places such as civic archives, harbors, court districts, mountains, temple hospitals, or battlefields.

## Migrated Sites

Dynastic and institutional anchors moved with people.

A dungeon founded in one region may now maintain branches in Frankfurt, London, New York, Singapore, Mumbai, Beijing, Cairo, Mexico City, orbital habitats, or Mars settlements.

The migration follows continuity, not simple population.

## New York

New York likely contains several migrated branches rather than one indigenous ancient core.

Possible sources include merchant and banking dynasties, diplomatic institutions, migrated family vaults, archive or treasury annexes, and portable anchors carried through immigration.

Its dungeons may interpret finance, media, property, identity, immigration, and celebrity through ancient institutional purposes.

## Frankfurt

Frankfurt is a strong location for merchant House continuities, banking and treasury dungeons, imperial legal succession, contract archives, debt systems, and dynastic vaults that migrated along European trade routes.

A Frankfurt dungeon may contain layers from Roman trade, medieval fairs, early banking, industrial finance, European integration, and algorithmic markets.

## Mars

Mars should contain only a few early branches, each with a traceable anchor such as a family heirloom, government continuity seed, military relic, corporate archive key, religious object, Template-bearing heir, or portable Titan-Forge interface.

Mars branches may initially be small: one extra room, a recurring corridor, a locked medical chamber, a training space, or a vault that opens only during local breaches.

## Network Behavior

Some dungeons may communicate as clearing improves. They may share modern interface Templates, exchange role definitions, transfer entrants, synchronize threats, compare authority claims, reconstruct road links, or form a distributed institutional intelligence.

Networking does not imply cooperation. Ancient rivals may resume old conflicts.

## Public Emergence

Early reopening will appear as disconnected incidents: missing persons, impossible rooms, sensor anomalies, shared dreams, unexplained injuries, artifacts appearing, building plans becoming inaccurate, AI systems reporting inaccessible internal volumes, or people returning with role marks and new skills.

Governments and corporations will initially classify incidents as security breaches, cyberattacks, hoaxes, psychological events, experimental technology, terrorism, contamination, or classified infrastructure.

## Narrative Use

The Earth network provides global cultural variety, ancient political history, modern institutional satire, progression environments, archaeology, family mysteries, off-world propagation, and conflicts over ownership.

No dungeon should exist merely to provide combat. Each should reveal a specific ancient institution and the modern concepts that changed it.
