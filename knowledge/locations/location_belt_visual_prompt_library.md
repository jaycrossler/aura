# Belt Visual Prompt Library

## Use Guidance

These prompts are designed for two purposes:
- Visual generation tools.
- Chapter-writing checks for whether a scene includes believable station details.

The tone should remain realistic. Avoid shiny fantasy corridors, pointless greebling, huge decorative windows in rotating sections, and spotless surfaces. These are working industrial settlements with good engineering, careful safety discipline, and limited slack.

Recurring visual rules:
- Fortuna's rotating ring corridors have few windows to prevent dizziness and spin disorientation.
- Public ceilings often use LED cloudscapes, artificial skies, tree canopies, or calm abstract weather.
- Triple redundancy is visible in emergency lockers, backup panels, duplicate valves, signs, and personal breathers.
- Robots are common but mostly functional: cleaning, inspection, carrying, repairing, pruning, loading, sealing, and checking.
- One-third gravity changes motion: longer steps, softer falls, foot loops, rails, grip soles, and occasional over-lifting.
- Surface asteroid work happens in extremely low gravity with tethers, anchors, crawlers, and careful movements.

## Fortuna Station Prompts

### 1. Ring Two Main Street

A spacious rotating-habitat corridor on Fortuna Station, about 6 meters wide and 3.5 meters high, curving subtly with the ring. There are almost no real windows. The ceiling is an LED cloudscape showing a slow artificial morning with pale blue sky and soft clouds. Shops, benches, wall gardens, public status displays, emergency mask lockers, pressure bulkhead seams, and breather recharge points line the corridor. People walk in one-third gravity with long, careful steps. Clothing is practical: layered work jackets, soft boots, tool clips, ID patches, and emergency breathers at the hip. Cleaning robots glide along the walls while small courier bots carry sealed food drawers. The style is realistic, industrial, lived-in, and over-engineered.

### 2. Founders Ring Old Corridor

An older corridor in Ring One of Fortuna Station, about 3 meters wide, with mismatched pressure panels, patched conduit covers, worn handrails, old pod doors, memorial plaques, and labels printed over older labels. The ceiling has a warm LED sky strip rather than a full cloudscape. A wall-crawling inspection robot checks a pressure seam. Residents carry breathers and move with relaxed one-third gravity habits. The space feels safe because it has been repaired hundreds of times, not because it is new.

### 3. Inter-Ring Commons

A broad public commons between Fortuna's first two rotating rings, with gantries, pod stacks, vertical transit towers, market decks, pedestrian bridges, greenhouse alcoves, and robot lanes. The ceiling panels show calm projected weather. Cargo robots follow marked routes while children and workers cross at designated points. Signs point to Sky Gardens, the Market, Founders Ring, Civic Ring, Docking, and the Foundry. The visual mood is busy, civic, practical, and frontier-industrial.

### 4. Sky Gardens

Fortuna Station's Sky Gardens, with hydroponic trees, vertical plant racks, mossy seating walls, narrow water features, and a ceiling LED sky showing slow clouds. Residents sit quietly or walk in one-third gravity along a 4-meter-wide path with handrails. A small pruning robot works in the background. The lighting is bright and therapeutic. The space feels like a life-support garden that became a park because humans needed it.

### 5. The Barn

Interior of Fortuna Station's Barn, a bright animal and agriculture facility with sealed livestock pods, veterinary windows, aquaculture tanks, feed conveyors, and low exercise lanes for goats, rabbits, chickens, working dogs, and a small number of larger animals. The corridor is about 5 meters wide with washable floors, drainage channels, humidity haze, full-spectrum lights, and biosecurity stations. Children watch through thick transparent panels while farm workers in coveralls and breathers check animal health. Cleaning robots scrub constantly. The scene feels warm, organic, and precious inside a metal station.

### 6. Barn Birth Watch

A small crowd gathers outside a transparent animal-care room in Fortuna's Barn during a live birth or hatching. Children, engineers, farmers, and off-shift miners watch quietly. A veterinary worker in clean coveralls checks monitors. The walls show humidity readings, oxygen balance, biosecurity status, and live camera feeds. The crowd wears normal station clothes with breathers clipped at the hip. The emotional tone is restrained but intense because animal life is community life.

### 7. Dockside Bar

A small dockside bar near Fortuna's transient berths, with a low ceiling, old pressure panels mounted as wall decor, bolted tables, foot rails for one-third gravity, and a menu board listing vat stew, algae cakes, goat cheese, yeast bread, and rare real eggs. Ship crews, miners, and station workers crowd the room in scuffed work clothing. A courier bot waits by the kitchen hatch. The air looks slightly hazy from warm food and too many people in a sealed room. The tone is rough but not lawless.

### 8. Gold Horizon Inn

A quieter inn near the Commons, used by visiting specialists, executives, senior engineers, and negotiators. The room has warm light, acoustic panels, a projected ceiling sunset, small tables, privacy booths, and a controlled view into a planted alcove rather than a window to space. Service robots move quietly with sealed trays. The patrons wear better-maintained work clothing, but everyone still carries emergency gear.

### 9. The Forge Market

A compact barter market inside Fortuna Station, with tables of printed parts, repaired tools, homemade clothing patches, small electronics, sealed spice packets, and custom brackets. The ceiling shows a projected cloudy afternoon. The crowd is a mix of miners, engineers, teachers, farmers, and ship crews. Cargo bots wait in marked lanes. The space feels social, improvised, and regulated by safety signs rather than police.

### 10. Maker Hall

A community fabrication hall with small 3D printers, tool benches, work lights, safety shields, children supervised at training stations, and adults repairing household equipment. The room is crowded but orderly. Wall signs remind users that unauthorized pressure fittings require inspection. A retired miner helps a child print a bracket while a maintenance robot sweeps filament scraps.

### 11. The Tank

A low-g recreation and therapy pool inside Fortuna Station. The pool is enclosed by thick transparent panels, with safety nets, handrails, rescue equipment, and humidity control vents. People float and exercise in one-third gravity while a therapist supervises a worker recovering from injury. Signage notes that the pool is also an emergency water reserve. The scene is both relaxing and utilitarian.

### 12. Observation Gallery

A non-rotating observation gallery attached to Fortuna's hub, with large protected windows looking out at 3 Fortuna, station trusses, radiators, docked ships, and distant construction lights. Residents grip handrails in low or zero gravity. A child presses a gloved hand against the glass while adults watch a cargo vessel approach. The gallery is quiet, reverent, and strongly engineered.

### 13. Shift Change at Transit Towers

A busy transit hub connecting Ring One, Ring Two, the spine, and the Inter-Ring City. Elevators, stairs, cargo lifts, pressure doors, and robot lanes converge. Workers in different colored coveralls move between shifts. A public screen shows air-loop status, Ring Three construction status, and docking alerts. The ceiling sky transitions from sunrise to workday blue.

### 14. Ring Three Construction

Interior view of Ring Three under construction, partly pressurized and partly sealed behind temporary bulkheads. Scaffolds, pressure panels, cables, portable lights, temporary pods, construction robots, and human crews fill the space. Floor markings show future corridors. A warning sign marks an unpressurized section. Workers wear coveralls, hard caps, tool harnesses, and breathers. The mood is optimistic but messy.

### 15. Foundry Floor

The Foundry inside Fortuna Station, a loud manufacturing hall with metal printers, robotic arms, smelter housings behind thick safety glass, cooling lines, marked emergency lanes, and cargo bots moving ingots. Workers in rugged coveralls and eye protection supervise machines. Heat shimmer and warning lights are practical, not decorative. The structure feels overbuilt with redundant rails, valves, and backup panels.

### 16. Emergency Redundancy Drill

A Fortuna corridor during a safety drill. Bulkhead doors are half-closed, amber lights flash, residents calmly take breathers from wall lockers, and small robots deploy pressure patch kits. Children follow drill lines painted on the floor. No panic. The scene shows a community trained to survive.

### 17. Late Night Main Street

Ring Two Main Street during night cycle. The LED cloudscape ceiling shows dark blue with slow artificial clouds. Shops are mostly closed, but a noodle counter, bar, and maintenance kiosk remain open. Cleaning robots outnumber people. A tired worker in coveralls eats alone under a tree mural while a cargo bot hums past.

## Fortuna Surface and Industrial Prompts

### 18. Asteroid Surface Work Crew

A crew on the surface of 3 Fortuna inspecting a mining robot hive. The asteroid has extremely low gravity, so workers are tethered to anchor points and use cautious hopping movements. Dust clings to suit boots and tool edges. The station rings are visible nearby as a complex structure of rotating habitats, spine, radiators, and cargo arms. Small repair robots crawl over mining machines. The scene is realistic hard-space industry with no unnecessary neon.

### 19. Surface Warehouse

A giant low-gravity warehouse anchored to the surface of 3 Fortuna, with vacuum-rated trusses, cargo containers, ore bins, water tanks, oxygen cylinders, spare pressure panels, mining robot parts, and huge doors opening to black space. The asteroid surface outside is dark carbonaceous regolith, with anchor cables, beacons, crawler tracks, and distant mining machines. Workers in pressure suits move by tethers and handholds rather than normal walking. Robots do most lifting.

### 20. Exterior Construction Gantry

A huge construction gantry attached to Fortuna's surface operations and spine, assembling pressure-rated modules for Ring Three. Robotic arms move panels while suited workers inspect seals and alignment. The asteroid horizon is close and irregular. Radiators, solar collectors, cargo rails, and anchor lines frame the scene. Everything is practical, cold, and safety-marked.

### 21. Surface Airlock Dust Control

An asteroid-surface airlock entrance with electrostatic dust screens, suit racks, vacuum cleaning arms, dirty boot capture plates, and warning signs. A crew returns from surface work, red-black dust clinging to suits. Cleaning robots remove dust before the inner pressure door opens. The mood is procedural and serious because dust contamination can damage seals and lungs.

### 22. Ore and Volatiles Yard

A low-g exterior yard with ore bins, carbonaceous material hoppers, volatile tanks, and sealed transfer lines. Mining robots deliver canisters while cargo crawlers move slowly between beacons. The black sky and hard sunlight emphasize the vacuum. The visual focus is scale, stored material, and industrial discipline.

## Metis Station Prompts

### 23. Metis Shipyard Surface

A low-gravity shipyard on the surface of asteroid 9 Metis, with two partially completed spacecraft hulls held in giant anchored cradles. The asteroid surface is dark, dusty, and uneven. Vacuum-rated gantries, robotic welders, pressure test rigs, tether rails, tool lockers, and work lights surround the hulls. Suit crews move slowly on tethers while large construction robots handle heavy components. The scene should look like a real shipyard adapted to vacuum, not a sleek space fantasy dock.

### 24. The Rock Corridor

A pressurized tunnel inside Metis Station, about 3 meters wide with rough rock behind reinforced panels, exposed but orderly conduits, scuffed floor plates, and pressure doors with hand-marked maintenance notes. Workers in heavy coveralls and emergency breathers pass a cargo robot carrying hull brackets. Lighting is practical and slightly harsh. The space feels like a mine, machine shop, and submarine combined.

### 25. The Throat Docking Corridor

The main docking corridor at Metis Station during a Fortuna supply arrival. Cargo pallets, sealed food crates, oxygen bottles, ship parts, and personal duffels crowd the walls. A pressure door cycles in the distance. Crew members check manifests while robots move crates. The corridor is functional, crowded, and slightly dirty, with no attempt at civic beauty.

### 26. Ship Design Room

A cramped ship design room inside Metis Station, with rugged screens, physical printouts, hull diagrams, spare parts, mugs, cable trays, and a large table covered in system schematics. Artemis Grant and other engineers could plausibly work here. The room is practical, slightly chaotic, and deeply competent.

### 27. Pressure Test Bay

A Metis pressure test bay with a ship module sealed behind thick protective doors. Engineers watch readouts from a hardened control booth. The bay is scarred from past tests, with warning stripes, bolted floor plates, hoses, pressure gauges, and emergency shutters. Robots stand clear during the test window. The scene should feel dangerous and routine at the same time.

### 28. Siege Tunnel

A Metis tunnel converted into an improvised defensive position. Tool carts, pressure panels, cargo crates, welding screens, and emergency lights create barricades. The lighting is low and harsh. Workers look exhausted but disciplined. The scene feels like a shipyard under siege using its own infrastructure as defense.

## Negative Prompt Guidance

Avoid:
- Shiny empty white corridors.
- Vast clear windows in rotating rings.
- Decorative greebles with no function.
- Neon cyberpunk unless tied to an actual sign or display.
- Earthlike street layouts.
- People walking normally on asteroid surfaces.
- Clean luxury spaces without emergency equipment.
- Robots that are all humanoid.
- Unshielded glass-domed everyday housing.
- Single obvious life-support systems with no redundancy.

Prefer:
- Pressure seams.
- Bulkheads.
- Floor markings.
- Emergency lockers.
- Personal breathers.
- Utility panels.
- Repair labels.
- Dust control.
- Cargo robots.
- Wall crawlers.
- Practical clothing.
- Soft boots or grip soles.
- Handrails.
- LED skies.
- Worn but well-maintained surfaces.
