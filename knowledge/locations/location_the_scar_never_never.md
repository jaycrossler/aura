# The Scar — Never-Never Region

## Summary

The Scar is the higher-dimensional damage zone created by the supernova three thousand years ago. In physical space, it corresponds approximately to the region containing thousands of star systems including Earth's solar system. In the Never-Never, it is a distinct terrain region: a vast area of shattered roads, collapsed dimensional topology, ancient ruins, dense Echoes, and novel post-Scar ecology. It is the most archaeologically valuable region in this sector of the galaxy, and the most dangerous for dimensional travel.

As the Scar fades, the Never-Never terrain here becomes increasingly accessible — first in the outer solar system's Never-Never, then gradually inward. The first explorers into the Scar's Never-Never will be operating in conditions more similar to deep wilderness exploration than to navigation.

---

## Physical Extent

The Scar in physical space covers a roughly spherical volume with a diameter of several hundred light-years. Within this volume, all dimensional connectivity was severed. Tens of thousands of star systems were affected. The human solar system is near the interior of this zone.

At the Scar's boundary, the transition between intact galactic Never-Never and damaged Scar terrain is not a sharp line. It is a gradient: from reliable connectivity outside, to increasingly unreliable and difficult terrain at the periphery, to the deep damage zone in the interior.

The solar system sits in the interior. Three thousand years after the initial damage, the Scar is fading from the outside in — outer system first, inner system last. The topology of the fading mirrors the topology of the original damage: the boundary is irregular, influenced by local dimensional terrain that either absorbed or deflected the initial shockwave.

## Terrain Characteristics

### The Shattered Zones

The worst-damaged areas of the Scar's Never-Never are impassable. The dimensional topology was so severely damaged that it no longer has coherent geometry: the same location registers as being in multiple places simultaneously, distances do not add correctly, and travelers who enter without extreme caution find themselves unable to retrace their steps.

These zones are not distributed evenly. They cluster around where major road networks were — the nodes and junctions of the pre-Scar infrastructure, which were the most energetically significant sites and therefore received the most concentrated damage. The ancient highways that once connected star systems are now the most damaged corridors.

The shattered zones will take longer to fade than the surrounding Scar. Even at full Reopening, some areas may remain impassable or require active repair.

### The Wilderness

Between the shattered zones lies the Scar's wilderness: damaged but not impassable. Three thousand years of post-Scar ecology have colonized what was once maintained infrastructure. Roads that were once clear and reinforced are now overgrown or transformed. Terrain that was maintained by constant Will investment has reverted to something wilder.

This wilderness is the terrain that early human explorers will actually navigate. It is:
- **Densely layered with history.** Every centimeter of ground contains the residue of civilizations that died here. The emotional density is staggering — joy, terror, grief, curiosity, death — all preserved in the loam.
- **Ecologically aggressive.** Post-Scar organisms evolved in the absence of organized civilization and are not calibrated to tolerate travelers. The predatory and semi-predatory organisms are numerous.
- **Navigationally opaque.** The old roads exist but are unrecognizable without reference. Ancient maps — if any survive — would describe terrain that no longer resembles current conditions.
- **Echo-rich.** The three thousand years of Template Vault degradation have left Echoes everywhere. Sensing practitioners in the Scar wilderness are overwhelmed; the emotional and memorial residue is inescapable.

### The Partially Intact Segments

Not all pre-Scar infrastructure was destroyed. Some road segments, waystations, and dimensional structures survived in fragmentary form — isolated from the larger network but structurally intact. These are the prize destinations for early explorers.

A surviving waystation would contain:
- Pre-Scar road maps (potentially invaluable)
- Stored Will in crystallized form (a powerful magical resource)
- Template Vault remnants (possibly containing Echoes of pre-Scar navigators and administrators)
- Infrastructure that could be restored to function with sufficient Will investment
- Documentation of pre-Scar civilization in the region

The first intact waystation found in the Scar's Never-Never is a series-changing event. The competition over who finds it, who controls it, and what it contains is a major mid-arc narrative driver.

## Archaeological Stratigraphy

The Scar's Never-Never terrain is layered. A skilled explorer — or a well-trained Sensing practitioner — can read these layers like geological strata.

**Surface layer (0–500 years post-Scar):** Thin. Very little activity occurred here. Occasional exploration attempts from the Scar's periphery that did not go deep. Post-Scar ecology's initial colonization.

**Middle layer (500–2500 years post-Scar):** Post-Scar ecology in full development. Some intrusions from outside the Scar by explorers or missionaries who came to confirm the destruction. Their traces are here — often their remains, too.

**Collapse layer (immediate Scar — first decades):** The residue of the civilizational collapse. This is the emotionally densest layer: the preserved echoes of millions of deaths, political collapses, desperate last workings, and final communications. Extraordinarily valuable historically. Psychologically lethal to anyone with Sensing capability.

**Pre-Scar layer (deep):** The accumulated residue of millions of years of galactic civilization. This is what the galaxy's archaeologists are desperate to access. Cultural deposits from civilizations so ancient that even the oldest surviving entities know them only from legend. Here the Never-Never ground is not merely emotional residue but fossilized practice — crystallized Will patterns from traditions that no longer exist anywhere in the living galaxy.

## Known Pre-Scar Sites in the Solar System Region

Working list — all locations are "to be developed," with approximate physical-space counterparts where known:

**The Jupiter Node** — One of the major pre-Scar junction points in the solar system's Never-Never. Major road intersection. Extremely high-value, extremely high-risk. Likely the most damaged single site in the solar system Never-Never due to its infrastructure significance.

**The Belt Corridor** — The main trade route through the asteroid belt that connected inner-system to outer-system routes. Partially intact. Where human practitioners will first make sustained contact with navigable Never-Never terrain. Corresponds approximately to the asteroid belt in physical space.

**The Earth Approach** — The dimensional terrain around Earth was extensively developed in the pre-Scar era; Earth had millions of years of civilizational history. The Never-Never around Earth is deeply layered and likely to be among the richest archaeological sites in the solar system — but it is also the most blocked, deepest in the Scar, and last to open.

**The Echoing Plains** — TBD location in outer system Never-Never. A region where the collapse layer's density of Echoes is so high that they have formed a semi-coherent collective presence — not intelligent, but reactive. Named by the first practitioners who entered and found themselves surrounded by fragments of thousands of dead minds responding to their presence.

## External Galactic Interest

The wider galaxy has known for decades that the Scar is fading. Long-range Never-Never sensing from the Scar's boundary has detected increasing dimensional activity — the equivalent of lights coming on in a long-dark building.

This has attracted attention. Various galactic factions have interests:

- **Archaeologists and historians** — the Scar's territory contains the largest collection of lost civilizational material in this sector's history.
- **Former rulers and gods** — pre-Scar powerful entities who had holdings in the Scar region. Some survived outside it. Some have been waiting for three thousand years for the possibility of returning to what they lost.
- **Resource extractors** — the concentrated Will deposits, intact vault contents, and pre-Scar infrastructure materials have enormous practical value.
- **Political interests** — whoever controls access to the Scar's Never-Never controls the dimensional transportation infrastructure of this region. This is a civilizational-scale strategic asset.

The wider galaxy does not know that Earth survived. The Scar's reopening and the discovery of a surviving, technologically advanced human civilization are simultaneous shocks that the outside world is going to receive in close succession. Their response to each — and to both together — is a core driver of the series' later arcs.
