# Sylvia Station

## Summary

Sylvia Station is anchored to 87 Sylvia in the outer asteroid belt, approximately 3.49 AU from the Sun. Sylvia is the largest triple asteroid system known — 87 Sylvia itself, orbited by two natural moons: **Romulus** (~18km diameter) and **Remus** (~7km diameter). This triple system makes Sylvia Station uniquely positioned in the belt — not just one anchor but three distinct bodies, each with potential for separate operations.

The station is currently anchored to Sylvia itself. Romulus and Remus are surveyed but not yet fully developed — they represent known future expansion targets and possible plot locations.

## The Three Bodies

**87 Sylvia** — the main body. X-type, dark, outer belt. Likely rich in primitive materials — carbonaceous compounds, organics, ices. Station is here.

**Romulus** — the larger moon, ~18km. Already has an automated mining relay station. Human presence limited to rotation visits. Future expansion site.

**Remus** — the smaller moon, ~7km. Surveyed only. More distant from station. Possibly the site of future story events.

The three-body system means Sylvia Station has unusual orbital complexity to manage. Belt navigators consider Sylvia transits a technical challenge.

## Story Potential

The Romulus and Remus moons are deliberately left as future plot destinations. Possibilities:
- A secondary character takes refuge at Romulus during a crisis.
- Something is found at Remus that shouldn't be there.
- The isolation of moon operations creates a small community that develops magical practice independently.
- A station crisis forces evacuation to the moons.

These are open seeds. Do not close them prematurely.
