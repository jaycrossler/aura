# Psyche Station

## Summary

Psyche Station is anchored to 16 Psyche, one of the most extraordinary bodies in the asteroid belt — an M-type (metallic) asteroid composed predominantly of iron-nickel, with significant traces of precious and platinum-group metals. 16 Psyche is believed to be the exposed metallic core of a protoplanet shattered by ancient collisions. If its metallic content could be transported to Earth, it would overwhelm all metal markets. Instead, it feeds Mars and belt infrastructure with a steady supply of industrial metals.

Psyche Station is the most economically valuable of the five belt stations and is anchored primarily by Chinese interests through Tianhe Cosmics. The station's metallic output flows primarily to Chinese Mars settlement infrastructure projects, with significant secondary sales to other belt stations.

## Strategic Importance

Psyche's metallic wealth makes it geopolitically significant. The Chinese anchor on this station is not accidental — control of Psyche's output is a strategic resource advantage. American and European interests have formal arrangements to purchase from Psyche, but the pricing and terms are set by Tianhe management.

This is part of why Mei's assignment targets the Chinese community here. Not just because the anomalies appeared at Psyche too, but because whoever controls Psyche controls a substantial lever over belt metal supply.

## Cultural Character

More disciplined and hierarchical than Fortuna — the Tianhe corporate culture imports more of its structure than Origin Industries does at Fortuna. The Chinese-speaking majority (not exclusively Chinese national — Chinese-American, Chinese-Martian, and Chinese-diaspora workers are common) coexists with a significant non-Chinese technical workforce. Cross-cultural friction exists but is managed.

The station has a reputation for rigorous engineering and lower tolerance for the informal culture that other belt stations develop. Whether this changes under the pressure of the anomalies is a story question.

## Mei's Cover Zone

Mei is nominally bound for Fortuna (where Jace is going), but her actual operating target includes Psyche Station contacts — belt intelligence doesn't move in straight lines. Her cover as a Chinese-heritage farmer can flex to justify travel between stations, given the belt's food-supply network. She will eventually be moving between Fortuna and Psyche as her investigation develops.
