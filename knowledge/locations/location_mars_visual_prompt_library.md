# Mars Visual Prompt Library

Use these prompts as art-generation seeds or chapter-description checks. Mars should look practical, maintained, constrained, and lived-in. Avoid excessive chrome, needless glowing panels, fantasy greebling, and casual unshielded exposure.

## 1. Morning corridor in old Terminus

A wide underground pedestrian corridor in the old core of Terminus, Mars, about 6 meters wide and 3 meters high, with a gently arched ceiling cut into regolith and lined with pale composite panels. The floor is smooth, slightly worn, and marked with yellow emergency lanes and embedded cargo-cart tracks. Apartment doors, utility hatches, pressure bulkheads, and small shopfronts line the sides. People wear layered indoor clothing: fleece work jackets, soft boots, ID patches, wrist tablets, and lightweight emergency breathers clipped at the hip. Children carry compact school packs with clear emergency masks. Knee-high maintenance robots roll along the wall checking seals and air vents. Lighting is warm-white and practical.

## 2. Buggy ride outside the domes

View from inside a pressurized Mars buggy driving between Terminus surface domes at midday. The windshield is thick, slightly dusty, and has a projected route line, but most controls are physical switches and rugged screens. Outside, ocher terrain shows tire tracks, buried utility berms, marker poles, and low service roads scraped through dust. Large agricultural domes rise ahead, their lower halves buried under regolith berms, upper panels made of tinted radiation-treated glass and metal ribs. Service robots crawl along exterior seams. The sky is pale butterscotch. Dust hangs low near the ground, kicked up by distant trucks.

## 3. Greenhouse promenade under radiation shutters

Interior of a large Terminus agriculture dome during a mild radiation advisory. Rows of fruit trees, stacked hydroponic racks, water tanks used as radiation shielding, and a central pedestrian path about 4 meters wide. Partial storm shutters cover sections of the glass, casting striped shadows across leaves and wet walkways. Residents walk slowly in low gravity, with handrails along the paths. Clothing includes quilted vests, utility trousers, soft caps, emergency mask pouches, and washable overshoes. Agricultural robots prune leaves and scan fruit.

## 4. Ranch dome livestock corridor

A livestock facility inside the Terminus ranch dome complex. A long service corridor about 5 meters wide has sealed animal pens on both sides behind transparent polymer panels. Chickens, goats, rabbits, and small pigs fill most pens, with limited cattle visible in a larger high-status pen. The corridor has floor drains, washable wall panels, feed conveyors, biosecurity signs, hand-sanitizer stations, and veterinary windows. Workers wear white and green coveralls, hair covers, rubber boots, gloves, and compact emergency masks. Cleaning robots scrub the floor.

## 5. Small cafe in a buried neighborhood

A compact cafe in a residential tunnel neighborhood of Terminus, about 7 meters wide and 12 meters deep, with low ceilings, warm lighting, recycled wood panels, and an interior window into a herb-growing room. Tables are lightly bolted and chairs have foot loops. Customers wear work layers: soft boots, zip-front jackets, tool belts, ID lanyards, and emergency respirators clipped to chairs or hips. The menu board lists greenhouse coffee blend, yeast pastries, algae-protein noodles, goat cheese flatbread, and rare Earth chocolate.

## 6. Open amphitheater inside a civic dome

An open amphitheater in a public civic dome in Terminus, visually open under a broad shielded glass canopy. Seating is carved into a shallow bowl, with regolith-backed walls, water-shielding columns, and emergency shutter tracks overhead. The stage has a matte composite floor, acoustic panels, and old Mars settlement maps. Families carry emergency masks. Security and medical robots wait near exits. Through the glass: dusty orange sky and distant agriculture structures.

## 7. Pangu transit concourse

A rail transit concourse in Pangu, the Chinese-owned Mars city, newer and more standardized than Terminus. The corridor is about 8 meters across, with clean modular wall panels, red and white signage, polished gray floors, and disciplined traffic lanes for pedestrians, cargo bots, and inspection drones. People move in coordinated flows. Clothing is practical and uniform-influenced: fitted thermal jackets, city-issued safety badges, soft helmets for surface workers, and sealed commuter bags. Delivery carts, wall-climbing inspection units, and a rail-platform cleaning machine operate continuously.

## 8. Elysium business arcade

A commercial arcade in the international/European Mars city, population about 5,000, designed as a treaty-city business and diplomatic hub. The corridor is about 5 meters wide, with curved white walls, real plants behind glass, small restaurants, law offices, biotech storefronts, consular plaques, and multilingual signs. People wear tailored utility jackets, scarves, technical fabrics, soft boots, and discreet emergency breather packs. A pressure bulkhead at the far end reminds viewers that this is not an Earth mall.

## 9. Night utility tunnel maintenance

A maintenance crew walks through a restricted utility tunnel under Terminus during night cycle. The tunnel is narrow, about 2.5 meters wide and 2.4 meters high, with exposed but orderly pipes for water, air, heat exchange, waste, and data conduits. Everything is labeled with stenciled tags, QR plates, and color-coded bands. Workers wear insulated coveralls, hard caps, gloves, tool harnesses, compact emergency air bottles, and grip soles. A spider-like inspection robot crawls overhead.

## 10. Surface truck depot during dust cleanup

A Mars truck depot on the edge of Terminus after a dust storm. Large pressurized cargo trucks sit in rugged maintenance bays, with red dust coating wheels, seals, radiators, and lower hulls. The depot has high doors, compressed-air cleaning rigs, dust vacuum hoses, spare wheel assemblies, battery or fuel-cell racks, and overhead cranes. Workers in partial pressure suits and dust hoods inspect seals and replace filters. Autonomous cargo tugs move crates between airlocks. A rescue buggy sits ready near the exit.

## 11. Remote rail launch range

A remote rail launch facility outside Terminus, seen from an observation bunker or control room. A long electromagnetic launch rail stretches across the Martian plain toward low hills, with service gantries, power banks, buried cable routes, warning lights, and cleared debris zones. The main city domes are distant on the horizon. A sealed cargo payload sits on a launch sled, surrounded by robotic arms and suited technicians. The control room has thick glass, physical controls, checklists, launch authorization panels, and calm operators in practical uniforms.

## 12. Military cantonment near Terminus

A U.S. military cantonment near Terminus, isolated from the civilian hubs by distance, berms, fences, and controlled rail access. Low hardened buildings sit partly buried in regolith, with armored Mars rovers, drone hangars, comms masts, radar dishes, and a secure tunnel entrance. The base is practical: dust-stained walls, modular barracks, pressure doors, emergency lights, and marked exclusion zones. Personnel wear tan-gray utility uniforms indoors, with pressure suit components staged near airlocks.
