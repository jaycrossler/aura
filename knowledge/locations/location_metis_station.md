# Metis Station

## Overview

Metis Station is a working shipyard anchored to **9 Metis**, a large S-type
(silicaceous) asteroid in the inner belt at approximately 2.39 AU from the Sun.
9 Metis is roughly 222 km in diameter — large enough to provide meaningful
gravitational anchoring (~0.013g surface) and an extensive supply of metal-rich
silicate ore.

The station began as a salvage outpost, picking over the debris field associated
with a historical collision cluster near 9 Metis's orbital neighborhood. Over
decades it accumulated the infrastructure to perform its own fabrication, then
repair, then full ship construction. It is now the most capable shipyard in the
outer inner belt — which is not the same as the most famous or the best-funded.

It is run, for practical purposes, by **Dr. Eugene Hart**, who did not found it but
has dominated its technical direction long enough that the distinction is academic.

---

## Physical Layout

The station is built into and onto 9 Metis's surface rather than orbiting freely.
Low surface gravity makes surface anchoring practical and provides a stable reference
frame for precision fabrication work — orbital platforms wobble; rock doesn't.

Key sections:
- **The Yards** — external construction bays cut into the asteroid surface; currently
  holds two partially-completed hulls and the skeleton of a third
- **The Rock** — pressurized tunnels and chambers bored into 9 Metis; primary
  residential and operational space; protected from radiation by mass overhead
- **The Skin** — surface-mounted fabrication equipment, solar collectors, and
  communications arrays; requires EVA for maintenance
- **The Throat** — the main pressurized docking corridor; station receives roughly
  one supply run per month from Fortuna and intermittent visits from independent
  operators

## Relationship to Fortuna Station

Fortuna Station sits approximately **80 million km** from 9 Metis in physical space.
Fortuna designated Metis as a regional shipyard partner early in its hub-building
strategy — Metis builds and repairs vessels that Fortuna's operations require, and
Fortuna supplies Metis with goods and personnel it cannot produce locally.

The arrangement means some Fortuna residents know Artemis Grant by reputation before
Jace arrives — she is the ship architect who gets things built at the place that fixes
Fortuna's ships.

**Dimensional travel distances (Fortuna → Metis):**
- Physical space: ~80 million km
- The Astral (dist²): ~9,000 km
- Hyperspace (dist³): ~450 km

The 450 km Hyperspace distance is the basis for the Arc 2 hike. At sustained
Hyperspace travel pace, two days is brutal but possible.

## Personnel

**Dr. Eugene Hart** — de facto technical director. Large, belligerent, indispensable.
Built the core architecture of the {Aura} system as a grad project, released it publicly
under his own name, and has been mostly uninterested in what others did with it since.
He runs Metis because running a shipyard suits his need for hard problems and no
bureaucracy. He does not manage people well. He manages engineering problems
extraordinarily well.

**Artemis Grant** — ship architect, Jace's older sister. Has been at Metis long enough
that she and Hart have a functional working relationship built entirely on mutual
professional respect and avoidance of anything personal. She is the reason the station's
designs work as systems rather than collections of engineering solutions.

**Station crew** — small, skilled, self-selecting. People come to Metis because they
want to build things without being told how. Turnover is low among those who stay past
three months.

## Arc 2 — The Siege

When dimensional fauna breach the local Astral and begin targeting the station, Metis
goes dark. The distress message Jace receives is partial and degraded — enough to confirm
Artemis is alive and the situation is critical. Not enough to know how bad.

By the time the rescue mission arrives, the station has been under siege long enough
that:
- The crew has improvised defensive positions throughout the tunnels
- Artemis has sustained a serious leg injury (crush injury, functional in low-g,
  not walk-out-on-your-own functional)
- Hart has been fighting alongside the crew and is furious, exhausted, and
  completely unwilling to concede that any of the situation was beyond his ability
  to solve given more time
- Several crew members have begun manifesting early Will-adjacent abilities under
  extreme stress — not understood, not controlled, but present

The station's isolation meant no reinforcement. The crew held because they had nowhere
to go and the people who built the place knew every corner of it.

---

## Post-Rescue

Hart attempts to accompany the rescue team on departure. He is refused — the Hyperspace
return requires Kael's Will capacity and she will not carry an additional body.
Hart accepts this with visible restraint and the clear intention of raising it again
at the first opportunity.

Metis Station resumes operations after the siege, now with the additional complication
of dimensional awareness among its crew and a direct connection to Fortuna's developing
practitioner community.

## Notes

- The "problems at Odysseus" that triggered China's early withdrawal from belt operations
  is a separate, earlier event involving **Metis Station** (Jupiter observation post,
  not Artemis's station). These are distinct locations and distinct events. Do not
  conflate.
- 9 Metis's silicaceous composition makes it an excellent ore source but not exceptional
  by belt standards — it is the fabrication infrastructure, not the raw material, that
  makes Metis Station valuable.
