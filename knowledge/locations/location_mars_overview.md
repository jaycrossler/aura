# Mars (Inhabited World Overview)

## Summary

Mars is humanity's second inhabited world. By the story's present (circa 2105-2120), the planet hosts a tightly controlled population of exactly 12,000 permanent human residents. They are supported by a staggering workforce of approximately 20,000 robotic assistants. It is a developed, increasingly self-sufficient civilization driven by immense corporate and national interests, defined by a strict resource economy, and deeply tied to the asteroid belt's supply chains.

## Major Settlements

The population is primarily divided among three major city-states:

* **Terminus (United States of Mars):** The largest settlement and the 56th U.S. State. Sits on the equator. Home to roughly 60% of the Martian population (~7,200 people). It is a major military, commercial, and logistical hub.
* **Pangu (Chinese Settlement):** Located in the prime mining territories near the Tharsis volcanoes. It is heavily industrialized, dirty, and focused on fuel production and mineral extraction.
* **Elysium (International/European Hub):** A cosmopolitan center of grand construction, "Old Money" aesthetics, and intense legal regulation. Connected to Terminus via a two-hour surface train.

## The Resource Economy: Nothing is Free

Survival on Mars has been commodified. Upon arrival, every settler must register their biometric and financial data to pay for daily existence. 
* **Constitutional Minimums:** For Americans in Terminus, a baseline of air and water is considered a constitutional right, but its usage is strictly tracked with a complex taxing scheme that incentivizes work and provides free education, basic healthcare, and a subsidized one-way return trip to Earth if space is available and necessary.
* **Food Tiers:** Basic nutritional pastes and simple 3D-printed foods (like fortified rice) are incredibly cheap or free. Real, grown food is exorbitantly expensive.
* **Policing:** Law enforcement on Mars functions largely as resource protection. The primary crimes are failing to recycle, leaving airlocks unsecured, or misusing communal water.

## Habitat Architecture: Domes and Tunnels

Settlers live in a mix of environments. Heavy UV-shielded glass domes sit on the surface, requiring residents to wear strong sunblock even indoors. Beneath these domes are extensive, repurposed lava tubes and reinforced tunnel systems where the working class and heavy infrastructure reside. 

## The Timeslip and Martian Coffee Culture

Because a Martian Sol is roughly 39 minutes longer than an Earth day, the colonial clocks implement a "timeslip." At midnight, the clocks freeze for the extra duration to allow the Mars day to map onto the standard 24-hour Earth schedule. 

This wreaks havoc on the circadian rhythms of Earth-born colonists, causing chronic insomnia. To combat this, locally grown Martian coffee has become one of the most vital, highly prized, and expensive commodities on the planet.

## Trade and the New Frontier

Mars is no longer the final frontier; it is the staging ground. Large winged aircraft and multi-rotor drones constantly shuttle cargo between the three cities and orbital launchpads. Mars imports raw ingots and minerals from the asteroid belt, refines them using massive robotic labor forces, and ships finished goods, computer chips, and biological assets (livestock, insects, seeds) back out to the belt.
