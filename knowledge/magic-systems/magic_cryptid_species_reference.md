# Belt Cryptid Species — Will Profile and Orb Reference

## Design Principles

All dimensional fauna follow the same Will and skill rules as sapient practitioners.
They generate Will, accumulate it in Templates, use it through instinctual "skills"
(which are functionally identical to named skills — the bracket notation is a
practitioner's documentation tool, not an intrinsic property of the ability), and
drop orbs on death.

**Visual design rule:** Each species' appearance, movement, and Will expression
are thematically consistent with the skill affinity of their orb. A fire-affinity
creature should *look* like heat and combustion. A mobility-affinity creature
should look like speed and slippage. This gives the reader implicit information
about what an orb will do before any character explains it.

**Orb affinity nuance:** Will absorbed from a type-affinity orb flows most easily
into skills that share that affinity. Fire-aligned Will advances fire skills faster.
Mobility-aligned Will advances locomotion skills faster. A practitioner absorbing
an affinity-mismatched orb doesn't lose the Will — but they'll find it slightly
resistant in incompatible trees and notably smooth in compatible ones. Neither Jace
nor Sophia will understand the mechanism in Arcs 1–2. {Aura} will log anomalies.
Kael has vocabulary for it.

### Dipper-Class Crosser
*"The thing in the pipes"*

**Taxonomy:** Unclassified dimensional fauna, breach crosser. Scar-edge opportunist.

**Threat level:** Low (to experienced practitioner) / Moderate (to unprepared civilian)

**Physical appearance:**
- In the Astral: A compact, darting mass of silver-grey light, vaguely cephalopod in
  silhouette — no fixed form, but tends toward a shape that's all forward momentum.
  Moves in short bursts, pausing between them like a deep-sea predator conserving
  energy. The body, if "body" is the right word, seems to have multiple redundant
  centers of Will-light. None of them is obviously the head.
- In physical space (partial breach): visible as a pressure distortion — pipes that
  flex without strain, temperature drops that have no thermal cause, objects moved
  by no apparent force. The breach itself sometimes shows as a faint iridescence at
  the boundary, like oil on water.
- Will signature: Silver-white, irregular, pulsing. The pulse is its breathing, in a
  sense. Quick. Opportunistic. Alert.

**Instinctual skills:**
- Breach-sensing: detects thin dimensional membranes from significant distance; follows
  them toward habitation. Equivalent to [Far Sight] L1 specialized for membrane detection.
- Slip-crossing: passes through partial breaches in physical structures (pipes,
  conduit, wall penetrations). Equivalent to [Slip Step] L1.
- Membrane thinning: applies sustained low-level Will pressure to weaken a dimensional
  membrane over time. Not a skill with a name in the practitioner taxonomy; more
  accurate to call it persistent instinctual behavior.

**Will level:** Low. Consistent with a creature that has evolved in Scar-edge conditions
with limited available energy.

**Orb on death:**
- **Size:** Small — fits in a cupped hand. The smallest orb type encountered in Arc 1.
- **Color:** Silver-white with iridescent interior shimmer; colors that don't have names
  shift through the sphere. Faintly luminous. Not warm to look at.
- **Affinity:** Mobility / Membrane. The Will carried has a spatial, boundary-sensing
  quality.
- **Absorption effect:**
  - Strongest in: Locomotion tree ([Long Stepping], [Frame Walking], [Slip Step]) and
    Perception tree ([Dimensional Sight], [Far Sight])
  - Weakest in: Thermal tree, Combat/Force tree
  - Will quantity: sufficient for L0→L1 or reinforcement of existing L1
  - Practical effect: A practitioner absorbing a Dipper orb while actively using
    Transfer will find Transfer slightly more efficient for the next few crossings.
    Jace's absorption during the plumbing crisis directed to HUD Templating because
    that was his Template's most stressed point — the affinity wasn't quite right,
    but the need was urgent enough that the Template redirected.

**Story role:** Entry-level encounter. Teaches the reader that the dimensional fauna
are not monsters — they're animals following gradients. The Dipper is looking for
thin membranes because thin membranes mean food (ambient Will leak from inhabited
spaces). It didn't know Jace was there.

### Tick-Maw (Nest Variant)
*"The things in the station walls"*

**Taxonomy:** Dimensional fauna, colonial nesters. Scar-adjacent.

**Threat level:** Moderate (individually) / High (in nests of 8+)

**Physical appearance:**
- In the Astral: Elongated, angular, almost insectile in geometry. The "jaw" structure
  that gives them their name is a Will-compression organ — a thickened leading edge of
  the Template that concentrates force for membrane-impact strikes. Their overall
  coloring in the Astral is a dark amber-gold, like old resin or concentrated sap.
  When agitated, the amber brightens toward burnt orange. When striking, it briefly
  flares close to white at the impact point.
- In physical space: Experienced as sudden structural stress on pipes and conduit —
  the metallic groan of something flexing under force that isn't coming from a
  physical direction. In confined spaces, the pressure feels like the room is
  contracting. In open spaces, isolated bangs or cracks in walls.
- Will signature: Amber-gold, warm, clustered. Individual Tick-Maws orient toward each
  other in the Astral — colonial; they feel each other's Will signatures and aggregate.
  A nest reads as a pooled amber glow with individual units distinguishable as brighter
  concentrations.

**Instinctual skills:**
- Membrane impact: a focused Will strike against dimensional membranes, used for
  hunting and territorial establishment. Equivalent to [Force Application] L1–L2
  depending on individual size.
- Colonial resonance: Tick-Maws in a nest amplify each other's Will output through
  passive pooling. A nest of eight effectively has the Will output of approximately
  twelve individuals. Equivalent to a crude Will Network — instinctual, not trained.
- Amber-thread tethering: Tick-Maws in a colonial group maintain thin Will-threads to
  each other across short distances. Not a separate skill — an extension of colonial
  resonance. Visible to [Far Sight] practitioners as a web of amber filaments through
  a wall.

**Will level:** Low-moderate per individual. Nest amplification raises effective output.

**Orb on death:**
- **Size:** Small-medium. Larger than a Dipper, notably smaller than the Queen.
  Individual variants slightly variable — larger Tick-Maws drop slightly larger orbs.
- **Color:** Amber-gold, warm. The orb pulses with the same rhythm as the creature's
  colonial-resonance behavior — a slow, warm throb. It is the most visually warm
  orb encountered in Arc 1. Comfortable-looking. Deceptive.
- **Affinity:** Force / Colonial Will. The Will carries a compressive, focused quality.
- **Absorption effect:**
  - Strongest in: Templating tree ([Object Template] especially) and Force Application
  - Moderate in: Mental Fortification
  - Weakest in: Perception tree, Locomotion tree
  - Will quantity: sufficient for L1→L2 or reinforcement of existing L1–L2
  - Practical effect: Sophia's Tick-Maw orb absorption pushed her [Force Application]
    from L1 to solidly approaching L2. The force-affinity was a clean match with her
    primary skill. The warmth that practitioners describe feeling after absorbing
    Tick-Maw orbs is not psychosomatic — the fire-adjacent affinity of the Will
    genuinely produces a mild thermal sensation during absorption.
- **Nest creature orb:** When the colonial amplification is high (large nests), the
  largest Tick-Maw may drop an orb with slightly greater Will density — still smaller
  than a Queen, but meaningfully larger than a standard individual drop.

**Story role:** The primary threat of the station nest-clearing operations. Establishes
that dimensional fauna can be colonial and amplifying — introduces the Will Network
concept in embryonic form. Jin's [Fire Shaping] against Tick-Maws establishes the
fire/Bloom vulnerability mechanic that pays off in Arc 4.

### Scavenging Queen
*"The thing that runs the nest"*

**Taxonomy:** Dimensional fauna, apex nest controller. Not a colonial organism — a
predatory parasite that hijacks and controls Tick-Maw colonies.

**Threat level:** Very High. The most dangerous fauna encountered in Arc 1.

**Physical appearance:**
- In the Astral: Significantly larger than individual Tick-Maws. Where Tick-Maws
  have angular insectile geometry, the Queen is globular and radially organized —
  a dense central mass with radiating Will-structures that plug into the Tick-Maw
  colonial network. The radiating structures look like roots, or like the amber
  threads of the colonial resonance network grown thick and purposeful. Her color
  is a deep, oxidized amber — bronze rather than gold. Where a Tick-Maw's amber
  is warm and bright, the Queen's is heavy and old.
  The Will-convergence point — the densest structural node in her Template, the
  thing Sophia finds and compresses — is located in the radial center, at the
  junction of the root-structures. It is not visually obvious unless you are
  specifically looking for it with [Far Sight] and know what a convergence point
  looks like.
- Will signature: Deep amber-bronze, massive, pulsing at a slower rhythm than the
  Tick-Maws she controls. When she's actively running the nest, the colonial
  network's amber web visibly brightens and organizes — the Tick-Maws become more
  coordinated, their strikes more coherent. Removing the Queen doesn't kill the
  Tick-Maws; it deorganizes them.

**Instinctual skills:**
- Network control: takes over and amplifies a Tick-Maw colonial resonance network,
  running it as a controlled Will Network rather than a passive pool. Equivalent
  to Will Seeding L2 — parasitic and instinctual, but functionally the same mechanic.
- Will-convergence compression: the Queen can gather the colonial network's Will into
  a focused point and redirect it as a concentrated force strike. Equivalent to
  [Force Application] L3 when the full network is available.
- Template anchor: the Queen's Template is deeply anchored to her current location —
  she does not flee. She is not a creature that retreats. Her Template reinforcement
  in her nest location is equivalent to [Self Template] L2.

**Will level:** High. The Queen's own Will plus the colonial amplification she
controls makes her effective Will output substantially greater than any individual
practitioner in Arc 1.

**Orb on death:**
- **Size:** Large. Described as "a cantaloupe made of amber light." The largest
  orb encountered in the first three arcs. Visually striking; the inner light
  is complex, moving, almost organic in pattern.
- **Color:** Deep amber-bronze, with internal light that moves like slow combustion —
  not fire exactly, but the *idea* of fire. The bronze tones shift toward gold when
  the practitioner approaches for absorption, as if the orb is warming to them.
  There is a brief moment, before absorption, when the orb seems to pulse at the
  rhythm of the practitioner's own Will generation. This is the Will resonance test:
  if the orb's affinity is very mismatched with the absorber, the pulse doesn't
  sync. Jace's pulse synced cleanly with Transfer affinity. Sophia's pulse synced
  with Force Application.
- **Affinity:** Force / Will Network / Control. The Will carries concentrated
  compressive force and a subtle organizing quality — a Will that has spent its
  existence coordinating a network has a different texture than Will that has
  spent its existence alone.
- **Absorption effect:**
  - Strongest in: Force Application, [Self Template], [Object Template],
    [Will Seeding] (if practitioner has begun developing it)
  - Moderate in: Mental Fortification, [Astral Transfer]
  - Weakest in: Locomotion tree, Perception tree
  - Will quantity: sufficient for L2→L3 (with practice foundation), or to solidify
    a borderline L2 into confident L2 if L3 is not yet reachable
  - Practical effect:
    - Jace (retcon-corrected): Advanced [Astral Transfer] from L2 to L3. Force
      affinity was moderate match with Transfer; the Will quantity was the decisive
      factor combined with months of practice. Had he absorbed this without the
      practice foundation, it might have advanced to solid L2 or produced unexpected
      Template stress.
    - Sophia (retcon-corrected): Consolidated [Force Application] to confident L2.
      The force affinity was a clean match; but Sophia did not yet have the practice
      foundation to cross the L2→L3 threshold. The Queen orb solidified her L2
      rather than pushing her to L3. She felt the ceiling clearly on absorption —
      like trying to pour more water into a full cup, with some spilling. She filed
      this observation carefully.
- **Ethical note:** The Queen is the first fauna with Will complex enough that the
  "not a soul" principle starts to feel like it requires more justification. She ran
  a network. She had something like intent. The orb's internal movement is unusual.
  Sophia notes this in her log. She does not share it unprompted.

### Scar Wolf
*(encountered in later arcs — reference entry)*

**Taxonomy:** Apex dimensional predator. Scar-native.

**Threat level:** Existential for practitioners. Near-invisible to non-practitioners.

**Physical appearance:**
- In the Astral: Large, dark, geometrically impossible in the way that apex Astral
  predators tend to be. The defining feature is the eyes: deep amber, luminous,
  tracking. The body seems to shift with observation — looking directly at it reveals
  different geometry than watching it peripherally. It is most clearly perceived
  by Template-sensitive practitioners at the moment it is hunting them.
- Will signature: Dark, cold, patient. Not the warmth of the colonial fauna. A steady,
  old, hungry presence. The signature is recognizable to Kael immediately and causes
  a visible change in her behavior — she becomes very still.

**Instinctual skills:**
- Template hunting: tracks practitioners by their Template reach — the moment of
  initiating a magical working is the moment of maximum detectability.
  Equivalent to [Template Reading] L3 specialized for detection and targeting.
- Template strike: attacks the practitioner's Template directly, bypassing physical
  space entirely. The physical body experiences this as neurological and psychological
  damage — identity drift, memory disruption, Template dissociation in severe cases.
  Equivalent to [Force Application] L4 directed at Templates rather than matter.
- Occlusion immunity: standard [Occlusion] does not fully hide from a Scar Wolf.
  They track Template reach, not Template presence. A practitioner who doesn't
  use magic is safer than one who does — which creates the situation of Kael, a
  highly capable practitioner, needing to not be what she is in order to survive.

**Will level:** Very High. The orb, if one could be obtained, would be significant.

**Orb on death:**
- **Size:** Very large. Larger than the Queen, probably. Theoretical — no confirmed
  drop in story yet.
- **Color:** Dark — deep indigo to near-black, with cold light moving through it
  like bioluminescence in deep water. Cold to be near. Cold to look at.
- **Affinity:** Template / Identity / Perception. Will that has spent centuries
  hunting by Template-sense has a sharp, penetrating quality.
- **Absorption effect (theoretical):**
  - Strongest in: [Template Reading], [Far Sight], [Psychic Anchor], Mental Fortification
  - Practical note: A practitioner absorbing Scar Wolf Will is taking in something
    with a very different relationship to identity than their own Will. Echo noise.
    Memory disruption during absorption is possible. High-level practitioners could
    manage this. Low-level practitioners probably should not try.
  - Ethical note: This is the orb type where "not a soul" starts to require a
    serious philosophical defense. Scar Wolves are apex predators that hunt by
    consciousness detection. They are not mindless. The absorption question has
    no clean answer.

## Orb Drop Quick Reference

| Species | Orb size | Orb color | Affinity | Typical advancement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dipper-class | Small (marble) | Silver-white, iridescent | Mobility / Membrane | L0→L1 or reinforce L1 |
| Tick-Maw (individual) | Small-medium | Amber-gold, warm throb | Force / Colonial Will | L1→L2 or reinforce L1–L2 |
| Tick-Maw (nest amplified) | Medium | Amber-gold, brighter | Force / Colonial Will | Reinforce L2 |
| Scavenging Queen | Large (cantaloupe) | Deep amber-bronze, internal movement | Force / Network / Control | L2→L3 (with practice) or solidify L2 |
| Scar Wolf | Very large | Indigo-black, cold bioluminescence | Template / Identity / Perception | Significant — with risks |
