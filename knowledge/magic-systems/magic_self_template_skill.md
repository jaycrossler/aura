# [Self Template] — Skill Specification

## Summary

[Self Template] is the foundational reinforcement skill — the act of impressing a
coherent pattern onto one's own body within the Cognitive Layer, and drawing that
pattern back out as a continuous maintenance signal to the physical body. It is not
primarily a combat skill. It is a survival skill: without it, dimensional travel
accumulates damage faster than the body can heal, and high-level practice eventually
kills the practitioner through slow Template dissolution.

Kael's first instruction to any new practitioner is not "learn to fight" or "learn
to move." It is: "Reinforce your pattern. Everything else you can survive failing.
This you cannot."

[Self Template] is also the gateway into the Cognitive Layer — the fourth dimension
that is not a place but a space of structures, programs, and stored configurations.
Understanding why the skill works requires understanding what the Cognitive Layer is.

---

## The Four Dimensional Layers

The Never-Never has four layers, not two. Early human practitioners discover the
first two quickly. The third is mentioned as theoretical. The fourth most
practitioners never consciously engage with — but have been using unconsciously
since their first crossing.

### Layer 1 — Physical Space

The ordinary universe. Gravity, light speed, thermodynamics. Three spatial
dimensions plus time. Where humans live by default.

### Layer 2 — The Astral (dist²)

Distance compresses by the square within gravity wells. The layer of local
Will-interaction: dimensional fauna, mind-glows, Template reading, crossing within
a solar system. What most early belt practitioners experience as "the Never-Never."

### Layer 3 — Hyperspace (dist³)

Distance compresses by the cube beyond gravity wells. Accessible only after
exiting a gravity well entirely. The layer of interstellar travel, deeper fauna,
and the Scar's most damaged terrain.

### Layer 4 — The Cognitive Layer

**This layer does not have spatial distance in any conventional sense.** It is
not traversed. It is *addressed*. The Cognitive Layer is the dimensional substrate
in which patterns, programs, and structures exist as pure configuration — not
as matter in space, but as *idea given dimensional form*.

**What the Cognitive Layer is:**
- The layer in which Templates themselves are stored. A practitioner's Template
  is not located somewhere in the Astral — it is a Cognitive Layer structure that
  the physical body continuously reads and expresses.
- The layer in which dungeon programs run. A Titan-Forge dungeon is not a
  building — it is a Cognitive Layer construct that projects architecture into
  Layers 1–3. The "building" is the output of the program, not the program itself.
- The layer in which stored configurations live. A practitioner who Templates a
  food source is not creating matter from nothing — they are storing the
  *pattern* of that food in the Cognitive Layer and then *reading it out* with
  Will as the construction energy.
- The layer that the Sol human unconscious has been reinforcing for 3,000 years.
  The Mental Fortification tree is not Astral armor. It is Cognitive Layer
  architecture — dense, over-engineered, and nearly impossible to read or modify
  from outside.

**What the Cognitive Layer is not:**
- A place you can "go to." There is no crossing into it. It is addressed through
  practice, intent, and Template access.
- A source of matter. Everything the Cognitive Layer produces in physical space
  requires Will as the construction energy. The pattern tells the Will what to build.
  The Will does the building. Without sufficient Will, the pattern produces nothing.
- A storage medium with infinite capacity. Complex patterns cost more to maintain.
  Practitioners who Template many sub-structures carry an ongoing maintenance burden.

**What Kael calls it:**
Kael does not call it the Cognitive Layer. She calls it the Pattern Library, the
Deep Store, or (rarely, in older Woven vocabulary) the Unmoved. She is describing
the same thing. The terminology will not converge cleanly; this is intentional.

## [Self Template] — Core Mechanics

The skill has two linked operations that are taught separately but develop together:

**STORE:** Impress a coherent snapshot of the body's current pattern into the
Cognitive Layer. This creates a reference — a stored configuration the body can
be measured against. At low levels, this is rough and partial. At high levels, it
is precise, detailed, and includes sub-structures for specific functions.

**MAINTAIN:** Draw the stored pattern back as a continuous low-level signal to the
physical body, guiding cellular and Template-level repair toward the reference
configuration. This is passive once established. The body runs toward the stored
pattern the way a self-correcting system runs toward its target state.

The maintenance burden is real but modest at L1–L2. At L3+ the pattern is detailed
enough that maintenance becomes largely self-sustaining — the system runs efficiently
enough that it consumes minimal conscious Will.

**The Kael formulation:** "Your body knows how to be your body. You are giving it
a document to refer back to when it forgets."

## Skill Levels

### L1 — Pattern Impression (rough)

The practitioner can store a low-resolution snapshot of their overall body pattern.
Maintenance is conscious and requires daily attention.

- **Physical effect:** Dimensional stress accumulation rate reduced ~10%. Minor
  acceleration of healing from physical injury. Recovery from crossing fatigue
  marginally improved.
- **Crossing implication:** Extends survivable crossing distance by ~10%. Not
  dramatic. But it is the first improvement, and Kael will point out that without
  it, the ceiling never rises.
- **Cost:** Significant upfront Will investment to establish the store. Daily
  maintenance is minor. The initial impression costs roughly as much as a full
  crossing.
- **What it feels like:** Jace describes it as "taking a very boring photograph
  of yourself from the inside." Kael does not find this funny. {Aura} finds it
  accurate and files the phrasing.

**Kael's note:** L1 [Self Template] is the minimum viable practitioner baseline.
Below L1, crossing should be limited to Astral distances measured in station-local
range. Anyone traveling without this is a practitioner with a timer running.

### L2 — Pattern Impression (structured)

The practitioner can store sub-templates alongside the body pattern — separate
stored configurations for specific systems or functions. The maintenance signal
becomes more automatic, requiring less conscious attention.

- **Physical effect:** Stress accumulation reduced ~30%. Healing from injury
  accelerated significantly. Practitioners at L2 recover from crossing fatigue
  in hours rather than days.
- **Crossing implication:** Effective distance roughly 1.5× the L1 ceiling.
  Belt-to-belt travel becomes viable.
- **Sub-template unlock:** The practitioner can now store and maintain a small
  number of sub-templates (2–3 stable configurations). See sub-template types below.
- **What it feels like:** The daily maintenance fades from conscious effort to
  background awareness — like breathing. Jace notices it when it's working by the
  absence of the dimensional headache that used to follow every crossing.

### L3 — Pattern Architecture (expert ceiling)

The stored pattern is detailed and multi-layered. Maintenance is nearly fully
automatic. Sub-template capacity expands significantly. Healing becomes
rapid enough to affect combat and crisis outcomes.

- **Physical effect:** Stress accumulation reduced significantly. Healing from
  non-fatal injury within hours. Long crossing campaigns become viable.
- **Crossing implication:** Effective distance roughly double the L1 baseline.
  This is Kael's operational baseline — what allows her to travel through the
  Scar for extended periods.
- **Sub-template capacity:** 6–8 stable sub-templates simultaneously; more with
  effort, but with increasing maintenance cost per additional structure.
- **Advanced capability:** The practitioner can extend a partial [Self Template]
  signal to a bonded companion (familiar, oath-bound partner) — a simplified
  maintenance signal that slows Template damage for the companion during crossing.
  This is not equivalent to the companion having [Self Template] themselves; it
  is a crude external maintenance scaffold.
- **Expert status:** Most practitioners who dedicate to this skill spend their
  careers at L3. This is Kael's level. She has been at L3 for nearly two
  centuries — not because she cannot improve, but because L4 requires something
  she has not yet understood.

### L4 — Pattern Mastery (rare)

The stored pattern is so precise that the body maintains itself against nearly any
damage short of Template destruction. The practitioner becomes very difficult to
permanently harm through physical means alone.

- **Physical effect:** Near-complete healing from dimensional stress between travel
  periods. Recovery from severe physical injury over days, not weeks.
- **Sub-template capacity:** Near-unlimited with sufficient Will. The practitioner
  can maintain an extensive library of stored configurations.
- **What changes qualitatively at L4:** The practitioner begins to understand the
  Cognitive Layer as *addressable* — not just writable (store) and readable
  (maintain) but navigable. L4 is where the Pattern Library becomes an actual
  library rather than a single shelf. This is what L3 practitioners cannot conceptualize
  from outside.
- **Who has this:** No one in the Arc 1–3 cast. Pre-Scar practitioners, some very
  old galactic elders, the entities the Woven call "archived minds."

### L5+ — Pattern Sovereignty (legendary)

Template stabilization against aging. The practitioner's body pattern is so precisely
maintained that biological entropy slows. The longevity path. Pre-Scar master level.

- **Physical effect:** The practitioner ages at a dramatically reduced rate.
  Physical damage that would be fatal to others is survivable if the Template
  remains intact.
- **Open question:** At L5+, can the practitioner store a full Template snapshot
  external to their body — a complete soul-archive in the Cognitive Layer?
  The answer is yes. This is what Template Vaults are. The implications are
  the spine of Arc 3–4.

## Sub-Templates — The Cognitive Library

[Self Template] at L2+ allows the practitioner to store additional configurations
in the Cognitive Layer beyond the body pattern itself. These are called sub-templates
informally; Kael calls them "stores" or "impressions." They are structures in the
Cognitive Layer that can be read back with Will to produce effects in physical space.

**Critical rule:** Sub-templates do not create matter or energy from nothing. They
provide the *pattern*; the practitioner provides the *Will as construction energy*.
A food sub-template stores the molecular configuration of a meal. Reading it back
out requires Will proportional to the mass and complexity being manifested. There
is no free lunch. There is only a very precise recipe and a significant Will cost.

### Body Sub-Templates

**What they store:** A configuration of the body optimized for a specific purpose —
peak combat readiness, extreme cold tolerance, high-altitude breathing efficiency,
surgical precision fine motor control.

**What reading them out does:** Temporarily biases the body's maintenance signal
toward the stored configuration. Not an instant transformation — a gradual shift
over minutes or hours as the maintenance signal pushes the body toward the
sub-template. Faster at higher [Self Template] levels.

**Duration:** Active maintenance required. Drop the intent and the body drifts back
toward the base pattern.

**Narrative use:** A practitioner who has stored a cold-tolerance sub-template can
survive Hyperspace cold that would kill an unprepared crosser. A practitioner with
a combat-readiness sub-template enters heightened states more easily. These are not
superpowers; they are optimizations within the body's natural range.

**Story caution:** Body sub-templates that push outside the natural range (enhanced
strength, extreme sensory boosting) carry physical costs — tissue stress, recovery
demands, diminishing returns with repeated use. The body is not infinitely plastic.

### Memory Sub-Templates

**What they store:** A coherent, high-fidelity record of a specific experience,
skill-set, or knowledge structure.

**What reading them out does:** Gives the practitioner reliable access to the stored
state — a skill performed with the clarity of the moment it was first learned, a
memory accessed without the degradation of time, a procedure recalled under pressure
with unusual precision.

**Why this matters:** Memory Sub-Templates are the closest thing to anti-drift
insurance. In high-Echo terrain, in the Scar's corrupted substrate, in situations
where identity pressure is real — a practitioner with memory sub-templates can
anchor against drift by reading out who they know they are.

**Kael's use:** Her Memory Sub-Templates are the reason she remains coherent after
three centuries in the Scar. She has stored critical self-definition structures.
She does not know what she would be without them.

**Story caution:** Memory Sub-Templates of traumatic events carry the full fidelity
of the trauma. Storing and accessing them is not neutral.

### Idea Sub-Templates

**What they store:** A configuration, design, or abstract pattern that exists as
pure information — a blueprint, a spell-structure, a mathematical proof, a
dimensional topology map.

**What reading them out does:** Makes the stored pattern available as a precise
working reference — for replication, for teaching, for accessing under conditions
where the practitioner couldn't normally reconstruct it from scratch.

**Why this matters for Jace:** Jace stores engineering schematics, dimensional
crossing protocols, and {Aura}'s documented skill frameworks as Idea Sub-Templates.
This is why {Aura}'s bracket taxonomy becomes load-bearing for him personally —
the naming convention makes the idea addressable as a stored structure. He is
essentially creating indexed KB entries in his own Cognitive Layer.

**Why this is strange:** No galactic practitioner tradition has developed Idea
Sub-Templates as a primary tool. They exist, but they are rarely prioritized.
Sol humans, shaped by 150 years of games, movies, and software, find them intuitive
in a way that is deeply unusual. Kael finds this difficult to understand. She stores
memories and body configurations. The idea of storing a schematic is foreign to her.

### Food and Object Sub-Templates

**What they store:** The precise molecular configuration of a material — a specific
meal, a medicinal compound, a structural alloy, a tool.

**What reading them out does:** Provides the pattern for Will-construction of that
material. Given sufficient Will, a practitioner can manifest the configured object
in physical space — effectively conjuring it from pattern and Will.

**The cost is real:** Mass costs Will. A full meal requires Will proportional to
the mass of food conjured. A small belt snack is cheap; a multi-course dinner is
exhausting; conjuring water in an emergency on a Scar crossing is lifesaving but
leaves the practitioner dangerously depleted.

**The flavor of the manifested object:** Will-manifested food is nutritionally
complete and molecularly accurate, but practitioners describe it as tasting slightly
wrong — not unpleasant, but missing something. The pattern is right; the provenance
is not. Whether this is a real phenomenon or psychosomatic is an open question.
Kael thinks it is real. Jace thinks Kael has strong opinions about coffee.

**The Will-affinity nuance:** Food sub-templates manifested using fire-aligned Will
(absorbed from a fire-type creature orb) produce food that runs slightly warm, cooks
more easily, has a faintly caramelized quality to the flavor. Will affinity bleeds
into construction. High-level practitioners learn to compensate; low-level
practitioners produce food that has a subtle tint of whatever Will type they're
running on.

### Weapon and Tool Sub-Templates

**What they store:** The configuration of a weapon, instrument, or tool — not just
its shape, but its Templated properties, material properties, and dimensional affinity.

**What reading them out does:** Manifests a Will-constructed version of the stored
item. Higher [Self Template] levels produce more precise manifestations — lower
levels produce crude approximations that may have structural weaknesses.

**Why this matters:** A practitioner who loses their weapon can reconstruct it from
sub-template. A practitioner who breaks a tool can template-print a replacement.
This is the dimensional layer's version of having a fabrication database in your head.

**The Jace angle:** Jace's engineering brain and his [Object Template] skill mean
that his stored tool sub-templates are unusually precise. He does not think of this
as magic. He thinks of it as having very detailed CAD files that he can build from.
This is approximately correct.

**The Titans' mastery:** The Titan-Forge Lithoids operate at L4–L5 [Self Template]
with Weapon/Object Sub-Template capacity that borders on industrial. What they
build, they can Template-store and Template-reconstruct. What they Template-store
in the Cognitive Layer becomes permanent Cognitive Layer architecture. Their
dungeons are Weapon/Object Sub-Templates written at civilizational scale.

### Place and Anchor Sub-Templates

**What they store:** The dimensional address and configuration of a specific
location — its Astral topology, its physical-space correlate, its Will-resonance
signature.

**What reading them out does:** Provides the practitioner with a precise return
address for that location — dramatically reducing the Will cost and time required
to navigate back to it.

**Kael's operational use:** Kael has a library of Place Sub-Templates spanning
decades of Woven scouting — anchor points across the Scar, safe crossing zones,
friendly ports, and the dimensional signatures of every station she has ever used
as a waypoint. She does not navigate the Scar from memory. She navigates it from
her stored library.

**The Fortuna anchor:** When Kael commits to the oath and begins teaching at
Fortuna, one of her first practical acts (off-page, unannounced) is to store a
Place Sub-Template for Fortuna. This is the most significant trust signal she
extends to the station in Arc 2. Jace doesn't know she's done it until Arc 3.

## Teaching Sequence — Kael and Jace

Kael teaches [Self Template] before she teaches anything else. Her sequence:

1. **Explain why first.** She tells him about Template dissolution — the slow death
   that comes from extended dimensional travel without reinforcement. She is
   not gentle about it. She has watched it happen.
2. **The store attempt.** Jace tries to impress his body pattern into the Cognitive
   Layer. His first attempt produces a crude, patchy result — like a photograph
   taken with motion blur. Kael tells him it's enough to start. She means it.
3. **The maintenance practice.** Daily: ten minutes of holding the maintenance
   signal. Jace finds this extremely boring. He asks {Aura} to help him structure
   the practice session. {Aura} obliges. The {Aura}-structured practice turns out
   to be marginally more efficient than Kael's traditional approach. Kael notices.
   She says nothing.
4. **The first crossing with L1.** The difference is subtle — the headache is
   slightly less. The recovery is slightly faster. Jace is underwhelmed. Kael
   tells him: "You will not be underwhelmed in six months." She is right.

## The "Sparky" Implication

Kael believes Jace is focused on the wrong skills. His [Astral Transfer] L3 is, in
her assessment, a parlor trick — useful for moving equipment, not for surviving, not
for fighting, not for doing anything that matters in the parts of the galaxy she
knows. She is teaching him [Self Template] because without it he will die. She is
not teaching him to respect his Transfer skill.

She is wrong to dismiss it. The reason she is wrong will not be visible until Arc 3.

**The engineering accumulation:** Every sub-template Jace stores is an engineering
specification. Every piece of equipment he Transfers carries an Object Template he
has developed. By Arc 3, he has a Cognitive Layer library of tools, materials, and
configurations that no practitioner tradition has ever built — because no
practitioner tradition has ever thought in terms of fabrication databases. When
the moment comes that requires this library, Kael will not have the vocabulary to
explain what she is watching him do.

The name "Sparky" — her early dismissal, picked up as ironic affection by the team
— pays off in the moment it stops being ironic.
