# Will and Templates

## Overview

Two concepts underlie all magical practice in this universe: **Will** and **Templates**. Everything else — the cultural schools, the five categories of effect, the practices of the Returning Arts — is built on top of these. Understanding them is not required to practice magic at the level early-story human practitioners reach. But not understanding them is why human practitioners are working with an incomplete map.

---

## Templates

Every conscious being simultaneously exists in physical space and in the Never-Never. Their physical body is the component everyone can see. Their **Template** is their higher-dimensional pattern — a multidimensional blueprint of their identity encoded into the Never-Never layer of reality.

The Template is not the soul in a religious sense. It is closer to a higher-dimensional signature: a stable pattern of information that persists even as the physical body changes.

The body is impermanent. Cells replace themselves. Injuries occur. Age accumulates. The Template persists.

This has practical consequences:

**Healing.** Damaged bodies partially restore themselves toward the Template. This is why wounds heal, why bones knit, why infections are fought. The body is always tending toward its higher-dimensional pattern. Magic that works with Templates can accelerate this dramatically — not by creating new tissue, but by reinforcing the pull toward the Template.

**Aging.** Physical aging is partly a matter of Template drift — the slow degradation of the higher-dimensional pattern over time. Long-lived magical civilizations learned to reinforce Templates using Will, which dramatically slows this drift. This is the mechanism behind the apparent immortality of legendary figures: their Templates are simply maintained. They are not truly immortal. They are individuals with stabilized higher-dimensional patterns.

**Identity.** A sufficiently degraded Template produces identity drift — the person becomes less themselves over time. This is why severe Never-Never travel stress (see travel costs) causes psychological instability. The traveler is being stretched across incompatible geometries, which stresses the Template.

**Death.** When the Template fully dissociates from the physical body, the physical body dies. In most circumstances this is the end. However: Echoes (see below).

### Template Vaults

The most advanced pre-Scar civilizations built Template Vaults — facilities that could store a stable copy of a Template independent of the physical body. A Template Vault is a checkpoint: a preserved version of a person's higher-dimensional pattern at the moment of storage.

Template Vaults enabled:
- **Backup and restoration.** If a Template-stabilized individual was severely injured or killed, restoration from vault was possible — though the restored individual would not remember anything after the vault's last update.
- **Long-duration travel.** Travelers on interstellar journeys could store Templates before departure and restore on arrival, bypassing travel stress.
- **Governance continuity.** A powerful Will-Network leader could maintain a vault as political insurance.

When the Scar severed dimensional connectivity, most Template Vaults failed. The stored patterns degraded without the Never-Never connectivity that sustained them. The result: **Echoes**.

### Echoes

Echoes are fragments of Templates preserved through Scar-era vault failures.

An Echo is not a person. It is a partial record: memories, skills, languages, magical techniques, and personality fragments, preserved in varying degrees of completeness and coherence. The richest Echoes approach personality. The most degraded are mere skills or sensory impressions.

Explorers in the Never-Never can encounter and sometimes absorb Echoes — a process that grants the knowledge or capability the Echo contains, but which may also alter the absorber's own Template. The more powerful the Echo, the more significant the alteration. Absorbing an Echo is not without risk. Absorbing multiple Echoes is profoundly dangerous.

This is the mechanism behind many magical traditions, relics, and legendary figures on Earth: distorted memories of genuine pre-Scar capabilities, preserved in oral tradition or in partial Echoes that sensitive individuals encountered without understanding what they were touching.

## Will

**Will** is the foundational force underlying all magic.

Every conscious being generates Will. It is the ability of consciousness to influence higher-dimensional reality — to exert directed intent on the Never-Never layer of the universe. Most beings generate Will unconsciously and passively, doing nothing with it. Some learn to direct it.

Will can be:
- **Stored** — accumulated over time within a practitioner or in a physical object.
- **Transferred** — given voluntarily from one being to another.
- **Amplified** — increased through practice, tradition, tool, and community.
- **Shared** — pooled between multiple practitioners for collective effect.
- **Invested** — permanently committed to a purpose, object, location, or person.

Will is the fuel that powers magical effects. It is also the fuel that sustains Templates, powers Template Vaults, enables Never-Never travel, and ultimately feeds Will-Networks and gods.

In the human science framework, Will maps approximately to what is called "resonance sensitivity" or "flux interaction." The resonance flux from the supernova creates the conditions under which Will can be exercised. Will is the capacity; flux is the environment that permits it to act. Both are required.

### Will Seeding

The most consequential magical discovery in galactic history was that Will can be linked between individuals.

A person may voluntarily entrust a portion of their Will to another. This is not a metaphor. It is a literal higher-dimensional transfer of a fraction of the giver's capacity to influence reality. The recipient can use this Will as their own for the purposes of the gift.

Will Seeding emerged naturally from:
- Family loyalty
- Religious devotion
- Citizenship and civic identity
- Military oaths
- Patronage relationships

A leader with many Will Seeders has dramatically more capacity to act than one without. Over time, civilizations developed vast hierarchical structures: thousands of individuals seeding to local leaders, local leaders to regional leaders, regional leaders to powerful figures who concentrated Will at scales that allowed them to operate at civilization-scale.

This became the foundation of governments, religions, noble houses, and eventually what Earth remembered as gods.

**Will Seeding has costs.** The giver does not lose Will permanently — it regenerates — but the seeded fraction is not available to them for independent use while the link persists. A heavily seeded individual has reduced personal magical capacity in exchange for providing power to their network. This creates the characteristic social pattern of Will-hierarchy civilizations: broad masses of individuals with diminished personal agency, providing power to a narrow apex that wields it collectively.

**Will Seeding also has compounding effects.** A powerful Will-Network leader who seeds Will upward to an even more powerful figure amplifies that figure's capacity non-linearly. Will-Networks are not merely additive; they are exponential. This is why gods — in the Will-Network sense — are not simply powerful individuals. They are civilizational-scale power concentrations.

## Galactic Stagnation and the Template Trap

The most important long-term consequence of Template technology — and one that outsider civilizations rarely understand until they are already caught in it — is **galactic stagnation**.

Template reinforcement and vaulting solved aging and death for those with access to the technology. But immortality, or near-immortality, has consequences:

**Long lifespans reduce urgency.** There is no hurry when you have centuries. Decisions that would take a human lifetime to play out are merely medium-term planning for a Template-stabilized individual.

**Template vaulting reduces risk tolerance.** If you can restore from a vault, catastrophic risk-taking becomes less common — not because the individual cannot take risks, but because the version of themselves in the vault is always available as a fallback. Why transform when you can restore? The vault is a conservative force.

**Will hierarchies reward stability.** Power accumulates upward in Will-Networks. The apex of a Will-Network has every incentive to maintain the structure that concentrates power to them. Innovation that disrupts hierarchy disrupts Will-Network power. Conservatism is not incidental to Will-Network civilization — it is structurally incentivized.

**Power accumulates upward indefinitely.** The oldest and most stable Will-Network leaders have had millennia to accumulate Will, reinforce Templates, build political structures, and eliminate threats. The gap between the apex and the base of these hierarchies is not merely wealth inequality — it is a metaphysical capacity gap that compounds over centuries.

The result: the galaxy is ancient, powerful, and deeply conservative. The civilizations outside the Scar are not stagnant because they are stupid or lazy. They are stagnant because their social and metaphysical technology actively suppresses change.

**Earth is the exception.** Three thousand years of forced disconnection from the Never-Never meant three thousand years without Template stabilization, without Will-Network hierarchy, without access to the galactic power structures that reinforce conservatism. Humanity developed science, computation, and AI — not as alternatives to magic, but as the natural products of a species that had to solve its problems without higher-dimensional shortcuts.

When magic returns, humanity approaches it the way it approaches physics: as a system to be understood, measured, modeled, and eventually engineered. This is profoundly alien to galactic civilization's traditions, and potentially far more dangerous — to both humanity and the galaxy — than anyone yet understands.

## The Scar and Templates

The Scar's primary damage to galactic civilization was not physical. It was Template and Will-Network damage.

When Never-Never connectivity severed:
- Will Seeding links broke. Will-Network hierarchies lost their structure instantly.
- Template Vaults lost their dimensional maintenance. Stored Templates degraded.
- Individuals who depended on Will-Network support for Template stabilization began to age and die at accelerated rates.
- The complex magical infrastructure — transportation, healing, communication, governance — that depended on Will and Template mechanics simply stopped.

Civilizations did not merely lose a technology. They lost the metaphysical substrate of their social order. In many cases this was equivalent to losing physics itself: the rules of how power, authority, and survival worked ceased to apply.

Most could not adapt in time. They died.

## Implications for the Returning Arts (Human Practice)

Human practitioners of the Returning Arts are, without knowing it, learning to work with Will.

The five categories (Kinesis, Thermal, Sensing, Resonance, Working) are real and functional descriptions of what human practitioners can observe themselves doing. They are also incomplete: they describe the physical-layer effects of Will manipulation without naming the mechanism.

The most significant gap in human understanding is **Templates**. Human practitioners do not yet know that:
- They have Templates — higher-dimensional patterns that their magical practices are already interacting with.
- Travel stress in the Never-Never damages Templates (the first exploration accidents will teach this painfully).
- Healing through magic is Template reinforcement, not tissue creation.
- Resonance effects on living minds are Template interactions.

The most significant gap regarding **Will** is **Will Seeding**. Human practitioners will independently rediscover this — probably in a belt community context where group practice emerges from necessity — and will not initially understand its political implications. The rediscovery of Will Seeding is one of the long-arc narrative triggers that connects early-series human magic to the galactic civilization context.
