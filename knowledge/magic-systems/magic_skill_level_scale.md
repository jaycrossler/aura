# Skill Level Scale — Canonical Reference

## The Core Principle

Skill levels are not a 1–15 ladder where L7 is "mid-tier." They are a curve that
becomes exponentially harder to climb. Most practitioners never reach L3 in any
skill. L3 is considered expert-level mastery. L4 is rare. L5 and above are the
territory of pre-Scar civilization records and galactic legends.

This framing is intentional. The story must feel like Jace's Arc 1 progress is
remarkable — because it is. An untrained practitioner reaching L3 in a single
arc, even with extraordinary orb influx, is the kind of event that makes Kael
recalibrate. Decimals can be tracked to show progress and unlock sub-skills, eg
L2.1 or L2.9 to indicate they are close to the next level of mastery. These are
to be used as guides, not formal markings of progress.

---

## Scale Reference

| Level | Label | Description | Acquisition rate |
|-------|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| L0 | Latent | Skill not yet activated; potential present in Template | Default for untrained practitioners |
| L1 | Awakened | First real use; crude, costly, unreliable; succeeds under ideal conditions | Single significant Will event or orb absorption |
| L2 | Functional | Works consistently under good conditions; practitioner understands the skill's shape | Months of practice or a strong orb |
| L3 | Expert | Reliable under adverse conditions; notable capability; ceiling most practitioners ever reach | Years of dedicated practice, or exceptional circumstances + large orb influx |
| L4 | Rare | Decade-scale dedication or exceptional innate affinity; clearly distinguished from L3 by practitioners who know what they're looking at | Decades of practice; or extraordinary event (queen-class orb absorbed directly into a practiced skill) |
| L5 | Legendary | Pre-Scar master level; galactic civilizations produce practitioners here through institutional investment | Centuries of practice; not achievable in a human lifetime without dimensional augmentation |
| L6–L10 | Mythic | What the old myths encode; what the Titan-Forge built with; what the Woven Network's oldest members are rumored to carry | Effectively non-human timescales |
| L11+ | Cosmological | Not individually acquired; emergent from civilization-scale Will investment | AI Will arrays; dungeon infrastructure; pre-Scar planetary engineering |

Template that her initial read didn't surface.

She does not say this to him. She asks him more questions about the plumbing crisis
than he expects, and he doesn't understand why.

## Advancement Rules

### Orb Absorption

Will orbs advance the skill most actively exercised at the moment of absorption, or
the skill the practitioner's Template most urgently needs. The advancement is
permanent — not a temporary boost. The Will is invested into Template structure.

Larger orbs advance further. A Dipper-class orb (small creature): typically L0→L1
or L1→L2. A nest creature orb: L1→L2 or reinforces existing L2. A Scavenging Queen
orb: exceptional — enough Will to push an established L2 into L3, or to solidify
a borderline L3 into confident L3.

**Jace's progression:**
- Plumbing crisis Dipper orb: [Astral Transfer] L0→L2 (exceptional starting level
  because he had been unconsciously practicing for months)
- Tick-Maw orbs (goat + one smaller): reinforcement, not level advancement
- Queen orb: [Astral Transfer] L2→L3 (combined with months of intensive practice
  since the Dipper absorption)

**Sophia's progression:**
- Tick-Maw orb (first): [Force Application] L1→borderline L2
- Queen orb: [Force Application] borderline L2→solid L3 (the large influx solidifies
  months of practice into structural advancement)

### Practice Without Orbs

Practice advances skills from L1 to L2 over months of regular use. L2 to L3 through
practice alone takes years under normal circumstances. Orbs dramatically accelerate
this — but they still require that the practitioner has been exercising the skill.
An orb absorbed by someone who has never used a skill goes to whichever skill their
Template most needs, which may not be the intended one.

### The L3 Ceiling

Most galactic practitioners spend their careers between L1 and L3. Institutional
training (Will Networks, academies, centuries-old traditions) is specifically
designed to push practitioners reliably to L2 and, for the dedicated, L3. L4 is
considered an exceptional achievement that marks someone as genuinely gifted, not
merely trained.

The reason most practitioners don't exceed L3 is not Will cost alone — it is that
L3 represents the natural ceiling of what individual practice can reach. L4 requires
a qualitative shift in how the skill is understood, not just more repetition. Most
practitioners never achieve that shift.

Sol humans reaching L3 quickly — before any formal training — will eventually be
understood as an emergent consequence of their 3,000-year passive accumulation
giving them an unusually large reservoir to burn through early advancement. Kael
doesn't have this framework yet. She knows what she sees. She doesn't yet know why.

## Retcon Note for magic_skills_framework.md

The existing framework describes skill capabilities at L1, L5, L10, and L15+.
These descriptions are still valid — they just need to be renumbered in a future
revision to align with this scale:

| Old label | New label | Meaning |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| L1 | L1 | First use; crude |
| L5 | L3 | Expert; practical ceiling for most practitioners |
| L10 | L4–L5 | Rare to legendary |
| L15+ | L6+ | Mythic and beyond |

Until that revision is done, treat the old L5 descriptions as roughly equivalent
to L3 in this canon, and old L10 descriptions as roughly L4–L5. The narrative
descriptions of what the skills do are accurate; only the numbers are being
renormalized.
