# Transient Breach-Crossers — The Flash-Freeze Class

## Purpose of This File

This extends the established fauna canon (do **not** treat as a new system). The repo already defines:

- **Breach feeders / breach-crossers** in [[spec_scar_clearing_mechanics]] — Astral organisms that exploit breach zones, some of which "partially cross into physical space during a strong breach event, briefly existing as semi-physical entities before the breach closes or their physiology fails."
- The **four-tier threat escalation** in [[spec_threat_escalation]].
- The **Glass-Infestation / Silicon class** in [[magic_cryptids_silicon]] — manifestations that *precipitate* from concentrated industrial consciousness at Fortuna.

This file adds two things the author asked for: (1) a **thermal / flash-freeze signature** for active crossings, and (2) the **crossing-modality taxonomy** ("some only dip into the Real briefly; some can persist; some exist in both at once"), plus the **Falcon transit incident** as an early *harbinger* outlier ahead of the normal escalation curve.

> Author note: "Many things from the Astral can cross over. Many cause flash freezing, some only descend into the Real for a bit of time, some exist in both places. This [Falcon] bug is an advance warning of a threat coming later."

---

## Two Distinct Mechanisms (keep them straight)

The story now has **two** ways something hostile shows up in physical space, and they are not the same:

1. **Manifestation / precipitation** (Glass-Infestation, early Mars events): magical energy *condenses into form* near concentrations of conscious thought, shaped by the quality of that consciousness. Local origin. See [[magic_overview]], [[magic_cryptids_silicon]].
2. **Breach-crossing** (this file): a pre-existing **Astral organism** comes *through* the membrane at a thin/breach point. Foreign origin. See [[spec_scar_clearing_mechanics]].

The *Falcon* incident is **mechanism #2** — a crosser. Whether crossers and the silicon class are ultimately related (same Astral ecology expressing two ways) is an **open question** the series can exploit; their visual rhyme is deliberate.

## Crossing-Modality Taxonomy

| Modality | Time in physical space | Behavior | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dippers (transient)** | Seconds to minutes | Partially manifest during a strong breach pulse, thrash/feed/recoil, then snap back as the breach closes or their physiology fails | The *Falcon* bug. Hardest to document — gone before instruments resolve it. |
| **Persisters** | Hours to days | Make a fuller crossing, survive in the Real temporarily before depletion | The "breach-crossers" of [[spec_scar_clearing_mechanics]] that belt crews will start meeting late Book 1. |
| **Dual-existers** | Indefinite | Maintain a foothold in both layers simultaneously; physical-space body is a partial projection | Rarer, later, more dangerous. Behaves like it can't be fully killed in the Real because part of it isn't there. |

## The Flash-Freeze Signature (NEW — propagate to specs)

This is the physical tell of an **active crossing event**, distinct from the ambient -20°C of the Astral near the belt ([[spec_astral_phenomenology]]).

**Mechanism.** Crossing the membrane forces a redistribution of thermal energy (the same physics that makes deep crossings dangerously cold for humans — heat bleeds across as a field). A creature partially manifesting acts as a sudden **local heat sink**, dumping the immediate area toward Astral-cold for the duration of the breach. Ambient humidity and any free water flash-freeze.

**Observable result:**
- **Anomalous chunks of ice** appearing where there is no thermal/condensation reason for them — inside wall and ceiling cavities, around the breach point. Distinct in character from ordinary frost or from water that froze conventionally.
- A sharp, localized **temperature differential with no thermal source** (already listed as a breach-zone signature in [[spec_scar_clearing_mechanics]] — this sharpens it into a *flash*-freeze for active crossings).
- Pairs with the established manifestation signatures: **electronics/AI fail** nearby; **cameras show patterned static**; **dense metal is interacted with destructively** (here: rent/torn rather than the silicon class's slow molecular replacement, because the event is violent and brief).

**Why this matters for detection:** ice + torn metal + a dead sensor cluster does not fit any single catalogued cause. A micrometeorite can punch a hole; it cannot tear long rents, kill an entire sensor cluster, *and* leave interior ice. The mismatch is the clue — and, early on, the thing nobody can act on because "micrometeorite" is the only box that exists.

## The Falcon Harbinger Incident (Book 1, mid-transit)

**The first documented physical breach-crosser encounter in human space — though no one documents it as that.**

During the *Falcon*'s plumbing crisis (see [[book01-ch02-plumbing-crisis]]), at the height of the emergency, a **Dipper-class transient** partially crosses near the ring's fluid lines:

- It tears long **rents in a primary water pipe** (the proximate cause of the catastrophic spill) and **destroys the sensor cluster** that should have caught the leak early.
- It dumps the cavity toward Astral-cold: Jace, cutting into a ceiling panel, finds **large chunks of ice** that the vented-water explanation does not fully cover.
- He glimpses it half-manifested — **a large dark insectoid shape, red eye-points, sharp pincers; "like a kid's toy"** — and on second look it is gone. He half-convinces himself it was adrenaline and exhaustion.
- It is logged as a **micrometeorite strike** because that is the only catalogued cause that fits a punctured pipe. The rents, the dead cluster, and the interior ice do not fit it, and Jace knows it.

At the moment the shape vanishes, Jace notices a glowing ball where it was —
iridescent, purple, shifting through colors he doesn't have names for, too many at
once. He swats it aside to reach the manual cutoff valve. It absorbs into his hand
on contact. He doesn't register the sensation consciously — his attention is entirely
on the valve. That night, the low-grade headache his HUD implant has been causing
since installation is gone. He assumes the stress of the emergency resolved it
somehow. He will not think about the purple ball again until Sophia shows him what
Will orbs look like from the Astral, months later. By then he will understand what
he spent, and why he felt like he had the flu for the entire day after the disaster.


### Why it appears *here*, ahead of the curve

[[spec_threat_escalation]] places active fauna at late Book 1 and is explicit that early station-interior crossings encounter *no* active fauna. The *Falcon* incident is a deliberate **outlier**, justified by:

- **A Will beacon at a high-stress moment.** The ship runs dense AI ({Aura}, the majordomo AI `{Falcon}`, the fluid-management AI, Jace's drones) generating steady Will, and during the crisis four terrified humans plus panicking animals spike biological Will sharply. Per [[spec_crossing_mechanics]], crossings cluster around high processor density and high stress. For a few minutes the *Falcon* is the brightest, most frightened thing in a famine-dark stretch of Astral.
- **A transient, not a population.** One Dipper, briefly, not an infestation. It does not recur on the ship. It is a single early contact, not the escalation arriving early.

### Foreshadowing payload

- Its insectoid morphology **prefigures the Glass-Infestation Bugs** Jace will meet at Fortuna ([[magic_cryptids_silicon]]). The reader/Jace connect them retroactively. (Open question: literally related, or convergent shapes from the same Astral ecology?)
- Its flash-freeze signature is the same **frost tell** that may mark Helena's pod ([[char_helena]]) — a possible thread linking the *Falcon* incursion to the Fortuna disappearance.
- Some on Fortuna will later (wrongly) read the *Falcon* damage as **sabotage**, feeding the China-withdrawal paranoia ([[timeline_book01_incursions]]). Dramatic irony: the reader knows it was neither sabotage nor micrometeorite.
