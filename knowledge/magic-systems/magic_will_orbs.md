# Will Orbs — Death-Drop Mechanic and Skill Currency

## Overview

Every Will-capable creature carries accumulated Will in its Template. When it dies, that
Will is no longer anchored — it bleeds free of the dissolving Template and coalesces
into a visible orb in whatever dimensional layer the death occurred. These orbs can be
absorbed by any Will-sensitive being in proximity, providing a burst of Will that can be
spent on skill advancement, Template reinforcement, or held as reserve capacity.

This mechanic is ancient, universal, and poorly understood by early-story human
practitioners. Jace's first encounter with it is accidental and entirely unrecognized
for what it is.

---

## The Drop Mechanic

### Why Orbs Form

Will is generated continuously by conscious beings and accumulates within the Template
over a lifetime. In death, the Template dissolves. Unanchored Will has no container —
it coalesces through surface tension into the nearest stable form: a sphere. The orb
is not a soul. It is unstructured energy that once had a home.

### Color and Appearance

Orb color reflects the nature and dimensional affinity of the creature's Will:

| Creature type | Orb appearance |
|---|---|
| Human (Sol) | Warm gold-white, complex internal structure, slightly irregular |
| Astral fauna (dimensional native) | Iridescent, shifted spectrum — purples, ultraviolets, colors without human names |
| Woven practitioner | Dense, geometrically faceted, jewel-like — reflects their organized Template discipline |
| Animal (non-sapient) | Dim, simple, small — less Will generated, less structure |
| AI system (if applicable) | Crystalline geometric patterns, distinct from biological orbs |

The Falcon cryptid's orb appeared to Jace as *a glowing purple ball with too many
colors* — characteristic of a dimensional-native organism whose Will operated at
frequencies outside human-standard.

### Orb Stability

Orbs are not permanent. Unabsorbed, they slowly dissipate back into the ambient
dimensional medium — minutes to hours depending on the Will density of the creature
that produced them. A practitioner who finds an orb should absorb it promptly or
watch it fade.

## The Template Exception — Why Most Sentients Don't Drop

Advanced sentient species almost never produce significant orbs on death.

Over a lifetime of practice, skilled practitioners distribute their Will into:
- **Permanent Templates** — body reinforcement, equipment, constructs
- **Will-Network links** — seeded Will committed to others in hierarchy
- **Stored reserves** — compressed Will held in external dimensional structures
- **Ongoing anchors** — maintaining active spells, constructs, or bonds

When they die, there is little free Will left to coalesce. Their Will was already
committed. The Template dissolves, but the invested Will remains in whatever it was
anchored to — their Templates persist briefly, their Network links discharge gracefully,
their stored reserves remain until claimed.

**Practical implication:** An orb drop is diagnostic. It means the creature was either:
1. **Primitive or untrained** — had accumulated Will with nowhere to put it
2. **Young** — hadn't yet learned to invest Will productively
3. **Caught by surprise** — killed before they could discharge Will into safe storage
4. **Dimensional fauna** — operating on instinct rather than trained practice

Most of the fauna Jace encounters in early arcs drop orbs. Most advanced galactic
practitioners would not. Kael would drop almost nothing — her Will is distributed
across her Template armor, Kai's bond, her reserves, and her equipment. The bond to
Kai would ensure most of her remaining Will discharged into the familiar before her
Template fully dissolved.

## Absorption

Any Will-sensitive being in physical contact or close dimensional proximity to an orb
can absorb it. The process:

1. The orb contacts the practitioner's Template boundary
2. The Will flows inward along the path of least resistance — typically toward whatever
   skill or Template is most active or stressed at the moment
3. The practitioner experiences a brief warmth, a sense of fullness, occasionally
   disorientation if the absorbed Will was dimensionally foreign in character

**Unconscious absorption is possible and common.** A practitioner who has never heard
of Will orbs can still absorb one by accident if they touch it. The Will goes somewhere
— their Template directs it according to immediate need.

**The Jace/Falcon incident:** Jace swatted the orb aside to reach the manual cutoff
valve. Contact was sufficient. The Will absorbed on touch. His Template, under stress
from a headache-inducing HUD implant containing gold and platinum, directed the entire
absorbed reserve toward Templating the implant. He was unconscious of the process. The
next morning, the headache was gone.

## Will as Skill Currency

Absorbed Will (and accumulated personal Will generation over time) can be spent on:

### Skill Advancement
Each [Skill] level costs Will to unlock. Higher levels cost more. The Will is not
consumed in the sense of being destroyed — it is *invested* into the practitioner's
Template as a stable pattern that enables the new capability. See
`magic_skills_framework.md` for the skill system.

### Template Reinforcement
Will can be spent to reinforce body or equipment Templates, reducing atomic tension
accumulation, accelerating healing, or extending the Template's stability against
dimensional stress.

### Reserve Storage
Excess Will can be held as compressed internal reserve — the trained version of what
Sol humans do unconsciously. Stored Will can be deployed rapidly without waiting for
natural generation.

### Empowering Others
Will can be voluntarily transferred to another practitioner — the foundation of Will
Seeding (see `magic_will_and_templates.md`). Gifting Will from an orb to another
practitioner is possible; the giver absorbs first, then transfers.

## The Jace Incident — Detailed Mechanics

**What happened during the Falcon plumbing crisis:**

1. Jace cut through the anomalous ice in the ceiling cavity with a plasma torch
2. A Dipper-class Astral crosser (see `magic_breach_crossers_transient.md`) partially
   manifested, tore the pipe, and was disrupted by the torch
3. The creature died at the boundary of dimensional layers — its Will coalesced into
   an iridescent purple orb where the body had been
4. Jace, focused on the manual shutoff valve, swatted the orb aside — contact was
   sufficient for absorption
5. His Template received a significant Will influx from a dimensionally-native source

**What the Will found when it arrived:**

Jace at 26 had spent his entire life generating Will with no outlet. Sol humans
unconsciously fortify their cognitive Templates — all his self-generated Will had gone
into mental reinforcement. His physical-body Template and equipment were essentially
unmaintained. The HUD implant — containing gold (Z=79) and platinum (Z=78), both
far above copper's practical crossing-cost ceiling — had been creating micro-Template
stress wherever it interfaced with his skull, causing low-grade headaches since
installation.

The absorbed orb's Will, arriving under Template direction, found the most acute need
and responded: an unconscious [Object Template] working on the HUD implant.

**The cost:**

Templating a gold/platinum electronics package is extraordinarily expensive — far more
so than aluminum or organic material. The atomic weight squared rule means gold costs
roughly 37x more to Template than aluminum (79² vs 13² ≈ 37×). The orb provided a
boost, but Jace's remaining personal reserve — already depleted from unconscious mental
fortification — was consumed entirely in completing the working.

**The aftermath:**

The day after the plumbing crisis, Jace is listless, emotionally flat, unable to
concentrate, mildly feverish. {Aura} logs anomalous biometric readings and suggests
rest. The crew attributes it to the stress of the emergency. He attributes it to
adrenaline crash. Neither is wrong about the surface presentation. Neither knows that
he has just spent 26 years of accumulated Will in a single unconscious burst.

The HUD never causes him another headache. When he eventually enters the Astral
deliberately, the Templated implant creates a smooth interface between his physical
senses and the dimensional layer — a foundation he didn't know he'd built.

## Narrative Uses

- **Early story:** Will orbs from fauna kills are Jace's primary skill advancement
  mechanism before he learns to generate and store Will efficiently
- **Mid story:** The diagnostic value of orb drops — who drops, how much, what color —
  becomes a practitioner intelligence tool
- **Late story:** The political implications of Will orbs from sapient beings are
  significant; most galactic cultures have strong norms around this
