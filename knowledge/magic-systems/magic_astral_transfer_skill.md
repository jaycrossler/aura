# [Astral Transfer] — Skill Specification

## Summary

[Astral Transfer] is the discrete skill of moving matter across dimensional
boundaries — from physical space into the Astral, or returning it. It is distinct
from the cost of crossing one's own body (governed by base crossing mechanics) and
from Will-powered movement *within* the Astral.

What [Astral Transfer] does: it reduces the effective Will cost of moving an object
across the membrane, and extends the practitioner's practical ceiling for what
objects can be transferred at all. An untrained attempt pushes raw Will against the
membrane's resistance. [Astral Transfer] creates a template-compatible pathway — a
practiced groove the object slides through, rather than being forced.

Jace is the first Sol human known to develop this skill to L3. He is, at Arc 2
open, more capable at object transfer than Kael — whose own [Astral Transfer] sits
at L2. This surprises her. It surprises her enough that she asks questions about
the plumbing crisis he doesn't understand the significance of.

---

## Acquisition — The Plumbing Crisis (Accidental, L2)

During the Falcon plumbing crisis, Jace absorbed a Will orb from the dying
Dipper-class crosser. His Template directed the influx toward the highest-need
area: the HUD implant, which contained gold and platinum creating chronic
micro-Template stress.

The HUD Templating was not merely object maintenance. It was an act of pulling an
object — already integrated into living tissue — into Template alignment with the
Astral layer, while under extreme stress, with a massive Will influx doing the
work he couldn't consciously direct.

The Template registered this as [Astral Transfer] at L2, not L1, for one reason:
Jace had been unconsciously practicing for months — pushing Cerberus partway across
during sleep crossings, pulling equipment through in ways he didn't understand.
The Dipper orb wasn't the first use. It was the crystallization of accumulated
unconscious practice into a named skill.

**L2 at acquisition:** Jace can bring objects across at significantly reduced Will
cost compared to an untrained practitioner. He can bring Cerberus reliably. He can
bring standard personal equipment without meaningful cost. He can bring simple
aluminum equipment at manageable cost. Complex electronics and heavy metals still
resist strongly.

---

## Advancement to L3 — The Queen (Arc 1 End)

Between the plumbing crisis and the swarm fight, Jace spent months doing nothing
but exercising [Astral Transfer] — the camera rig, the buggy, the Tick-Maw
equipment work, the laser lidar system, Sparky, the asteroid survey tools. He was
at borderline L3 before the Queen fight: the capability was there but not yet
structurally integrated.

The Queen orb was the largest Will influx he had received since the plumbing crisis.
His Template, with months of intensive [Astral Transfer] practice behind it, directed
the influx exactly where it needed to go. L2 became solid L3.

L3 [Astral Transfer] at Arc 1 end is what Kael finds when she scans him. She has
never seen a Sol human reach L3 in anything within months of first contact. She has
barely seen *trained* practitioners of other species reach L3 this fast. She does
not say any of this to him. She becomes very interested in the details of the
plumbing crisis in a way that seems disproportionate.

## Skill Progression

### L0 (untrained baseline)
All practitioners begin here. Crossing one's own body is governed by base mechanics
(breathing, preparation, Will expenditure). Carrying anything separate requires
significant additional Will per kilogram, with the atomic weight² cost rule applying
in full. Most practitioners stay here and manage by packing light.

*Practical ceiling: small organic items (clothing, food, notebooks). Even a simple
metal tool costs real effort.*

### L1 (awakened — first deliberate transfer)
The practitioner has consciously exercised the skill at least once. Crossing cost
for standard personal kit is meaningfully reduced. Simple tools and light aluminum
items transfer with effort.

*Sophia at Arc 1 end sits here. She can bring her kit and her notebooks. She cannot
bring any electronics above the simplest analog components. She cannot bring another
person's equipment.*

### L2 (functional — Kael's level; Jace at acquisition)
Personal equipment and a familiar cross at near-zero cost. Simple aluminum structures
transfer efficiently. Light electronics (analog, 2010-era simplicity) transfer with
effort. Complex modern electronics still resist strongly. Can pass a small object to
a second practitioner in the Astral.

*This is where most dedicated practitioners settle after years of work. Kael's
[Astral Transfer] is L2 — sufficient for her kit, her bracer, and Kai's crossing
assistance, which is all she has ever needed. She has not pushed against novel
object types.*

*Key capability: bringing a large hiking pack's worth of aluminum/organic material
in one crossing.*

### L3 (expert — Jace at Arc 1 end; the practical ceiling for most practitioners)
This level represents structural Template integration — the skill is no longer
effortful in the way it was at L2. The practitioner has internalized the membrane's
resistance and learned to route around it rather than push through it.

**What L3 enables:**
- Sparky crosses reliably in ~20 minutes of prep (down from hours of failed attempts
  at L2). The success is consistent, not occasional.
- Assembled light structures — the rocket buggy frame — cross in one piece rather
  than component by component.
- Tear-pull at range: can draw an object from physical space while remaining in the
  Astral without requiring contact. Short range (a few meters). Costs more than
  contact transfer but works.
- Simple AI hardware (Nick's basic {Aura} nodes) becomes feasible with a day's
  preparation.
- Transfer to Sophia in the Astral is now fast and easy for small objects.
- The crossing cost difference between L3 [Astral Transfer] and L2 is visible to
  any trained observer watching both practitioners attempt the same object.

**What L3 does not enable:**
- Modern computers, dense semiconductor packages, or refined heavy metals remain
  prohibitive. The atomic weight² rule doesn't disappear — it just becomes less
  painful for everything below copper.
- Full working machines with powered components cannot be crossed. The buggy
  crosses as a frame; fuel crosses separately; it is assembled inside.
- Living animals beyond Cerberus cross with significant effort, not casually.
  (He crossed a chicken once. It was fine. It was confused. He told no one.)

**The Kael comparison at L3:** When Kael tests them both, she asks Jace to transfer
a copper block. He manages it in about thirty minutes. She asks Sophia. Sophia cannot
move the copper block at all — the atomic weight ceiling is real for her at L1.
Kael then attempts the copper block herself. She manages it, but it costs her
noticeably. Her L2 versus his L3 is visible in the effort differential.

She asks Jace how long he spent on the camera rig before it worked.

He tells her about the hundred-node array, the 2010-era circuit specs, the months
of iteration. She listens in the specific way of someone updating a model they
thought was complete.

### L4 and beyond (not Jace's arc; reference only)

L4 [Astral Transfer] would allow crossing of assembled working electronics, simple
powered vehicles, and bulk unTemplated materials. This is not achievable within a
single arc of practice — it represents decade-scale dedicated investment in this
specific skill tree. No known Sol human is projected to reach L4 within the story's
first four arcs.

The theoretical ceiling of [Astral Transfer] (L5+) — whether it can eventually move
a fully operational AI system — is an open question. The Bloom will have opinions.

## The Sophia Comparison (Canonical)

Sophia is a significantly stronger practitioner than Jace across most dimensions:
deeper Will reserve, more developed perception, [Force Application] at L3,
[Far Sight] at L2. Her [Astral Transfer] is L1 — functional for her own kit and
nothing more.

This is a specialization asymmetry, not a failure. Sophia prioritized understanding
the Astral over moving things through it. Her seventeen notebooks represent a depth
of observational capability Jace doesn't have. Her [Force Application] at L3 makes
her genuinely dangerous in ways he isn't. [Astral Transfer] is simply not the skill
tree she developed.

The gap between them in this specific skill is what makes the partnership work:
he brings things across that she cannot; she maps the space and kills things he
cannot. Neither is complete without the other, and neither's advantage is superior
in the abstract.

---

## The Kael Comparison (Canonical)

Kael at Arc 2 arrival has [Astral Transfer] at L2, developed over 300 years of Woven scouting. Her L2 is extremely refined within its ceiling — she can cross her kit in moments, brings Kai through effortlessly, and maintains object Templates across long dimensional transits.

Furthermore, she has a specialized sub-skill, **[Astral Transfer - Imbued Items] L1**, which allows her to pull Will-templated and runic-inscribed items (like her ancient runed iron bracer) across dimensional boundaries at negligible cost despite their heavy atomic weight (iron, Z=26). This is because the bracer has a highly-developed synthetic Template that her specialized sub-skill can align and route through the dimensional membrane with minimal brute force.

However, she has never needed to push her general Transfer capability against novel, un-Templated object types. Her three centuries of practice were optimized for her specific operational needs and her imbued gear, not for general Transfer development.

Jace has been at this for months. But those months involved constant iteration against novel objects: hundred-node camera arrays, rocket buggies, lidar systems, asteroid survey rigs. He has exercised the skill against more types of objects than Kael has attempted in a century.

This is not a story of raw talent exceeding experience. It is a story of a specific
engineering mindset — constant novel iteration — producing faster skill development
in a narrow domain than centuries of optimized operational use. Kael understands
this once she has enough information. It does not make her feel outdone. It makes
her think carefully about what else Sol humans might develop quickly, given the right
conditions.
