# The Never-Never

## Two Different Places

The Never-Never is not a single uniform landscape. It is two dramatically different environments.

**Outside the Scar:** The wider galactic Never-Never is a dense, layered, living landscape — ancient forest, archaeological ruin, trade highway, dreamscape. Millions of years of civilizational traffic have saturated it with history. Rich, complex, ecologically diverse, dangerous in the way of a true wilderness.

**Inside the Scar:** Dead. Blighted. A poisoned wasteland.

Every piece of knowledge about the Never-Never from pre-Scar navigators or galactic tradition describes the healthy version. None of it describes what exists inside the Scar. Human practitioners awakening in the asteroid belt are not waking into the rich landscape of galactic tradition. They are waking at the edge of an Upside Down.

---

## The Scar Interior

The Never-Never inside the Scar resembles a permanently blighted dead landscape — one that was once alive, and is now its desiccated remains.

**Dead flora.** The Never-Never's native plant-analogues — root networks, memory moss, fungal forests — are present but dead. Killed by the initial shockwave three thousand years ago and never regrown because the soil is poisoned. Their remains are everywhere: enormous desiccated trunks, collapsed canopy hanging in geometry that no longer supports it, dry root systems extending for kilometers connecting to nothing. The largest dead structures would dwarf sequoias. They had millions of years to grow.

**Poisoned soil.** The dimensional substrate is still there — the history of thousands of civilizations encoded in the loam. But it is contaminated. The Scar's shockwave blighted it: dimensionally unstable, radiating the preserved experiential signature of mass civilizational collapse. Contact with the soil is contact with the psychic residue of the worst moment in the history of thousands of worlds, simultaneously.

**Swamp zones.** Natural low points in the dimensional topology collect blighted material into toxic dimensional swamps. They glow with a faint sickly luminosity — concentrated blighted residue, the corrupted inverse of healthy Will-glow. Maximum soil toxicity, maximum insect swarm density, hallucinatory emotional bleed extending outward. The most dangerous terrain in the Scar.

**Darkness.** The wider Never-Never has ambient light from healthy Will-residue. The Scar interior does not. The darkness is not merely absence of light — it absorbs ambient dimensional energy. Bringing your own light makes you maximally visible to everything hungry in the landscape. Traveling dark means not being able to see.

**Silence and Echoes.** No ambient dimensional currents. No sound except what the traveler generates. What the Scar does have: Echoes. Enormous numbers of them. Every failed Template Vault deposited its contents here. The Scar is saturated. In the silence, Echoes drift and cluster around any Will signature, creating continuous involuntary intrusive experiences — memories that are not your own, pressing in from every direction.

## Geometry: Two Regimes

### Within Gravity Wells (Dist²)

Every significant mass creates a zone where Never-Never geometry compresses at approximately **distance-squared** relative to physical space.

For the Sun, this zone extends to roughly the **asteroid belt** (~2-4 AU). Within it:

- A traveler on foot crosses sun-to-outer-planets in approximately **one month**.
- Geometry is "anchored" — the traveler has a stable reference frame.
- Return to physical space is easier than departure — the gravity well acts as a dimensional attractor.

**The stretch problem.** The dist² geometry is not uniform. The dimensional space bends differently depending on where you entered and where you intend to exit. Two practitioners entering the same zone from different physical-space positions experience different topologies — the landscape looks different to each of them from the same objective location. In the healthy Never-Never, navigators spent years learning these patterns for major systems. In the blighted Scar, that knowledge is gone and the featureless dead landscape makes orientation nearly impossible.

### Beyond Gravity Wells (Dist³)

Outside the gravitational zone — between stars — the geometry deepens to **distance-cubed**.

| Journey | Physical distance | Never-Never time (foot) |
|---------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Sun → outer planets (dist²) | ~30-50 AU | ~1 month |
| Solar system → Proxima Centauri (dist³) | 4.2 ly | ~1 week |
| Solar system → Scar boundary (dist³) | ~800+ ly | ~1 month |
| Scar boundary → healthy civilization | varies | Days–weeks beyond |

The shift between regimes is perceptible — the landscape changes, compression deepens, the gravitational anchor disappears. Navigators describe it as stepping off a cliff into open sea.

Inside the Scar, the dist³ regime is equally blighted. More compressed geometry means faster transit, which means less exposure time — not better conditions.

## Crossing the Boundary: The Mass Problem

Moving between physical space and the Never-Never requires Will. The cost scales with the complexity and mass of what is being moved.

**The principle:** Matter with a Template — a higher-dimensional pattern — crosses with relative ease. The crossing aligns the object with its own dimensional representation. Inert matter without a Template has no such affinity. Moving it imposes dimensional presence on something that has none. This is fundamentally harder.

**The hierarchy of crossing cost:**

| What | Cost |
|------|------|
| Living human (practitioner assisted) | Minutes; manageable |
| Living human with heavy metal implants | Noticeably harder |
| Simple organic cargo (food, water, plants) | Easy |
| Small manufactured equipment | Moderate; one practitioner can manage |
| Complex technology (weapons, advanced electronics) | High; significant fatigue |
| Refined heavy metals (lead, tungsten, dense alloys) | Very high; disproportionate to mass |
| Small scout vessel (1-3 crew, alloys + electronics) | **~1 week of concerted Will use** |
| Warship or industrial vessel | Effectively impossible |

### What This Changes

**Reconnaissance is cheap; logistics is expensive.** Practitioners can cross themselves and light organic cargo easily. A scout is nearly undetectable. An army with its equipment is nearly immovable.

**Organic trade makes sense; industrial trade doesn't.** Food, medicines, biological materials, organisms — cheap to move. Bulk metal and complex tech — stay in physical space.

**Novel vessel design.** First purpose-built Never-Never vehicles will be constructed from low-resistance materials — organic composites, grown structural elements, minimal refined metal. They will look nothing like conventional spacecraft. Lighter, stranger, and by conventional standards, fragile. But they will cross in hours rather than weeks.

**Weapons asymmetry.** In the Never-Never, your weapons are Will, improvisation, and surprise. The enemy's heavy weapons probably didn't make it through. This advantage runs both ways.

## Cumulative Poisoning

Time inside the Scar's Never-Never accumulates Template damage for any Will-sensitive traveler.

| Duration | Effects |
|----------|---------|
| Hours | Wrong-feeling. Working slightly harder. The insects find you. |
| Days | Fatigue beyond physical cause. Working unreliable. Involuntary death-residue Sensing. Constant insect drain. Echo clustering. |
| Weeks | Significant Template stress. Identity coherence slipping. Intrusive Echo experiences difficult to suppress — hard to distinguish your memories from absorbed fragments. |
| Months | Active Template damage. Recovery requires time outside and Will support. Some damage may not reverse. |

Pre-Scar navigators had techniques for managing damaged terrain. Human practitioners have none of this. The survivors of early expeditions will develop techniques from experience. The first person to discover that hugging a large asteroid's gravity zone provides partial relief from the poisoning will have rediscovered something basic — but they'll have rediscovered it the hard way.

## What Healthy Never-Never Feels Like (For Contrast)

When human practitioners eventually cross the Scar boundary into the wider galaxy's Never-Never, everything they learned inside the Scar will be wrong.

Living ecology. Ambient light. Dimensional currents moving like weather. Ancient maintained roads. Waystations. Other travelers. The overwhelming richness of millions of years of civilization pressing through the terrain.

Every instinct developed for the Scar — suppress your Will, minimize your signature, the silence is normal, the dark is normal — becomes a liability. The first human to cross into healthy Never-Never terrain will have no framework for what they experience. It will feel like stepping from a dead winter forest into a living jungle.

This transition is one of the story's major experiential events.
