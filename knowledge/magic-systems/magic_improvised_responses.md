# Improvised Responses to the Outbreak — Belt Field Adaptations

## Overview

When standard electronics fail in the presence of manifestations and the supply chain from Earth has been embargoed or blocked, Fortuna's population does what belt populations always do: they MacGyver it. The improvised arsenal developed over the outbreak's first months represents the intersection of desperation, engineering competence, and materials availability.

These are not weapons in any conventional sense. They are responses — improvised tools built from whatever is at hand to address a problem nobody trained for.

The repair-and-reuse culture of belt life, and the 3D-printing/recycling infrastructure, are central to all of these. Parts are cannibalized from failing equipment, designs are shared through Aura module sync, and the manufacturing collective at Fortuna runs print batches of proven designs when materials allow.

## The Arsenal

### The Rust-Gun (O2 Flare)
**Problem addressed:** Silicon-class manifestations (Glass-Vines, Bugs)
**Build:** A repurposed atmospheric scrubber nozzle attached to a high-pressure O2 canister. The scrubber nozzle provides directional output; the O2 canister provides the concentrated oxygen that acts as necrotic agent against silicon manifestations.
**Use:** Spray a concentrated stream of O2 at a Glass-Vine or Silicon Bug. The Vines discolor, crack, and begin molecular reversal. The Bugs crystallize rapidly and then shatter.
**Warning:** The shattering produces razor-sharp silicon shrapnel in all directions. Use in confined tunnel spaces requires armor protection on all exposed surfaces. In the rotating station arms, the variable gravity means shrapnel trajectories are unpredictable. Two operators have been seriously injured by shrapnel from their own Rust-Gun deployments.
**Print-batch status:** Fortuna manufacturing collective has 3D-printed seventeen nozzle assemblies. O2 canister availability is the limiting factor.

---

### The Faraday Poncho
**Problem addressed:** Sentient Fear — the psychic pressure effect produced by certain high-energy manifestation events
**Build:** Stolen lead-foil radiation shielding (from EVA suit storage) taped to the inside of a standard EVA suit.
**Use:** Worn during excursions into active manifestation zones, particularly when investigating new Vine incursions or entering areas where large manifestations have been observed. The lead foil disrupts the electromagnetic component of the Sentient Fear effect — the psychic pressure that induces panic attacks, hallucinations, or acute confusion in unshielded personnel.
**Limitation:** Heavy. Awkward. Heat-buildup is significant for extended wear. The shielding is imperfect — high-energy manifestation events can overwhelm it. It is better than nothing, which is the correct standard.
**[CONFIRM]** Whether "Sentient Fear" as a manifestation sub-phenomenon is canon. Currently treating it as an observed effect at Fortuna rather than a named, fully theorized thing.

---

### The Pneumatic Spear
**Problem addressed:** Large, mobile manifestations in areas where electronic weapons are unreliable
**Build:** A heavy tungsten mining drill-bit (standard inventory) loaded into a pressurized CO2 pipe. The CO2 provides propulsion; the tungsten provides mass and penetration.
**Use:** A cold-launch weapon. No fire, no electronics. Pure kinetic force that does not trigger magical feedback loops. The tungsten drill-bit, moving at several hundred meters per second, delivers sufficient physical force to interrupt large manifestation events.
**Why cold-launch matters:** Electronic firing systems near active manifestations fail with increasing frequency (see `/knowledge/magic-systems/magic_technological_adaptation.md`). A purely pneumatic mechanism has no electronic components to fail.
**Limitation:** Single shot. Reload takes approximately ninety seconds with two operators. Not suited to fast-moving small Bugs.
**Print-batch status:** Seven units in circulation.

---

### The Grounding Spike
**Problem addressed:** High-energy manifestations that produce electrical surges or electromagnetic pulses
**Build:** A magnetic harpoon connected to a thick copper cable, tethered directly to the station's structural backbone (effectively, the nearest asteroid rock).
**Use:** Impale or contact a high-energy manifestation with the magnetic harpoon. The copper cable dumps the manifestation's magical/electrical charge into the rock mass. The asteroid's mass is sufficient to absorb significant energy discharge without damage.
**Why it works:** High-energy manifestations appear to follow electromagnetic gradient lines — they seek out differential potential. The Grounding Spike creates a lower-resistance path to a very large ground, drawing the manifestation's energy discharge away from the station's electrical infrastructure.
**Design origin:** A Fortuna electrical engineer noticed that manifestation events near the station's exposed rock surface (in the original asteroid mass sections) were less severe than in the constructed sections. The cable simply extends this observation into a deployable tool.
**Print-batch status:** Two heavy units; four lighter expedition versions.

## Materials Constraints and Trade-offs

All improvised responses are limited by materials availability. The embargo from Earth has blocked several critical supplies:

- **High-purity lead/bismuth ingot** — needed for expanded Faraday Poncho production and hull shielding. Currently the most critical shortage.
- **Analog medical supplies** — improvised arsenal deployments injure people; the medical bay needs supplies that don't fail under manifestation interference.
- **Redundant AI Arrays** — blocked by Earth specifically because of concerns about Aura subversion. The Fortuna science council argues these would help manage Mute Protocol failures; Earth doesn't believe them or doesn't care.

The items Artemis lists in her message to Jace are directly related to these shortages. The coffee beans are for morale and trade. The analog medical supplies, the lead, and the paper and gall ink — these are for survival.

## Why This Matters for Jace

When Jace arrives at Fortuna, the improvised arsenal is the operational reality. He has drone-operations training and a military background; he has no training in fighting silicon monsters with pressurized oxygen. He will learn. The Rust-Gun, the Pneumatic Spear, and the Faraday Poncho will become familiar tools.

More importantly: the existence of this arsenal is the proof that the people at Fortuna have been living with the outbreak for months while Mars and Earth argued about comms filtering. They didn't wait for authorized responses. They built what they needed. That is the belt.
