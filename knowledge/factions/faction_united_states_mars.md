# The United States of Mars

## Summary

The United States of Mars is the largest American settlement on the Martian surface and is, in formal political terms, the 56th state of the United States of America. Its admission to statehood was politically contested and constitutionally novel; the argument for admission rested on the Insular Cases doctrine, the precedent of non-contiguous states (Alaska, Hawaii), and a successful campaign by Mars-resident citizens demanding full federal representation. The first Martian senators were sworn in approximately a decade before the story begins.

It is the largest single political-administrative unit on Mars. Its population at story start is approximately 8,000-12,000 and growing. It hosts substantial U.S. military presence, significant corporate investment, and is the diplomatic anchor of American interests on the planet.

## Government and Political Structure

The United States of Mars functions as a U.S. state, with:

- A state government modeled on the standard U.S. state framework (governor, state legislature, state courts).
- Two U.S. senators and a U.S. representative (with apportioned representatives based on population).
- Federal facilities including a U.S. Air Force base, U.S. Space Force installations, NASA research facilities, and various federal agency offices.
- State-level administrative authority over Martian land claims within the territory's recognized boundaries.

The Governor of Mars holds an unusually consequential office. Mars's distance from Washington means the governor effectively administers federal-state relations with substantial autonomy. The current governor (TBD) is a political figure of national importance in the U.S.

There is ongoing constitutional debate about the legal status of non-citizen residents in the territory and about the relationship between U.S. Mars law and international Mars treaties. These debates are unresolved.

## Population and Demographics

- **U.S. citizens (resident):** majority of the population.
- **Non-citizen residents:** corporate employees on visa, foreign nationals in U.S.-based facilities, family members.
- **Military personnel and dependents:** significant subset.
- **Mars-born:** growing fraction; the first generation born on Mars are now young adults. They are U.S. citizens by birth and increasingly distinct culturally from Earth-born Americans.

Mars-born Americans are an emerging political force. They tend to be more independent-minded, less deferential to Earth-based institutions, and more cooperative with their counterparts in the Chinese, Indian-led, and other Mars settlements than their Earth-born parents are. Some advocate for greater Mars autonomy or even eventual independence; this is not majority position but is no longer fringe.

## Economy

- **Government and military spending** remains the largest single sector, though shrinking as a fraction of total economy.
- **Corporate operations** — tech, life sciences, materials, and aerospace firms maintain Mars facilities. Some are research-only; some are productive.
- **Agriculture and life support** — extensive greenhouses, hydroponics, and increasingly local livestock supply (which is part of why ferries to the belt are a logical next industrial step).
- **Construction and infrastructure** — substantial domestic industry building habitat, manufacturing, and surface transport.
- **Trade with other Mars settlements** — increasingly important; cross-faction commerce is a major economic engine.

## Relations with Other Mars Factions

- **Chinese Mars Settlement** — formal diplomatic relations, regular intergovernmental meetings, substantial commercial trade. Strategic competition exists, particularly around resource claims and military presence, but relations are largely functional.
- **Indian-led Multinational Settlement** — friendly. The Indian-led settlement was founded with significant American investment and remains commercially intertwined.
- **Russian, European, Brazilian enclaves** — varies; mostly cordial.
- **Belter stations and corporate consortia** — complicated. The U.S. position on belt autonomy is officially supportive; in practice, U.S. interests favor strong oversight.

## Relations with Earth

The relationship with Washington is the central political tension. Distance, time-lag, and the practical autonomy of Mars administration have created a system in which Mars officials regularly act first and inform Earth later. Some Earth political factions advocate tighter control; some advocate eventual Mars independence (for various reasons, not all idealistic). The governorship is increasingly a stepping stone to national U.S. office, which means governors are politically incentivized to manage Earth perceptions carefully.

## Military Presence

The U.S. Air Force Mars Detachment (Jace's former unit), U.S. Space Force, U.S. Army (small contingent), and U.S. Marine Corps (security detachment for diplomatic and corporate facilities) all maintain presence. Total uniformed personnel: several hundred. Drone fleets, surface vehicles, surveillance and security systems are extensive. There is no offensive military posture against any other Mars faction; the military presence is officially defensive and "presence" oriented.

The drone fleets Jace commanded a portion of are a significant operational capability. They handle surface security, hazard clearance (debris, micrometeorite impacts), surveillance, search and rescue, and limited interdiction.

## Cultural Character

The United States of Mars is recognizably American but increasingly its own thing. American holidays are observed, but Mars-specific traditions have emerged: the Landing Day commemoration (anniversary of the first U.S. crewed Mars landing), the Synod festivals (timed to Earth-Mars launch windows), and various community-specific traditions in the largest settlement zones.

Religious diversity reflects broader American patterns. Mars has churches, mosques, synagogues, temples, and a notably high per-capita representation of various non-traditional spiritual practices — a feature of the population that will become significant when magic returns.

## How This Faction Will Change

Over the story arc:

- **Communications blackout** with the belt creates internal political pressure. Mars residents have family and business in the belt. Congressional and corporate pressure to restore comms is significant.
- **The radiation front** approaching Mars (in the 10-15 year window) prompts increasingly desperate research and policy debate.
- **First magical manifestations on Mars** (eventual) will be politically explosive. Will the U.S. government try to suppress, manage, recruit, or regulate sensitives?
- **Mars-born identity politics** intensify as the situation strains traditional Earth-loyalty frames.
