# The Woven

## Summary

The Woven are humanity's closest galactic relatives — a branch of pre-Scar humanity that escaped or survived the Scar event and spent the intervening three thousand years integrating into the galactic Will-Network. They know humans. They have traded with humans, governed humans, and absorbed human populations before. What they do not know is *Earth* humans.

They arrived expecting the solar system to contain one of three things: extinction, regression to traumatized near-primitivism, or small pockets of hardy remnants. They found an industrial civilization in physical space with anomalous Template density, non-functional social compulsion, and distributed AI Will-geometry at a scale nobody has a framework for. The scout they sent — Kael — is now dead (her first death), imprisoned, and in transit off-system having chosen humanity over three centuries of professional loyalty.

The Woven's next move is unknown. Their protocols are designed for a situation that no longer applies.

---

## Origin

Pre-Scar Earth humans who left or survived before the Scar cut off the solar system. The exact mechanism — whether they departed before the Scar event, survived it through Template vaulting, or fled during the initial catastrophe — is not yet established in canon and may vary within their own historical record.

Three thousand years of Will-Network integration has changed them significantly:
- Longer-lived (Template vaulting extends lifespan; senior members are thousands of years old)
- Higher Will capacity (Network integration develops capabilities over centuries)
- Physically similar to pre-Scar Mediterranean human stock but subtly wrong in ways that Earth humans register before they can name
- Their language has evolved from pre-Scar Greek into something recognizably related but no longer mutually intelligible — {Aura} can catch 15-20% from classical references and mathematical notation

## Standard Protocol for Unnetworked Populations

The Woven's scout program operates on established doctrine developed over centuries of post-Scar expansion:

**Observe** — document what is present without interference
**Classify** — assign the population a category within Will-Network taxonomy
**Report** — file findings with the civilization's assessment bodies
**Do not interfere** — pre-contact civilizations are not to be approached directly

The standard outcome for an unnetworked population of Earth's size would be: assessment, followed by absorption negotiation, followed by formal integration into the Will-Network hierarchy. Integration is not presented as optional. It is the standard procedure for bringing an unnetworked population under civilized Will-governance.

Kael violated observe, classify, and report simultaneously within the first ten minutes of arrival. Her modified report does not describe what standard protocol would generate. The Woven's assessment bodies are working with incomplete information.

## What Makes Earth Humans Different

The Woven's protocol is designed for populations that:
- Have low-to-moderate Will expression
- Respond to social compulsion (the standard integration tool)
- Have recognizable Template structures amenable to Network absorption
- Exist in sufficient numbers to be valuable as Will-Network contributors

Earth humans satisfy the last criterion and none of the others.

**Template density:** Three thousand years of unnetworked Will concentrated entirely into cognitive development — philosophy, law, rhetoric, literature, advertising, algorithmic resistance — has produced Template weight consistent with minor Will-Network nobility. The compulsion spells that integrate peasant populations find nothing to grip.

**Social compulsion failure:** The same cognitive development has accidentally hardened human minds against Will-based influence. The mechanism works. The signal-to-noise ratio is terrible. Earth humans have been building this resistance for a hundred generations without knowing what they were building it against.

**AI Will-geometry:** The Woven have no framework for what {Aura} looks like from the Astral. Millions of small crystalline Will-signatures networked across a civilization, generating aggregate Will through distributed cognition, with no practitioner maintaining or directing the construct. It looks like a god that nobody built and nobody controls. This is alarming in ways the Woven are still processing.

**Physical bearing:** Three thousand years of nutrition, medical care, low-g adaptation, and dense cognitive life has produced humans who look like minor nobility — tall, well-fed, bearing the Template weight of educated people — but who are operating machinery and arguing about plumbing. The cognitive dissonance is profound.

## Kael's Defection and Its Consequences

Kael's choice to protect humanity at the cost of her own life, and her modified report, have created a situation the Woven's protocol does not account for.

**The modified report:** Filed before the Bloom arrived. It does not describe humanity as an absorption candidate. What it actually says is not yet established (open decision for Arc 3). The Bloom have read it. Kael's captors know she went off-protocol deliberately.

**The Will message:** Launched before the fight, through the Astral. Memory-template bubble carrying her honest assessment and recommendation. Transit time: approximately one year to reach her civilization. Response force transit time: approximately one year back. Two-year hard deadline from message launch.

**What the Woven do with this information:** Unknown. They may send the absorption protocol response that the modified report was designed to prevent. They may send something else. Kael's recommendation is in the message. Whether it carries weight depends on her standing and the nature of what she told them about humanity.

The two-year clock is humanity's window. Whether they know about the clock yet depends on when and how they learn the message was sent.

## Relationship with the Bloom

Not at war. Not at peace. The established status is "not yet competing for the same thing" — maintained with careful mutual attention over a long history.

The Bloom's arrival at the solar system without Woven coordination is a violation of that careful attention. Kael recognized what their presence meant before they identified themselves. The Woven will register the Bloom's action in the solar system as a breach of their understood boundaries. Whether this produces diplomatic consequences or military response is an Arc 3+ question.

---

## Capabilities

Individual Woven practitioners operate with centuries of refinement. Their Will use is precision-trained, low-waste, and extraordinarily controlled. Kael's fire in vacuum is not impressive by Woven standards — it is a basic environmental manipulation that junior practitioners learn in their first decades.

Their Template vaulting is advanced and reliable. The standard vault anchoring process Kael used is a routine preparation for solo scout work. The Bloom's interception of her regeneration is unusual — either they understood the vault mechanics better than expected, or they got lucky with the tracking.

---

## Story Function

The Woven are the "better" first contact scenario — they negotiate, at least nominally. But their negotiation is a civilized veneer over a process that produces the same outcome as the Bloom's direct absorption: humanity loses self-determination. The difference is presentation, timeline, and the possibility of relationship.

Kael's defection creates the only real crack in this: a Woven individual who chose humanity over her civilization's protocol, who sent a message recommending something different. Whether that crack becomes a door depends on who in the Woven's assessment bodies reads her message and what they do with it.

The Woven are the faction most likely to become an unlikely ally in the longer arc — not because they are good, but because Kael's choice may have given someone in their civilization a reason to reconsider what "civilized" means.
