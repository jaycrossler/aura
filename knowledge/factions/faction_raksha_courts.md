# The Raksha Courts

## Summary

The Raksha are shapeshifting predatory sophonts organized into aristocratic courts. They are not naturally avian, but certain hunting forms include wing membranes, taloned limbs, elongated faces, and feather-like sensory vanes — ancient Greeks remembered these forms as Harpies. Other Raksha infiltration forms, seductive and night-hunting, became Empusae in the mythological record.

Their civilization values appetite, deception, status, and restraint. A crude Raksha kills openly. A refined Raksha engineers consent, betrayal, humiliation, and dependency before feeding. They do not see hypocrisy in combining diplomacy and predation — they consider both arts of court life.

## Biology and Glamour

Raksha glamour works through recognition. A Raksha does not merely mimic a person — it pressures the victim's *expectation* that the person should be real. It leans into the emotional shape already present in the observer.

- A grieving mother sees a lost child.
- A lonely officer sees a trusted lover.
- A tired technician sees the supervisor who belongs in the room.

The Raksha then leans into the emotional shape already present. The glamour is not exactly visual illusion — it is a Will-pressure that makes the observer's own pattern-recognition confirm what the Raksha wants them to see. Fighting it requires either strong Will-resistance or breaking the emotional anchor the glamour is exploiting.

They are voracious language-learners. They pick up linguistic patterns, social tells, mannerism details, and relationship history with unsettling speed. A Raksha that has been observing a human for three days can pass as a known friend for longer than is comfortable.

## Earth Humans

Raksha have preyed on humans for thousands of years. Human diaspora cultures preserve anti-Raksha customs: invitation taboos, salt lines, name-tests, shadow-tests, guest-right laws, and ritualized suspicion of sudden intimacy. Earth humans have none of these.

But Earth is covered in mechanical eyes.

Raksha can fool people. They struggle against access logs, biometrics, DNA scans, gait recognition, thermal imaging, drone witnesses, challenge codes, and AI anomaly detection. A Raksha can pass a room full of humans. It cannot reliably pass a room full of humans and their {Aura} instances simultaneously. The AI does not have the emotional anchor the glamour requires.

This is new. This is worrying to the Raksha in ways they have not yet processed.

## Story Function — Arc 3+

Excellent espionage antagonists. Their presence makes ordinary engineering discipline matter — a monster may imitate a commander, but it cannot answer a rotating maintenance challenge code it never heard. They work best in storylines where human trust infrastructure is tested and where the question is not "is there a Raksha here" but "which one."

## Strengths
- Shapeshifting and voice/manner mimicry
- Emotional manipulation and glamour
- Indoor lethality in close quarters
- Fast language learning
- Court intrigue and social navigation

## Weaknesses
- Ego competition within courts
- High Will cost for sustained disguise
- Poor performance against automated security systems
- Difficulty maintaining consistency across recorded histories
- Tendency to underestimate bureaucratic procedure
