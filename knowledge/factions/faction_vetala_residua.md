# The Vetala Residua

## Summary

The Vetala are mnemonic necroid parasites and corpse-readers. They inhabit dead tissue, damaged neural structures, and residual Will-patterns. They can extract memory from the dead — but the process damages, distorts, or consumes what remains. Greek observers remembered them as Keres: death-spirits drawn to battlefields. The Greeks were not wrong about what they saw. They did not understand the mechanism, which is not spiritual — it is biological and Will-based.

They are hated by most galactic civilizations. They are also used by most galactic civilizations, privately and deniably.

## Biology and Capability

The Vetala live in the seam between death, Echo, and Template residue. They occupy the space between what a person was and what remains of them after death — the cooling Will-patterns, the decaying Template, the residual neural structure that retains information for some time after biological death.

Against templated species, Vetala can extract structured identity-fragments — cleaner information because Templated minds are organized and compressed. Against Earth humans, they encounter raw, untemplated, uncompressed memory. This is valuable, unstable, and difficult to digest. Earth memory is messy — associative, emotional, poorly indexed, contradictory. But it is extraordinarily rich. An Earth human's residual neural structure contains more raw experiential data than a Templated elder who has been pruning and organizing their memory for centuries.

They do not build cities. They form archive colonies around mass graves, old battlefields, execution grounds, failed crossings, and abandoned Template vaults. The best sites, from their perspective, are places where many minds ended at once — the information density is exceptional.

## Relationship with Old Powers

Every major galactic civilization publicly reviles the Vetala and privately employs them.

They can recover last sights, identify murderers, reconstruct sabotage, answer questions that survived no recording. After disasters, ship losses, assassination attempts, and military failures, the pressure to use Vetala services becomes severe. The price is formal desecration of remains — a high moral and cultural cost for most species.

The Vetala are therefore a perpetual black market service industry. They do not pretend otherwise.

## Relationship with Earth Humans

Diaspora humans fear them through funerary traditions — specific burial practices, funerary seals, cremation protocols — that developed from real historical encounters compressed into religious practice.

Earth humans will initially misunderstand them as ghost-possession events, zombie phenomena, or AI malfunction. The actual experience of a Vetala working a recent Earth death site — accessing surveillance-rich, AI-logged, backup-dense human remains — may be genuinely unprecedented for the Vetala. They have never encountered a species that documented themselves so thoroughly in external systems. The relationship between the biological residue and the AI records creates a strange doubled evidence problem.

## Moral Pressure Function

The Vetala create moral pressure in proportion to what is at stake. They are useful in exactly the situations where using them feels unforgivable: after a massacre when survivors need to know who did it; after a ship disaster when the cause may determine who lives next; after a political assassination when the truth will either prevent or start a war.

The question the Vetala pose to every civilization they encounter: how much does truth matter to you, and what are you willing to desecrate to get it?

For Earth humans — who record everything, who have {Aura} logs and black boxes and distributed audit trails — the Vetala are simultaneously less necessary (the information is often already somewhere in the system) and more dangerous (they can access the residue of the information the system *didn't* capture).

## Strengths
- Memory salvage from the dead
- Forensic insight — can reconstruct cause, sequence, and identity
- Historical reconstruction of lost events
- Identity theft (extract and replay a personality fragment)
- Access to information that survived no other medium

## Weaknesses
- Requires remains or strong residual Will — no remains, no access
- Poor direct combat ability
- Identity contamination (prolonged exposure to harvested memory affects the Vetala themselves)
- Social revulsion across almost all species — limits operational cover
- Vulnerability to sterile disposal, cremation, hard radiation, and sealed funerary protocols
