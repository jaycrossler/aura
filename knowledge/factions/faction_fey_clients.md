# The Fey Client-Swarms

## Summary

The Fey Client-Swarms are not one species. They are a category — very small, Will-dense sophonts and semi-sophonts who survive as clients, parasites, performers, couriers, artisans, scouts, or protected enclaves within larger civilizations. Many are physically weak and fragile. Some possess extreme Will density. All can move through spaces larger species cannot use.

Greek observers remembered some as Satyrs (trickster spirit-folk, perception-active, sexually aggressive in some accounts — probably behavioral traits of species using pheromone influence or perception manipulation), Dactyls (secretive metalworking craftsmen — probably high-skill Will-artisans operating in Titan-Forge adjacent roles), and Kouretes (ritual guards — probably a protective-function Fey group employed as security for a larger patron's temple site).

The variation is high. "Fey Client-Swarm" is a category, not a civilization. Different Fey groups have radically different biologies, cultures, and allegiances.

## Why They Matter

Small does not mean weak in the Astral.

Fey-sized beings can move through cracks larger species cannot use. They can hide in Will-noise, ride larger crossings without being detected, and manipulate tiny but critical perception events. They are not battlefield powers. They are margin powers — the kind of threat that does not appear on any tactical assessment because it operates below the detection threshold of conventional threat models.

They are ideal for sabotage. A station does not need a dragon attack if a thumb-sized saboteur can misalign one valve, spoil one sensor, or whisper one false certainty at the wrong moment.

## Relationship with Earth Humans

Humans underestimate them. This has been true for thousands of years, across every human population in the galaxy that has encountered them.

Earth humans may be especially vulnerable at first because they lack cultural protocols for dealing with invisible or near-invisible Will interference. The Fey's advantage is operating below notice.

However, Earth humans also build sealed systems, sensor grids, air filters, microdrones, and automated anomaly detection. Every Earth station runs AI monitoring that flags statistical anomalies in system behavior. A Fey saboteur can misalign one valve on a pre-AI belt station without detection. On Fortuna, with {Aura} running across every system, the anomaly will appear in a log within hours.

The Fey will love and hate Earth machines in roughly equal measure. The machines are both the primary obstacle to their preferred operating methods and the most interesting puzzle they have encountered in centuries.

## Allegiance Structure

Most Fey groups survive as clients of larger powers. They maintain hidden internal loyalties that do not always match their apparent patron relationships. A Fey group that appears to serve the Naga Houses may be selling information to the Anzu Clades. A Fey group that appears to serve the Mirror-Crowned may have a three-generation grudge against them that they are executing very slowly.

They are rarely harmless. They are rarely straightforward about who they work for.

## Strengths
- High Will density in very small bodies
- Stealth and operation in sub-detection margins
- Fine Astral manipulation — thread-level precision
- Courier work through spaces larger travelers cannot use
- Sabotage via micro-scale intervention
- Social infiltration through patron networks

## Weaknesses
- Physical fragility — easily killed in direct confrontation
- Low carrying capacity — cannot move large things
- Dependence on patrons for protection and resources
- Vulnerability to sealed environments and environmental monitoring
- Internal factionalism — Fey groups rarely coordinate effectively

## Story Function — Arc 3+
The Fey make small problems dangerous. They also make the background of galactic political life feel genuinely inhabited — every major faction has Fey clients, every diplomatic event has Fey observers nobody officially acknowledges, every treaty has Fey witnesses serving a patron whose interests are not fully aligned with the treaty's parties.

For Earth humans, the Fey are the first species that may make {Aura}'s anomaly detection genuinely life-saving in a non-obvious way.
