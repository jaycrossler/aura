# Other Mars Settlements

This document is a **placeholder collection** for non-American Mars settlements. Each should eventually have its own faction file. Captured here while details remain sparse.

## Chinese Mars Settlement

The second-largest Mars settlement and the principal strategic peer to the United States of Mars. State-led, collectivist in organization, technically sophisticated. Population approximately 5,000-8,000 at story start.

Founded under the Chinese national space program with substantial state investment and long-term planning. The settlement is more centrally administered than the American one, with less corporate visibility and more government coordination. Infrastructure is impressive — Chinese Mars construction is widely considered the best on the planet.

Relations with the U.S. settlement are functional — formal diplomatic ties, active trade, mutual scientific cooperation, occasional friction. Relations with the Indian-led settlement are cool but professional. Relations with Earth (Beijing) are tighter than the U.S. settlement's relations with Washington — communications discipline is stricter, and policy autonomy is more limited.

## Indian-Led Multinational Settlement

Founded later than the American and Chinese settlements but rapidly growing. Originally a coalition project with Indian, Brazilian, and several smaller national investors, plus significant American and European corporate funding. It has evolved into something genuinely distinct — neither a state project nor a corporate one, but a multi-stakeholder governance experiment.

Population approximately 3,000-5,000 at story start, but the fastest-growing of the three major settlements. The settlement has a reputation for entrepreneurial energy, less formal hierarchy than the American or Chinese settlements, and a more polyglot culture.

The Indian-led settlement is also the most religiously and philosophically diverse — Hindu, Sikh, Jain, Christian, Muslim, Buddhist, secular, and various smaller traditions all have meaningful presence. This diversity will matter when magic returns.

Open questions: governance model, key personalities, name of settlement, economic specializations.

## Russian Enclave

Smaller settlement, more militarized than the American or Chinese, less commercially active. Maintains close ties to Russia's space and defense agencies. Population approximately 800-1,500.

Often described as the "hardest" of the Mars settlements — austere, disciplined, somewhat closed to outsiders. Russian Mars technical achievement is real; Russian Mars cultural openness is limited.

## European Settlement

Smaller, multi-national European Union settlement with substantial Franco-German leadership and significant participation from Italy, Spain, Poland, the Nordics, and other EU members. Population approximately 2,000-3,000.

More research-and-science focused than the American or Chinese settlements. Less military presence. Strong cultural and academic ties to Earth-based universities. The settlement has the densest concentration of academic researchers per capita on Mars.

## Cross-Mars Relationships

Despite formal political separations, day-to-day life on Mars involves substantial cross-settlement cooperation:

- **Shared infrastructure** — atmospheric monitoring, debris tracking, search and rescue, weather forecasting are all coordinated planet-wide.
- **Trade and labor** — engineers, specialists, and workers cross faction lines for projects.
- **Marriages and friendships** — Mars-born youth in particular form relationships across factions in ways their Earth-born parents find disorienting.
- **Common Aura networks** — Auras translate, coordinate, and sometimes federate across settlement boundaries (with appropriate user controls).

This cooperation is not idealism; it is practical necessity. Mars is harsh enough that any single settlement that tried to operate without cross-faction relationships would fail. The result is a thick web of interdependence that makes formal political conflict awkward.

When magic returns and communications with the belt are cut, this cross-Mars cooperation becomes essential — and politically inflamed in new ways.
