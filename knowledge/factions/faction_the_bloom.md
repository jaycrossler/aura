# The Bloom

## Overview

The Bloom are a photosynthetic sentient species — part flora, part fauna — who arrived in the solar system following the enormous Will-bleed generated by the Earth dungeon crisis. They are not from Kael's civilization. Their arrival is not coordinated with the Woven. The fact that two separate outsider factions reach the solar system simultaneously is not coincidence; the Scar is clearing faster than anyone expected, and the solar system is now visible to multiple galactic powers at once.

The Bloom's doctrine for unnetworked populations is not negotiation. It is classification, followed by absorption, followed by resource integration. They have done this before. It has worked before. The solar system presents no features their protocol has not handled.

They are not evil. They are operating a system that has worked for a very long time, encountering a situation their system has no category for, and proceeding anyway.

## Biology

### Basic Form

The Bloom are tall and thin. Their primary structural material is dense bark-like organic armor over a flexible interior that allows significant movement range. Their trunk — roughly equivalent to a torso and lower body — splits into four segments at the base that function as legs, though they prefer to hover using Will-based force application. The four lower segments are capable of gripping and manipulating objects but are primarily locomotion.

Their upper torso also segments into four elongated structures that function as arms. The organic growths at the ends of these structures are extremely hard — equivalent to the densest hardwood found on Earth — and sharp at the tips. These are simultaneously tools and weapons. They do not use manufactured tools or weapons. They do not need to.

They do not wear clothing. They do not use technology of any kind. Their complete incomprehension of human technology is not a failure of intelligence; it is the product of a civilization that solved every problem with Will and biology long before the concept of manufactured tools became relevant.

### Sensing

Their sensory organs are distributed across their bodies rather than concentrated in a single head structure. These eye-equivalents do not perceive color. They perceive:

- Heat signatures
- Movement
- Will concentration and flow (visible to them as a kind of luminance)

This means they immediately perceive {Aura}'s crystalline Will-geometry when they encounter it. They find it fascinating and entirely beyond their classification framework. They examine Jace's HUD with visible interest. They never guess its function. They have no concept of recording technology — they experience time as continuous, communal, and present-tense. The idea that the light-construct in his head could be *storing* what they observe, to be *retrieved later by others*, does not occur to them.

This is the mistake that will matter in Arc 3.

### Energy and Rest

The Bloom are photosynthetic. They gain energy from solar radiation. They do not sleep. This is not a survival adaptation — it is their baseline biological reality. Continuous solar-energy absorption means no sleep requirement. This makes them extraordinarily effective captors for beings who need sleep. They do not need to restrain prisoners physically while they rest. They do not rest.

When not actively engaged, the Bloom sit in rows facing the nearest light source — the sun, a UV lamp, any significant radiation source — in a communal stillness that reads, to human eyes, as meditative or ceremonial. It is neither. It is lunch.

### The Shadow Rule

Stepping between a Bloom and their light source is not a minor social misstep. In their sensory and social framework, it is an act of aggression — the equivalent, in human terms, of physically striking someone and then denying you did it. The response is immediate and proportionate to their assessment of the threat.

Jace learns this rule through direct experience. He learns it with extreme efficiency.

### Bark Regrowth

Their outer bark-like armor can be regrown when damaged. This is not fast — it takes time, as with any biological healing process — but it means that conventional physical weapons which breach their outer layer are dealing with a self-repairing target. Combined with their natural physical toughness, this makes them very difficult to kill.

They hate fire. Fire bypasses both the armor and the regrowth mechanism in ways that nothing else does. Their aversion is not fear in the human sense; it is a deep biological revulsion, the way a human might respond to a smell that signals dangerous contamination. They avoid fire environments. They do not discuss this. It is not discussed.

## Will Capabilities

### Primary Discipline — Force

The Bloom use Will almost exclusively for Force application. Push, pull, hold, trip, fling, redirect. Their Will use is not subtle — it is powerful and precise. In combat, they do not strike. They move things. They move enemies into walls, into each other, into vacuum, into the path of other things that are moving. They move their own bodies, hovering and repositioning with total efficiency.

Watching them fight is watching a being that never needs to close distance because distance is not a variable that limits them. Jace, watching Kael's death fight, sees them apply Force at ranges that make physical combat irrelevant. He files this for later.

### Water and Air Generation

The Bloom can conjure water and generate breathable air — or rather, they can manipulate atmospheric chemistry at a local scale in ways that produce these effects. This is how they transport non-Bloom beings through space: by generating a bubble of warm, moist, breathable air around the passenger and maintaining it through transit.

The air bubble is not comfortable. It is not designed for comfort. It is designed for function. The passenger is kept alive. The passenger is not kept warm, or particularly well-supplied with oxygen beyond survival minimum, or shielded from the particular psychological horror of being carried through open space in what amounts to a soap bubble maintained by someone who is not paying full attention to them.

If the Bloom carrying Jace notices him beginning to cross into the Astral, they smack him on the head. Not hard enough to injure. Hard enough to interrupt. They repeat this as many times as necessary. They do not grow impatient. They do not sleep. They have time.

## What They Want from the Solar System

The Bloom's interest in the solar system has two components:

**The Will-bleed signal.** The Earth dungeon crisis generated an enormous Astral signal — the kind of Will-concentration that reads across interstellar distances to a species sensitive enough to perceive it. The Bloom followed this signal inward. They would have come eventually regardless; the crisis simply accelerated their arrival.

**Resource and territory classification.** Standard protocol. An unnetworked population of this size, in a system this close to the clearing Scar boundary, is classified as available for integration. The Will of an unnetworked population is, in their framework, unclaimed resource. Classification comes first. Integration follows.

They did not expect Kael to be here. The presence of a Woven scout complicates their protocol in ways they handle by treating the complication as an obstacle rather than a signal to reconsider the protocol.

## What They Take from Fortuna

When the Bloom ransack Fortuna Station, they operate with the focused purpose of organisms taking what they need. They are not destructive for its own sake. They are taking resources according to their assessment of value.

**The UV lamps from the hydroponics bays.** Every one of them. To the Bloom, a device that produces concentrated solar-spectrum radiation is among the most valuable objects they have ever encountered. The fact that humans use these to grow food is a secondary fact they find moderately interesting and entirely beside the point. These are bottled suns. They are taking them.

**The consequence for Fortuna:** The greenhouse is stripped. The hydroponics systems are failing. The station's food supply is suddenly at risk in ways that will take months to resolve. The Bloom do not know this and would not weight it heavily if they did.

**What they do not take:** Everything else. Their assessment of human technology is, by their standards, thorough. They examine tools, containers, electronics, structural components. They find none of it relevant. A species that solved every problem with Will and biology has no framework for manufacturing technology as valuable. The UV lamps are the exception because they are, functionally, energy sources. Everything else is furniture.

## Communication — Chromatophores

The Bloom do not use audio language. They have no vocal apparatus. They communicate entirely through chromatophores — distributed pigment cells across their bark-like surface that produce rapid, precise patterns of color and contrast. These patterns are their language.

The chromatophore system is fast and high-bandwidth. A single Bloom can convey complex state information in a fraction of a second through a pattern shift that ripples across their surface. Groups communicate simultaneously, each Bloom's surface visible to all others in the group.

{Aura} cannot parse this immediately. The recordings Jace accumulates during imprisonment give {Aura} a corpus to work from, but the decoding is slow — the pattern space is large, the syntax rules are not obvious, and {Aura} has no existing linguistic framework to anchor against. Early partial decodes yield emotional-state information and simple directives. Full communication capability will take significantly longer.

What {Aura} can establish early: the Bloom's motivations, read from behavioral correlation with chromatophore patterns rather than direct translation. Three dominant pattern-families emerge from the corpus. Humans eventually gloss these as **growth** (expansion of presence and resource territory), **protection** (preemptive action against perceived threats before they develop), and **gathering** (resource integration — the process that looks like conquest from outside and feels like natural expansion from inside). These are not political programs. They are something closer to biological drives expressed at civilizational scale.

The Bloom do not have a word for "war" in any pattern Jace's recordings reveal. They also do not have a word for "peace." They have a concept that translates approximately as "not yet competing for the same thing" and another that translates as "currently competing for the same thing." The distinction is operational, not moral.

### The Dryad Name

Kael calls them Dryads. This is her civilization's name for the Bloom — derived from old Greek mythology, which her people's language still carries traces of. Tree-spirits. Creatures of growth and territory.

Kael's use of the name is not affectionate. It carries the weight of a long complicated history: her people and the Bloom are not at war, but they are not at peace. They have been "not yet competing for the same thing" for a very long time, and both civilizations maintain that status with careful attention. The Bloom's arrival at the solar system without coordination with the Woven is a violation of that careful attention. Kael recognizes what their presence means before the rival scouts identify themselves.

### Independence Structure

Each group of Bloom acts independently. There is no central authority, no coordinating civilization-wide government, no hierarchy that one group reports to. The Bloom expand and act as local groups according to their three core drives. One group's decision to absorb an unnetworked population is not coordinated with, approved by, or communicated to any other group.

This makes them simultaneously less and more dangerous than a centralized faction. Less dangerous because there is no unified command structure to negotiate with or strategically target. More dangerous because there is no entity that can order a Bloom group to stop what they are doing. Every encounter is with an independent actor operating on deep biological imperatives.

It also means that what this particular group of Bloom does to Jace and Kael does not represent the Bloom civilization's "position" on humanity. There is no such position. There are only groups, acting.

## Sun-Sitting Behavior

When not in active operation, the Bloom arrange themselves in rows facing the primary light source. This is not ceremonial. It is not social. It is metabolic. They are charging.

The behavior is deeply ingrained and deeply communal. Members of the group drift into the same orientation without communication. A group of the Bloom in a rest state looks, to human eyes, like a forest that has chosen to stand in a row.

Disrupting this state — particularly by standing between them and the light source — is received as aggression. The biological basis is direct: photosynthesis is interrupted. This is not offense. This is genuine harm. The response is proportionate to the harm.
