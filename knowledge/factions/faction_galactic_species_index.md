# Nearby Galactic Civilians — Species Index

## Core Worldbuilding Principle

These are not monsters. They are alien peoples whose biology, Will-use, politics, and social behavior were compressed into myth by ancient humans during earlier periods of contact.

Ancient Greek names are used as Earth-facing mythic labels. These labels are not always accurate. In several cases, different Greek regions applied different names to the same alien species, or confused one species with another because humans encountered them under stress, in ritual settings, or through secondhand accounts.

**Ancient myth preserved distorted field reports. The myth was not false. It was low-resolution.**

## Why Greek Names Are Imperfect Taxonomy

Ancient Greek names should not map perfectly to alien taxonomy. The Greeks named encounters by appearance, effect, and story role.

- A single species could receive several names. The Naga could be *Sirens* when encountered through song at sea, *Lamiae* when encountered as beautiful serpent-women, and *Echidnae* when remembered as monstrous breeding queens.
- The Raksha could be *Harpies* when encountered as winged snatchers, *Empusae* when encountered as seductive night predators.
- The Mirror-Crowned could be *Gorgons* generally, while *Medusa* may refer to one specific Crowned individual whose encounter became famous.
- The Long could be *drakons* when seen as serpents, storm gods when seen at scale, and *Typhon-spawn* when associated with catastrophe.
- The Vetala could be *Keres* on battlefields and *shades* in tombs.

Ancient people did not have a xeno-taxonomic registry. They had sailors, priests, terrified survivors, poets, and rulers trying to turn contact events into memory.

## Galactic Human Context

Humanity is not unique to Earth. Before the Scar isolated Earth, humans had already spread across hundreds of planets and stations across thousands of light years. Most Astral-capable species know humans. They have traded with humans, ruled them, enslaved them, married into them, fought beside them, hunted them, protected them, and used them as oath-clients or Will-contributors for millennia.

**Most outside powers consider humans common, adaptable, noisy, short-lived, socially volatile, and useful.**

Earth humans are different.

For roughly 3,000 years, Earth was cut off from the Astral network. During that time, Earth humans lost active access to Will. Their unused Will did not vanish — much of it was siphoned into dungeon systems, Scar residue, and local hardening effects. The result is a branch of humanity with almost no mature magical technique but unusually hardened minds.

Earth humans are magically untrained but psychologically resistant. They are difficult to charm, dominate, enthrall, possess, pacify, memory-edit, or panic through standard Will-pressure. They also spent 3,000 years developing technical civilization without magical shortcuts — externalizing memory into books, code, sensors, databases, cameras, black boxes, distributed institutions, and machine audit trails.

**To old Astral civilizations, Earth is not merely a lost human world. It is an impossible human world: overpopulated, untemplated, technically accelerated, mentally hardened, and culturally undomesticated.**

## Will, Size, and Travel

Larger species often possess larger Will reservoirs. Whale-sized, titan-sized, and leviathan-class beings can cross enormous distances, carry passengers, anchor routes, or tear open difficult Astral terrain. Such beings function as strategic infrastructure.

Small species can also be dangerous. Fey-sized organisms may have fragile bodies and little physical strength, but some possess extreme Will density — they can slip through narrow Astral seams, ride in the Will-wakes of larger travelers, manipulate perception, or serve as couriers, saboteurs, assassins, and parasites.

Humans occupy a middle range. Normal diaspora humans have moderate Will and high social flexibility. Earth humans currently have weak active Will expression, high cognitive resistance, and extreme technical leverage.

## Template Civilization — The Civilizational Trap

Most Will-capable species eventually discover self-templating. A Template preserves the self at a chosen point, allowing healing, regeneration, lifespan extension, and identity restoration after injury.

It is also a civilizational trap.

Templating freezes growth. The body returns to the saved pattern, but the mind also returns *toward* the saved pattern. Injury heals. Trauma fades. Skill persists. But deep change becomes harder because the Template treats deviation as damage.

Old species can produce individuals with thousands of years of experience. However, those individuals may have only a few centuries of true adaptation. Their societies preserve mastery, ritual, hierarchy, and oath continuity. They do not develop technology rapidly because they have little incentive to accept instability, generational turnover, disposable prototypes, or disruptive education.

**Earth humans never templated.** They learned, died, replaced themselves, documented, argued, industrialized, automated, and kept changing.

This is why old civilizations are individually stronger, while Earth is systemically stranger.

## Earth Contact Implications

The nearby galactic civilian species do not discover "humans" when Sol reconnects. They discover *Earth humans*. That distinction matters.

To most old powers, humans are familiar. Earth humans are not. Earth humans are human-shaped but wrong in several ways:

- Too numerous
- Too unruled
- Too technically advanced
- Too mentally resistant
- Do not know the etiquette of submission
- Do not respect old route law
- Record everything
- Trust machines with memory
- Treat miracles as phenomena to instrument
- Have almost no magical skill, but their civilization behaves like a distributed anti-glamour engine

This makes Earth both vulnerable and dangerous. A single skilled Astral traveler can outperform almost any individual Earth human. A single old slaver can ruin a room full of untrained civilians. A single titan can cross distances no human ship can match.

**But Earth does not fight as individuals. Earth measures, records, distributes, automates, manufactures, trains, revises, and scales.**

The old galaxy has stronger people. Earth has stronger systems.
