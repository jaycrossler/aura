# United States Military and Intelligence (Mars Command)

## Summary

The United States Military and Intelligence (Mars Command) represents the armed forces and security agencies of the United States of America stationed on Mars. Operating out of Joint Base Terminus, they function under the political umbrella of the **United States of Mars** (the 56th U.S. State). The core political concept behind their presence is that statehood does not require geographical contiguity on the same planet; Mars is treated as a natural extension of U.S. territory. 

Mars Command maintains local planetary defense, orbit-to-surface security, drone surveillance, and covert intelligence-gathering networks. They represent a highly organized, technologically advanced military machine that is increasingly isolated from Washington by comms lag and political drift.

## Origin and History

Following the initial colonization waves in the mid-to-late 21st century, the United States established a permanent military footprint to protect its industrial investments, colony infrastructure, and resident citizens. When the territory was formally admitted as the 56th State, the military presence transitioned from an expeditionary force to a domestic federal establishment. Joint Base Terminus was constructed to anchor this presence, carved into the Martian bedrock alongside the expanding tunnels of the city of Terminus.

## Government and Structure

Mars Command operates as a joint task force consisting of:
- **U.S. Air Force (Mars Detachment):** Primary operators of the planetary drone surveillance fleets. Handled surface security, aerial reconnaissance, and logistics coordination.
- **U.S. Space Force:** Controls orbital tracking grids, communication relays, and deep-space threat detection.
- **U.S. Intelligence Operations:** A covert network including representatives from the Defense Intelligence Agency (DIA) and specialized operatives (such as {Mr. Sylvester} and {Mei}) who monitor political stability, corporate espionage, and foreign enclaves.

Decisions are formally made by the Base Commander (a Space Force Colonel), but operational latitude is massive due to the 20-minute time delay back to Earth. The intelligence division operates with near-total autonomy.

## Membership and Demographics

The personnel are a mix of:
- **Earth-born assignees:** Career officers and intelligence specialists rotated in from Earth commands. They tend to maintain strong institutional loyalties to Washington.
- **Mars-born enlistees:** A growing number of second-generation Martian citizens who join for career advancement, debt relief, or citizenship benefits. They are culturally distinct and hold a greater local loyalty to Mars itself.

## Ideology and Values

Officially, Mars Command stands for the defense of the 56th State, the preservation of American liberties, and the stability of Martian trade routes. In practice, they are heavily aligned with megacorporate logistical partners (like Origin Industries) and are obsessed with information security and containing the rumors of "belt anomalies." They treat the first signs of magic not as a spiritual event, but as an existential threat to electronic infrastructure and geopolitical stability.

## Military and Security Capabilities

Mars Command utilizes advanced technology to project power across the Martian surface:
- **Drone Fleets:** Thousands of automated atmospheric and surface drones that maintain a constant surveillance grid over the U.S. sector. Jace Grant was a decorated commander of these fleets before his medical discharge.
- **MilX Implants:** Military personnel are augmented with advanced neural HUD implants that stream real-time telemetry, targeting data, and tactical overrides.
- **Orbital Relays:** The Space Force controls a network of surveillance satellites that monitor the surface and coordinate with inner-system relays.

## External Relations

- **Chinese Mars Settlement (Pangu):** Relations are characterized by cold-war style strategic competition. They share border coordinates and trade protocols but actively monitor each other's research and military developments.
- **The Belter Stations:** Officially supportive of trade security; covertly, intelligence operatives monitor belt stations for destabilizing technologies or resource monopolies.

## Story Function

Mars Command serves as the institutional backdrop that shapes Jace Grant's early life and skills. His Air Force training, drone-operator competence, and HUD-augmented reflexes are products of this faction. After Jace's medical discharge, the intelligence branch (via {Mr. Sylvester}) becomes his shadow sponsor, offering to reactivate his HUD and clear his debts in exchange for reporting on anomalies in the belt. This intelligence arrangement links Jace to the active operative {Mei} and drags him into the heart of the magical outbreak.
