# The Belter Stations

## Summary

The Belter Stations are not a single political entity. They are a loose network of five major mining and habitation stations distributed across the asteroid belt and Jupiter trojan space, each anchored to a named large asteroid and serving as a central hub for dozens of smaller worker ships representing different companies or nations. At story start, "Belter" identity is a description, not a political identity — but that is changing rapidly, and the communications blackout will accelerate the change.

Total belter population at story start: approximately 1,000-2,000 across all major stations, with additional population at smaller outposts and the worker ships that cluster around each hub. Each major station holds 80-450 people. Smaller mining outposts and waypoints hold dozens.

## The Hub-and-Spoke Model

Each major station is a hub: it provides life support infrastructure, communications relay, medical facilities, fuel, food, and trade for a surrounding network of smaller corporate and national operator ships that use the station's resources. The hub is not the only presence near its asteroid — typically dozens of smaller vessels are in the vicinity at any given time, prospecting, mining, or transiting. The hub is where everyone goes for resupply, medical care, and human contact.

This model means that "Fortuna Station" refers both to the permanent habitat and to the larger operational community — the ships, the claims, the workers who cycle through — anchored around 3 Fortuna.

## The Five Major Stations

### Fortuna Station (3 Fortuna — inner belt, C-type, ~225km)
The largest and most agriculturally developed station. American-anchored, Origin Industries primary operator, with strong U.S. government backing. Population approximately 300-450 and growing.

Fortuna is the agricultural hub of the inner belt — its livestock program and food production capacity make it strategically important for belt-wide food supply. It is Jace Grant's destination, and the primary setting of Book 1's belt chapters.

See `/locations/location_fortuna_station.md`

### Psyche Station (16 Psyche — middle belt, M-type metallic, ~226km)
The most economically valuable station in the belt. Chinese-anchored, Tianhe Cosmics primary operator. Population approximately 250-400.

16 Psyche is an exposed planetary core — predominantly iron-nickel with significant platinum-group metals. Psyche Station's metallic output is strategically significant for both belt and Mars construction. Chinese control of this station is a geopolitical fact that other factions work around.

See `/locations/location_psyche_station.md`

### Juno Station (3 Juno — inner belt, S-type, ~233km)
A well-established construction-materials station. Governance TBD (European or American-adjacent). Population approximately 200-350.

S-type composition provides high-quality silicates and metallic minerals for habitat construction. Juno and Fortuna are natural trading partners in the inner belt.

See `/locations/location_juno_station.md`

### Sylvia Station (87 Sylvia — outer belt, X-type, ~286km, with moons Romulus and Remus)
Outer belt station with a unique triple-body anchor. Governance TBD. Population approximately 150-280.

The most complex orbital environment of the five — navigating the Sylvia system requires skill. The two moons (Romulus and Remus) are surveyed and partially operational, representing significant future expansion capacity. Higher flux exposure than inner-belt stations.

See `/locations/location_sylvia_station.md`

### Odysseus Station (143 Odysseus — Jupiter L4, D-type trojan, ~125km)
The farthest permanent human station, in the Jupiter trojan cluster at ~5.2 AU. Multinational consortium. Population approximately 80-150.

Odysseus is the smallest and most isolated of the five, but the most advanced in magical exposure. The resonance flux front reached it first. Artemis Grant is stationed here. Communications with Odysseus degraded before other stations and will cut out first.

See `/locations/location_odysseus_station.md`

## Inter-Station Relations

Despite different national/corporate anchors, the major stations cooperate substantially:

- **Safety standards.** Universal protocols for emergency response, life support failures, and environmental hazards. Set by an informal Belt Safety Council that all stations participate in.
- **Trade.** Stations exchange specialized goods, expertise, and labor. Fortuna supplies food and livestock; Psyche supplies metals; Juno supplies construction silicates; Sylvia supplies outer-belt specialty materials.
- **Communication networks.** A belt-wide low-bandwidth communication network operates independently of Earth-Mars comms. When the blackout hits, this network becomes critical.
- **Scientific cooperation.** Belt-wide research collaborations exist and intensify under the blackout.
- **Hub services.** Each station serves dozens of smaller operator ships — a practical interdependence that creates relationship webs across national and corporate lines.

Tensions also exist:

- **Resource claims** to specific asteroids and orbital regions.
- **Labor poaching** between corporate operators.
- **Regulatory disputes** when one station's practices affect another.
- **The Psyche problem** — Chinese control of the most valuable metallic asteroid creates ongoing friction with other stations.

## The Emerging "Belter" Identity

At story start, "Belter" is mostly a descriptor. Belters identify primarily by their station, their employer, or their national origin.

By later in the story arc — accelerated by the communications blackout, shared experience of magical phenomena, corporate behavior that benefits Earth-Mars over belters, and cross-station cooperation in crisis — "Belter" becomes a genuine identity. The hub-and-spoke model, which forces constant cross-cultural contact, accelerates this.

## Governance

At story start, no formal belt-wide government exists. The Belt Safety Council is informal and limited. Each station's own governance varies — typically corporate management with limited resident council participation.

Over story time, the blackout and magical emergence force formalization. Fortuna Station, as the largest and most food-independent station, is likely to be the political center of whatever formal belter governance emerges.
